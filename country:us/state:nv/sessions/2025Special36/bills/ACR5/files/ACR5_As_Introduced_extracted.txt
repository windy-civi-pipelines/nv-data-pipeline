Section 1:
A.C.R. 5
ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 5–SELECT
COMMITTEE ON HEALTH AND WELLNESS
PREFILED NOVEMBER 12, 2025
____________
SUMMARY—Directs the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
Government Affairs to conduct an interim study on
the activities of federal officials engaging in the
investigation or enforcement of immigration laws in
this State. (BDR R-19)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION—Directing the Joint
Interim Standing Committee on Government Affairs to
conduct an interim study on the activities of federal
officials engaging in the investigation or enforcement of
immigration laws in this State.
1 WHEREAS, Reports have emerged of federal immigration
2 enforcement agencies engaging in increasingly aggressive tactics to
3 enforce civil immigration laws, including, without limitation,
4 conducting federal immigration enforcement activities at or around
5 schools, hospitals, courthouses and places of worship; and
6 WHEREAS, In the past year, numerous persons, including United
7 States citizens and lawful residents, have alleged that they were
8 unlawfully detained, arrested or physically assaulted by federal
9 officials engaged in immigration enforcement activities; and
10 WHEREAS, The State of Nevada has an interest in ensuring that
11 the legal rights of its residents are not infringed by the Federal
12 Government; now, therefore, be it
13 RESOLVED BY THE ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, THE
14 SENATE CONCURRING, That the Joint Interim Standing Committee
15 on Government Affairs is hereby directed to conduct a study during
16 the 2025-2026 legislative interim on the activities of federal officials
17 engaging in the investigation or enforcement of immigration laws in
18 this State; and be it further
19 RESOLVED, That the study must include, without limitation:
-
*ACR5*
– 2 –
1 1. A review of any relevant data and other information
2 concerning the activities of federal officials engaging in the
3 investigation or enforcement of immigration laws in this State; and
4 2. Recommendations for legislation to ensure that such federal
5 officials comply with the laws of this State when performing such
6 duties; and be it further
7 RESOLVED, That any recommended legislation proposed by the
8 Committee must be approved by a majority of the members of the
9 Senate and a majority of the members of the Assembly appointed to
10 the Committee; and be it further
11 RESOLVED, That the Committee, pursuant to subsection 4 of
12 NRS 218E.330, shall prepare and submit a report of the study and
13 any recommendations for legislation to the Director of the
14 Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the 84th Session of the
15 Nevada Legislature; and be it further
16 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon
17 adoption.
H
