EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION—Directing the Joint Interim
Standing Committee on the Judiciary to conduct a study relating to
road safety.
1 RESOLVED BY THE ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, THE SENATE
2 CONCURRING, That the Joint Interim Standing Committee on the Judiciary is
3 hereby directed to conduct a study during the 2025-2026 legislative interim relating
4 to road safety; and be it further
5 RESOLVED, That the study must include, without limitation:
6 1. A review of the laws of this State concerning the operation of electric
7 bicycles , electric motorcycles and electric scooters and whether additional
8 regulation of electric bicycles , electric motorcycles and electric scooters is
9 necessary to promote public safety;
10 2. An examination of [data] :
11 (a) Data and other information concerning traffic violations committed in
12 school zones and an analysis of whether increasing the penalties associated with
13 traffic violations committed in school zones would promote public safety; and
14 (b) Best practices for improving safety in school zones; and
15 3. An evaluation of the power of local governments, including, without
16 limitation, school districts, to regulate electric bicycles , electric motorcycles and
17 electric scooters in their respective jurisdictions and to regulate traffic violations
18 committed in school zones, and whether additional powers related to these topics
19 should be afforded to local governments, including, without limitation, school
20 districts; and be it further
21 RESOLVED, That any recommended legislation proposed by the Committee
22 must be approved by a majority of the members of the Senate and a majority of the
23 members of the Assembly appointed to the Committee; and be it further
24 RESOLVED, That the Committee, pursuant to subsection 4 of NRS 218E.330,
25 shall prepare and submit a report of the study and any recommendations for
26 legislation to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the
27 84th Session of the Nevada Legislature; and be it further
28 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon adoption.
