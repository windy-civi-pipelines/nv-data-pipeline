Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT A.C.R. 4
ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 4–SELECT
COMMITTEE ON PUBLIC SAFETY AND SECURITY
PREFILED NOVEMBER 12, 2025
____________
Referred to Select Committee on Public Safety and Security
SUMMARY—Directs the Joint Interim Standing Committee on the
Judiciary to conduct a study relating to road safety.
(BDR R-29)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION—Directing the Joint
Interim Standing Committee on the Judiciary to conduct a
study relating to road safety.
1 RESOLVED BY THE ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, THE
2 SENATE CONCURRING, That the Joint Interim Standing Committee
3 on the Judiciary is hereby directed to conduct a study during the
4 2025-2026 legislative interim relating to road safety; and be it
5 further
6 RESOLVED, That the study must include, without limitation:
7 1. A review of the laws of this State concerning the operation
8 of electric bicycles, electric motorcycles and electric scooters and
9 whether additional regulation of electric bicycles, electric
10 motorcycles and electric scooters is necessary to promote public
11 safety;
12 2. An examination of:
13 (a) Data and other information concerning traffic violations
14 committed in school zones and an analysis of whether increasing the
15 penalties associated with traffic violations committed in school
16 zones would promote public safety; and
17 (b) Best practices for improving safety in school zones; and
18 3. An evaluation of the power of local governments, including,
19 without limitation, school districts, to regulate electric bicycles,
20 electric motorcycles and electric scooters in their respective
21 jurisdictions and to regulate traffic violations committed in school
22 zones, and whether additional powers related to these topics should
-
*ACR4_R1*
– 2 –
1 be afforded to local governments, including, without limitation,
2 school districts; and be it further
3 RESOLVED, That any recommended legislation proposed by the
4 Committee must be approved by a majority of the members of the
5 Senate and a majority of the members of the Assembly appointed to
6 the Committee; and be it further
7 RESOLVED, That the Committee, pursuant to subsection 4 of
8 NRS 218E.330, shall prepare and submit a report of the study and
9 any recommendations for legislation to the Director of the
10 Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the 84th Session of the
11 Nevada Legislature; and be it further
12 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon
13 adoption.
