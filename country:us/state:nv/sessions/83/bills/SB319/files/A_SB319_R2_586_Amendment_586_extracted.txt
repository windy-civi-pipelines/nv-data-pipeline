SUMMARY—Requires the Washoe County Board of County Commissioners,
Reno City Council and Sparks City Council to establish a board
to study the creation of a county fire protection district within
certain territory. (BDR S-1018)

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to county fire protection districts; requiring the Washoe County
Board of County Commissioners, Reno City Council and Sparks City
Council to establish a board to conduct a study relating to the creation
of a county fire protection district within certain territory; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes contiguous unincorporated territory lying within one or more
2 counties or incorporated territory lying within a consolidated municipality and not included in
3 any other fire protection district to, by petition and election, be formed into a county fire
4 protection district. (NRS 474.010-474.125) Existing law also authorizes a board of county
5 commissioners to, by ordinance, organize unincorporated territory within the county into a
6 county fire protection district. (NRS 474.460) Existing law further authorizes a board of
7 county commissioners in a county whose population is less than 700,000 (currently all
8 counties other than Clark County) to consolidate two or more county fire protection districts
9 within the county under certain circumstances. (NRS 474.533)
10 Section 15 of this bill requires: (1) the Washoe County Board of County Commissioners,
11 the Reno City Council and the Sparks City Council to establish, by interlocal agreement, a
12 board to study the creation of a county fire protection district to consolidate fire protection
13 services within certain territory within Washoe County, Reno and Sparks; and (2) the board to
14 prepare a written report of the results and any recommendations.

13 Sec. 15. 1. The Washoe County Board of County Commissioners shall, by
14 interlocal agreement adopted in accordance with the provisions of NRS 277.080 to
15 277.180, inclusive, establish a board to study the creation of a county fire protection
16 district to consolidate fire protection services within territory within Washoe
17 County and the Cities of Reno and Sparks. Washoe County and the Cities of Reno
18 and Sparks shall share the costs of the study, the costs of any additional consultants
19 and experts and any other cost necessary to conduct the study.
20 2. The board established pursuant to subsection 1 must be composed of:
21 (a) Two members of the Washoe County Board of County Commissioners,
22 appointed by the Washoe County Board of County Commissioners;
23 (b) Two members of the Reno City Council, appointed by the Reno City
24 Council; and
25 (c) Two members of the Sparks City Council, appointed by the Sparks City
26 Council.
27 3. The board shall:
28 (a) Evaluate the impact a consolidated county fire protection district will have
29 on the response times, preparation and costs of providing fire protection services in
30 Washoe County and the Cities of Reno and Sparks;
31 (b) Determine any legal mechanisms necessary to consolidate fire service
32 between the County and the Cities;
33 (c) Determine any assessments necessary to support the district;
34 (d) Determine and review the amount of any debt and liabilities of each former
35 fire protection district or fire department consolidated into a county fire protection
36 district for the purposes of determining how any such debt and liabilities may be
37 settled or paid;
38 (e) Evaluate any potential impacts on policies of insurance for fire;
39 (f) Perform any other acts necessary, proper and convenient to accomplish the
40 purposes of this section; and
41 (g) Not later than [July 1,] December 31, 2026, prepare a written report for
42 transmission to the Washoe County Board of County Commissioners, Reno City
43 Council and Sparks City Council with the results and recommendations of the
44 board and any recommendations for legislation.
45 4. A majority of members constitutes a quorum at any meeting. Any action of
46 the board must be approved by a majority of members and at least one member
47 appointed by each participating governing body.
48 5. The board may contract with other entities to assist the board in carrying
49 out the requirements of this section.
50 6. The board established pursuant to subsection 1 is a public body and is
51 subject to the requirements set forth in chapter 241 of NRS.
52 Sec. 15.5. (Deleted by amendment.)
53 Sec. 16. (Deleted by amendment.)
