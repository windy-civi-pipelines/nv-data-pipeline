SUMMARY—[Requires] Authorizes certain counties and cities [and certain
nonprofit organizations] to establish a program relating to the
adoption of certain animals. (BDR 20-1004)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to animals; [requiring] authorizing the governing body of [a
county or incorporated city and certain nonprofit organizations that
operate an animal shelter] certain counties and incorporated cities to
establish a program to waive the adoption fee of certain [pets] animals
adopted by a person who is a veteran, law enforcement officer or first
responder; [requiring the Department of Veterans Services to submit a
report to the Legislature concerning such programs;] and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes a board of county commissioners and city council to exercise
2 certain powers relating to the control of animals. (NRS 244.359, 266.325)
3 Sections 1 and 2 of this bill [require] authorize the governing body of a county or
4 incorporated city, respectively, that owns and operates an animal shelter , or appropriates
5 money to an animal shelter, to establish a program to waive the adoption fee of certain [pets]
6 animals adopted by a person who is a veteran, law enforcement officer or first responder.
7 [Sections 1 and 2 also require the governing body of a county or incorporated city that does
8 not own or operate an animal shelter, as a condition of any appropriation or contract or other
9 legal agreement with a nonprofit organization that operates an animal shelter in the county or
10 city, as applicable, to require the nonprofit organization to establish a program to waive the
11 adoption fee of certain pets adopted by a person who is a veteran, law enforcement officer or
12 first responder.]
13 Sections 1 and 2 require that , if established, these programs: (1) waive the adoption fee
14 for not more than one eligible [pet] animal from the animal shelter for a period of 6 months
Senate Amendment No. 512 to Senate Bill No. 325 Page 4
15 for each veteran, law enforcement officer or first responder who adopts [a pet;] an animal; (2)
16 require such a veteran, law enforcement officer or first responder to provide a valid form of
17 identification; (3) require the animal shelter to report to the governing body of the county or
18 city, as applicable, certain information concerning the program; and (4) require the animal
19 shelter to maintain records of each adoption made under the program. On or before October 1
20 of each even-numbered year, after the governing body of a county or city establishes such
21 a program, the governing body of the county or city is required to submit a report to the
22 [Department of Veterans Services] Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for
23 transmittal to the next regular session of the Legislature containing certain information
24 concerning the program. [Section 4 of this bill requires the governing body of the county or
25 incorporated city to submit the first report required by section 1 or 2, as applicable, on or
26 before October 1, 2026.
27 Section 3 of this bill requires the Department to submit a report to the Legislature on or
28 before December 31 of each even-numbered year that compiles the information from the
29 reports the Department received pursuant to sections 1 and 2.]
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 244 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. If a county owns and operates an animal shelter [,] or the board of
4 county commissioners appropriates money to an animal shelter to provide care
5 and shelter to animals, the board of county commissioners [shall] may establish a
6 program to waive the adoption fee of [a pet] an animal that is 6 months or older,
7 has been at the animal shelter for at least 7 consecutive days and is adopted from
8 the animal shelter by a person who is a veteran, law enforcement officer or first
9 responder.
10 2. [If a county does not own and operate an animal shelter, the board of
11 county commissioners shall require, as a condition of any appropriation or
12 contract or other legal agreement with a nonprofit organization that operates an
13 animal shelter in the county, that the animal shelter operated by the nonprofit
14 organization establish a program to waive the adoption fee of a pet at the animal
15 shelter that is 6 months or older, has been at the animal shelter for at least 7
16 consecutive days and is adopted from the animal shelter by a person who is a
17 veteran, law enforcement officer or first responder.
18 3.] A program established pursuant to subsection 1 [or 2] must:
19 (a) Waive the adoption fee for not more than one [pet] animal that meets the
20 requirements of subsection 1 [or 2, as applicable,] for a period of 6 months for
21 each veteran, law enforcement officer or first responder who adopts such [a pet]
22 an animal from the animal shelter;
23 (b) Require a veteran, law enforcement officer or first responder who wishes
24 to adopt [a pet] an animal under the program to present a valid form of
25 identification that confirms that he or she is a veteran, law enforcement officer or
26 first responder;
27 (c) Report to the board of county commissioners the number of [pets]
28 animals adopted under the program and whether each [pet] animal was adopted
29 by a veteran, law enforcement officer or first responder; and
30 (d) Require the animal shelter to maintain records of each adoption made
31 under the program.
32 [4.] 3. On or before October 1 of each [year,] even-numbered year after the
33 board of county commissioners establishes a program pursuant to subsection 1,
Senate Amendment No. 512 to Senate Bill No. 325 Page 5
1 the board of county commissioners shall submit a report to the [Department of
2 Veterans Services] Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to
3 the next regular session of the Legislature that includes the number of [pets]
4 animals adopted under a program established pursuant to subsection 1 [or 2] and
5 whether each [pet] animal was adopted by a veteran, law enforcement officer or
6 first responder.
7 [5.] 4. As used in this section:
8 (a) “Adoption fee” does not include a licensing fee, vaccination fee or any
9 fees for the medical treatment of the [pet.] animal.
10 (b) “Emergency medical provider” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS
11 450B.199.
12 (c) “Firefighter” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 450B.071.
13 (d) “First responder” means a firefighter or emergency medical provider.
14 (e) “Law enforcement officer” means any officer of the United States or this
15 State or a political subdivision thereof who is empowered by law to conduct
16 investigations or make arrests.
17 (f) “Veteran” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 417.125.
