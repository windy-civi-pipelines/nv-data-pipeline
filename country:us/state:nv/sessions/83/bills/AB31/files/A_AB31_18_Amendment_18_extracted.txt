A.B. No. 31—Provides for certain Medicaid reimbursement of providers of
nonemergency secure behavioral health transport services.
(BDR 38-368)

PREFILED NOVEMBER 14, 2024
_______________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Provides for certain Medicaid reimbursement of providers of
nonemergency secure behavioral health transport services.
(BDR 38-368)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; providing for certain reimbursement under Medicaid
of providers of nonemergency secure behavioral health transport
services; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services, through the
2 Division of Health Care Financing and Policy of the Department, to administer Medicaid.
3 (NRS 422.2357, 422.270) Section 1 of this bill requires the Director of the Department to
4 include under Medicaid a requirement that providers of nonemergency secure behavioral
5 health transport services that are covered by Medicaid be reimbursed for certain distances
6 traveled while going to pick up or returning from dropping off a patient. Section 2 of this bill
7 makes a conforming change to require the Director to administer the provisions of section 1 in
8 the same manner as the provisions of existing law governing Medicaid. Section 3 of this bill
9 requires the Director to apply for any federal authority that is necessary to increase by: (1) at
10 least 15 percent the rate of reimbursement for nonemergency secure behavioral health
11 transport services covered by Medicaid when the pick-up location or drop-off location, or
12 both, are located in a county whose population is less than 100,000 (currently all counties
13 other than Clark and Washoe Counties); and (2) at least 10 percent for all other nonemergency
14 secure behavioral health transport services covered by Medicaid.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 422 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. The Director shall, to the extent that federal financial participation is
4 available, include under Medicaid a requirement that providers of nonemergency
Assembly Amendment No. 18 to Assembly Bill No. 31 Page 4
1 secure behavioral health transport services that are covered by Medicaid be
2 reimbursed for the distance traveled between:
3 (a) The location where a vehicle of the provider is based and the location at
4 which the vehicle picks up a patient who is being transported; and
5 (b) The location at which a vehicle of the provider drops off a patient who is
6 being transported and the location where the vehicle is based.
7 2. The Department shall:
8 (a) Apply to the Secretary of Health and Human Services for any waiver of
9 federal law or apply for any amendment of the State Plan for Medicaid that is
10 necessary for the Department to receive federal funding to provide the
11 reimbursement described in subsection 1.
12 (b) Fully cooperate in good faith with the Federal Government during the
13 application process to satisfy the requirements of the Federal Government for
14 obtaining a waiver or amendment pursuant to paragraph (a).
15 3. As used in this section, “nonemergency secure behavioral health
16 transport services” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 433.3317.
17 Sec. 2. NRS 232.320 is hereby amended to read as follows:
