EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to land use planning; requiring the governing body of certain
cities and counties to include a heat mitigation element in the master
plan; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires a planning commission to develop a master plan as a
2 comprehensive, long-term general plan for the physical development of the city, county or
3 region. A master plan may include certain elements as appropriate to the city, county or
4 region, with the exception of certain cities and counties which must include all or a portion of
5 certain elements in a master plan. (NRS 278.150-278.170)
6 Sections 1 and 3 of this bill require that the master plan in a county whose population is
7 100,000 or more (currently Clark and Washoe Counties) includes a heat mitigation element.
8 Section 2 of this bill sets forth the requirements for the heat mitigation element of a master
9 plan, including a plan to develop heat mitigation strategies such as public cooling spaces,
10 public drinking water and shade over paved surfaces.
