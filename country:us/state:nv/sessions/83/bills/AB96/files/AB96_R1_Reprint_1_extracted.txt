ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 96–COMMITTEE
ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS)

Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to master plans.
(BDR 22-397)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: No.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§§ 1, 3)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to land use planning; requiring the governing
body of certain cities and counties to include a heat
mitigation element in the master plan; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires a planning commission to develop a master plan as a
2 comprehensive, long-term general plan for the physical development of the city,
3 county or region. A master plan may include certain elements as appropriate to the
4 city, county or region, with the exception of certain cities and counties which must
5 include all or a portion of certain elements in a master plan. (NRS
6 278.150-278.170)
7 Sections 1 and 3 of this bill require that the master plan in a county whose
8 population is 100,000 or more (currently Clark and Washoe Counties) includes a
9 heat mitigation element. Section 2 of this bill sets forth the requirements for the
10 heat mitigation element of a master plan, including a plan to develop heat
11 mitigation strategies such as public cooling spaces, public drinking water and shade
12 over paved surfaces.

1 Section 1. NRS 278.150 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 278.150 1. The planning commission shall prepare and adopt
3 a comprehensive, long-term general plan for the physical
4 development of the city, county or region which in the
5 commission’s judgment bears relation to the planning thereof.
6 2. The plan must be known as the master plan, and must be so
7 prepared that all or portions thereof, except as otherwise provided in
8 subsections 3, 4 and 5, may be adopted by the governing body, as
9 provided in NRS 278.010 to 278.630, inclusive, as a basis for the
10 development of the city, county or region for such reasonable period
11 of time next ensuing after the adoption thereof as may practically be
12 covered thereby.
13 3. In counties whose population is less than 100,000, if the
14 governing body of the city or county adopts only a portion of the
15 master plan, it shall include in that portion an aboveground utility
16 plan of the public facilities and services element, as described in
17 subparagraph (3) of paragraph (e) of subsection 1 of NRS 278.160.
18 4. In counties whose population is 100,000 or more but less
19 than 700,000, if the governing body of the city or county adopts
20 only a portion of the master plan, it shall include in that portion:
21 (a) A conservation plan of the conservation element, as
22 described in subparagraph (1) of paragraph (a) of subsection 1 of
23 NRS 278.160;
24 (b) The housing element, as described in paragraph (c) of
25 subsection 1 of NRS 278.160;
26 (c) A population plan of the public facilities and services
27 element, as described in subparagraph (2) of paragraph (e) of
28 subsection 1 of NRS 278.160; [and]
29 (d) An aboveground utility plan of the public facilities and
30 services element, as described in subparagraph (3) of paragraph (e)
31 of subsection 1 of NRS 278.160 [.] ; and
32 (e) A heat mitigation element, as described in paragraph (i) of
33 subsection 1 of NRS 278.160.
34 5. In counties whose population is 700,000 or more, the
35 governing body of the city or county shall adopt a master plan for all
36 of the city or county that must address each of the elements set forth
37 in paragraphs (a) to [(h),] (i), inclusive, of subsection 1 of
38 NRS 278.160.

21 (1) A historic neighborhood preservation plan which:

27 neighborhoods.

34 (1) An inventory of housing conditions and needs, and plans
35 and procedures for improving housing standards and providing
36 adequate housing to individuals and families in the community,
37 regardless of income level.
38 (2) An inventory of existing affordable housing in the
39 community, including, without limitation, housing that is available
40 to rent or own, housing that is subsidized either directly or indirectly
41 by this State, an agency or political subdivision of this State, or the
42 Federal Government or an agency of the Federal Government, and
43 housing that is accessible to persons with disabilities.
44 (3) An analysis of projected growth and the demographic
45 characteristics of the community.

4 affordable housing and the development of policies to mitigate those
5 impediments.
6 (6) An analysis of the characteristics of the land that is
7 suitable for residential development. The analysis must include,
8 without limitation:
9 (I) A determination of whether the existing infrastructure
10 is sufficient to sustain the current needs and projected growth of the
11 community; and
12 (II) An inventory of available parcels that are suitable for
13 residential development and any zoning, environmental and other
14 land-use planning restrictions that affect such parcels.

16 construction of affordable housing or the conversion or
17 rehabilitation of existing housing to affordable housing.
18 (8) A plan for maintaining and developing affordable
19 housing to meet the housing needs of the community for a period of
20 at least 5 years.

22 (1) Provisions concerning community design, including
23 standards and principles governing the subdivision of land and
24 suggestive patterns for community design and development.

27 comprehensive plans for the most desirable utilization of land. The
28 land use plan:

30 transit-oriented development, master-planned communities and

37 and use of land that is under federal management within the city,
38 county or region, including, without limitation, a plan or statement
39 of policy prepared pursuant to NRS 321.7355.
40 (3) In any county whose population is 700,000 or more, a
41 rural neighborhoods preservation plan showing general plans to
42 preserve the character and density of rural neighborhoods.
43 (e) A public facilities and services element, which must include:
44 (1) An economic plan showing recommended schedules for
45 the allocation and expenditure of public money to provide for the

2 plan.
3 (2) A population plan setting forth an estimate of the total
4 population which the natural resources of the city, county or region
5 will support on a continuing basis without unreasonable impairment.
6 (3) An aboveground utility plan that shows corridors
7 designated for the construction of aboveground utilities and
8 complies with the provisions of NRS 278.165.
9 (4) Provisions concerning public buildings showing the
10 locations and arrangement of civic centers and all other public
11 buildings, including the architecture thereof and the landscape
12 treatment of the grounds thereof.
13 (5) Provisions concerning public services and facilities

17 NRS 278.145. If a public utility which provides electric service

19 substation will be required to support the master plan, those
20 facilities must be included in the master plan. The utility is not
21 required to obtain an easement for any such transmission line as a
22 prerequisite to the inclusion of the transmission line in the master
23 plan.
24 (6) A school facilities plan showing the general locations of
25 current and future school facilities based upon information furnished
26 by the appropriate county school district.
27 (f) A recreation and open space element, which must include a
28 recreation plan showing a comprehensive system of recreation areas,
29 including, without limitation, natural reservations, parks, parkways,
30 trails, reserved riverbank strips, beaches, playgrounds and other
31 recreation areas, including, when practicable, the locations and
32 proposed development thereof.
33 (g) A safety element, which must include:
34 (1) In any county whose population is 700,000 or more, a
35 safety plan identifying potential types of natural and man-made
36 hazards, including, without limitation, hazards from floods,
37 landslides or fires, or resulting from the manufacture, storage,
38 transfer or use of bulk quantities of hazardous materials. The safety
39 plan may set forth policies for avoiding or minimizing the risks from
40 those hazards.
41 (2) A seismic safety plan consisting of an identification and
42 appraisal of seismic hazards such as susceptibility to surface
43 ruptures from faulting, to ground shaking or to ground failures.
44 (h) A transportation element, which must include:

1 (1) A streets and highways plan showing the general
2 locations and widths of a comprehensive system of major traffic
3 thoroughfares and other traffic ways and of streets and the
4 recommended treatment thereof, building line setbacks, and a
5 system of naming or numbering streets and numbering houses, with
6 recommendations concerning proposed changes.
7 (2) A transit plan showing a proposed multimodal system of
8 transit lines, including mass transit, streetcar, motorcoach and
9 trolley coach lines, paths for bicycles and pedestrians, satellite
10 parking and related facilities.
11 (3) A transportation plan showing a comprehensive
12 transportation system, including, without limitation, locations of
13 rights-of-way, terminals, viaducts and grade separations. The
14 transportation plan may also include port, harbor, aviation and
15 related facilities.
16 (i) A heat mitigation element, which must include a plan to
17 develop heat mitigation strategies. The plan to develop heat
18 mitigation strategies may include, without limitation, such factors
19 as access to public cooling spaces, public drinking water, cool
20 building practices, shade over paved surfaces and other mitigation
21 measures to address heat in the community. Shade over paved
22 surfaces may include, without limitation, shade structures and
23 urban tree canopies, with preference for native tree or drought-
24 tolerant species.
25 (j) An urban agricultural element, which must include a plan to
26 inventory any vacant lands or other real property owned by the city
27 or county and blighted land in the city or county to determine
28 whether such lands are suitable for urban farming and gardening.
29 The plan to inventory any vacant lands or other real property may
30 include, without limitation, any other real property in the city or
31 county, as deemed appropriate by the commission.
32 2. The commission may prepare and adopt, as part of the
33 master plan, other and additional plans and reports dealing with such
34 other elements as may in its judgment relate to the physical
35 development of the city, county or region, and nothing contained in
36 NRS 278.010 to 278.630, inclusive, prohibits the preparation and
37 adoption of any such element as a part of the master plan.
