EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to interscholastic activities; requiring the Nevada Interscholastic
Activities Association to adopt regulations that set forth the process
for the Association to designate [lacrosse as] a sanctioned sport;
providing certain requirements for such regulations; requiring the
Association to [establish a fund to support certain infrastructure and
services for pupils who participate in a sanctioned sport; requiring the
Association to complete a study examining the development of pupils
as athletes and the infrastructure necessary to transform pupils into
professional athletes;] publish such regulations on the Internet
website maintained by the Association; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes the formation of the Nevada Interscholastic Activities
2 Association to control, supervise and regulate all interscholastic athletic events and other
3 interscholastic events in public schools in this State. (NRS 385B.050) Under existing law, the
4 Association is required to adopt rules and regulations as is necessary to govern such
5 interscholastic activities and events. (NRS 385B.060) Section 2 of this bill requires the
6 Association to adopt regulations that: (1) set forth the process for the Association to
7 designate [lacrosse as] a sanctioned sport; [and] (2) [include provisions] set forth standards
8 for determining, without limitation, the rules governing [lacrosse competitions.] such
9 sanctioned sports; and (3) prioritize the designation of certain club sports, including,
10 without limitation, lacrosse, as a sanctioned sport. Section 2 additionally requires such
11 regulations to be published on the Internet website maintained by the Association.
12 Section 5 of this bill requires the Association to adopt such regulations not later than
13 [January] October 1, [2026.
14 Section 3 of this bill requires the Association to establish the Pipeline Infrastructure Fund
15 to support the development and management of infrastructure and services necessary for
16 pupils to participate in sanctioned sports. Section 3 additionally: (1) sets forth approved uses
Senate Amendment No. 271 to Senate Bill No. 305 Page 4
17 for money deposited in the Fund; (2) authorizes the Association to accept gifts, grants and
18 donations from any source for deposit in the Fund; and (3) requires the interest and income
19 earned on the money in the Fund to be credited to the Fund. Finally, section 3 requires the
20 Executive Director of the Association to: (1) prepare an annual report containing certain
21 information concerning the Fund; and (2) submit the report to the Director of the Legislative
22 Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education.
23 Section 4 of this bill requires the Association to conduct a study regarding: (1) the
24 development of pupils as athletes in this State; and (2) the infrastructure required to transform
25 pupils who participate in sanctioned sports into professional athletes. Section 4 additionally
26 sets forth: (1) the required contents of the study; (2) the deadline for completion of the study;
27 and (3) a requirement that a report of the findings of the study be submitted to the Director of
28 the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
29 Education. Furthermore, section 4: (1) authorizes the Association to request the drafting of not
30 more than 1 legislative measure which relates to matters within the scope of the study; and (2)
31 sets forth requirements for the submission of the legislative measure for drafting.] 2025.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 385B of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. 1. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association shall adopt
4 regulations that set forth the process for the Association to designate [lacrosse as]
5 a sanctioned sport. The regulations adopted pursuant to this section must
6 include, without limitation, provisions:
7 [1. Governing]
8 (a) That establish the process for:
9 (1) Determining the rules governing the eligibility and participation of
10 pupils [;
11 2.] in a sanctioned sport;
12 (2) Prescribing the rules used in competition;
13 [3.] (3) Establishing the length of a season of [lacrosse] a sanctioned sport
14 and the date upon which a school may begin practicing for a season of [lacrosse;
15 4.] a sanctioned sport;
16 (4) Establishing guidelines for the safety of pupils that comply with any
17 regulations adopted pursuant to NRS 385B.080; [and
18 5.] (5) Prescribing any other guidelines necessary to govern participation in
19 [lacrosse competitions.] a sanctioned sport; and
20 (b) That prioritize the designation of club sports that have been established at
21 schools, including, without limitation, lacrosse, as a sanctioned sport.
22 2. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association shall publish any
23 regulations adopted pursuant to this section on the Internet website maintained
24 by the Association.
25 Sec. 3. [1. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association shall
26 establish a fund to be known as the Pipeline Infrastructure Fund to support the
27 development and management of infrastructure and services necessary for pupils
28 to participate in sanctioned sports, including, without limitation, lacrosse. The
29 Association must use the money deposited in the Fund to:
30 (a) Hire or contract with persons to assist in implementing and managing the
31 growth of athletics programs at schools;
32 (b) Provide resources to schools to facilitate the athletic development and
33 guidance of pupils who participate in a sanctioned sport; and
34 (c) Provide wrap-around services.

1 2. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association may accept gifts,
2 grants and donations from any source for deposit in the Fund.
3 3. The interest and income earned on the money in the Fund, after
4 deducting any applicable charges, must be credited to the Fund.
5 4. The Executive Director of the Nevada Interscholastic Activities
6 Association shall, on or before July 1 of each year:
7 (a) Prepare a report that describes:
8 (1) The total amount of money deposited in the Fund;
9 (2) Any expenditures of money made from the Fund; and
10 (3) Progress made in achieving the goals of the Fund set forth in
11 subsection 1; and
12 (b) Submit the report to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for
13 transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education.
14 5. As used in this section, “wrap-around services” means support services
15 provided to pupils who participate in a sanctioned sport, including, without
16 limitation, tutoring or other supplemental academic achievement programs,
17 college and career counseling and mentoring. ] (Deleted by amendment.)
18 Sec. 4. [1. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association shall study
19 the development of pupils as athletes in this State and the infrastructure necessary
20 to develop a pipeline to transform pupils who participate in a sanctioned sport into
21 professional athletes. The study must include, without limitation:
22 (a) A review of existing:
23 (1) Athletic facilities, including, without limitation, practice facilities,
24 arenas, stadiums, playing fields and playing surfaces;
25 (2) Resources for coaches; and
26 (3) Athletic programs at schools which are members of the Nevada
27 Interscholastic Activities Association; and
28 (b) An examination of, and recommendations for:
29 (1) Legislation to promote the ability of Nevada to produce professional
30 athletes;
31 (2) A statewide program to promote participation in sports, including,
32 without limitation, lacrosse, for pupils in underserved communities; and
33 (3) Establishing wrap-around services in public schools to promote
34 academic achievement and the development of life skills for pupils who are
35 athletes.
36 2. On or before June 30, 2026, the Nevada Interscholastic Activities
37 Association shall submit a report of the findings of the study conducted pursuant to
38 this section to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the
39 Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education.
40 3. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association may request the drafting
41 of not more than 1 legislative measure which relates to matters within the scope of
42 the study conducted pursuant to this section. The request must be submitted to the
43 Legislative Counsel on or before December 31, 2026, and must be on a form
44 prescribed by the Legislative Counsel. A legislative measure requested pursuant to
45 this subsection must be prefiled on or before the first day of the regular session of
46 the Legislature in 2027. A legislative measure that is not prefiled on or before that
47 day shall be deemed withdrawn.
48 4. As used in this section:
49 (a) “Sanctioned sport” means any athletic competition that is approved by the
50 Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association.
51 (b) “Wrap-around services” means support services provided to pupils who
52 participate in a sanctioned sport, including, without limitation, tutoring or other
Senate Amendment No. 271 to Senate Bill No. 305 Page 6
1 supplemental academic achievement programs, college and career counseling and
2 mentoring. ] (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 5. The Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association shall adopt the
4 regulations [designating lacrosse as a sanctioned sport, as] required by section 2 of
5 this act [,] not later than [January] October 1, [2026.] 2025.
6 Sec. 6. [The provisions of NRS 218D.380 do not apply to any provision of
7 this act which adds or revises a requirement to submit a report to the Legislature.]
8 (Deleted by amendment.)
9 Sec. 7. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
