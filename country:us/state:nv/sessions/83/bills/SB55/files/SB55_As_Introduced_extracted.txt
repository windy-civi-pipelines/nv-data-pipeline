Section 1:
REQUIRES TWO-THIRDS MAJORITY VOTE
(§§ 14, 15 & NRS 561.153)
S.B. 55
REVENUE AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT
(ON BEHALF OF THE LIEUTENANT GOVERNOR)
PREFILED NOVEMBER 20, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Revenue and
Economic Development
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to agricultural
tourism. (BDR 18-508)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to tourism; requiring the Commission on Tourism
to develop and administer a grant program relating to
agritourism; defining certain terms relating to agritourism;
requiring the Director of the State Department of
Agriculture to adopt regulations establishing the types of
businesses that qualify as agritourism; requiring the
Department to carry out certain marketing and outreach
duties relating to agritourism; authorizing the Director to
issue special use permits relating to agritourism under
certain circumstances; establishing certain provisions
relating to the liability of operators of businesses engaged
in agritourism; making appropriations; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Commission on Tourism in the Department of Tourism
2 and Cultural Affairs, which is charged with carrying out certain duties relating to
3 the promotion of tourism and travel in this State and oversees a grant program for
4 the development of projects relating to tourism. (NRS 231.8117, 231.8121,
5 231.8127, 231.8171-231.8179) Section 1 of this bill requires the Commission to
6 develop and administer a grant program for businesses that are engaged in or wish
7 to engage in agritourism in this State. Section 19 of this bill makes an appropriation
8 to the Commission for this grant program to award grants.
-
*SB55*
– 2 –
9 Sections 1 and 6 of this bill define “agritourism” as the combination of
10 agriculture, horticulture or viticulture with tourism to attract members of the
11 general public to visit a business for enjoyment, education, recreation or
12 participation in agritourism activities. Sections 1 and 7 of this bill define
13 “agritourism activity” as an activity offered by a business engaged in agritourism
14 that invites members of the general public to view or participate in activities
15 relating to agriculture, horticulture or viticulture.
16 Section 2 of this bill applies the definitions in existing law to section 1.
17 Existing law creates the Fund for Tourism and Cultural Affairs to support the
18 operations of the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs in carrying out
19 certain duties under existing law. (NRS 231.8141) Section 3 of this bill provides
20 that the money in the Fund may also be used to support the Department of Tourism
21 and Cultural Affairs in carrying out any duties under section 1.
22 Existing law creates the State Department of Agriculture and charges the
23 Department with administering various duties relating to agriculture, livestock and
24 food and other commodities. (NRS 561.035, Titles 49, 50 and 51 of NRS) Section
25 13 of this bill requires the Director of the State Department of Agriculture to adopt
26 regulations establishing the types of businesses that qualify as agritourism. Sections
27 8-12 of this bill define certain other terms relating to agritourism.
28 Section 14 of this bill requires the State Department of Agriculture to: (1)
29 develop opportunities for businesses engaged in or that wish to engage in
30 agritourism; (2) develop marketing campaigns for agritourism and agritourism
31 activities in this State; and (3) provide support and outreach to businesses engaged
32 in or that wish to engage in agritourism. Section 14 also requires the Director to
33 employ a qualified professional to assist the State Department of Agriculture in
34 carrying out these duties. Section 20 of this bill makes appropriations to the State
35 Department of Agriculture: (1) to carry out duties relating to the marketing and
36 development of agritourism in this State; (2) for membership in an organization
37 relating to agritourism and attendance at the convention of the organization; and (3)
38 for the salary of the qualified professional assisting the State Department of
39 Agriculture.
40 Section 15 of this bill authorizes the Director to issue a special use permit to an
41 operator of a business engaged in or that wishes to engage in agritourism if the
42 operator is denied a permit required to operate or offer agritourism activities on the
43 basis of a local ordinance, resolution or regulation adopted by a local governing
44 body. Section 15 also requires that any fees collected for a special use permit be
45 accounted for separately by the State Department of Agriculture and paid on an
46 annual basis to the county in which the special use permit was issued.
47 Section 17 of this bill requires an operator of a business engaged in agritourism
48 to post certain warning signs at the entrance of the business and at the site of any
49 agritourism activities and include in every contract offered or entered into by the
50 operator certain language that warns the reader that the operator is not liable for any
51 injury, loss, damage or death to a person or the personal property of a person that
52 results from the inherent risks of an agritourism activity.
53 Section 18 of this bill provides that an operator of a business engaged in
54 agritourism is not liable for any injury, loss, damage or death to a person or the
55 personal property of a person that results from the inherent risks of an agritourism
56 activity if the operator has posted the warning signs and included the warning
57 language in any contracts, as required by section 17. Section 16 of this bill sets
58 forth the inherent risks of an agritourism activity for the purposes of sections 17
59 and 18.

20 3. Nothing in this section shall be construed:
21 (a) To prevent or limit the liability of an operator of a business

23 (1) If the operator:

28 participant; or
29 (II) Has actual knowledge or reasonably should have
30 known of a dangerous condition on the land, facilities or
31 equipment used in the agritourism activity or the dangerous
32 propensity of a particular animal used in such agritourism activity
33 and does not make the danger known to the participant, and the
34 danger proximately causes an injury, loss, damage or death to a
35 participant or the personal property of a participant.
36 (2) In an action for product liability; or
37 (b) To prohibit an operator of a business engaged in
38 agritourism from asserting the assumption of risk as an
39 affirmative defense.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 231 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Commission on Tourism shall, subject to the
4 availability of funds appropriated for such purpose, develop and
5 administer a grant program to award grants to businesses engaged
6 in or that wish to engage in agritourism in this State.
7 2. The Commission shall adopt regulations to carry out the
8 provisions of this section. The regulations must prescribe, without
9 limitation:
10 (a) The criteria for eligibility to receive a grant from the grant
11 program; and
12 (b) Procedures for the submission and review of applications
13 to receive money from the grant program.
14 3. In adopting the regulations required pursuant to
15 subsection 2, the Commission shall consult with the Department of
16 Agriculture to establish the types of businesses which qualify as
17 agritourism.
18 4. As used in this section:
19 (a) “Agritourism” means the combination of agriculture,
20 horticulture or viticulture with tourism by a business, such as a
21 farm or ranch, to attract members of the general public to visit the
22 business for enjoyment, education, recreation or participation in
23 agritourism activities. The term does not include any business
24 which grows, farms, acquires, cultivates, processes, produces,
25 delivers, transports, supplies or sells cannabis, cannabis products,
26 hemp or a commodity or product made using hemp.
27 (b) “Agritourism activity” means any activity offered by a
28 business engaged in agritourism, regardless of whether there is a
29 charge for the activity, that invites members of the general public
30 to view or participate in activities relating to agriculture,
31 horticulture or viticulture, including, without limitation, farming,
32 ranching, harvest-your-own operations, activities relating to
33 natural resources, camping, brewing, wine-making, distilling and
34 preparing food items.
35 (c) “Cannabis” has the meaning ascribed to it in
36 NRS 678A.085.
37 (d) “Cannabis product” has the meaning ascribed to it in
38 NRS 678A.120.
39 (e) “Hemp” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 557.160.

3 Sec. 13. 1. The Director shall adopt regulations
4 establishing the types of businesses that qualify as agritourism for
5 the purposes of sections 5 to 18, inclusive, of this act which must
6 include, without limitation, farms, ranches and other outdoor
7 businesses that offer agritourism activities, such as wineries,
8 breweries, event venues, cottage food operations and businesses
9 that offer overnight camping on the premises.
10 2. As used in this section, “cottage food operation” has the
11 meaning ascribed to it in NRS 446.866.
12 Sec. 14. 1. The Department shall:
13 (a) Develop opportunities for businesses engaged in or that
14 wish to engage in agritourism in this State;
15 (b) Develop marketing campaigns to promote agritourism and
16 agritourism activities in this State;
17 (c) Provide outreach and support to businesses engaged in or
18 that wish to engage in agritourism in this State; and
19 (d) Coordinate with the Department of Tourism and Cultural
20 Affairs in carrying out the duties set forth in this subsection.
21 2. To assist in carrying out the duties set forth in subsection
22 1, the Director shall employ a full-time qualified professional and
23 determine the qualifications, duties and compensation of the
24 professional. Such employee is in the classified service of the
25 State.
26 Sec. 15. 1. If an operator of a business engaged in or that
27 wishes to engage in agritourism is denied a permit which is
28 required by an ordinance, resolution or regulation of a local
29 governing body to operate or offer agritourism activities, the
30 operator may appeal that decision to the Director. The Director
31 may issue a special use permit to the operator which supersedes
32 the ordinance, resolution or regulation of the local governing body
33 and enables the operator to engage in agritourism or offer an
34 agritourism activity.
35 2. All money collected from any fee charged for a special use
36 permit issued pursuant to this section must be accounted for
37 separately and paid on an annual basis to the county in which the
38 special use permit is issued.
39 3. The Director shall adopt regulations to carry out the
40 provisions of this section. Such regulations must include, without
41 limitation, a process whereby a governing body of a county may
42 review the special use permit and approve or deny the issuance of
43 the special use permit.
44 4. As used in this section, “local governing body” means:
45 (a) The governing body of a county, city or town; and

5 of NRS, NRS 269.024 to 269.0248, inclusive, and 450.550 to

7 Sec. 16. For the purposes of sections 17 and 18 of this act,
8 the inherent risks of an agritourism activity include, without
9 limitation:
10 1. Any dangers or conditions that are an integral part of the
11 agritourism activity, including, without limitation, surface and
12 subsurface conditions, the natural condition of the land,
13 vegetation or water, the behavior of wild or domestic animals and
14 the ordinary dangers of structures or equipment used in farming
15 and ranching operations; and
16 2. The potential for a participant to act in a negligent manner
17 that may contribute to injury, loss, damage or death to a
18 participant or the personal property of the participant or other
19 participants, including, without limitation, failing to follow
20 instructions given by an operator of a business engaged in
21 agritourism or failing to exercise reasonable caution while
22 participating in an agritourism activity.
23 Sec. 17. 1. An operator of a business engaged in
24 agritourism shall prominently post and maintain warning signs in
25 a clearly visible location at the entrance of the business and at the
26 site of any agritourism activity offered by the business to members
27 of the general public. The warning signs must, without limitation:
28 (a) Be written in black letters, with each letter not less than 1
29 inch in height; and
30 (b) Include the language set forth in subsection 3.
31 2. Every contract entered into by an operator of a business
32 engaged in agritourism on or off the site and every contract
33 offered by an operator at the location of any agritourism activity
34 must contain in clearly readable print the language set forth
35 subsection 3.
36 3. Every warning sign posted pursuant to subsection 1 and
37 contract subject to the provisions of subsection 2 must include the
38 following language:
39
40 WARNING
41 Under Nevada law, an operator of a business engaged in
42 agritourism is not liable for an injury, loss, damage or death
43 to a participant or the personal property of a participant of
44 an agritourism activity at this location if the injury or death
45 results from an inherent risk of the agritourism activity.

7 Sec. 18. 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 3, an
8 operator of a business engaged in agritourism is not liable for an
9 injury, loss, damage or death to a participant or the personal
10 property of a participant resulting from any inherent risk of an
11 activity offered by the business if the warnings required pursuant
12 to section 17 of this act are posted and included in any contracts in
13 accordance with the requirements of that section.
14 2. An operator of a business engaged in agritourism may
15 claim as an affirmative defense that an injury, loss, damage or
16 death to a participant or the personal property of a participant
17 resulted exclusively from an inherent risk of an agritourism
18 activity offered by the business and the warning was posted in
19 accordance with the requirements of section 17 of this act.
20 3. Nothing in this section shall be construed:
21 (a) To prevent or limit the liability of an operator of a business
22 engaged in agritourism:
23 (1) If the operator:
24 (I) Commits or omits an act if the act or omission
25 constitutes willful or wanton disregard for the safety of the
26 participant and that act or omission proximately causes an injury,
27 loss, damage or death to a participant or the personal property of a
28 participant; or
29 (II) Has actual knowledge or reasonably should have
30 known of a dangerous condition on the land, facilities or
31 equipment used in the agritourism activity or the dangerous
32 propensity of a particular animal used in such agritourism activity
33 and does not make the danger known to the participant, and the
34 danger proximately causes an injury, loss, damage or death to a
35 participant or the personal property of a participant.
36 (2) In an action for product liability; or
37 (b) To prohibit an operator of a business engaged in
38 agritourism from asserting the assumption of risk as an
39 affirmative defense.
