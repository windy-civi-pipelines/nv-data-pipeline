Section 1:
REQUIRES TWO-THIRDS MAJORITY VOTE (§ 1)
THIRD REPRINT S.B. 426
SENATE BILL NO. 426–COMMITTEE ON NATURAL RESOURCES
(ON BEHALF OF THE LEGISLATIVE COMMITTEE
FOR THE REVIEW AND OVERSIGHT OF THE
TAHOE REGIONAL PLANNING AGENCY AND
THE MARLETTE LAKE WATER SYSTEM)
MARCH 24, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Natural Resources
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to the Lake Tahoe
Basin Scenic Byway Corridor Recreation Safety
Zone. (BDR 43-374)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public safety; creating the Lake Tahoe Basin
Scenic Byway Corridor Recreation Safety Zone;
authorizing certain entities to establish certain parking
restrictions and prohibitions and enter into agreements
with other governmental entities to provide parking
enforcement and collect parking fees within the Safety
Zone; providing a penalty; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law sets forth the Tahoe Regional Planning Compact, an interstate
2 agreement between the States of California and Nevada pursuant to which the
3 bistate Tahoe Regional Planning Agency regulates environmental and land-use
4 matters within the Lake Tahoe Basin. (NRS 277.190-277.220) The Tahoe Regional
5 Planning Compact provides for the creation of the Tahoe transportation district as a
6 special purpose district managed by a board of directors which develops and
7 implements transportation plans and programs for the Lake Tahoe Basin.
8 Existing law also establishes the State Route 159 Safety Speed Zone and
9 authorizes: (1) the Director of the Department of Transportation to set the
10 maximum speed in the Speed Zone; and (2) the Department to ensure that adequate
11 signage and other forms of notice are installed. (NRS 484B.621) Section 1 of this
12 bill creates the Lake Tahoe Basin Scenic Byway Corridor Recreation Safety Zone,
13 consisting of any portion of U.S. Highway 50, Nevada State Route 28, Nevada
-
*SB426_R3*
– 2 –
14 State Route 431 and Nevada State Route 207 that is located in the Lake Tahoe
15 Region and directly connected to a recreational destination or that has otherwise
16 been designated as a scenic byway. Section 1 authorizes the Department and
17 certain local law enforcement agencies to: (1) establish certain traffic control
18 measures and parking restrictions and prohibitions within the Safety Zone; and (2)
19 enter into an agreement with one or more state agencies, local governments, local
20 governmental agencies in the Region or the Tahoe transportation district to develop
21 public parking, provide parking enforcement and issue parking citations or notices
22 of noncompliance within the Safety Zone. Section 1 further authorizes the
23 Department to: (1) establish and collect fees for public parking on certain land
24 within the Safety Zone; and (2) enter into agreements with certain governmental
25 entities to enforce and collect fees for parking within the Safety Zone. The proceeds
26 of: (1) such fees must be used to manage parking in the Lake Tahoe Region; and
27 (2) any fines imposed for violations of a parking restriction or prohibition, with
28 certain exceptions, must be used for parking enforcement within the Safety Zone.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 484B of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Lake Tahoe Basin Scenic Byway Corridor Recreation
4 Safety Zone is hereby created in the Lake Tahoe Region,
5 consisting of any portion of U.S. Highway 50, Nevada State Route
6 28, Nevada State Route 431 and Nevada State Route 207 that is
7 located in the Lake Tahoe Region and directly connected to a
8 recreational destination or that has otherwise been designated as a
9 Scenic Byway.
10 2. Within the Lake Tahoe Basin Scenic Byway Corridor
11 Recreation Safety Zone, the Department of Transportation and
12 any local law enforcement agency with jurisdiction in the Region
13 may:
14 (a) Place traffic control devices on any portion of highway in
15 the Safety Zone and implement measures of traffic calming;
16 (b) Prohibit or restrict parking on the shoulder of any highway
17 in the Safety Zone; and
18 (c) Prohibit or restrict a person from stopping, leaving
19 standing or parking a vehicle anywhere the Department of
20 Transportation or the local law enforcement agency determines
21 such activities are dangerous to other persons operating a vehicle
22 on a highway in the Safety Zone or would duly interfere with the
23 free movement of traffic on such highway.
24  A person shall not stop, park or leave standing any vehicle in
25 violation of a traffic control device, prohibition or restriction
26 established pursuant to this subsection.
27 3. In addition to the provisions of subsection 2:
-
*SB426_R3*
– 3 –
1 (a) The Department of Transportation may, on any land
2 owned or held by the Department of Transportation:
3 (1) Establish and collect fees for public parking; and
4 (2) Enter into an agreement with one or more state
5 agencies, local governments, local governmental agencies or the
6 Tahoe transportation district, as applicable, to enforce and collect
7 any fee established pursuant to this paragraph for public parking;
8 and
9 (b) The Department of Transportation or a law enforcement
10 agency may enter into agreements with one or more state
11 agencies, local governments with jurisdiction in the Region, local
12 governmental agencies or the Tahoe transportation district
13 relating to parking in the Safety Zone, including, without
14 limitation:
15 (1) The development of public parking in any right-of-way
16 or land owned by the State and pedestrian pathways for residents
17 and visitors who use such public parking to move from the public
18 parking to a recreational destination; and
19 (2) The enforcement of parking restrictions and
20 prohibitions established pursuant to subsection 2.
21 4. Any agreement entered into pursuant to subsection 3:
22 (a) Except as otherwise provided in paragraph (b), may
23 authorize the state agency, local government, local governmental
24 agency or the Tahoe transportation district to appoint employees,
25 who must be directly employed by the state agency, local
26 government, local governmental agency or Tahoe transportation
27 district, as applicable, to issue citations or notices of
28 noncompliance, manually or electronically, for a violation of any
29 parking restriction or prohibition established pursuant to
30 subsection 2 or a requirement to pay a fee established pursuant to
31 subsection 3. A citation or notice of noncompliance authorized by
32 this paragraph and issued pursuant to the agreement has the same
33 force and effect as a citation or notice issued by a peace officer.
34 The employee must file the original or a copy of the citation or
35 notice of compliance in the manner required pursuant to
36 NRS 484A.680.
37 (b) Must prohibit the issuance of citations or notices of
38 noncompliance in any area within the Lake Tahoe Basin Scenic
39 Byway Corridor Recreation Safety Zone where there is no access
40 to a recreation destination from the public parking using public
41 transit or pedestrian pathways.
42 (c) Must require the state agency, local government, local
43 governmental agency or Tahoe transportation district, as
44 applicable, for any employee appointed pursuant to paragraph (a),
45 to:
-
*SB426_R3*
– 4 –
1 (1) Establish minimum qualifications;
2 (2) Provide training to the employee before the employee
3 may issue citations or notices of noncompliance; and
4 (3) Provide appropriate equipment to the employee,
5 including, without limitation, uniforms or identifying attire and
6 books or electronic devices for issuing citations or notices of
7 noncompliance; and
8 (d) If the agreement is made with the Tahoe transportation
9 district, must require that any fees or fines collected by the Tahoe
10 transportation district be used in the Lake Tahoe Region to
11 manage parking in the Region.
12 5. A state agency, local government or local governmental
13 agency that appoints an employee to issue citations or notices of
14 noncompliance pursuant to subsection 4 is not liable for the
15 negligent acts or omissions of such an employee unless:
16 (a) The employee made a specific promise or representation to
17 a natural person who relied upon the promise or representation to
18 the person’s detriment; or
19 (b) The conduct of the employee affirmatively caused the
20 harm.
21  The provisions of this section are not intended to abrogate the
22 principle of common law that the duty of governmental entities to
23 provide services is a duty owed to the public, not to individual
24 persons.
25 6. Any fee established by the Department of Transportation
26 pursuant to subsection 3 and collected by the Department of
27 Transportation, a state agency, a local government or a local
28 governmental agency must be used to manage parking in the Lake
29 Tahoe Region, including, without limitation:
30 (a) The operation and maintenance of public parking and
31 pedestrian pathways;
32 (b) The enforcement of parking restrictions and prohibitions
33 and fees for public parking;
34 (c) The installation of adequate signage to support safety and
35 provide information to any person using the public parking; and
36 (d) The development, operation and maintenance of amenities
37 for any person using the public parking, including, without
38 limitation, restrooms and trash cans.
39 7. Any fine collected by the Department of Transportation, a
40 law enforcement agency, a state agency, a local government or a
41 local governmental agency pursuant to this section must be used
42 in the Lake Tahoe Region for enforcing parking restrictions and
43 prohibitions in the Safety Zone.
44 8. As used in this section:
-
*SB426_R3*
– 5 –
1 (a) “Lake Tahoe Region” or “Region” includes Lake Tahoe
2 and the adjacent parts of Douglas and Washoe Counties and
3 Carson City lying within the Tahoe Basin in the State of Nevada.
4 (b) “Public parking” means a parking space or parking
5 facility, including, without limitation, a parking deck, parking
6 garage, parking structure or paved or unpaved parking lot, that
7 members of the public are invited or permitted to enter and use.
8 (c) “Scenic Byway” has the meaning ascribed to it in the
9 National Scenic Byways Program, as issued by the United States
10 Federal Highway Administration in 60 Federal Register 26,759 on
11 May 18, 1995.
12 (d) “Tahoe transportation district” means the Tahoe
13 transportation district established by Article IX of the Tahoe
14 Regional Planning Compact.
15 (e) “Traffic calming”:
16 (1) Means a combination of measures and techniques
17 intended to:
18 (I) Reduce vehicular speeds;
19 (II) Promote safe and pleasant conditions for motorists,
20 bicyclists, pedestrians and residents;
21 (III) Improve the environment and usability of
22 roadways;
23 (IV) Improve real and perceived safety for nonmotorized
24 traffic; or
25 (V) Achieve any combination of the purposes set forth in
26 sub-subparagraphs (I) to (IV), inclusive.
27 (2) Does not include any combination of measures and
28 techniques that reduces the number of lanes for traveling on any
29 portion of U.S. Highway 50, Nevada State Route 28, Nevada State
30 Route 431 or Nevada State Route 207 that is part of the Lake
31 Tahoe Basin Scenic Byway Corridor Recreation Safety Zone
32 created by subsection 1.
