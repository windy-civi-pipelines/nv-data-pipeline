Section 1:
S.B. 30
(ON BEHALF OF THE STATE BOARD
OF PAROLE COMMISSIONERS)
PREFILED NOVEMBER 14, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions governing requests for public
information relating to parole. (BDR 16-252)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to the State Board of Parole Commissioners;
requiring the Board to disseminate records of decisions
regarding parole under certain circumstances; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the State Board of Parole Commissioners to compile and
2 maintain certain information relating to decisions regarding parole. (NRS
3 213.10887) This bill requires the Board to disseminate records of decisions
4 regarding parole to any person who requests such a record of a named prisoner.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 213.10887 is hereby amended to read as
2 follows:
3 213.10887 1. The Board shall compile and maintain detailed
4 information concerning all decisions regarding parole. The
5 information must include, but is not limited to:
6 (a) The Board’s reasons for each decision to grant, deny, revoke
7 or continue parole.
8 (b) The number of decisions made by the Board granting parole,
9 denying parole, revoking parole and continuing parole.
10 2. The Board shall:
-
*SB30*
– 2 –
1 (a) Organize and tabulate the information compiled pursuant to
2 this section at regular intervals, which must not exceed 3 months;
3 [and]
4 (b) Publish such information on its Internet website [.] ; and
5 (c) Upon request, disseminate records of decisions regarding
6 parole to any person who requests such a record of a named
7 prisoner, excluding any personal identifying information, after the
8 prisoner and any victims have been notified of the decision.
9 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
