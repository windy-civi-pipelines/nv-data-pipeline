Section 1:
A.B. 138
PREFILED JANUARY 28, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to curriculum standards
for teaching the personal safety of children.
(BDR 34-922)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring the Department of
Education to develop age-appropriate curriculum
standards for teaching the awareness and prevention of
sexual abuse to pupils; requiring the instruction of pupils
in personal safety to include dangers of physical and
sexual abuse; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Education, in consultation with
2 persons and organizations who possess knowledge and expertise in the personal
3 safety of children, to develop age-appropriate curriculum standards for teaching the
4 personal safety of children to pupils in kindergarten and grades 1 to 12. (NRS
5 389.031) Section 1 of this bill: (1) additionally requires the Department to develop
6 age-appropriate curriculum standards for teaching the awareness and prevention of
7 sexual abuse to pupils; and (2) revises the definition of “personal safety of
8 children” to include an age-appropriate recognition of various hazards and dangers
9 that are particular to children, including physical and sexual abuse.
10 Existing law requires the board of trustees of each school district and the
11 governing body of each charter school to ensure that instruction in the personal
12 safety of children, based on the standards developed by the Department, is
13 implemented as part of the course of study in health which is required to be taught
14 as applicable for grade levels and to the extent practicable in all public schools.
15 (NRS 389.018, 389.064) Section 2 of this bill specifies that the awareness and
16 prevention of sexual abuse is included in the personal safety of children for the
17 purposes of the instruction which must be implemented as part of the course of
18 study in health.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 389.031 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 389.031 1. The Department, in consultation with persons and
3 organizations who possess knowledge and expertise in the teaching
4 of personal safety of children, shall develop:
5 (a) Age-appropriate curriculum standards based on best
6 practices for teaching the personal safety of children to pupils in
7 kindergarten and grades 1 to 12, inclusive.
8 (b) Age-appropriate curriculum standards based on best
9 practices for teaching the awareness and prevention of sexual
10 abuse to pupils in kindergarten and grades 1 to 12, inclusive,
11 including, without limitation:
12 (1) Methods for increasing awareness among teachers,
13 pupils and parents, guardians or other caretakers of pupils of
14 issues regarding the sexual abuse of children, including, without
15 limitation, recognizing common indicators of sexual abuse;
16 (2) Actions that a child who is a victim of sexual abuse
17 should take to obtain assistance and intervention; and
18 (3) Counseling options that are available to pupils affected
19 by sexual abuse.
20 (c) Recommendations to assist a school district or charter school
21 in developing:
22 (1) A training plan to ensure that at least one employee at
23 each school, as designated by the principal, receives training on the
24 personal safety of children;
25 (2) Educational materials and information to be distributed to
26 parents, guardians or other caretakers of pupils regarding the
27 personal safety of children and how and when to teach and reinforce
28 concepts and skills of the personal safety of children; and
29 (3) Policies and procedures for the referral of a child who has
30 reported or experienced an incident that did or could have
31 threatened his or her personal safety, and his or her family or
32 guardian, if appropriate, to various services, including, without
33 limitation, counseling or any other available services or resources.
34 [(c)] (d) Recommendations of existing research-based programs
35 and curriculum samples to be considered for implementation.
36 2. The Department will review the standards and
37 recommendations developed pursuant to subsection 1 on an annual
38 basis to ensure that those standards and recommendations contain
39 current information.
40 3. The Department may apply for and accept grants, gifts,
41 donations, bequests or devises from any public or private source to
42 carry out the provisions of this section.
-
*AB138*
– 3 –
1 4. As used in this section, “personal safety of children” means
2 an age-appropriate recognition of various hazards and dangers that
3 are particular to children, including, without limitation, the danger
4 associated with unsafe persons, both known and unknown to the
5 child, physical and sexual abuse, becoming lost or separated from a
6 parent or guardian, and an awareness of age-appropriate steps a
7 child may take to avoid, lessen or alleviate those hazards and
8 dangers, including, without limitation, reporting threats of harm to a
9 responsible adult.
10 Sec. 2. NRS 389.064 is hereby amended to read as follows:
11 389.064 1. The board of trustees of each school district and
12 the governing body of each charter school shall ensure that
13 instruction in the personal safety of children, including, without
14 limitation, the awareness and prevention of sexual abuse, based on
15 the standards developed by the Department pursuant to NRS
16 389.031, be implemented as part of a course of study in health
17 prescribed pursuant to paragraph (c) of subsection 3 of
18 NRS 389.018.
19 2. The school district and the charter school, in accordance
20 with the recommendations provided by the Department pursuant to
21 subsection 1 of NRS 389.031, shall determine, for the instruction
22 required by subsection 1:
23 (a) The content of and materials to be used to provide the
24 instruction; and
25 (b) The grade levels in which the instruction will be provided.
26 3. A person who provides the instruction required by
27 subsection 1 must be:
28 (a) A licensed teacher;
29 (b) An employee of the school district with special knowledge
30 or training in the teaching of the personal safety of children;
31 (c) An employee of an agency which has as its primary purpose
32 the teaching of the personal safety of children;
33 (d) An employee of a law enforcement agency; or
34 (e) A volunteer of an agency which has as its primary purpose
35 the teaching of the personal safety of children and who meets the
36 requirements of subsection 8.
37 4. The school district and the charter school shall develop a
38 procedure for the notification of the parent or guardian of each pupil
39 to whom the instruction required by subsection 1 is to be provided.
40 The procedure must inform the parent or guardian that:
41 (a) The parent or guardian may submit a written request that the
42 pupil be excused from some or all of the instruction, except when
43 the instruction is included in a course which is required for
44 graduation; and
-
*AB138*
– 4 –
1 (b) All instructional materials to be used in the instruction
2 required by subsection 1 are available for inspection by the parent or
3 guardian at reasonable times and locations before the instruction is
4 provided.
5 5. A pupil whose parent or guardian submits a written request
6 pursuant to paragraph (a) of subsection 4 must be excused from such
7 instruction without any penalty as to credits or academic standing.
8 6. The school district and the charter school shall consider the
9 recommendations developed by the Department pursuant to
10 paragraph [(b)] (c) of subsection 1 of NRS 389.031 and, to the
11 extent money is available for this purpose, develop and implement:
12 (a) A training plan to ensure that all school employees receive
13 training as to the teaching of the personal safety of children;
14 (b) Educational materials and information to be distributed to
15 parents, guardians or other caretakers of pupils regarding the
16 teaching of the personal safety of children; and
17 (c) Policies and procedures for the referral of a child who has
18 reported or experienced an incident that did or could have
19 threatened his or her personal safety, and his or her family or
20 guardian, if appropriate, to various services, including, without
21 limitation, counseling or any other available services or resources.
22 7. On or before August 1 of each year, each board of trustees
23 and each governing body shall report to the Department for the
24 preceding year:
25 (a) The grade levels in which the instruction required by
26 subsection 1 was conducted;
27 (b) The curriculum content and materials distributed and utilized
28 for the instruction required by subsection 1;
29 (c) The person, persons or agency utilized to provide the
30 instruction required by subsection 1; and
31 (d) The number of reports or disclosures by pupils of incidents
32 that did or could have threatened their personal safety during the
33 preceding school year.
34 8. An agency which has as its primary purpose the teaching of
35 the personal safety of children, before allowing any volunteer of the
36 agency to provide instruction pursuant to paragraph (e) of
37 subsection 3, must ensure that the volunteer has successfully
38 completed:
39 (a) A national background check, which must include, without
40 limitation, a report of the criminal history of the volunteer from the
41 Federal Bureau of Investigation and the Central Repository for
42 Nevada Records of Criminal History;
43 (b) A child abuse and neglect screening through the Statewide
44 Central Registry for the Collection of Information Concerning the
-
*AB138*
– 5 –
1 Abuse or Neglect of a Child established pursuant to NRS 432.100;
2 and
3 (c) Adequate and appropriate training specific to providing
4 instruction regarding the personal safety of children.
5 9. An agency which has as its primary purpose the teaching of
6 the personal safety of children shall, upon request from a school
7 district or charter school and to the extent allowed by federal law,
8 make available to the school district or charter school
9 documentation of the agency’s conclusions regarding a volunteer’s
10 successful completion of the requirements of subsection 8.
11 10. A board of trustees of a school district and a governing
12 body of a charter school may apply for and accept grants, gifts,
13 donations, bequests or devises from any public or private source to
14 carry out the provisions of this section.
15 11. As used in this section, “personal safety of children” has
16 the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 389.031.
17 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
