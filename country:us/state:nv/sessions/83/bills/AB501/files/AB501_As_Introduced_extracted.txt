Section 1:
A.B. 501
YEAGER AND JAUREGUI
MARCH 24, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Authorizes certain pupils to receive academic credit
for completing certain outdoor recreational activities.
(BDR 34-1142)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; authorizing the approval of
academic credit for outdoor recreational activities under
certain circumstances; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prescribes certain courses of study required for a pupil to be
2 promoted to high school or to graduate from high school. (NRS 390.600, 392.033)
3 Existing law requires that a pupil be allowed to apply not more than one credit
4 towards the number of credits required for graduation from high school if the pupil
5 completes certain approved projects. (NRS 389.165) This bill authorizes the board
6 of trustees of a school district or the governing body of a charter school to authorize
7 a pupil enrolled in junior high or middle school or high school to receive credit
8 toward the number of credits required for the pupil to be promoted to high school or
9 to graduate from high school, as applicable, for completing certain approved
10 outdoor recreational activities outside of school hours.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 389 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The board of trustees of a school district or the governing
4 body of a charter school may authorize a pupil enrolled in junior
5 high or middle school or high school who completes an outdoor
6 recreational activity that is approved pursuant to subsection 2 to
-

– 2 –
1 receive credit toward the total number of credits required for
2 promotion to high school or graduation from high school, as
3 applicable. Any credit earned for completing an outdoor
4 recreational activity pursuant to this section must be applied
5 towards the pupil’s elective course credit.
6 2. The board of trustees of a school district or the governing
7 body of a charter school may approve for credit pursuant to
8 subsection 1 an outdoor recreational activity that:
9 (a) Is completed outside of school hours;
10 (b) Requires a pupil to spend a significant amount of time
11 outdoors; and
12 (c) Teaches the pupil skills that are relevant to outdoor
13 education which may include, without limitation:
14 (1) Identifying plants and animals native to this State;
15 (2) Building trails;
16 (3) Restoring natural habitats;
17 (4) Outdoor survival skills;
18 (5) Outdoor recreational skills, including, without
19 limitation, hiking and archery; and
20 (6) Bird watching.
21 3. If the board of trustees of a school district or the governing
22 body of a charter school approves outdoor recreational activities
23 for credit pursuant to subsection 1, the board of trustees or
24 governing body, as applicable, shall prescribe the:
25 (a) Maximum amount of total credits that a pupil may earn for
26 completing outdoor recreational activities;
27 (b) Rules regarding how a pupil may apply for such credit
28 upon the completion of an approved outdoor recreational activity;
29 and
30 (c) Procedures for obtaining the consent of a parent or legal
31 guardian of a pupil before the pupil may participate in an outdoor
32 recreational activity for which credit will be granted.
33 4. This section shall not be construed to require the board of
34 trustees of a school district or the governing body of a charter
35 school to provide, facilitate or spend money to provide, facilitate or
36 sponsor any outdoor recreational activity.
37 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
