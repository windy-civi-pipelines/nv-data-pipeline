SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the designation of certain highways.
(BDR 35-148)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to transportation; designating State Route 163 as the Colorado
River Highway; designating the bridge connecting State Route 163
with Bullhead City, Arizona, as the Donald J. Laughlin Memorial
Bridge; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides designations for certain highways in this State. (NRS 408.303,
2 408.307; Chapter 71, Statutes of Nevada 1987, at page 136) This bill designates: (1) State
3 Route 163 as the Colorado River Highway; and (2) the bridge connecting State Route 163
4 with Bullhead City, Arizona, as the Donald J. Laughlin Memorial Bridge. This bill also
5 requires the Director of the Department of Transportation to place suitable markers along the
6 highway and bridge in locations where the Director deems appropriate.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 408 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. 1. State Route 163, as now or hereafter located in this State, is
4 designated as the Colorado River Highway.
5 2. The Director shall place suitable markers along State Route 163 and may
6 locate them at such points as the Director deems appropriate.
7 Sec. 3. 1. The bridge which connects State Route 163 with Bullhead City,
8 Arizona, as now or hereafter located in this State, is designated as the Donald J.
9 Laughlin Memorial Bridge.
10 2. The Director shall place suitable markers along the bridge and may
11 locate them at such points as the Director deems appropriate.
