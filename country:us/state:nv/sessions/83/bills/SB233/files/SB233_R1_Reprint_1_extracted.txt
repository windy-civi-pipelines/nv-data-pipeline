Section 1:

FIRST REPRINT S.B. 233
SENATE BILL NO. 233–SENATORS DOÑATE AND FLORES
FEBRUARY 25, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Natural Resources
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to agriculture.
(BDR S-704)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to agriculture; requiring the Council on Food
Security within the Department of Health and Human
Services to conduct a study on the Home Feeds Nevada
Agriculture Food Purchase Program; making an
appropriation; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Director of the State Department of Agriculture to
2 establish the Home Feeds Nevada Agriculture Food Purchase Program to
3 supplement the supply of nutritious food available to persons through food banks
4 and other providers. (NRS 561.515)
5 Section 2 of this bill requires the Council on Food Security within the
6 Department of Health and Human Services to study issues relating to the long-term
7 financial stability of the Home Feeds Nevada Agriculture Food Purchase Program,
8 including, without limitation, identifying sustainable sources of revenue to fund the
9 Program. Section 2 also: (1) requires the Council to submit a written report of
10 the results of the study to the Director of the Department and the Director of the
11 Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the 84th Session of the Legislature;
12 and (2) authorizes the Council to request the drafting of not more than one
13 legislative measure for prefiling on or before the first day of the regular session of
14 the 2027 Legislature.
15 Section 3 of this bill makes an appropriation of $800,000 to the Nutritious
16 Food Purchase Account for the Director of the State Department of Agriculture to
17 carry out the Home Feeds Nevada Agriculture Food Purchase Program.
-
*SB233_R1*
– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. 1. The Council on Food Security created by NRS
3 232.4966 shall conduct a study during the 2025-2026 interim on the
4 Home Feeds Nevada Agriculture Food Purchase Program
5 established pursuant to NRS 561.515 concerning issues relating to
6 the long-term financial stability of the Program, which must include,
7 without limitation, identifying sustainable sources of revenue to
8 fund the Program.
9 2. On or before December 1, 2026, the Council shall prepare
10 and submit a written report describing the results of the study to:
11 (a) The Director of the State Department of Agriculture; and
12 (b) The Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for
13 transmittal to the 84th Session of the Legislature.
14 3. The Council may request the drafting of not more than one
15 legislative measure which relates to matters within the scope of the
16 Home Feeds Nevada Agriculture Food Purchase Program. The
17 request must be submitted to the Legislative Counsel on or before
18 December 1, 2026, and must be on a form prescribed by the
19 Legislative Counsel. A legislative measure requested pursuant to
20 this subsection must be prefiled on or before the first day of the
21 regular session of the 84th Session of the Legislature. A legislative
22 measure that is not prefiled on or before that day shall be deemed
23 withdrawn.
24 Sec. 3. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
25 General Fund to the Nutritious Food Purchase Account created by
26 NRS 561.505 the sum of $800,000 for use by the Director of the
27 State Department of Agriculture to carry out the Home Feeds
28 Nevada Agriculture Food Purchase Program established pursuant to
29 NRS 561.515.
30 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
31 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
32 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
33 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
34 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
35 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
36 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
37 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
38 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
39 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
40 Sec. 4. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
