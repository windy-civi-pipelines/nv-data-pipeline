SENATE BILL NO. 341–SENATOR OHRENSCHALL
MARCH 12, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to criminal procedure.
(BDR 14-119)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to criminal procedure; revising provisions relating
to the testimony of a witness given through the use of
audiovisual technology at a preliminary examination or
grand jury proceeding; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law allows a witness to testify at a preliminary examination or grand
2 jury proceeding through the use of audiovisual technology if: (1) the witness
3 resides more than 100 miles from the place of the preliminary examination or grand
4 jury proceeding; (2) the witness is unable to attend the preliminary examination or
5 grand jury proceeding because of a medical condition; or (3) good cause otherwise
6 exists. Existing law also provides that if a witness testifies at a preliminary
7 examination or grand jury proceeding through the use of audiovisual technology,
8 before giving testimony, the witness must be sworn and must sign a written
9 declaration which acknowledges that the witness: (1) understands that he or she is
10 subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of this State and may be subject to criminal
11 prosecution for the commission of any crime in connection with his or her
12 testimony, including, without limitation, perjury; and (2) consents to such
13 jurisdiction. (NRS 171.1975, 172.138)
14 Sections 1 and 2 of this bill: (1) remove the authorization for a witness to
15 testify at a preliminary examination or grand jury proceeding through the use of
16 audiovisual technology on the ground that good cause otherwise exists to allow the
17 witness to so testify; (2) require the witness to sign a sworn, written declaration
18 before giving testimony and the State to file the declaration with the court not later
19 than 48 hours before the preliminary examination or grand jury proceeding; and (3)
20 require the State to file, together with the declaration, a notice stating whether it is
21 necessary for the witness to testify at the preliminary examination or grand jury
22 proceeding through the use of audiovisual technology because the witness resides
23 more than 100 miles from the place of the preliminary examination or grand jury

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 171.1975 is hereby amended to read as
2 follows:
3 171.1975 1. If a witness resides more than 100 miles from
4 the place of a preliminary examination or is unable to attend the
5 preliminary examination because of a medical condition, [or if good
6 cause otherwise exists,] the magistrate must allow the witness to
7 testify at the preliminary examination through the use of audiovisual
8 technology.
9 2. If a witness testifies at the preliminary examination through
10 the use of audiovisual technology:
11 (a) The testimony of the witness must be transcribed by a
12 certified court reporter; [and]
13 (b) Before giving testimony, the witness must [be sworn and
14 must] sign a sworn, written declaration, on a form provided by the
15 magistrate, which acknowledges that the witness understands that he
16 or she is subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of this State and
17 may be subject to criminal prosecution for the commission of any
18 crime in connection with his or her testimony, including, without
19 limitation, perjury, and that the witness consents to such jurisdiction
20 [.] ; and
21 (c) Not later than 48 hours before the time set for the
22 preliminary examination, the State must file with the court both
23 the declaration and a notice stating whether it is necessary for the
24 witness to testify at the preliminary examination through the use
25 of audiovisual technology because the witness:
26 (1) Resides more than 100 miles from the place of the
27 preliminary examination; or
28 (2) Is unable to attend the preliminary examination
29 because of a medical condition.
30 3. Audiovisual technology used pursuant to this section must
31 ensure that the witness may be:
32 (a) Clearly heard and seen; and
33 (b) Examined and cross-examined.
34 4. As used in this section, “audiovisual technology” includes,
35 without limitation, closed-circuit video and videoconferencing.

1 Sec. 2. NRS 172.138 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 172.138 1. If a witness resides more than 100 miles from the
3 place of a grand jury proceeding or is unable to attend the grand jury
4 proceeding because of a medical condition, [or if good cause
5 otherwise exists,] the district judge supervising the proceedings of
6 the grand jury must allow a witness to testify before the grand jury
7 through the use of audiovisual technology.
8 2. If a witness testifies at the grand jury proceeding through the
9 use of audiovisual technology:
10 (a) The testimony of the witness must be transcribed by a
11 certified court reporter appointed pursuant to NRS 172.215 in
12 accordance with the provisions of NRS 172.225; [and]
13 (b) Before giving testimony, the witness must [be sworn and
14 must] sign a sworn, written declaration, on a form provided by the
15 district judge, which acknowledges that the witness understands that
16 he or she is subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of this State and
17 may be subject to criminal prosecution for the commission of any
18 crime in connection with his or her testimony, including, without
19 limitation, perjury, and that the witness consents to such jurisdiction
20 [.] ; and
21 (c) Not later than 48 hours before the time set for the grand
22 jury proceeding, the State must file with the court both the
23 declaration and a notice stating whether it is necessary for the
24 witness to testify at the grand jury proceeding through the use of
25 audiovisual technology because the witness:
26 (1) Resides more than 100 miles from the place of the
27 grand jury proceeding; or
28 (2) Is unable to attend the grand jury proceeding because
29 of a medical condition.
30 3. Audiovisual technology used pursuant to this section must
31 ensure that the witness may be:
32 (a) Clearly heard and seen; and
33 (b) Examined.
34 4. As used in this section, “audiovisual technology” includes,
35 without limitation, closed-circuit video and videoconferencing.
36 Sec. 3. The amendatory provisions of this act apply to any
37 offense that is the subject of a preliminary examination or grand jury
38 proceeding commenced on or after October 1, 2025, regardless of
39 when the offense is committed.
