SENATE BILL NO. 281–SENATOR NGUYEN
MARCH 5, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to certain foster care and behavioral
health programs. (BDR S-144)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public welfare; requiring an allocation from the Fund for a
Resilient Nevada for a program for certain young adults; making [an
appropriation for a jail-based behavioral health program;]
appropriations; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) creates the Fund for a Resilient Nevada to hold the proceeds of certain
2 litigation by the State concerning the manufacture, distribution, sale or marketing of opioids;
3 and (2) provides for the use of the money in the Fund for statewide projects and distribution as
4 grants to address the impacts of opioid use disorder and other substance use disorders. (NRS
5 433.732-433.740) Existing law requires the Director of the Department of Health and Human
6 Services to administer the Fund. (NRS 433.732) Section 3 of this bill requires the Director to
7 make an allocation of $2,500,000 from the Fund to Clark County, Washoe County and the
8 Division of Child and Family Services of the Department for reimbursement of costs incurred
9 during the 2025-2027 biennium for providing a program to support persons who are 18 years
10 of age and older but less than 23 years of age and are transitioning from foster care to
11 independent living.
12 Section 3.5 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Division of Public and Behavioral
13 Health of the Department of Health and Human Services for allocation to Clark County and
14 Washoe County to provide a jail-based behavioral health program during the 2025-2027
15 biennium.
16 Section 3.7 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Center for the Application of
17 Substance Abuse Technologies within the School of Public Health at the University of
18 Nevada, Reno, for the provision of an online graduate certification concerning co-
19 occurring behavioral health and wellness.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 3. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the
4 Director of the Department of Health and Human Services shall allocate $2,500,000

1 of the money in the Fund for a Resilient Nevada created by NRS 433.732 for
2 reimbursement of costs incurred during the 2025-2027 biennium for providing a
3 program to support persons who are 18 years of age and older but less than 23 years
4 of age and are transitioning from foster care to independent living to:
5 1. Clark County for reimbursement of not more than the following amounts:
6 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ........................................................ $671,762
7 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ........................................................ $671,762
8 2. Washoe County for reimbursement of not more than the following
9 amounts:
10 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ........................................................ $518,257
11 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ........................................................ $534,296
12 3. The Division of Child and Family Services of the Department of Health
13 and Human Services for reimbursement of not more than the following amounts:
14 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 .......................................................... $51,962
15 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 .......................................................... $51,961
16 Sec. 3.5. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
17 the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and
18 Human Services the sum of $11,897,709 for the provision of a jail-based behavioral
19 health program during the 2025-2027 biennium.
20 2. Of the amount appropriated by subsection 1, the Division shall allocate for
21 use for the purpose set forth in subsection 1:
22 (a) The sum of $6,393,106 to Clark County; and
23 (b) The sum of $5,504,603 to Washoe County.
24 3. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1 must not
25 be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to which the
26 appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
27 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
28 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after September 17, 2027, by
29 either the entity to which the money was appropriated or the entity to which the
30 money was subsequently granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State
31 General Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
32 Sec. 3.7. 1. To the extent that money is available, there is hereby
33 appropriated from the Fund for a Resilient Nevada created by NRS 433.732 to
34 the Center for the Application of Substance Abuse Technologies within the
35 School of Public Health at the University of Nevada, Reno, the sum of $750,000
36 for the provision of an online graduate certification concerning co-occurring
37 behavioral health and wellness.
38 2. If there is insufficient money available to make the full appropriation
39 provided pursuant to subsection 1, there is hereby appropriated from the State
40 General Fund to the Center for the Application of Substance Abuse
41 Technologies within the School of Public Health at the University of Nevada,
42 Reno, a sufficient amount of money to pay the remaining amount of the
43 appropriation set forth in subsection 1 for the provision of an online graduate
44 certification concerning co-occurring behavioral health and wellness.
45 3. Any remaining balance of an appropriation made by subsection 1 or 2,
46 as applicable, must not be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by
47 the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity to which money
48 from the appropriation is granted or otherwise transferred in any manner,
49 and any portion of the appropriated money remaining must not be spent for
50 any purpose after September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money
51 was appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently granted
52 or transferred, and must be reverted to the appropriate fund on or before
53 September 17, 2027.
