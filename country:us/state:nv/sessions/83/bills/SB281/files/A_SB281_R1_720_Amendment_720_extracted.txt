SENATE BILL NO. 281–SENATOR NGUYEN
MARCH 5, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to certain foster care [.] and behavioral
health programs. (BDR S-144)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to [foster care;] public welfare; requiring an allocation from the
Fund for a Resilient Nevada for a program for certain young adults;
making an appropriation for a jail-based behavioral health
program; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) creates the Fund for a Resilient Nevada to hold the proceeds of certain
2 litigation by the State concerning the manufacture, distribution, sale or marketing of opioids;
3 and (2) provides for the use of the money in the Fund for statewide projects and distribution as
4 grants to address the impacts of opioid use disorder and other substance use disorders. (NRS
5 433.732-433.740) Existing law requires the Director of the Department of Health and Human
6 Services to administer the Fund. (NRS 433.732) Section 3 of this bill requires the Director to
7 make an allocation of $2,500,000 from the Fund to Clark County, Washoe County and the
8 Division of Child and Family Services of the Department for [use] reimbursement of costs
9 incurred during the 2025-2027 biennium for providing a program to support persons who are
10 18 years of age and older but less than 23 years of age and are transitioning from foster care to
11 independent living.
12 Section 3.5 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Division of Public and
13 Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and Human Services for allocation to
14 Clark County and Washoe County to provide a jail-based behavioral health program
15 during the 2025-2027 biennium.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 3. [1.] Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the
4 Director of the Department of Health and Human Services shall allocate $2,500,000
5 of the money in the Fund for a Resilient Nevada created by NRS 433.732 [to the
6 Division of Child and Family Services of the Department] for [use]
7 reimbursement of costs incurred during the 2025-2027 biennium for providing a
8 program to support persons who are 18 years
Senate Amendment No. 720 to Senate Bill No. 281 First Reprint Page 4
1 of age and older but less than 23 years of age and are transitioning from foster care
2 to independent living [.
3 2. The Division shall grant money from the allocation made pursuant to
4 subsection 1 to an agency which provides child welfare services, as defined in NRS
5 432B.030, to carry out such a program.]
6 to:
7 1. Clark County for reimbursement of not more than the following
8 amounts:
9 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ..................................................... $671,762
10 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ..................................................... $671,762
11 2. Washoe County for reimbursement of not more than the following
12 amounts:
13 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ..................................................... $518,257
14 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ..................................................... $534,296
15 3. The Division of Child and Family Services of the Department of Health
16 and Human Services for reimbursement of not more than the following
17 amounts:
18 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ....................................................... $51,962
19 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ....................................................... $51,961
20 Sec. 3.5. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund
21 to the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the Department of Health
22 and Human Services the sum of $11,897,709 for the provision of a jail-based
23 behavioral health program during the 2025-2027 biennium.
24 2. Of the amount appropriated by subsection 1, the Division shall allocate
25 for use for the purpose set forth in subsection 1:
26 (a) The sum of $6,393,106 to Clark County; and
27 (b) The sum of $5,504,603 to Washoe County.
28 3. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1
29 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to
30 which the appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the
31 appropriation is granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any
32 portion of the appropriated money remaining must not be spent for any
33 purpose after September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
34 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently granted or
35 transferred, and must be reverted to the State General Fund on or before
36 September 17, 2027.
37 Sec. 4. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
