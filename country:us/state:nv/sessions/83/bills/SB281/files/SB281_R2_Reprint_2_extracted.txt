Section 1:
EXEMPT
SECOND REPRINT S.B. 281
SENATE BILL NO. 281–SENATOR NGUYEN
MARCH 5, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to certain foster care and
behavioral health programs. (BDR S-144)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public welfare; requiring an allocation from the
Fund for a Resilient Nevada for a program for certain
young adults; making an appropriation for a jail-based
behavioral health program; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) creates the Fund for a Resilient Nevada to hold the proceeds
2 of certain litigation by the State concerning the manufacture, distribution, sale or
3 marketing of opioids; and (2) provides for the use of the money in the Fund for
4 statewide projects and distribution as grants to address the impacts of opioid use
5 disorder and other substance use disorders. (NRS 433.732-433.740) Existing law
6 requires the Director of the Department of Health and Human Services to
7 administer the Fund. (NRS 433.732) Section 3 of this bill requires the Director to
8 make an allocation of $2,500,000 from the Fund to Clark County, Washoe County
9 and the Division of Child and Family Services of the Department for
10 reimbursement of costs incurred during the 2025-2027 biennium for providing a
11 program to support persons who are 18 years of age and older but less than 23 years
12 of age and are transitioning from foster care to independent living.
13 Section 3.5 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Division of Public and
14 Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and Human Services for allocation
15 to Clark County and Washoe County to provide a jail-based behavioral health
16 program during the 2025-2027 biennium.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)

1 Sec. 3. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the
2 contrary, the Director of the Department of Health and Human
3 Services shall allocate $2,500,000 of the money in the Fund for a
4 Resilient Nevada created by NRS 433.732 for reimbursement of
5 costs incurred during the 2025-2027 biennium for providing a
6 program to support persons who are 18 years of age and older but
7 less than 23 years of age and are transitioning from foster care to
8 independent living to:
9 1. Clark County for reimbursement of not more than the
10 following amounts:
11 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 .................................. $671,762
12 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 .................................. $671,762
13 2. Washoe County for reimbursement of not more than the
14 following amounts:
15 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 .................................. $518,257
16 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 .................................. $534,296
17 3. The Division of Child and Family Services of the
18 Department of Health and Human Services for reimbursement of not
19 more than the following amounts:
20 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 .................................... $51,962
21 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 .................................... $51,961
22 Sec. 3.5. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
23 General Fund to the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the
24 Department of Health and Human Services the sum of $11,897,709
25 for the provision of a jail-based behavioral health program during
26 the 2025-2027 biennium.
27 2. Of the amount appropriated by subsection 1, the Division
28 shall allocate for use for the purpose set forth in subsection 1:
29 (a) The sum of $6,393,106 to Clark County; and
30 (b) The sum of $5,504,603 to Washoe County.
31 3. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
32 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
33 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
34 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
35 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
36 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
37 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
38 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
39 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
40 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
41 Sec. 4. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
