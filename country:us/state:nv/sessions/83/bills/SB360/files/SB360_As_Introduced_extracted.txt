Section 1:
S.B. 360
MARCH 17, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Imposes requirements relating to the safety of hotels.
(BDR 40-1034)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to hotels; requiring the State Board of Health to
adopt regulations requiring, with certain exceptions,
certain hotel operators in a county whose population is
100,000 or more to ensure that each occupied guest room
at the hotel receives daily room cleaning; requiring the
Board to adopt regulations requiring certain hotel
operators in a county whose population is 100,000 or
more to ensure that the interior of each occupied guest
room at the hotel is visually inspected by a room attendant
not less than every second consecutive day during a
guest’s occupancy to ensure the safety of the guest and
the public; authorizing a health authority to, upon
receiving a complaint, inspect a hotel to ensure and
enforce compliance with such regulations; establishing
that a person aggrieved by a violation of such regulations
may institute a civil action; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes the State Board of Health to adopt, amend and enforce
2 regulations consistent with law to regulate sanitation and sanitary practices in the
3 interests of public health. (NRS 439.200) Section 12 of this bill requires the Board
4 to adopt regulations to require, with certain exceptions, a hotel operator to ensure
5 that each occupied guest room at the hotel receives daily room cleaning if the hotel
6 is a: (1) resort hotel; or (2) hotel that contains more than 200 guest rooms or suites
7 of guest rooms. Section 13 of this bill requires the Board to adopt regulations to
8 require a hotel operator to ensure that the interior of each occupied guest room at
9 the hotel is visually inspected by a room attendant not less than every second
-
*SB360*
– 2 –
10 consecutive day during a guest’s occupancy to ensure the safety of the guest and
11 the public if the hotel is a: (1) resort hotel; or (2) hotel that contains more than 200
12 guest rooms or suites of guest rooms.
13 Existing law requires a health authority to enforce regulations relating to hotels.
14 (NRS 447.190) Section 14 of this bill authorizes a health authority to, upon
15 receiving a complaint, inspect a hotel to ensure and enforce compliance with the
16 regulations required by sections 12 and 13. Section 15 of this bill requires the
17 Director of the Department of Health and Human Services to adopt regulations to
18 prohibit a hotel operator from retaliating against a hotel employee for: (1)
19 participating in proceedings related to sections 11-16 of this bill; (2) disclosing or
20 threatening to disclose an activity, policy or practice that the hotel employee
21 reasonably believes to violate sections 11-16 or to otherwise pose a substantial and
22 specific danger to public health or safety; (3) refusing to participate in any such
23 activity, policy or practice; or (4) seeking enforcement of sections 11-16.
24 Section 16 establishes that a person aggrieved by a violation of the regulations
25 adopted pursuant to section 12, 13 or 14 may institute a civil action in a court of
26 competent jurisdiction.
27 Section 11 applies the provisions of sections 11-16 to a county whose
28 population is 100,000 or more (currently Clark and Washoe Counties).
29 Sections 2-10 of this bill define certain necessary terms.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 447 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 to 16, inclusive, of this
3 act.
4 Sec. 2. “Board” means the State Board of Health.
5 Sec. 3. “Department” means the Department of Health and
6 Human Services.
7 Sec. 4. “Guest room” means a private room made available
8 by a hotel for occupancy by a guest. The term includes, without
9 limitation, a guest room comprised of several interconnected
10 rooms.
11 Sec. 5. “Hotel employee” means any natural person in the
12 service of a hotel operator who provides such service under any
13 appointment or contract of hire or apprenticeship, express or
14 implied, oral or written, whether lawfully or unlawfully employed.
15 Sec. 6. “Hotel operator” means any owner or keeper of a
16 hotel. The term includes, without limitation, any person or
17 contractor who, in a managerial, supervisory or confidential
18 capacity, employs one or more hotel employees.
19 Sec. 7. “Occupied guest room” means a guest room for
20 which a guest is currently checked in, regardless of whether the
21 guest is physically present in the room.
22 Sec. 8. “Resort hotel” means:
23 1. A resort hotel, as defined in NRS 463.01865;
-
*SB360*
– 3 –
1 2. An establishment described in section 19 of chapter 452,
2 Statutes of Nevada 1997, at page 1713; or
3 3. A resort hotel described in section 20 of chapter 452,
4 Statutes of Nevada 1997, at page 1713.
5 Sec. 9. “Room attendant” means a hotel employee whose
6 principal duties are to clean and maintain guest rooms in a hotel.
7 Sec. 10. “Room cleaning” means the performance of
8 services or tasks that are necessary to maintain the cleanliness of
9 a guest room before, during or after a guest occupies the guest
10 room. The term does not include:
11 1. Organizing or maintaining inventory in a guest room;
12 2. Delivering inventory to a guest room;
13 3. Turndown service or other tasks associated with preparing
14 a bed that was already made; or
15 4. Conducting preventative or necessary maintenance to a
16 guest room, including, without limitation, repairs, replacement
17 and general maintenance of appliances, electronics, furniture,
18 doors, windows, carpets, walls, plumbing and other fixtures.
19 Sec. 11. The provisions of sections 11 to 16, inclusive, of this
20 act apply to a county whose population is 100,000 or more.
21 Sec. 12. 1. The Board shall adopt regulations requiring,
22 except as provided in subsection 2, a hotel operator to ensure that
23 each occupied guest room at the hotel receives daily room
24 cleaning if the hotel is:
25 (a) A resort hotel; or
26 (b) A hotel that contains more than 200 guest rooms or
27 designated suites of guest rooms.
28 2. The Board shall not require a hotel operator to ensure that
29 an occupied guest room receives daily room cleaning in
30 accordance with subsection 1 if:
31 (a) The hotel operator is unable to provide room cleaning due
32 to:
33 (1) The occurrence of an emergency; or
34 (2) A superseding government restriction prohibiting the
35 performance of the room cleaning; or
36 (b) The guest occupying the room:
37 (1) Conspicuously displays a “do not disturb” or “privacy”
38 marker on the door of the entrance to the guest room; or
39 (2) Opts out of daily room cleaning orally or in writing for
40 all or part of the duration of the guest’s stay in the guest room.
41 3. If a guest opts out of daily room cleaning in accordance
42 with paragraph (b) of subsection 2, the Board shall require the
43 hotel operator to maintain written documentation of the guest’s
44 choice to opt out for a period of not less than 3 years.
-
*SB360*
– 4 –
1 4. The Board shall adopt regulations prohibiting a hotel from
2 advising or incentivizing guests to decline daily room cleaning.
3 5. For the purposes of this section, “emergency” means an
4 occurrence or threatened occurrence for which action is necessary
5 to save lives, protect property or to protect the health and safety of
6 persons, or to avert the threat of damage to property or injury to
7 persons.
8 Sec. 13. 1. The Board shall adopt regulations requiring a
9 hotel operator to ensure that the interior of each occupied guest
10 room at the hotel is visually inspected by a room attendant not less
11 than every second consecutive day during a guest’s occupancy to
12 ensure the safety of the guest and the public if the hotel is a:
13 (a) Resort hotel; or
14 (b) Hotel that contains more than 200 guest rooms or
15 designated suites of guest rooms.
16 2. A room attendant performing a visual inspection of an
17 occupied guest room in accordance with subsection 1 may:
18 (a) Request the physical presence of either security personnel
19 or a manager for the hotel for the duration of an inspection of an
20 occupied guest room; and
21 (b) Decline to perform an inspection of an occupied guest
22 room until security personnel or a manager for the hotel is
23 present.
24 3. The Board shall adopt regulations prohibiting a room
25 attendant who performs a visual inspection of an occupied guest
26 room in accordance with subsection 1 from physically inspecting
27 or moving any property which belongs to the guest in the occupied
28 guest room.
29 Sec. 14. 1. The health authority may, upon receiving a
30 complaint, inspect a hotel to ensure compliance with the
31 provisions of the regulations adopted pursuant to sections 12 and
32 13 of this act.
33 2. Upon discovering a violation of the regulations adopted
34 pursuant to sections 12 and 13 of this act, and after notice and the
35 opportunity for a hearing, the health authority:
36 (a) Shall order the hotel operator to correct the violation.
37 (b) May impose an administrative fine of not more than $500
38 for each initial violation or $1,000 for each second or subsequent
39 violation.
40 (c) If the violation occurs at a hotel that is not a resort hotel,
41 may notify any local governmental entity responsible for licensing
42 or regulating the hotel. Upon receiving such notification, the local
43 governmental entity shall review the violation and may take
44 further action, including, without limitation, suspending or
45 revoking the license of the hotel, to enforce the regulations
-
*SB360*
– 5 –
1 adopted pursuant to sections 12 and 13 of this act. Such action
2 must be taken in accordance with any procedures established by
3 the local governmental entity for actions to enforce statutes or
4 regulations or impose disciplinary action generally.
5 (d) If the violation occurs at a resort hotel subject to the
6 jurisdiction of the Nevada Gaming Control Board, shall notify the
7 Nevada Gaming Control Board. Upon receiving such notification,
8 the Nevada Gaming Control Board may take further action to
9 enforce the regulations adopted pursuant to sections 12 and 13 of
10 this act. Such action must be taken in accordance with any
11 procedures established by the Nevada Gaming Control Board for
12 actions to enforce statutes or regulations or impose disciplinary
13 action generally.
14 Sec. 15. The Director of the Department shall adopt
15 regulations prohibiting a hotel operator from discharging,
16 reducing the compensation of, increasing the workload of,
17 imposing fees or charges on, changing the duties of or otherwise
18 taking adverse action against a hotel employee in retaliation for:
19 1. Participating in proceedings related to sections 11 to 16,
20 inclusive, of this act;
21 2. Disclosing or threatening to disclose to a supervisor or a
22 governmental entity an activity, policy or practice of a hotel
23 operator that the hotel employee reasonably believes to violate
24 sections 11 to 16, inclusive, of this act or otherwise poses a
25 substantial and specific danger to public health or safety;
26 3. Refusing to participate in any activity, policy or practice
27 described in subsection 2; or
28 4. Seeking enforcement of sections 11 to 16, inclusive, of this
29 act.
30 Sec. 16. 1. A person aggrieved by a violation of the
31 regulations adopted pursuant to section 12, 13 or 14 of this act
32 may institute a civil action in a court of competent jurisdiction.
33 2. A person instituting a civil action in accordance with this
34 section must:
35 (a) Notify the Department of the civil action; and
36 (b) Send the Department a copy of the complaint.
37  Failure to notify the Department of the civil action or send the
38 Department a copy of the complaint does not adversely affect such
39 a cause of action.
40 Sec. 17. NRS 447.003 is hereby amended to read as follows:
41 447.003 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise
42 requires, the words and terms defined in NRS 447.007 and 447.010 ,
43 and sections 2 to 10, inclusive, of this act have the meanings
44 ascribed to them in those sections.
-
*SB360*
– 6 –
1 Sec. 18. 1. This section and sections 1 to 10, inclusive, and
2 17 of this act become effective upon passage and approval.
3 2. Sections 11 to 16, inclusive, of this act become effective:
4 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
5 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
6 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
7 (b) On October 1, 2025, for all other purposes.
