Section 1:

MARCH 10, 2025

Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to waivers of fees
charged to students within the Nevada System of
Higher Education. (BDR 34-753)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; revising provisions governing
certain waivers of fees assessed against students enrolled
in a school within the Nevada System of Higher
Education; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides for a waiver of registration fees and laboratory fees, and
2 in certain cases, any other mandatory fees, assessed against a student enrolled in a
3 school within the Nevada System of Higher Education who is: (1) a member of the
4 active Nevada National Guard, including a recruit; (2) a child, widow or widower
5 of a person who was killed while performing his or her duties as a member of the
6 Nevada National Guard; (3) a veteran of the Armed Forces of the United States
7 who has been awarded the Purple Heart, or a child of such a veteran; (4) the spouse
8 or child of a person who is identified as a prisoner of war or missing in action while
9 performing his or her duties as a member of the Armed Forces of the United States;
10 (5) identified as a homeless or unaccompanied pupil; and (6) a member of a
11 federally recognized Indian tribe or nation, or certified as being a descendant of an
12 enrolled member of an Indian tribe or nation. Existing law provides varying criteria
13 for the programs and courses for which an eligible student may use these waivers.
14 (NRS 396.544, 396.5442, 396.5443, 396.5445, 396.5448, 396.5449) Sections 1-6
15 of this bill standardize the types of fees for which such waivers may be granted, and
16 the programs and courses for which such waivers may be used. Specifically,
17 sections 1-6 apply these waivers to the registration fees, laboratory fees and any
18 other mandatory fees for classes taken towards satisfying the requirements of an
19 undergraduate or graduate degree at a school within the Nevada System of Higher
20 Education.
