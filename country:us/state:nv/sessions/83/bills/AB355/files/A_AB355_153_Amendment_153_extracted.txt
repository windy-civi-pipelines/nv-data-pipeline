SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to education. (BDR 34-937)

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; providing that any money remaining in the Fund to
Assist School Districts in Financing Capital Improvements and the
Fund to Assist Rural School Districts in Financing Capital
Improvements at the end of the fiscal year does not revert to the State
General Fund; revising provisions relating to grants from the Fund
to Assist Rural School Districts in Financing Capital
Improvements; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates in the State Treasury the Fund to Assist School Districts in Financing
2 Capital Improvements and requires that the money in the Fund be used to provide grants to
3 school districts within which certain emergency conditions exist. (NRS 387.333, 387.3335)
4 Existing law additionally creates in the State Treasury the Fund to Assist Rural School
5 Districts in Financing Capital Improvements and requires that the money in the Fund be [used
6 to make] awarded by the Director of the Office of Finance in the Office of the Governor
7 as grants to school districts in counties whose population is less than 100,000 (currently all
8 counties other than Clark and Washoe Counties) and in which certain taxes have been
9 levied for [certain] capital projects [.] of the school district. (NRS 387.3341-387.3344) [This]
10 Sections 1 and 2 of this bill [provides] provide that any money remaining in those Funds at
11 the end of a fiscal year does not revert to the State General Fund and requires that the balances
12 in those Funds be carried forward to the next fiscal year.
13 Section 2.5 of this bill authorizes the Director of the Office of Finance to also make a
14 grant from the Fund to Assist Rural School Districts in Financing Capital Improvements
15 to a board of trustees of a school district in a county whose population is less than
16 100,000, regardless of whether certain taxes for capital projects of the school district
17 have been levied in that county, in an amount that is equal to: (1) any gift, grant or
18 donation made to the school district for a capital project of the school district located on
19 certain tribal land; or (2) any interest accrued on money received by a school district for
20 a capital project of the school district located on certain tribal land.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 387.333 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 387.333 1. The Fund to Assist School Districts in Financing Capital
3 Improvements is hereby created in the State Treasury, to be administered by the
4 Director of the Office of Finance. All money received and held by the State
5 Treasurer for the purpose of the Fund must be deposited in the Fund.
6 2. The Director of the Office of Finance may accept gifts and grants from any
7 source for deposit in the Fund.
8 3. The money in the Fund must be invested as the money in other state funds
9 is invested. All interest and income earned on the money in the Fund must be
10 credited to the Fund.
11 4. Claims against the Fund must be paid as other claims against the State are
12 paid.
13 5. Any money remaining in the Fund at the end of a fiscal year does not
14 revert to the State General Fund, and the balance in the Fund must be carried
15 forward to the next fiscal year.
16 Sec. 2. NRS 387.3341 is hereby amended to read as follows:
17 387.3341 1. The Fund to Assist Rural School Districts in Financing Capital
18 Improvements is hereby created in the State Treasury, to be administered by the
19 Director of the Office of Finance. All money received and held by the State
20 Treasurer for the purpose of the Fund must be deposited in the Fund.
21 2. The Director of the Office of Finance may accept gifts and grants from any
22 source for deposit in the Fund.
23 3. The money in the Fund must be invested as the money in other state funds
24 is invested. All interest and income earned on the money in the Fund must be
25 credited to the Fund.
26 4. Claims against the Fund must be paid as other claims against the State are
27 paid.
28 5. Money in the Fund must be used to make grants to school districts in
29 counties whose population is less than 100,000, for the following purposes:
30 (a) Construction, design or purchase of new buildings for schools, including,
31 but not limited to, teacherages, dormitories, dining halls, gymnasiums and
32 stadiums.
33 (b) Enlarging, remodeling or repairing existing buildings or grounds for
34 schools, including, but not limited to, teacherages, dormitories, dining halls,
35 gymnasiums and stadiums.
36 (c) Acquiring sites for building schools, or additional real property for
37 necessary purposes related to schools, including, but not limited to, playgrounds,
38 athletic fields and sites for stadiums.
39 6. Any money remaining in the Fund at the end of a fiscal year does not
40 revert to the State General Fund, and the balance in the Fund must be carried
41 forward to the next fiscal year.
42 Sec. 2.5. NRS 387.3343 is hereby amended to read as follows:
43 387.3343 1. Following any fiscal year in which a county levies a tax
44 imposed pursuant to NRS 387.3342, the board of trustees of the school district in
45 the county shall apply to the Director of the Office of Finance for a grant of money
46 from the Fund created pursuant to NRS 387.3341 on a form provided by the
47 Director.
48 2. Following any fiscal year in which a county levies a tax imposed pursuant
49 to NRS 387.3289, the board of trustees of the school district in the county may

1 apply to the Director of the Office of Finance for a grant of money from the Fund
2 created pursuant to NRS 387.3341 on a form provided by the Director.
3 3. In addition to the provisions of subsection 1 or 2, the board of trustees of
4 a school district in a county whose population is less than 100,000 may apply to

6 pursuant to NRS 387.3341, on a form provided by the Director, in any fiscal year
7 in which the board of trustees:

9 district that is located on qualified tribal land; or

13 4. The application for a grant of money from the Fund submitted pursuant to
14 this section must be accompanied by:
15 (a) A description of the capital project for which the grant is requested, which
16 must be a capital project described by subsection 5 of NRS 387.3341; and
17 (b) [Either:] As applicable:
18 (1) Documentation of the amount of the tax assessed pursuant to NRS
19 387.3342 for the capital project for which the grant is requested in the immediately
20 preceding fiscal year; [or]
21 (2) A statement by the board of trustees of the school district in a county
22 which has levied a tax imposed pursuant to NRS 387.3289 certifying the amount of
23 the proceeds of the tax imposed pursuant to NRS 387.3289 for the immediately
24 preceding fiscal year which will be dedicated to the capital project [.
25 4.] ; or
26 (3) Documentation relating to the amount of the gift, grant, donation or
27 accrued interest.
28 5. To the extent that money is available for that purpose in the Fund and if the
29 Director of the Office of Finance determines that the capital project for which the
30 grant is requested is a project for which a grant may be made from the Fund, the
31 Director shall make a grant of money from the Fund to a school district that submits
32 an application pursuant to [subsection 1 or 2] this section in an amount that is equal
33 to [:] , as applicable:
34 (a) The total amount of tax assessed by the county for the capital project
35 pursuant to NRS 387.3342 in the immediately preceding fiscal year; [or]
36 (b) If the school district is located in a county which levies a tax imposed
37 pursuant to NRS 387.3289, the amount of the proceeds of the tax imposed pursuant
38 to NRS 387.3289 for the immediately preceding fiscal year which the board of
39 trustees of the school district has certified will be dedicated to the capital project [.
40 5.] ; or
41 (c) One or both of the following:
42 (1) The gift, grant or donation made to the school district for the capital
43 project located on qualified tribal land.
44 (2) The interest accrued on money received by the school district for the
45 capital project on qualified tribal land.
46 6. The Director of the Office of Finance shall adopt regulations that prescribe
47 the annual deadline for submission of an application to the Director by a school
48 district pursuant to this section.
49 7. As used in this section, “qualified tribal land” means any real property:
50 (a) For which legal title is vested in, or held in trust for the benefit of, an
51 Indian tribe or an individual Native American, and which is subject to
52 restrictions against alienation pursuant to federal law; and
53 (b) Over which an Indian tribe exercises governmental power.
