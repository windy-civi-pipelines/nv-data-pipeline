Section 1:
SECOND REPRINT A.B. 433
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 433–COMMITTEE
ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS)
MARCH 13, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to public safety.
(BDR S-399)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public safety; creating the Purple Ribbon Task
Force on Firefighter and Peace Officer Wellness; setting
forth the membership and duties of the Task Force; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill creates the Purple Ribbon Task Force on Firefighter and Peace Officer
2 Wellness to review the existing policies of this State concerning personal wellness
3 initiatives that account for the mental and physical health of firefighters, peace
4 officers and other law enforcement personnel. This bill also: (1) prescribes the
5 membership and duties of the Task Force; and (2) requires the Task Force to submit
6 a written report describing its work and recommendations for transmittal to the
7 Senate and Assembly Standing Committees on Government Affairs for the 84th
8 Session of the Legislature.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. The Purple Ribbon Task Force on Firefighter
2 and Peace Officer Wellness is hereby created. The Task Force
3 consists of the following nine members:
4 (a) Two members appointed by the Majority Leader of the
5 Senate;
-
*AB433_R2*
– 2 –
1 (b) One member appointed by the Minority Leader of the
2 Senate;
3 (c) Three members appointed by the Governor;
4 (d) Two members appointed by the Speaker of the Assembly;
5 and
6 (e) One member appointed by the Minority Leader of the
7 Assembly.
8 2. To the extent practicable, membership of the Task Force
9 must include, without limitation:
10 (a) A representative from a local government;
11 (b) A representative of an employee organization, as defined in
12 NRS 288.040, that represents peace officers or a labor organization,
13 as defined in NRS 288.048, that represents peace officers;
14 (c) A resource officer with a behavioral health background;
15 (d) A representative from a state law enforcement agency,
16 including, without limitation, the Department of Public Safety or the
17 Department of Corrections; and
18 (e) A representative of an employee organization, as defined in
19 NRS 288.040, that represents firefighters or a labor organization, as
20 defined in NRS 288.048, that represents firefighters.
21 3. The Task Force shall:
22 (a) Review the existing policies of this State concerning
23 personal wellness initiatives that account for the mental and physical
24 health of firefighters, peace officers and other law enforcement
25 personnel; and
26 (b) Recommend personal wellness initiatives for state and local
27 law enforcement agencies and fire departments.
28 4. The members of the Task Force serve without
29 compensation.
30 5. Each member of the Task Force who is an officer or
31 employee of the State or a local government must be relieved from
32 his or her duties without loss of regular compensation so that the
33 member may prepare for and attend meetings of the Task Force and
34 perform any work necessary to carry out the duties of the Task
35 Force in the most timely manner practicable. A state agency or local
36 government shall not require an officer or employee who is a
37 member of the Task Force to make up the time the member is absent
38 from work to carry out his or her duties as a member, and shall not
39 require the member to take annual vacation or compensatory time
40 for the absence.
41 6. The Task Force shall, at its first meeting, elect a Chair and
42 Vice Chair from among its members.
43 7. The Task Force shall meet upon the call of the Chair or a
44 majority of the members of the Task Force.
-
*AB433_R2*
– 3 –
1 8. A majority of the members of the Task Force constitutes a
2 quorum for the transaction of business, and a majority of those
3 members present at the meeting is sufficient for any official action
4 taken by the Task Force.
5 9. The Task Force may apply for and accept any gift, donation,
6 bequest, grant or other source of money to carry out the duties of the
7 Task Force.
8 10. The Task Force shall prepare and submit a written report to
9 the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the
10 Senate and Assembly Standing Committees on Government Affairs
11 for the 84th Session of the Legislature. The report must include,
12 without limitation, a summary of the work and recommendations of
13 the Task Force.
14 Sec. 2. The members of the Purple Ribbon Task Force on
15 Firefighter and Peace Officer Wellness must be appointed pursuant
16 to section 1 of this act not later than October 1, 2025.
17 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025, and expires
18 by limitation on June 30, 2027.
