SUMMARY—Urges Congress to protect the public lands including and adjacent to

Effect on the State: No.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION—Urging Congress to protect the public lands
including and adjacent to Sunrise Mountain, Frenchman Mountain and
the Rainbow Gardens Area.
1 WHEREAS, The public lands including and adjacent to Sunrise Mountain,
2 Frenchman Mountain and the Rainbow Gardens Area are undeveloped natural
3 treasures located near the Las Vegas Valley; and
4 WHEREAS, These public lands contain colorful mountain ranges rising up from
5 the desert lowlands; and
6 WHEREAS, Gypsum Cave, which holds some of the earliest evidence of human
7 inhabitance in the western United States and is significant to Indian tribes, is
8 located on these public lands; and
9 WHEREAS, These public lands contain evidence of the Great Unconformity,
10 which consists of exposed surface rock that represents more than one quarter of the
11 history (1.2 billion years) of the Earth’s crust and tectonic plates; and
12 WHEREAS, Several plant species that have received special status designation
13 by the Bureau of Land Management are found on these public lands; and
14 WHEREAS, Many Nevada residents enjoy the natural desert landscape and the
15 outdoor recreational opportunities of Sunrise Mountain, Frenchman Mountain and
16 the Rainbow Gardens Area; now, therefore, be it
17 RESOLVED BY THE SENATE AND ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, JOINTLY,
18 That the members of the 83rd Session of the Nevada Legislature hereby urge
19 Congress to protect the public lands including and adjacent to Sunrise Mountain,
20 Frenchman Mountain and the Rainbow Gardens Area by designating them for
21 federal protection, which may include, without limitation, designating all or
22 portions of the area as a national conservation area, national recreation area or
Assembly Amendment No. 554 to Senate Joint Resolution No. 8 Page 4
1 national monument, or applying other federal protections that Congress deems
2 appropriate for these important and scenic lands; and be it further
3 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of the Senate prepare and transmit a copy of this
4 resolution to the Vice President of the United States as the presiding officer of the
5 Senate, the Speaker of the House of Representatives and each member of the
6 Nevada Congressional Delegation; and be it further
7 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon passage.
