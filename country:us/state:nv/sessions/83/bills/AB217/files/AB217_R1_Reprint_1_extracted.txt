FIRST REPRINT A.B. 217
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 217–ASSEMBLYMEMBER GONZÁLEZ
PREFILED FEBRUARY 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to law enforcement on
school property. (BDR 34-201)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; prohibiting school districts, public
schools and their employees from providing permission to
access school district facilities to certain federal officials
investigating or enforcing immigration laws; prohibiting
school districts, public schools and their employees from
providing information concerning pupils to certain
federal, state or local officials investigating or enforcing
immigration laws; providing a penalty; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Attorney General to publish model policies for limiting,
2 to the fullest extent possible and consistent with any applicable law, immigration
3 enforcement at certain public places, including public schools. (NRS 228.208)
4 Section 1 of this bill prohibits a school district, public school or any employee of a
5 school district or public school, except pursuant to a lawful court order or warrant,
6 from providing a United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement officer, or
7 other federal official engaging in the investigation or enforcement of immigration
8 laws: (1) permission to access the grounds, buildings or facilities of a school district
9 or public school; or (2) the education records of or information about a pupil or the
10 family or household of a pupil. Section 1 additionally applies the prohibition on the
11 provision of the education records of or information about a pupil or the family or
12 household of a pupil to a state or local law enforcement officer engaged in the
13 investigation or enforcement of immigration laws. Section 1 provides that a violation
14 of this prohibition: (1) for the first violation, subjects the person who violated the
15 provision to disciplinary action by the school district or public school that employs
16 the person; and (2) for each subsequent violation, is punishable as a misdemeanor.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. A school district or public school, and any employee of a
4 school district or public school, shall not grant a United States
5 Immigration and Customs Enforcement officer, or other federal
6 official engaging in the investigation or enforcement of
7 immigration laws, permission to access the grounds, buildings or
8 facilities of a school district or public school without a lawful
9 order or warrant issued by a court of competent jurisdiction.
10 2. A school district or public school, and any employee of a
11 school district or public school, shall not disclose or provide in
12 writing, verbally or in any other manner, the education records of
13 or any information about a pupil, or the family or household of a
14 pupil, to a United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement
15 officer or other federal official, or a state or local law enforcement
16 officer, engaging in the investigation or enforcement of
17 immigration laws, except pursuant to a lawful order or warrant
18 issued by a court of competent jurisdiction.
19 3. Any person who knowingly and willfully violates the
20 provisions of this section:
21 (a) For the first violation, is subject to disciplinary action by
22 the school district or public school which employs the person.
23 (b) For each subsequent violation, is guilty of a misdemeanor.
24 4. As used in this section, “public school” includes, without
25 limitation, a charter school or university school for profoundly
26 gifted pupils.
27 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
28 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
