EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to elections; requiring that rooms or spaces in certain public
buildings be provided to certain political parties without charge for
certain purposes; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Senate Bill No. 292 of the 2021 Legislative Session repealed a requirement that, upon
2 application by a state or county central committee, rooms or spaces in certain public buildings
3 be made available without charge to state or county central committees of major political
4 parties: (1) in presidential election years for any purpose; and (2) during other years for the
5 purpose of conducting precinct meetings. (Section 15 of chapter 331, Statutes of Nevada
6 2021, at page 1980) This bill : (1) reinstates this requirement [.] ; and (2) excepts from this
7 requirement any building owned or occupied by any component of the Nevada System of
8 Higher Education.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 293 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. If a room or space is available in a building that is open to the general
4 public and occupied by the government of this State or a political subdivision of
5 this State or an agency thereof, except a building that is owned or occupied by
6 any component of the Nevada System of Higher Education, the public officer or
7 employee in control of the room or space shall, upon application by a state or
8 county central committee of a major political party, grant the use of the room or
9 space to the state or county central committee:
10 (a) During a presidential election year, without charge for any purpose; and
11 (b) During any year that is not a presidential election year:
Assembly Amendment No. 381 to Assembly Bill No. 92 Page 4
1 (1) Without charge for the purpose of conducting a precinct meeting;
2 and
3 (2) At a rate that must not exceed the rate charged to any other group
4 during this time period for the use of the room or space, for any purpose other
5 than conducting a precinct meeting.
6 2. As used in this section, “precinct meeting” means a meeting of the state
7 or central committee of a major political party held to elect delegates and
8 alternates for a state or county convention of a major political party.
9 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
