SENATE BILL NO. 88–COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY)
PREFILED JANUARY 7, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to offenders. (BDR 16-491)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to offenders; requiring the Director of the Department of
Corrections to discharge certain debts upon the release of an offender
from prison; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Director of the Department of Corrections to take certain actions
2 upon the release of an offender from prison. (NRS 209.511) [This] Section 1 of this bill
3 requires the Director to discharge any outstanding medical debt owed by an offender to the
4 Department upon the release of the offender from prison. Section 1.5 of this bill requires the
5 Director of the Department to discharge any outstanding medical debt owed to the
6 Department by an offender who has been released from prison by the expiration of his
7 or her term of sentence or by pardon or parole before July 1, 2025.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 209.511 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 209.511 1. Before an offender is released from prison by expiration of his or
3 her term of sentence, by pardon or parole, the Director may provide mediation
4 services to the offender and the family members and friends of the offender who
5 provide emotional, psychological and financial support to the offender.
6 2. As soon as practicable after an offender is authorized to apply for
7 enrollment in Medicaid pursuant to NRS 422.272428 or 422.27487, the Director
8 shall complete the paperwork for the application if the offender may be eligible for
9 Medicaid:
10 (a) Not more than 90 days before the scheduled release of the offender
11 pursuant to NRS 422.272428; or
12 (b) Immediately upon the scheduled release of the offender pursuant to NRS
13 422.27487.
Senate Amendment No. 6 to Senate Bill No. 88 Page 4
1 3. Not later than 3 months before an offender is projected to be released from
2 prison by expiration of his or her term of sentence, by pardon or parole, the
3 Director may, if space is available, provide an eligible offender with one or more
4 evidence-based or promising practice reentry programs to obtain employment,
5 including, without limitation, any programs which may provide bonding for an
6 offender entering the workplace and any organizations which may provide
7 employment or bonding assistance to such a person.
8 4. When an offender is released from prison by expiration of his or her term
9 of sentence, by pardon or by parole, the Director:
10 (a) May furnish the offender with a sum of money not to exceed $100, the
11 amount to be based upon the offender’s economic need as determined by the
12 Director;
13 (b) Shall give the offender notice of the provisions of chapter 179C of NRS
14 and NRS 202.357 and 202.360;
15 (c) Shall require the offender to sign an acknowledgment of the notice required
16 in paragraph (b);
17 (d) Shall give the offender notice of the provisions of NRS 179.245 and the
18 provisions of NRS 213.090, 213.155 or 213.157, as applicable;
19 (e) Shall provide the offender with a photo identification card issued by the
20 Department and information and reasonable assistance relating to acquiring a valid
21 driver’s license or identification card to enable the offender to obtain employment,
22 if the offender:
23 (1) Requests a photo identification card;
24 (2) Requests such information and assistance and is eligible to acquire a
25 valid driver’s license or identification card from the Department of Motor Vehicles;
26 or
27 (3) Is not currently in possession of a photo identification card;
28 (f) Shall provide the offender with clothing suitable for reentering society;
29 (g) Shall provide the offender with the cost of transportation to his or her place
30 of residence anywhere within the continental United States, or to the place of his or
31 her conviction;
32 (h) If appropriate, shall release the offender to a facility for transitional living
33 for released offenders that is licensed pursuant to chapter 449 of NRS;
34 (i) Shall require the offender to submit to at least one test for exposure to the
35 human immunodeficiency virus;
36 (j) If the offender is eligible for Medicare, shall complete enrollment
37 application paperwork for the offender; [and]
38 (k) If the offender was receiving a prescribed medication while in custody,
39 shall ensure that the offender is provided with a 30-day supply of any such
40 prescribed medication [.] ; and
41 (l) Shall discharge any outstanding medical debt owed by the offender to the
42 Department.
43 5. The Director shall not provide an offender with a photo identification card
44 pursuant to paragraph (e) of subsection 4 unless the photo identification card clearly
45 indicates whether the Director:
46 (a) Has verified the full legal name and age of the offender by obtaining an
47 original or certified copy of the documents required by the Department of Motor
48 Vehicles pursuant to NRS 483.290 or 483.860, as applicable, furnished as proof of
49 the full legal name and age of an applicant for a driver’s license or identification
50 card; or
51 (b) Has not verified the full legal name and age of the offender pursuant to
52 paragraph (a).
Senate Amendment No. 6 to Senate Bill No. 88 Page 5
1 6. The costs authorized or required in paragraphs (a), (e), (f), (g), (i) and (k)
2 of subsection 4 must be paid out of the appropriate account within the State General
3 Fund for the use of the Department as other claims against the State are paid to the
4 extent that the costs have not been paid in accordance with subsection 5 of NRS
5 209.221 and NRS 209.246.
6 7. The Director is encouraged to work with the Nevada Community Re-Entry
7 Task Force established by the Governor pursuant to executive order, or its
8 successor body, if any, to align statewide strategies for the reentry of offenders into
9 the community and the implementation of those strategies.
10 8. Nothing in this section shall be construed to affect the existing obligation
11 of an offender to pay restitution to a victim of his or her crime.
12 9. As used in this section:
13 (a) “Eligible offender” means an offender who is:
14 (1) Determined to be eligible for reentry programming based on the
15 Nevada Risk Assessment System instrument, or its successor risk assessment tool;
16 and
17 (2) Enrolled in:
18 (I) Programming services under a reentry program at a correctional
19 facility which has staff designated to provide the services; or
20 (II) A community-based program to assist offenders to reenter the
21 community.
22 (b) “Facility for transitional living for released offenders” has the meaning
23 ascribed to it in NRS 449.0055.
24 (c) “Photo identification card” means a document which includes the name,
25 date of birth and a color picture of the offender.
26 (d) “Promising practice reentry program” means a reentry program that has
27 strong quantitative and qualitative data showing positive outcomes, but does not
28 have sufficient research or replication to support recognition as an evidence-based
29 practice.
30 Sec. 1.5. The Director of the Department of Corrections shall discharge
31 any outstanding medical debt owed to the Department by an offender who has
32 been released from prison before July 1, 2025, by expiration of his or her term
33 of sentence, by pardon or by parole.
34 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
