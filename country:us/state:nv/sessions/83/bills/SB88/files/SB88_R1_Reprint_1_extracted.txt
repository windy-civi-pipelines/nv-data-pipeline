FIRST REPRINT S.B. 88
SENATE BILL NO. 88–COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY)
PREFILED JANUARY 7, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to offenders.
(BDR 16-491)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to offenders; requiring the Director of the
Department of Corrections to discharge certain debts
upon the release of an offender from prison; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Director of the Department of Corrections to take
2 certain actions upon the release of an offender from prison. (NRS 209.511) Section
3 1 of this bill requires the Director to discharge any outstanding medical debt owed
4 by an offender to the Department upon the release of the offender from prison.
5 Section 1.5 of this bill requires the Director of the Department to discharge any
6 outstanding medical debt owed to the Department by an offender who has been
7 released from prison by the expiration of his or her term of sentence or by pardon
8 or parole before July 1, 2025.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 209.511 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 209.511 1. Before an offender is released from prison by
3 expiration of his or her term of sentence, by pardon or parole, the
4 Director may provide mediation services to the offender and the
5 family members and friends of the offender who provide emotional,
6 psychological and financial support to the offender.
-
*SB88_R1*
– 2 –
1 2. As soon as practicable after an offender is authorized to
2 apply for enrollment in Medicaid pursuant to NRS 422.272428 or
3 422.27487, the Director shall complete the paperwork for the
4 application if the offender may be eligible for Medicaid:
5 (a) Not more than 90 days before the scheduled release of the
6 offender pursuant to NRS 422.272428; or
7 (b) Immediately upon the scheduled release of the offender
8 pursuant to NRS 422.27487.
9 3. Not later than 3 months before an offender is projected to be
10 released from prison by expiration of his or her term of sentence, by
11 pardon or parole, the Director may, if space is available, provide an
12 eligible offender with one or more evidence-based or promising
13 practice reentry programs to obtain employment, including, without
14 limitation, any programs which may provide bonding for an
15 offender entering the workplace and any organizations which may
16 provide employment or bonding assistance to such a person.
17 4. When an offender is released from prison by expiration of
18 his or her term of sentence, by pardon or by parole, the Director:
19 (a) May furnish the offender with a sum of money not to exceed
20 $100, the amount to be based upon the offender’s economic need as
21 determined by the Director;
22 (b) Shall give the offender notice of the provisions of chapter
23 179C of NRS and NRS 202.357 and 202.360;
24 (c) Shall require the offender to sign an acknowledgment of the
25 notice required in paragraph (b);
26 (d) Shall give the offender notice of the provisions of NRS
27 179.245 and the provisions of NRS 213.090, 213.155 or 213.157, as
28 applicable;
29 (e) Shall provide the offender with a photo identification card
30 issued by the Department and information and reasonable assistance
31 relating to acquiring a valid driver’s license or identification card to
32 enable the offender to obtain employment, if the offender:
33 (1) Requests a photo identification card;
34 (2) Requests such information and assistance and is eligible
35 to acquire a valid driver’s license or identification card from the
36 Department of Motor Vehicles; or
37 (3) Is not currently in possession of a photo identification
38 card;
39 (f) Shall provide the offender with clothing suitable for
40 reentering society;
41 (g) Shall provide the offender with the cost of transportation to
42 his or her place of residence anywhere within the continental United
43 States, or to the place of his or her conviction;
-
*SB88_R1*
– 3 –
1 (h) If appropriate, shall release the offender to a facility for
2 transitional living for released offenders that is licensed pursuant to
3 chapter 449 of NRS;
4 (i) Shall require the offender to submit to at least one test for
5 exposure to the human immunodeficiency virus;
6 (j) If the offender is eligible for Medicare, shall complete
7 enrollment application paperwork for the offender; [and]
8 (k) If the offender was receiving a prescribed medication while
9 in custody, shall ensure that the offender is provided with a 30-day
10 supply of any such prescribed medication [.] ; and
11 (l) Shall discharge any outstanding medical debt owed by the
12 offender to the Department.
13 5. The Director shall not provide an offender with a photo
14 identification card pursuant to paragraph (e) of subsection 4 unless
15 the photo identification card clearly indicates whether the Director:
16 (a) Has verified the full legal name and age of the offender by
17 obtaining an original or certified copy of the documents required by
18 the Department of Motor Vehicles pursuant to NRS 483.290 or
19 483.860, as applicable, furnished as proof of the full legal name and
20 age of an applicant for a driver’s license or identification card; or
21 (b) Has not verified the full legal name and age of the offender
22 pursuant to paragraph (a).
23 6. The costs authorized or required in paragraphs (a), (e), (f),
24 (g), (i) and (k) of subsection 4 must be paid out of the appropriate
25 account within the State General Fund for the use of the Department
26 as other claims against the State are paid to the extent that the costs
27 have not been paid in accordance with subsection 5 of NRS 209.221
28 and NRS 209.246.
29 7. The Director is encouraged to work with the Nevada
30 Community Re-Entry Task Force established by the Governor
31 pursuant to executive order, or its successor body, if any, to align
32 statewide strategies for the reentry of offenders into the community
33 and the implementation of those strategies.
34 8. Nothing in this section shall be construed to affect the
35 existing obligation of an offender to pay restitution to a victim of
36 his or her crime.
37 9. As used in this section:
38 (a) “Eligible offender” means an offender who is:
39 (1) Determined to be eligible for reentry programming based
40 on the Nevada Risk Assessment System instrument, or its successor
41 risk assessment tool; and
42 (2) Enrolled in:
43 (I) Programming services under a reentry program at a
44 correctional facility which has staff designated to provide the
45 services; or
-
*SB88_R1*
– 4 –
1 (II) A community-based program to assist offenders to
2 reenter the community.
3 (b) “Facility for transitional living for released offenders” has
4 the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 449.0055.
5 (c) “Photo identification card” means a document which
6 includes the name, date of birth and a color picture of the offender.
7 (d) “Promising practice reentry program” means a reentry
8 program that has strong quantitative and qualitative data showing
9 positive outcomes, but does not have sufficient research or
10 replication to support recognition as an evidence-based practice.
11 Sec. 1.5. The Director of the Department of Corrections shall
12 discharge any outstanding medical debt owed to the Department by
13 an offender who has been released from prison before July 1, 2025,
14 by expiration of his or her term of sentence, by pardon or by parole.
15 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
