Section 1:

SECOND REPRINT A.B. 442
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 442–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS WATTS, NGUYEN,
MOSCA, BROWN-MAY; D’SILVA, GALLANT, O’NEILL AND ROTH
MARCH 17, 2025
____________
JOINT SPONSORS: SENATORS NGUYEN, FLORES; AND STONE
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to grants. (BDR 31-589)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to state financial administration; requiring a state
grant-making entity to provide written notice to a private
nonprofit corporation when a payment under a grant
agreement is not made within a certain period of time;
authorizing the Office of Federal Assistance to coordinate
and collaborate with state grant-making entities on certain
topics related to grants; revising provisions governing the
State Plan for Maximizing Federal Assistance; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Section 9 of this bill requires a state entity that makes a grant to a private
2 nonprofit corporation, which is defined in section 7 of this bill as a “state grant-
3 making entity,” that does not make a payment that is due and payable under a
4 written grant agreement within 30 calendar days after receipt of a proper invoice to:
5 (1) provide written notice to the private nonprofit corporation of the reason for the
6 delay in making the payment; and (2) submit an annual compilation of such records
7 to the Director of the Office of Finance and the Director of the Legislative Counsel
8 Bureau for transmittal to the Interim Finance Committee. Sections 2-6 of this bill
9 define certain terms relating to these requirements.
10 Existing law requires the Director of the Office of Federal Assistance, in
11 consultation with the Nevada Advisory Council on Federal Assistance, to develop a
12 State Plan for Maximizing Federal Assistance which is required to include, without
13 limitation, methods for the effective administration of grants. (NRS 223.486)
14 Section 11.7 of this bill requires the State Plan to include methods for the
15 administration of advanced payments of grants to private nonprofit corporations.
-
*AB442_R2*
– 2 –
16 Section 11.3 of this bill authorizes the Office to coordinate and collaborate with
17 state grant-making entities to identify certain policies, procedures and best practices
18 that are related to the advanced payment of a grant and methods for compliance
19 with certain federal regulations relating to grants. Section 11.3 authorizes the
20 Director to adopt regulations to carry out such coordination and collaboration.
21 Section 11.5 of this bill applies the definitions in existing law governing the Office
22 to the provisions of section 11.3.
23 Section 14 of this bill makes this bill effective on July 1, 2026, and section 13
24 of this bill excludes from the requirements of this bill grants which are executed,
25 renewed or extended before July 1, 2026.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 353 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 to 11, inclusive, of this
3 act.
4 Sec. 2. As used in sections 2 to 9, inclusive, of this act, unless
5 the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in
6 sections 3 to 7, inclusive, of this act have the meanings ascribed to
7 them in those sections.
8 Sec. 3. 1. “Grant” means a legal instrument of financial
9 assistance between a state grant-making entity and a private
10 nonprofit corporation that is:
11 (a) Used to enter into a relationship for which the principal
12 purpose is to transfer anything of value from the state grant-
13 making entity to the private nonprofit corporation to carry out a
14 public purpose authorized by law and not to acquire real property
15 or services for the direct benefit or use of the state grant-making
16 entity;
17 (b) Used to provide for one or more payments in
18 reimbursement for services or other performance under the
19 agreement on a scheduled or other incremental basis; and
20 (c) Distinguished from a cooperative agreement in that it does
21 not provide for substantial involvement between the state grant-
22 making entity and the private nonprofit corporation in carrying
23 out the activity contemplated by the award.
24 2. The term does not include an instrument that provides
25 only:
26 (a) Direct government cash assistance to a person;
27 (b) A subsidy;
28 (c) A loan;
29 (d) A loan guarantee;
30 (e) Insurance;
31 (f) State funding that is required annually and is calculated
32 based on a formula set in statute; or
-
*AB442_R2*
– 3 –
1 (g) Proceeds from a general obligation bond.
2 Sec. 4. “Payment” includes all processing and authorization
3 required by law from the State Board of Examiners, the Director
4 of the Office of Finance and the State Controller.
5 Sec. 5. “Private nonprofit corporation” means an
6 organization that has been granted tax-exempt status by the
7 Internal Revenue Service pursuant to the provisions of section
8 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. § 501(c)(3).
9 Sec. 6. “Proper invoice” means a bill, a request for
10 reimbursement, a written document or an electronic submission
11 readable by the state grant-making entity that is provided by a
12 private nonprofit corporation and which:
13 1. Requests an eligible amount that is due and payable by law
14 under a written grant agreement with the private nonprofit
15 corporation; and
16 2. Includes:
17 (a) Sufficient information to identify the grant agreement; and
18 (b) Any additional information or documentation required by
19 the grant agreement or for compliance with 2 C.F.R. Part 200 or
20 any other state or federal law or regulation.
21 Sec. 7. “State grant-making entity” means an agency,
22 bureau, board, commission, department, division, officer or
23 employee of the Executive Department of the State Government
24 that makes a grant. The term does not include the Nevada System
25 of Higher Education or any institution of the Nevada System of
26 Higher Education.
27 Sec. 8. (Deleted by amendment.)
28 Sec. 8.5. (Deleted by amendment.)
29 Sec. 9. 1. If a state grant-making entity does not make a
30 payment for an amount that is due and payable by law under a
31 written grant agreement with a private nonprofit corporation
32 within 30 calendar days after receipt of a proper invoice, the state
33 grant-making entity shall, as soon as practicable after that 30-day
34 period, provide written notice to the private nonprofit corporation
35 of the reason for the delay in making the payment.
36 2. On or before October 1 of each year, the state grant-
37 making entity shall submit to the Director of the Office of Finance
38 and the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal
39 to the Interim Finance Committee a compilation of the notices
40 provided pursuant to subsection 1 during the immediately
41 preceding fiscal year.
42 Sec. 10. (Deleted by amendment.)
43 Sec. 11. (Deleted by amendment.)
-
*AB442_R2*
– 4 –
1 Sec. 11.3. Chapter 223 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Office may coordinate and collaborate with state
4 grant-making entities to identify:
5 (a) Existing policies and procedures utilized by state grant-
6 making entities to administer advanced payments of grants;
7 (b) Best practices for monitoring and assessing the risks
8 associated with administration of advanced payments of grants;
9 and
10 (c) Methods for state grant-making entities to comply with 2
11 C.F.R. Part 200 and any other federal regulations governing the
12 administration of grants.
13 2. The Director may adopt regulations to carry out the
14 provisions of this section.
15 3. As used in this section, “state grant-making entity” has the
16 meaning ascribed to it in section 7 of this act.
17 Sec. 11.5. NRS 223.460 is hereby amended to read as follows:
18 223.460 As used in NRS 223.460 to 223.498, inclusive, and
19 section 11.3 of this act, unless the context otherwise requires, the
20 words and terms defined in NRS 223.462 to 223.470, inclusive,
21 have the meanings ascribed to them in those sections.
22 Sec. 11.7. NRS 223.486 is hereby amended to read as follows:
23 223.486 1. The Director shall, in consultation with the
24 Nevada Advisory Council on Federal Assistance created by NRS
25 358.020, develop and may periodically revise a State Plan for
26 Maximizing Federal Assistance, which must identify:
27 (a) Methods for expanding opportunities for obtaining federal
28 assistance, including, without limitation, expanding opportunities
29 for obtaining matching funds for federal assistance through the
30 Nevada Grant Matching Program created by NRS 223.490;
31 (b) Methods for streamlining process, regulatory, structural and
32 other barriers to the acquisition of federal assistance that exist at
33 each level of federal, state or local government;
34 (c) Methods for the effective administration of grants, including,
35 without limitation, the administration of advanced payments of
36 grants to private nonprofit corporations and best practices relating
37 to indirect cost allocation;
38 (d) Opportunities for:
39 (1) Reducing administrative costs associated with obtaining
40 federal assistance; and
41 (2) Coordination between state agencies, local agencies,
42 tribal governments and nonprofit organizations to avoid duplication
43 and achieve common goals;
44 (e) Specific tasks which must be performed to improve the
45 administration of grants and maximize the amount of federal
-
*AB442_R2*
– 5 –
1 assistance received by this State and a schedule for implementing
2 any such tasks;
3 (f) Performance metrics and targets relating to obtaining and
4 maximizing federal assistance and improving the administration of
5 grants; and
6 (g) Best practices for considering whether to respond to a grant
7 opportunity, including, without limitation, the monetary and
8 programmatic cost of implementing a grant.
9 2. The Director shall post the State Plan for Maximizing
10 Federal Assistance on the Internet website maintained by the Office.
11 Sec. 12. The provisions of NRS 218D.380 do not apply to any
12 provision of this act which adds or revises a requirement to submit a
13 report to the Legislature.
14 Sec. 13. The provisions of this act apply only to a grant, as
15 defined in section 3 of this act, which is executed, renewed or
16 extended on or after July 1, 2026.
17 Sec. 14. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2026.
