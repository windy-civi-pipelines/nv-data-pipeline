Section 1:
REQUIRES TWO-THIRDS MAJORITY VOTE
(§ 2 & NRS 477.031)
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 25
SENATE BILL NO. 25–COMMITTEE ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS
(ON BEHALF OF THE STATE FIRE MARSHAL DIVISION

PREFILED NOVEMBER 8, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to fire protection.
(BDR 42-249)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to fire protection; revising the duties of the State
Forester Firewarden; revising the composition of the State
Fire Marshal Division within the Department of Public
Safety; expanding the list of facilities for which the State
Fire Marshal is required to enforce laws and adopt certain
regulations to include cannabis production facilities;
eliminating the requirement that the State Fire Marshal
cooperate with the State Forester Firewarden to mitigate
the risk of certain fires; revising provisions relating to the
investigation of certain fires by the State Fire Marshal to
include a fire that results in an injury or certain financial
losses; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law sets forth the powers and duties of the State Forester Firewarden,
2 which include a requirement to cooperate with the State Fire Marshal in the
3 enforcement of laws and the adoption of certain regulations relating to fire
4 prevention through the management of vegetation in this State. (NRS 472.040)
5 Section 1 of this bill removes this requirement.
6 Existing law establishes within the Department of Public Safety the State Fire
7 Marshal Division, which consists of the Fire Protection and Control Section, Fire
8 Investigation Section, Public Education Section, Fire Service Training Section and
-
*SB25_R1*
– 2 –
9 Fire Data Section. (NRS 477.010) Section 1.5 of this bill eliminates all of the
10 statutory sections comprising the State Fire Marshal Division.
11 Existing law requires, amongst other things, the State Fire Marshal to: (1)
12 enforce laws and adopt regulations related to the safety, access, means and
13 adequacy of exits in case of fire at certain institutions, facilities and buildings; (2)
14 cooperate with the State Forester Firewarden in mitigating the risk of fire hazard
15 from vegetation in the State; and (3) with certain exceptions, investigate in certain
16 counties any fire which results in a death or which is of a suspicious nature.
17 (NRS 477.030) Section 2 of this bill: (1) expands the list of facilities for which the
18 State Fire Marshal enforces laws and adopts regulations to include cannabis
19 production facilities; (2) removes the requirement that the State Fire Marshal
20 cooperate with the State Forester Firewarden in mitigating the risk of fire hazard
21 from vegetation in the State; and (3) expands the requirement that the State Fire
22 Marshal investigate certain fires in certain counties to include the investigation of
23 any fire which results in an injury or a financial loss greater than $2,000,000.
