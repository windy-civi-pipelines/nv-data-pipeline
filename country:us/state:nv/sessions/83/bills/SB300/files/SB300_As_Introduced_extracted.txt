EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; requiring Medicaid to cover certain
mental health services provided at a federally-qualified
health center; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 The policies of the Department of Health and Human Services provide that
2 Medicaid will provide coverage of services provided by a psychiatrist,
3 psychologist, advanced practice registered nurse, marriage and family therapist or
4 licensed clinical social worker at a federally-qualified health center. (Medicaid
5 Services Manual 2903(B)(1)) In 2023, federal law was amended to authorize
6 federal financial participation for Medicaid coverage of services provided by
7 mental health counselors, including alcohol and drug counselors and problem
8 gambling counselors who hold a master’s or doctoral degree and clinical
9 professional counselors, when such services are provided in a federally-qualified
10 health center. (42 U.S.C. § 1396a(10), 42 U.S.C. § 1396d(a)(2)(c), 42 U.S.C. §
11 1396d(1)(2); Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2023, Pub. L. No. 117-328, §
12 4121) This bill: (1) codifies into law existing requirements that Medicaid cover
13 services provided by a psychiatrist, psychologist, advanced practice registered
14 nurse, marriage and family therapist or licensed clinical social worker at a
15 federally-qualified health center; and (2) newly requires Medicaid to cover services
16 provided by a mental health counselor.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 422.272366 is hereby amended to read as
2 follows:
3 422.272366 1. [The] To the extent that federal financial
4 participation is available, the Director shall include [in the State
5 Plan for] under Medicaid [a requirement that the State must pay the
6 nonfederal share of expenditures incurred] coverage for behavioral
7 health services, including, without limitation, mental health services
8 and services for the treatment of a substance use disorder, that are
9 delivered [through] :
10 (a) Through evidence-based, behavioral health integration
11 models, including, without limitation, collaborative care
12 management services [.] ; or
13 (b) In a federally-qualified health center by a psychiatrist,
14 psychologist, advanced practice registered nurse, licensed
15 marriage and family therapist, licensed clinical social worker or
16 mental health counselor.
17 2. The Department shall:
18 (a) Apply to the Secretary of Health and Human Services for
19 any waiver of federal law or apply for any amendment of the State
20 Plan for Medicaid that is necessary for the Department to receive
21 federal funding to provide the coverage described in subsection 1.
22 (b) Fully cooperate in good faith with the Federal Government
23 during the application process to satisfy the requirements of the
24 Federal Government for obtaining a waiver or amendment
25 pursuant to paragraph (a).
26 3. As used in this section:
27 (a) “Behavioral health integration model” means a model of
28 delivering behavioral health services that integrates such services
29 with primary care. The term includes, without limitation, the
30 delivery of behavioral health services using collaborative care
31 management services.
32 (b) “Collaborative care management services” means a
33 combination of services and structured care management with
34 regular assessments directed and provided by a team of providers of
35 primary care and providers of behavioral health care.
36 (c) “Federally-qualified health center” has the meaning
37 ascribed to it in 42 U.S.C. § 1396d(l)(2)(B).
38 (d) “Mental health counselor” has the meaning ascribed to it
39 in 42 U.S.C. § 1395x(lll)(4).
40 Sec. 2. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
41 approval.
42 2. Section 1 of this act becomes effective:
-
