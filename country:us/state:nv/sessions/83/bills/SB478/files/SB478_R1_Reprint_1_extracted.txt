SENATE BILL NO. 478–COMMITTEE ON FINANCE
(ON BEHALF OF THE OFFICE OF FINANCE
IN THE OFFICE OF THE GOVERNOR)
MAY 15, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Finance
SUMMARY—Makes appropriations and requires the issuance of
bonds for costs associated with the Judicial
Department of State Government. (BDR S-1187)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation included in
Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT making appropriations and requiring the issuance of
certain bonds for costs associated with the Judicial
Department of State Government; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
2 General Fund to the Supreme Court of Nevada the sum of $814,720
3 for the Administrative Office of the Courts budget account for the
4 costs of upgrading the multicounty integrated justice information
5 system.
6 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
7 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
8 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
9 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
10 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
11 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
12 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
13 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
-
*SB478_R1*
– 2 –
1 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
2 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
3 Sec. 2. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
4 General Fund to the Supreme Court of Nevada the sum of
5 $1,413,605 for the Administrative Office of the Courts budget
6 account for the replacement of audiovisual equipment.
7 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
8 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
9 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
10 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
11 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
12 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
13 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
14 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
15 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
16 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
17 Sec. 3. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
18 General Fund to the Supreme Court of Nevada for the
19 Administrative Office of the Courts budget account for the
20 replacement of computer and other technological equipment
21 the following sums:
22 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 .................................. $294,400
23 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 .................................. $107,040
24 2. Any balance of the sums appropriated by subsection 1
25 remaining at the end of the respective fiscal years must not be
26 committed for expenditure after June 30 of the respective fiscal
27 years by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
28 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
29 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
30 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
31 September 18, 2026, and September 17, 2027, respectively, by
32 either the entity to which the money was appropriated or the entity
33 to which the money was subsequently granted or transferred, and
34 must be reverted to the State General Fund on or before
35 September 18, 2026, and September 17, 2027, respectively.
36 Sec. 3.5. 1. The State Board of Finance shall issue general
37 obligation bonds of the State of Nevada in the face amount of not
38 more than $9,000,000 in the 2025-2027 biennium, the proceeds of
39 which must be used to acquire the real property described in
40 subsection 3.
41 2. The State Land Registrar, on behalf of the Department of
42 Administration, may negotiate, enter into and execute an agreement
43 with the owner for the purchase and sale of the real property
44 described in subsection 3.

1 3. The real property, with its improvements, that the State Land
2 Registrar is authorized to acquire pursuant to subsection 2 is
3 commonly known as the Nevada Supreme Court Building, located
4 at 408 East Clark Avenue in Las Vegas, Nevada, which is Clark
5 County Assessor’s Parcel Number 139-34-303-002 and which is
6 described by the Clark County Assessor, which description may be
7 further refined through due diligence, as follows:
8
9 Lots One (1) through Seven (7), inclusive, in Block
10 Thirty-Nine (39) of Clark’s Las Vegas Townsite, as shown by
11 the Map thereof on file in Book 1 Plats, page 37m in the
12 Office of the County Recorder of Clark County, Nevada,
13 being a portion of the North Hald (N 1/2) of the Southwest
14 Quarter (SW 1/4) of Section 34, Township 20 South, Range
15 61 East, more particularly described as follows:
16 Commencing at the centerline intersection of Clark
17 Avenue (80 feet wide) and Fourth Street (80 feet wide);
18 thence South 62'15'00" East along the centerline of Clark
19 Avenue, 40.00 feet; thence South 27'45'00" West, departing
20 said centerline, 40.00 feet to the intersection of the Easterly
21 and Southerly Right of Way lines of Fourth Street and Clark
22 Avenue, said point also being the Point of Beginning; thence
23 South 62'15'00" East along the Southerly Right of Way line
24 of Clark Avenue, 140.00 feet; thence South 27'45'00" West
25 departing said Southerly Right of Way, 175.00 feet; thence
26 North 62'15'00" West, 140.00 feet to point on the Easterly
27 Right of Way line of Fourth Street; thence North 27'45'00"
28 East along said Easterly Right of Way line, 170.00 feet to the
29 Point of Beginning.
30 Excepting therefrom that certain spandrel area dedicated
31 to the City of Las Vegas by document, Book 991206,
32 Instrument 00228 dated December 6, 1999, Clark.
33 Sec. 4. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
