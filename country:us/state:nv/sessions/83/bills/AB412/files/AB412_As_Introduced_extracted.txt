Section 1:
A.B. 412
DALIA AND JAUREGUI
MARCH 12, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Creates a program to provide grants of money to
school districts for the purpose of facilitating
emergency response by providing school mapping
data. (BDR 43-1006)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to emergency response; creating the Account for
the Nevada School Safety Data Grant Program within the
Department of Public Safety to award grants to school
districts to procure school mapping data for schools in
this State; prescribing certain requirements for the use of
grants received from the Account; making an
appropriation; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires: (1) the board of trustees of each school district and the
2 governing body of each charter school and private school to establish a
3 development committee to develop a plan to be used by the schools in the district or
4 the charter school or private school in responding to a crisis, emergency or suicide;
5 and (2) such a development committee to annually review and, as appropriate,
6 update the plan. (NRS 388.241, 388.245, 394.1685, 394.1688) Section 1 of this bill
7 creates the Account for the Nevada School Safety Data Grant Program within the
8 Department of Public Safety to award grants to school districts in this State to
9 procure school mapping data for each public school and private school located in
10 the county in which the school district is located. Section 1: (1) authorizes a school
11 district to apply for grants from the Program; (2) requires any grant money awarded
12 from the Program to be used for purposes of providing school mapping data,
13 including, without limitation, contracting with a vendor to provide school mapping
14 data; (3) requires an entity that produces school mapping data to provide the school
15 mapping data to the school district and any public safety agency in the county in
16 which the school district is located; (4) requires such school mapping data to
-
*AB412*
– 2 –
17 comply with certain requirements; and (5) requires the Department to adopt
18 regulations to carry out the provisions of the Program. Section 2 of this bill makes
19 an appropriation of $2,500,000 to the Account for the purposes of making grants
20 from the Account.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 480 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Account for the Nevada School Safety Data Grant
4 Program is hereby created in the State General Fund. The
5 Director or a designee of the Director shall administer the
6 Account.
7 2. The Director or designee may apply for and accept any
8 gift, donation, bequest, grant or other source of money for the use
9 of the Account. All money received for the use of the Account
10 pursuant to this subsection or from any other source must be
11 deposited with the State Treasurer for credit to the Account.
12 3. A school district, in consultation with any public safety
13 agency in the county in which the school district is located, may
14 apply to the Department for a grant of money from the Account. A
15 school district receiving a grant of money from the Account shall
16 use the money to provide school mapping data for the public
17 schools and private schools in the county in which the school
18 district is located for the purpose of facilitating an efficient and
19 effective response to emergencies in those schools by public safety
20 agencies in the county, including, without limitation, by
21 contracting with a vendor to provide such school mapping data.
22 4. The school mapping data provided pursuant to subsection
23 3 must:
24 (a) Be compatible with software platforms used by public
25 safety agencies in the county in which the school district is located
26 without requiring a public safety agency to purchase additional
27 software or pay a fee to access the data;
28 (b) Be compatible with security software platforms used by any
29 school district or any public school or private school for which the
30 school mapping data is provided without requiring the school
31 district to purchase additional software or pay a fee to access the
32 data;
33 (c) Be in a printable format in addition to an electronic or
34 digital format that can be integrated into an interactive mobile
35 platform in use by public safety agencies;
-
*AB412*
– 3 –
1 (d) Be verified for accuracy by the entity that produces the
2 school mapping data by a walk-through of the buildings and
3 grounds of the public school or private school;
4 (e) Be oriented true north and overlaid with gridded x and y
5 coordinates for reference, and z coordinates for elevation data;
6 (f) Contain site-specific labeling that matches the structure of
7 school buildings, including, without limitation, room labels,
8 hallway names, external doors or stairwell numbers, locations of
9 hazards, critical utility locations, key boxes, automated external
10 defibrillators and trauma kits;
11 (g) Contain site-specific labeling that matches the school
12 grounds, including, without limitation, parking areas, athletic
13 fields, surrounding roads and neighboring properties; and
14 (h) Be provided to the school district and public safety
15 agencies in the county in which the school district is located at no
16 additional cost beyond the initial cost of production of the school
17 mapping data.
18 5. The Department shall adopt regulations necessary to carry
19 out the provisions of this section, including, without limitation,
20 regulations to ensure compliance with the requirements for the
21 use of money from grants received from the Account pursuant to
22 this section.
23 6. As used in this section:
24 (a) “Private school” has the meaning ascribed to it in
25 NRS 394.103
26 (b) “Public safety agency” has the meaning ascribed to it in
27 NRS 388.2345.
28 (c) “Public school” has the meaning ascribed to it in
29 NRS 385.007.
30 (d) “School mapping data” means structural data in a
31 printable format in addition to an electronic or digital format of
32 school buildings and grounds, including, without limitation, floor
33 plans, aerial imagery and site-specific labels identifying rooms,
34 hallways, stairwells and emergency resources to assist a public
35 safety agency in responding to emergencies at a public or private
36 school.
37 (e) “Software platforms” means the digital tools and systems
38 used by a public safety agency to access, share and use school
39 mapping data and related emergency information.
40 Sec. 2. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
41 General Fund to the Department of Public Safety the sum of
42 $2,500,000 to the Account for the Nevada School Safety Data Grant
43 Program created by section 1 of this act, for the purpose of making
44 grants of money to school districts from the Account.
-
*AB412*
– 4 –
1 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
2 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
3 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
4 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
5 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
6 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
7 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
8 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
9 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
10 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
11 Sec. 3. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
12 approval.
13 2. Sections 1 and 2 of this act become effective:
14 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting
15 regulations and performing other preparatory administrative tasks
16 that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
17 (b) On October 1, 2025, for all other purposes.
