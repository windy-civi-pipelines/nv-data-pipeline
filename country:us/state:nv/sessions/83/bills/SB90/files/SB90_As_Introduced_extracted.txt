Section 1:
S.B. 90
PREFILED JANUARY 13, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Finance
SUMMARY—Makes an appropriation to the Department of
Education for the provision of grants to
organizations to provide certain supplies and
materials to teachers and specialized instructional
support personnel. (BDR S-107)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT making an appropriation to the Department of Education
for the creation of a grant program to allow certain
teachers and specialized instructional support personnel to
obtain certain supplies or materials for classrooms or
school operations; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
2 General Fund to the Department of Education the sum of
3 $15,000,000 for the Other State Education Programs budget account
4 for the purpose of creating a grant program to allow teachers and
5 specialized instructional support personnel to obtain necessary
6 supplies and materials for their classrooms or school operations,
7 respectively.
8 2. Of the sum appropriated by subsection 1, the Department
9 shall not expend more than $7,500,000 in Fiscal Year 2025-2026.
10 3. The Department shall enter into an agreement with one or
11 more organizations to provide such organizations with a grant from
12 the Department to administer a program whereby the organizations
13 expend grant money to directly provide teachers and specialized
-
*SB90*
– 2 –
1 instructional support personnel with necessary supplies and
2 materials for their classrooms or school operations, respectively, in
3 response to a request from such persons.
4 4. Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, an
5 agreement made pursuant to subsection 3 shall require that an
6 organization that receives grant money expend not more than $500
7 per individual teacher or specialized instructional support personnel
8 following a request made pursuant to subsection 3. An individual
9 teacher or specialized instructional support personnel may request
10 the organization expend money in excess of $500 and, to the extent
11 that money from a source other than the grant program established
12 pursuant to this section is available, such a request may be fulfilled.
13 5. As used in this section, “specialized instructional support
14 personnel” includes persons employed by a school to provide
15 necessary services such as assessment, diagnosis, counseling,
16 educational services, therapeutic services and related services, as
17 defined in 20 U.S.C. § 1401(26), to pupils. Such persons employed
18 by a school include, without limitation:
19 (a) A school counselor;
20 (b) A school psychologist;
21 (c) A school social worker;
22 (d) A school nurse;
23 (e) A speech-language pathologist;
24 (f) A school library media specialist; and
25 (g) Any other qualified professional.
26 Sec. 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
27 section 1 of this act must not be committed for expenditure after
28 June 30, 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or
29 any entity to which money from the appropriation is granted or
30 otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the
31 appropriated money remaining must not be spent for any purpose
32 after September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money
33 was appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
34 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
35 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
36 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
