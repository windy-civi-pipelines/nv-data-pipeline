EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to employment practices; [prohibiting] making it unlawful for
an employer, with certain exceptions, [from taking] to take or
threaten to take certain adverse employment actions against an
employee who declines to attend certain meetings or participate in
certain communications where the purpose of the meeting or
communication is to communicate the opinion of the employer
regarding religious matters or political matters; providing a [penalty;]
remedy for a violation committed by an employer; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes various unlawful employment practices. (Chapter 613 of NRS)
2 This bill [prohibits] makes it unlawful for an employer [from taking or threatening] to take
3 or threaten to take certain adverse employment actions against an employee because the
4 employee declines to attend a meeting sponsored by the employer or declines to participate in,
5 receive or listen to any communication by the employer or an agent or representative thereof
6 where the purpose of the meeting or communication is to communicate the opinion of the
7 employer regarding religious or political matters. This bill provides an exception from
8 existing provisions concerning religious matters for a religious corporation, association or
9 society. (NRS 613.320) This bill also provides an exception if the declination by the
10 employee substantially or materially interferes with the bona fide job performance of the
11 employee or the working relationship between the employee and the employer. Under
12 this bill, an employer who violates the provisions of this bill is [guilty of a misdemeanor.]
13 liable for: (1) damages caused by the adverse employment action; (2) any punitive
14 damages that the facts may warrant; (3) the gross loss of wages or compensation of the
15 employee; and (4) costs and reasonable attorney’s fees as may be allowed by the court.
16 This bill also provides that if the court determines that an action brought by an
17 employee under this bill was brought without substantial justification, the court may
18 award costs and reasonable attorney’s fees to the employer.
Assembly Amendment No. 438 to Assembly Bill No. 434 Page 4
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 613 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 2, it is unlawful for any
4 employer in this State to discharge, discipline, discriminate, retaliate or take any
5 other adverse employment action against, or threaten to take any such action
6 against, an employee because the employee declines to attend a meeting
7 sponsored by the employer or declines to participate in, receive or listen to any
8 communication by the employer or an agent or representative thereof where the
9 purpose of the meeting or communication is to communicate the opinion of the
10 employer regarding:
11 (a) Religious matters; or
12 (b) Political matters.
13 2. The provisions of paragraph (a) of subsection 1 do not apply [to an] :
14 (a) To an employer described in paragraph (b) of subsection 1 of NRS
15 613.320 [.] ; or
16 (b) If the declination by the employee substantially or materially interferes
17 with the bona fide job performance of the employee or the working relationship
18 between the employee and the employer.
19 3. An employer who violates the provisions of subsection 1 [is guilty of a
20 misdemeanor.] is liable to the employee against whom the violation is committed
21 for:
22 (a) Damages caused by the adverse employment action;
23 (b) Any punitive damages that the facts may warrant;
24 (c) The full amount of gross loss of wages or compensation; and
25 (d) Costs and reasonable attorney’s fees as may be allowed by the court.
26 4. If the court determines that an action brought by an employee pursuant
27 to subsection 3 was brought without substantial justification, the court may
28 award costs and reasonable attorney’s fees to the employer.
29 [4.] 5. As used in this section:
30 (a) “Political matters” means matters relating to elections for political office,
31 political parties, proposals to change legislation or regulations or the decision to
32 join or support any political party or political, civic, community, fraternal or
33 labor organization.
34 (b) “Religious matters” means matters relating to religious affiliation or
35 practice or the decision to join or support any religious organization or
36 association.
