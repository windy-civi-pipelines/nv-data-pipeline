Section 1:
S.B. 394
MARCH 17, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the sale of fireworks.
(BDR 42-1077)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to fireworks; prohibiting the sale of fireworks to a
person who is less than 18 years of age; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes a board of county commissioners to pass ordinances
2 prohibiting, restricting, suppressing or otherwise regulating the sale, use, storage
3 and possession of fireworks in the county. (NRS 244.367) Existing law further
4 requires the State Fire Marshal to enforce all laws and adopt regulations relating to
5 the storage and use of fireworks under those circumstances that are not otherwise
6 regulated by the Division of Industrial Relations of the Department of Business and
7 Industry. (NRS 477.030) Section 1 of this bill prohibits the sale of fireworks to any
8 person who is less than 18 years of age. Section 2 of this bill provides that an
9 ordinance passed by a board of county commissioners must not conflict with the
10 provisions of section 1.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 477 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 A person shall not sell or offer to sell fireworks to any person
4 who is less than 18 years of age.
-
*SB394*
– 2 –
1 Sec. 2. NRS 244.367 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 244.367 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 3, the
3 board of county commissioners shall have power and jurisdiction in
4 their respective counties to pass ordinances:
5 (a) Prohibiting, restricting, suppressing or otherwise regulating
6 the sale, use, storage and possession of fireworks; and
7 (b) Providing for the imposition of criminal or civil penalties, or
8 both, for the violation thereof. A civil penalty imposed pursuant to
9 such an ordinance must not be:
10 (1) In an amount less than $250 or more than $1,000 for a
11 violation involving less than 100 pounds in gross weight of
12 fireworks, including packaging;
13 (2) In an amount less than $1,000 or more than $5,000 for a
14 violation involving not less than 100 pounds or more than 5,000
15 pounds in gross weight of fireworks, including packaging;
16 (3) In an amount less than $5,000 or more than $10,000 for a
17 violation involving more than 5,000 pounds in gross weight of
18 fireworks, including packaging; or
19 (4) Imposed against a person who has been issued a license
20 or permit pursuant to the ordinance.
21 2. An ordinance passed pursuant to subsection 1 must:
22 (a) Provide that any license or permit that may be required for
23 the sale of fireworks must be issued by the licensing authority for:
24 (1) The county, if the fireworks are sold within the
25 unincorporated areas of the county; or
26 (2) A city located within the county, if the fireworks are sold
27 within the jurisdiction of that city; and
28 (b) Establish factors for determining the severity of any criminal
29 penalty or any civil penalty within the limits contained in paragraph
30 (b) of subsection 1 that take into account, without limitation, the
31 number and severity of any previous violations.
32 3. An ordinance passed pursuant to subsection 1 must not
33 apply to a child under the age of 18 years [unless the child is
34 emancipated.] or conflict with the provisions of section 1 of this
35 act.
36 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
