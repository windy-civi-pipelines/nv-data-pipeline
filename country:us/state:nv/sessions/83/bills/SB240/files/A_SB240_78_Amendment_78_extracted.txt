EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to state business licenses; reducing the fee for the issuance of a
state business license for certain businesses; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires certain businesses to obtain a state business license from the
2 Secretary of State. The fee for the issuance of a state business license is $500 for certain
3 corporations and $200 for certain other businesses. (NRS 76.100) This bill reduces the fee for
4 the issuance of a state business license for certain businesses which employ not more than 50
5 full-time or part-time employees to $100.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 76.100 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 76.100 1. A person shall not conduct a business in this State unless and until
3 the person obtains a state business license issued by the Secretary of State. If the
4 person is:
5 (a) An entity required to file an initial or annual list with the Secretary of State
6 pursuant to this title, the person must obtain the state business license at the time of
7 filing the initial or annual list.
8 (b) Not an entity required to file an initial or annual list with the Secretary of
9 State pursuant to this title, the person must obtain the state business license before
10 conducting a business in this State.
11 2. An application for a state business license must:
12 (a) Be made upon a form prescribed by the Secretary of State;
13 (b) Set forth the name under which the applicant transacts or intends to transact
14 business, or if the applicant is an entity organized pursuant to this title and on file
15 with the Secretary of State, the exact name on file with the Secretary of State, the
16 business identification number as assigned by the Secretary of State pursuant to
17 NRS 225.082, and the location in this State of the place or places of business;
Senate Amendment No. 78 to Senate Bill No. 240 Page 4
1 (c) [Be] Unless a reduced fee is otherwise provided in subsection 8, be
2 accompanied by a fee in the amount of $200 [, except that] or, if the applicant is a
3 corporation organized pursuant to chapter 78, 78A or 78B of NRS, or a foreign
4 corporation required to file an initial or annual list with the Secretary of State
5 pursuant to chapter 80 of NRS, [the application must be accompanied by] a fee of
6 $500; and
7 (d) Include any other information that the Secretary of State deems necessary.
8  If the applicant is an entity organized pursuant to this title and on file with the
9 Secretary of State and the applicant has no location in this State of its place of
10 business, the address of its registered agent shall be deemed to be the location in
11 this State of its place of business.
12 3. The application must be signed pursuant to NRS 239.330 by:
13 (a) The owner of a business that is owned by a natural person.
14 (b) A member or partner of an association or partnership.
15 (c) A general partner of a limited partnership.
16 (d) A managing partner of a limited-liability partnership.
17 (e) A manager or managing member of a limited-liability company.
18 (f) An officer of a corporation or some other person specifically authorized by
19 the corporation to sign the application.
20 4. If the application for a state business license is defective in any respect or
21 the fee required by this section is not paid, the Secretary of State may return the
22 application for correction or payment.
23 5. A state business license issued pursuant to this section must contain the
24 business identification number assigned by the Secretary of State pursuant to NRS
25 225.082.
26 6. The state business license required to be obtained pursuant to this section is
27 in addition to any license to conduct business that must be obtained from the local
28 jurisdiction in which the business is being conducted.
29 7. For the purposes of this chapter, a person:
30 (a) Shall be deemed to conduct a business in this State if a business for which
31 the person is responsible:
32 (1) Is organized pursuant to this title, other than a business organized
33 pursuant to:
34 (I) Chapter 82 or 84 of NRS; or
35 (II) Chapter 81 of NRS if the business is a nonprofit unit-owners’
36 association or a nonprofit religious, charitable, fraternal or other organization that
37 qualifies as a tax-exempt organization pursuant to 26 U.S.C. § 501(c);
38 (2) Has an office or other base of operations in this State;
39 (3) Except as otherwise provided in NRS 76.103, has a registered agent in
40 this State; or
41 (4) Pays wages or other remuneration to a natural person who performs in
42 this State any of the duties for which he or she is paid.
43 (b) Shall be deemed not to conduct a business in this State if:
44 (1) The business for which the person is responsible:
45 (I) Is not organized pursuant to this title;
46 (II) Does not have an office or base of operations in this State;
47 (III) Does not have a registered agent in this State; and
48 (IV) Does not pay wages or other remuneration to a natural person who
49 performs in this State any of the duties for which he or she is paid, other than wages
50 or other remuneration paid to a natural person for performing duties in connection
51 with an activity described in subparagraph (2);
Senate Amendment No. 78 to Senate Bill No. 240 Page 5
1 (2) The business for which the person is responsible is conducting activity
2 in this State solely to provide vehicles or equipment on a short-term basis in
3 response to a wildland fire, a flood, an earthquake or another emergency; or
4 (3) The Secretary of State determines that the person is not conducting a
5 business in this State.
6 8. If an applicant for a state business license is a [corporation described in
7 paragraph (c) of subsection 2,] sole proprietorship, limited-liability company,
8 limited partnership or general partnership which employs not more than 50 full-
9 time or part-time employees, the application must be accompanied by a fee of
10 $100.
11 9. As used in this section, “registered agent” has the meaning ascribed to it in
12 NRS 77.230.
13 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
