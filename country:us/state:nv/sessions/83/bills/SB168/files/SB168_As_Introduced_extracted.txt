Section 1:
S.B. 168
PREFILED FEBRUARY 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to cannabis.
(BDR 56-553)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to cannabis; authorizing an agent of the Cannabis
Compliance Board to issue a hold order for cannabis or a
cannabis product only under certain circumstances;
requiring the Board to adopt regulations governing hold
orders; revising requirements concerning the amount of
usable cannabis, THC and concentrated cannabis that may
be contained in a single package; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides for the licensure and regulation of persons and
2 establishments in the cannabis industry in this State by the Cannabis Compliance
3 Board. (Title 56 of NRS) Section 1 of this bill authorizes an agent of the Board to
4 issue a hold order for cannabis or a cannabis product to prohibit a person from
5 transferring, moving or disturbing the cannabis or cannabis product without the
6 approval of the Board or an agent of the Board only in accordance with regulations
7 adopted by the Board. Section 1 requires the Board to adopt such regulations
8 governing hold orders and sets forth certain requirements for the content of those
9 regulations.
10 Existing law requires an adult-use cannabis product to be sold in a single
11 package. (NRS 678D.420) Section 2 of this bill revises requirements concerning
12 the amount of usable cannabis, THC and concentrated cannabis that a single
13 package may contain.
-
*SB168*
– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 678A of NRS is hereby amended by
2 adding thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. An agent of the Board may issue a hold order for cannabis
4 or a cannabis product to prohibit a person from transferring,
5 moving or disturbing the cannabis or cannabis product subject to
6 the order without the approval of the Board or an agent of the
7 Board only in accordance with the regulations adopted by the
8 Board pursuant to this section.
9 2. The Board shall adopt regulations setting forth procedures
10 and requirements for the issuance of a hold order for cannabis or
11 a cannabis product. The regulations must:
12 (a) Authorize an agent of the Board to issue a hold order only
13 under circumstances in which cannabis or a cannabis product
14 fails to satisfy a requirement set forth in this title or the
15 regulations adopted pursuant thereto and constitutes a substantial
16 hazard to the public health.
17 (b) Require that at the time of issuance of a hold order notice
18 be provided to the cannabis establishment whose cannabis or
19 cannabis product is subject to the hold order stating the reasons
20 for the hold order.
21 (c) Require a hold order to be automatically terminated not
22 later than 14 days after its issuance unless the Board, after notice
23 and a hearing and for good cause shown, extends the duration of
24 the hold order.
25 (d) Set forth the circumstances under which a hold order may
26 be terminated. Such circumstances must include, without
27 limitation, for cannabis or a cannabis product subject to a hold
28 order because of the failure of any test conducted by a cannabis
29 independent testing laboratory, the passing of that test upon
30 retesting of the cannabis or cannabis product.
31 3. A person shall not transfer, move or disturb any cannabis
32 or cannabis product for which a hold order has been issued
33 pursuant to the regulations adopted pursuant to this section
34 without the approval of the Board or an agent of the Board.
35 Sec. 2. NRS 678D.420 is hereby amended to read as follows:
36 678D.420 1. An adult-use edible cannabis product or an
37 adult-use cannabis-infused product must be labeled in a manner
38 which indicates the number of servings of THC in the product,
39 measured in servings of a maximum of 10 milligrams per serving.
40 2. An adult-use cannabis product must be sold in a single
41 package. A single package must not contain:
-
*SB168*
– 3 –
1 (a) More than [1 ounce] 2.5 ounces of usable cannabis or [one-
2 eighth of an ounce] 3,500 milligrams of concentrated cannabis.
3 (b) For an adult-use cannabis product sold as a capsule, more
4 than 100 milligrams of THC per capsule or more than [800] 1,000
5 milligrams of THC per package.
6 (c) For an adult-use cannabis product sold as a tincture, more
7 than [800] 1,000 milligrams of THC.
8 (d) For an adult-use edible cannabis product, more than 100
9 milligrams of THC.
10 (e) For an adult-use cannabis product sold as a topical product, a
11 concentration of more than 6 percent THC or more than [800] 2,500
12 milligrams of THC per package.
13 (f) For an adult-use cannabis product sold as a suppository or
14 transdermal patch, more than 100 milligrams of THC per
15 suppository or transdermal patch or more than [800] 1,000
16 milligrams of THC per package.
17 (g) For any other adult-use cannabis product, more than [800]
18 1,000 milligrams of THC.
19 3. To the extent that they are inconsistent or otherwise conflict
20 with the regulations adopted by the Board pursuant to NRS
21 678D.480, the requirements of this section do not apply to a ready-
22 to-consume cannabis product prepared and sold by a cannabis
23 consumption lounge.
