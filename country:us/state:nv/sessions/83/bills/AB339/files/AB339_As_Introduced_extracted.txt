Section 1:
A.B. 339
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING COMMITTEE
ON HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES)
MARCH 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Creates the Office of Children’s Mental and
Behavioral Health within the Office of the Director
of the Department of Health and Human Services.
(BDR 39-358)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to mental health; revising the duties of the
Commission on Behavioral Health; creating the Office of
Children’s Mental and Behavioral Health within the
Office of the Director of the Department of Health and
Human Services; requiring certain entities to coordinate
with and submit certain documents to the Office;
establishing the powers and duties of the Office;
providing for the appointment of a Director of the
Office; eliminating the subcommittee on the mental health
of children of the Commission on Behavioral Health;
making appropriations; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Department of Health and Human Services, which
2 consists of a Director, various divisions, including the Division of Public and
3 Behavioral Health and Division of Child and Family Services, and certain other
4 entities. (NRS 232.300) Section 5 of this bill creates the Office of Children’s
5 Mental and Behavioral Health within the Office of the Director of the Department.
6 Section 4 of this bill defines the term “Office” to refer to the Office of Children’s
7 Mental and Behavioral Health for the purposes of this bill, and section 8 of this bill
-
*AB339*
– 2 –
8 applies that definition to applicable existing law. Section 5 requires: (1) the
9 Director of the Department to appoint a Director of the Office; and (2) requires
10 the Director of the Office to perform certain duties relating to the oversight of the
11 Office. Section 6 of this bill requires the Office to perform various duties relating
12 to the mental and behavioral health of children in this State. Section 7 of this bill
13 authorizes the Office to: (1) apply for and accept gifts, grants and donations; and
14 (2) seek assistance from and cooperate with public and private entities. Section 7
15 also requires state and local governmental entities to provide the Office with such
16 assistance as the Office may reasonably require in discharging its duties. Sections
17 11 and 12 of this bill make appropriations to the Department for the personnel and
18 operating costs of the Office and the cost of procuring equipment and office
19 supplies for the Office, respectively.
20 Existing law creates the Commission on Behavioral Health within the
21 Department. (NRS 232.361) Existing law requires the Commission to appoint a
22 subcommittee on the mental health of children. (NRS 433.317) Existing law also:
23 (1) establishes mental health consortia for different geographic areas of this State;
24 and (2) requires each mental health consortium to prepare and submit to the
25 Director of the Department a long-term strategic plan for the provision of mental
26 health services to children with emotional disturbance within the jurisdiction of the
27 consortium. (NRS 433B.333, 433B.335) Existing law requires the subcommittee on
28 the mental health of children to: (1) create a statewide plan for the provision of
29 mental health services to children; and (2) review the findings and
30 recommendations of each mental health consortium. (NRS 433.317) Section 13 of
31 this bill eliminates the subcommittee. Instead, section 6 requires the Office to: (1)
32 develop a statewide plan for the provision of mental and behavioral health services
33 to children in this State; and (2) review the long-term strategic plans, budget
34 requests and reports of each mental health consortium. Section 9 of this bill
35 accordingly requires each mental health consortium to submit to the Office its long-
36 term strategic plan and certain other reports. Section 9 further requires each
37 consortium to collaborate with the Office as necessary to prepare and submit such
38 reports and plans. Section 10 of this bill requires each mental health consortium to
39 coordinate with the Department and the Office to avoid duplicating or contradicting
40 the efforts of the Department and the Office to provide mental health services to
41 children. Section 1 of this bill requires the Commission to: (1) coordinate with the
42 Office regarding the mental and behavioral health of children in this State; and (2)
43 review the statewide plan developed by the Office pursuant to section 6 and
44 integrate the statewide plan into the policies established by the Commission where
45 appropriate.
46 Existing law: (1) establishes five behavioral health regions within this State; (2)
47 establishes a behavioral health policy board for each region; and (3) requires each
48 policy board to advise the Department, the Division of Public and Behavioral
49 Health and the Commission regarding, among other things, the behavioral health
50 needs of adults and children in each behavioral health region. (NRS 433.425-
51 433.4295) Section 2 of this bill requires each regional behavioral health policy
52 board to coordinate with the Office to provide unified and coordinated
53 recommendations to the Department, Division and Commission regarding the
54 behavioral health needs of children in each behavioral health region.

ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 339–COMMITTEE ON
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING COMMITTEE
ON HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES)
MARCH 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Creates the Office of Children’s Mental and
Behavioral Health within the Office of the Director
of the Department of Health and Human Services.
(BDR 39-358)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to mental health; revising the duties of the
Commission on Behavioral Health; creating the Office of
Children’s Mental and Behavioral Health within the
Office of the Director of the Department of Health and
Human Services; requiring certain entities to coordinate
with and submit certain documents to the Office;
establishing the powers and duties of the Office;
providing for the appointment of a Director of the
Office; eliminating the subcommittee on the mental health
of children of the Commission on Behavioral Health;
making appropriations; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Department of Health and Human Services, which
2 consists of a Director, various divisions, including the Division of Public and
3 Behavioral Health and Division of Child and Family Services, and certain other
4 entities. (NRS 232.300) Section 5 of this bill creates the Office of Children’s
5 Mental and Behavioral Health within the Office of the Director of the Department.
6 Section 4 of this bill defines the term “Office” to refer to the Office of Children’s
7 Mental and Behavioral Health for the purposes of this bill, and section 8 of this bill

8 applies that definition to applicable existing law. Section 5 requires: (1) the
9 Director of the Department to appoint a Director of the Office; and (2) requires
10 the Director of the Office to perform certain duties relating to the oversight of the
11 Office. Section 6 of this bill requires the Office to perform various duties relating
12 to the mental and behavioral health of children in this State. Section 7 of this bill
13 authorizes the Office to: (1) apply for and accept gifts, grants and donations; and
14 (2) seek assistance from and cooperate with public and private entities. Section 7
15 also requires state and local governmental entities to provide the Office with such
16 assistance as the Office may reasonably require in discharging its duties. Sections
17 11 and 12 of this bill make appropriations to the Department for the personnel and
18 operating costs of the Office and the cost of procuring equipment and office
19 supplies for the Office, respectively.
20 Existing law creates the Commission on Behavioral Health within the
21 Department. (NRS 232.361) Existing law requires the Commission to appoint a
22 subcommittee on the mental health of children. (NRS 433.317) Existing law also:
23 (1) establishes mental health consortia for different geographic areas of this State;
24 and (2) requires each mental health consortium to prepare and submit to the
25 Director of the Department a long-term strategic plan for the provision of mental
26 health services to children with emotional disturbance within the jurisdiction of the
27 consortium. (NRS 433B.333, 433B.335) Existing law requires the subcommittee on
28 the mental health of children to: (1) create a statewide plan for the provision of
29 mental health services to children; and (2) review the findings and
30 recommendations of each mental health consortium. (NRS 433.317) Section 13 of
31 this bill eliminates the subcommittee. Instead, section 6 requires the Office to: (1)
32 develop a statewide plan for the provision of mental and behavioral health services
33 to children in this State; and (2) review the long-term strategic plans, budget
34 requests and reports of each mental health consortium. Section 9 of this bill
35 accordingly requires each mental health consortium to submit to the Office its long-
36 term strategic plan and certain other reports. Section 9 further requires each
37 consortium to collaborate with the Office as necessary to prepare and submit such
38 reports and plans. Section 10 of this bill requires each mental health consortium to
39 coordinate with the Department and the Office to avoid duplicating or contradicting
40 the efforts of the Department and the Office to provide mental health services to
41 children. Section 1 of this bill requires the Commission to: (1) coordinate with the
42 Office regarding the mental and behavioral health of children in this State; and (2)
43 review the statewide plan developed by the Office pursuant to section 6 and
44 integrate the statewide plan into the policies established by the Commission where
45 appropriate.
46 Existing law: (1) establishes five behavioral health regions within this State; (2)
47 establishes a behavioral health policy board for each region; and (3) requires each
48 policy board to advise the Department, the Division of Public and Behavioral
49 Health and the Commission regarding, among other things, the behavioral health
50 needs of adults and children in each behavioral health region. (NRS 433.425-
51 433.4295) Section 2 of this bill requires each regional behavioral health policy
52 board to coordinate with the Office to provide unified and coordinated
53 recommendations to the Department, Division and Commission regarding the
54 behavioral health needs of children in each behavioral health region.
