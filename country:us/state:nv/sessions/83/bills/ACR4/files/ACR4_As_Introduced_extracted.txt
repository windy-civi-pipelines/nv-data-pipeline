Section 1:
A.C.R. 4
ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 4–COMMITTEE
ON LEGISLATIVE OPERATIONS AND ELECTIONS
JUNE 2, 2025
____________
Read and Adopted
in recognition of their service to the 83rd Session of
the Nevada Legislature. (BDR R-1244)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION—Granting
administrative leave to legislative employees in
recognition of their service to the 83rd Session of the
Nevada Legislature.
1 WHEREAS, The staff of the Nevada Legislature and the
2 Administrative, Audit, Fiscal Analysis, Legal and Research
3 Divisions of the Legislative Counsel Bureau provide services to the
4 Legislature which are vital to an efficient and productive session of
5 the Nevada Legislature; and
6 WHEREAS, The additional employees hired specifically for the
7 legislative session also provide essential services with dedication,
8 competence and enthusiasm throughout the long days of the session;
9 and
10 WHEREAS, Extraordinary demands are routinely placed on
11 legislative employees as a result of the time constraints of the 120-
12 day limitation on the length of the legislative session, and the entire
13 legislative staff has demonstrated their amazing work ethic and
14 ability to overcome obstacles, meet deadlines, assist legislators and
15 exhibit patience, flexibility and dedication; and
16 WHEREAS, Former Assemblyman Bob Price and Former Senator
17 Debbie Smith had a tradition of introducing legislation to allow the
18 Legislature to recognize the hard work and dedication of legislative
19 employees by granting administrative leave following each
20 legislative session; and
21 WHEREAS, In keeping with that tradition, the members of the
22 83rd session of the Nevada Legislature do hereby recognize and
-
*ACR4*
– 2 –
1 express their sincere gratitude for the outstanding effort made by the
2 permanent employees of the Legislature and Legislative Counsel
3 Bureau and by all other employees hired temporarily for the
4 legislative session; now, therefore, be it
5 RESOLVED BY THE ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, THE
6 SENATE CONCURRING, That 5 days of administrative leave are
7 hereby granted to each:
8 1. Permanent employee of the Legislature and Legislative
9 Counsel Bureau who is employed on the last day of session; and
10 2. Employee of the Legislature and Legislative Counsel Bureau
11 hired temporarily for the legislative session who:
12 (a) Is employed by the Legislature or Legislative Counsel
13 Bureau on the last day of this session; and
14 (b) If requested to do so by the employee’s supervisor, remains
15 in that employment after the last day of this session until all tasks
16 assigned to the employee during the session are completed; and be it
17 further
18 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon
19 adoption.
H
