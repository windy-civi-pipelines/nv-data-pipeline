Section 1:
A.B. 97
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY)
PREFILED JANUARY 13, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to public safety.
(BDR 14-499)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public safety; revising provisions governing
requirements for the submission of certain information to
the Central Repository for Nevada Records of Criminal
History; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) requires each agency of criminal justice and any other agency
2 dealing with crime to maintain certain records and submit certain information to the
3 Central Repository for Nevada Records of Criminal History; and (2) establishes
4 certain requirements for the submission of such information to ensure compliance
5 with the policies, procedures and definitions prescribed by the Uniform Crime
6 Reporting Program of the Federal Bureau of Investigation. (NRS 179A.075)
7 Section 3 of this bill instead requires each such agency to make such a
8 submission through the use of the National Incident-Based Reporting System.
9 Section 1 of this bill creates a definition of the term “National Incident-Based
10 Reporting System,” and section 2 of this bill makes that definition applicable to
11 chapter 179A of NRS.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 179A of NRS is hereby amended by
2 adding thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 “National Incident-Based Reporting System” means the system
4 operated by the Bureau of Justice Statistics established in 34
5 U.S.C. § 10132.
6 Sec. 2. NRS 179A.010 is hereby amended to read as follows:
7 179A.010 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise
8 requires, the words and terms defined in NRS 179A.020 to
9 179A.073, inclusive, and section 1 of this act have the meanings
10 ascribed to them in those sections.
11 Sec. 3. NRS 179A.075 is hereby amended to read as follows:
12 179A.075 1. The Central Repository for Nevada Records of
13 Criminal History is hereby created within the Records,
14 Communications and Compliance Division of the Department.
15 2. Each agency of criminal justice and any other agency
16 dealing with crime shall:
17 (a) Collect and maintain records, reports and compilations of
18 statistical data required by the Department; and
19 (b) Submit the information collected to the Central Repository:
20 (1) In the manner approved by the Director of the
21 Department; and
22 (2) [In accordance with the policies, procedures and
23 definitions of the Uniform Crime Reporting Program of the Federal
24 Bureau of Investigation.] Through the use of the National
25 Incident-Based Reporting System.
26 3. Each agency of criminal justice shall submit the information
27 relating to records of criminal history that it creates, issues or
28 collects, and any information in its possession relating to the DNA
29 profile of a person from whom a biological specimen is obtained
30 pursuant to NRS 176.09123 or 176.0913, to the Division. The
31 information must be submitted to the Division:
32 (a) Through an electronic network;
33 (b) On a medium of magnetic storage; or
34 (c) In the manner prescribed by the Director of the Department,
35  within 60 days after the date of the disposition of the case. If an
36 agency has submitted a record regarding the arrest of a person who
37 is later determined by the agency not to be the person who
38 committed the particular crime, the agency shall, immediately upon
39 making that determination, so notify the Division. The Division
40 shall delete all references in the Central Repository relating to that
41 particular arrest.
