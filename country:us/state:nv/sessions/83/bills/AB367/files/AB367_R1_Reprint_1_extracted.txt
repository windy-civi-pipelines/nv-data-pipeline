FIRST REPRINT A.B. 367
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 367–COMMITTEE ON
LEGISLATIVE OPERATIONS AND ELECTIONS
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING COMMITTEE
ON LEGISLATIVE OPERATIONS AND ELECTIONS)
MARCH 6, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Makes various changes related to language access in
elections. (BDR 24-364)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to elections; setting forth certain language access
requirements relating to elections; requiring the Secretary
of State to employ a language access coordinator;
providing that a registered voter with a physical disability
may use a mobile device to access interpretive services;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, each voting system used by a county or city is required to
2 provide voting materials in English and every language in which voting materials
3 are required to be prepared pursuant to federal law. (52 U.S.C. § 10503; NRS
4 293.2699) Section 5 of this bill requires the Secretary of State to ensure that voting
5 materials and other elections information are posted on the Internet website of the
6 Office of the Secretary of State in certain languages. Section 4 of this bill defines
7 the term “voting materials.” Section 11 of this bill applies that definition to the
8 provisions of existing law relating to elections.
9 Section 6 of this bill requires the Secretary of State to establish a toll-free
10 telephone number which may be used by any person to receive language
11 interpretation or assistance with translation relating to an election.
12 Section 9 of this bill requires the Secretary of State to employ a person to serve
13 as the language access coordinator for the Office of the Secretary of State, who is in
14 the classified service of the State.
15 Existing law provides that, with certain exceptions, any registered voter who by
16 reason of a physical disability or an inability to read or write English is unable to
17 mark a ballot or use any voting device without assistance is entitled to assistance
18 from a consenting person of his or her own choice. (NRS 293.296) Section 16 of
-
*AB367_R1*
– 2 –
19 this bill provides that a registered voter with a physical disability may also use a
20 mobile device to access interpretive services to assist him or her in casting a vote in
21 an election. Such interpretive services include, without limitation, interpretive
22 services using American Sign Language.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 293 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 to 10, inclusive, of this
3 act.

5 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
6 Sec. 4. “Voting materials” means the following written
7 materials:
8 1. Applications to preregister or register to vote;
9 2. Ballots and the instructions for the use of such ballots,
10 including, without limitation, mail ballots, sample ballots and
11 provisional ballots;
12 3. Voting instructions;
13 4. Information and instructions relating to the location of
14 polling places, including, without limitation, vote centers,
15 permanent polling places for early voting and temporary branch
16 polling places and the hours during which such polling places will
17 be open;
18 5. Any notices required to be provided for an election
19 pursuant to title 24 of NRS;
20 6. Ballot questions;
21 7. Any forms prepared for an election to assist voters; and
22 8. Any other voting materials required to be translated
23 pursuant to the regulations adopted by the Secretary of State
24 pursuant to section 5 of this act.
25 Sec. 5. The Secretary of State shall ensure that voting
26 materials and other elections information that is posted on the
27 Internet website of the Office of the Secretary of State are
28 available on the website in any language in which voting materials
29 are required to be prepared pursuant to 52 U.S.C. § 10503 and
30 American Sign Language. If there are less than seven languages
31 in which voting materials are required to be prepared pursuant to
32 52 U.S.C. § 10503, the Secretary of State shall ensure that voting
33 materials and other elections information are posted pursuant to
34 this subsection in at least the seven most commonly spoken
35 languages in the State and American Sign Language. If any such
36 language does not have a standard writing system, the information
37 required to be posted must be set forth in an audio recording in
-

– 3 –
1 the language. Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, the
2 Secretary of State shall use a translator who is certified in the
3 specific language to translate the information required pursuant
4 to this subsection. If such a certified translator is not available to
5 translate the information as required by this subsection, the
6 Secretary of State shall make a continuous effort to find such a
7 certified translator or coordinate with the respective minority
8 group for assistance with the translation.
9 Sec. 6. 1. The Secretary of State shall:
10 (a) Establish a toll-free telephone number which may be used
11 by any person in this State to receive language interpretation or
12 assistance with translation relating to an election, including,
13 without limitation, voter registration or other voter assistance; and
14 (b) Post the toll-free telephone number established pursuant to
15 paragraph (a) on the Internet website of the Office of the
16 Secretary of State.
17 2. The Secretary of State shall ensure that the toll-free
18 telephone number established pursuant to subsection 1 provides
19 language interpretation or assistance with translation relating to
20 an election in the 200 most commonly spoken languages in the
21 State and American Sign Language.
22 Sec. 7. (Deleted by amendment.)
23 Sec. 8. (Deleted by amendment.)
24 Sec. 9. 1. The Secretary of State shall employ a person to
25 serve as the language access coordinator for the Office of the
26 Secretary of State.
27 2. A language access coordinator employed pursuant to
28 subsection 1 is in the classified service of the State.
29 Sec. 10. In addition to any other requirement relating to
30 language access, the Secretary of State shall ensure that all
31 elections-related communications and information are made
32 available to voters in English and Spanish.

5 2. A person providing assistance pursuant to this section to a
6 voter in casting a vote shall not disclose any information with
7 respect to the casting of that ballot.
8 3. A registered voter with a physical disability may use a

19 request of a registered voter with a physical disability, make
20 reasonable accommodations to allow the voter to vote at a polling
21 place at which he or she is entitled to vote.
