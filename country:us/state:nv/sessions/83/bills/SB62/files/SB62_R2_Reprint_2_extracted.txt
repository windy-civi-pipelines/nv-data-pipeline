SECOND REPRINT S.B. 62
SENATE BILL NO. 62–COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY
(ON BEHALF OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL)
PREFILED NOVEMBER 20, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to crimes. (BDR 16-507)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to crimes; expanding the definition of “victim” for
the purposes of eligibility for compensation from the
Fund for the Compensation of Victims of Crime to
include a person targeted because of certain actual or
perceived characteristics of the person; authorizing such a
person to receive compensation from the Fund for the
remediation of property owned by the person under
certain circumstances; making appropriations; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes certain victims of crime to receive compensation from
2 the Fund for the Compensation of Victims of Crime. (NRS 217.100, 217.260)
3 Section 4.3 of this bill expands the definition of “victim” for the purposes of
4 eligibility for compensation from the Fund to include a person targeted because of
5 certain characteristics. Section 4.7 of this bill authorizes such a victim to receive an
6 amount not to exceed $1,000 per incident from the Fund for the remediation of
7 certain property damaged in the commission of the crime perpetrated against him or
8 her. Section 5 of this bill makes an appropriation to the: (1) Fund for the purposes
9 of providing aid to certain victims; and (2) Office of the Attorney General for the
10 purposes of providing support and resources to survivors of crimes prosecuted by
11 the Office of the Attorney General.
12 Existing law creates the Account for Victims of Human Trafficking in the State
13 General Fund and requires a recipient of an allocation of money from the Account
14 to use the money only for establishing or providing programs or services to victims
-
*SB62_R2*
– 2 –
15 of human trafficking. (NRS 217.530, 217.540) Section 5 makes an appropriation to
16 the Account.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
4 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
5 Sec. 4.3. NRS 217.070 is hereby amended to read as follows:
6 217.070 1. “Victim” means a person who suffers direct or
7 threatened physical, financial or psychological harm as a result of
8 the commission of a crime, including, without limitation:
9 (a) A person who is injured or killed as the direct result of a
10 criminal act;
11 (b) A minor who was involved in the production of pornography
12 in violation of NRS 200.710, 200.720, 200.725 or 200.730;
13 (c) A minor who was sexually abused, as “sexual abuse” is
14 defined in NRS 432B.100;
15 (d) A person who is physically injured or killed as the direct
16 result of a violation of NRS 484C.110 or any act or neglect of duty
17 punishable pursuant to NRS 484C.430 or 484C.440;
18 (e) A pedestrian who is physically injured or killed as the direct
19 result of a driver of a motor vehicle who failed to stop at the scene
20 of a crash involving the driver and the pedestrian in violation of
21 NRS 484E.010;
22 (f) An older person or a vulnerable person who is abused,
23 neglected, exploited, isolated or abandoned in violation of NRS
24 200.5099 or 200.50995;
25 (g) A person who is physically injured or killed as the direct
26 result of an act of international terrorism as defined in 18 U.S.C. §
27 2331(1);
28 (h) A person who is trafficked in violation of subsection 2 of
29 NRS 201.300;
30 (i) A veteran who experienced an act of sexual assault while
31 serving on active duty, active duty for training or inactive duty
32 training;
33 (j) A person who is subjected to facilitating sex trafficking in
34 violation of subsection 1 of NRS 201.301; [or]
35 (k) A person who is the victim of a crime for which an
36 additional penalty is imposed pursuant to NRS 193.1675 or an
37 enhanced penalty is imposed pursuant to NRS 207.185; or
38 (l) A person who is an immediate family member of a victim
39 who:
-
*SB62_R2*
– 3 –
1 (1) Is a minor;
2 (2) Is physically or mentally incompetent; or
3 (3) Was killed.
4 2. The term includes any person who was harmed by an act
5 listed in subsection 1, regardless of whether:
6 (a) The person is a resident of this State, a citizen of the United
7 States or is lawfully entitled to reside in the United States; or
8 (b) The act was committed by an adult or a minor.
9 Sec. 4.7. NRS 217.160 is hereby amended to read as follows:
10 217.160 1. The compensation officer may order the payment
11 of compensation:
12 (a) To or for the benefit of the victim.
13 (b) If the victim has suffered personal injury, to any person
14 responsible for the maintenance of the victim who has suffered
15 pecuniary loss or incurred expenses as a result of the injury.
16 (c) If the victim dies, to or for the benefit of any one or more of
17 the dependents of the victim.
18 (d) To a minor who is a member of the household or immediate
19 family of a victim of a battery which constitutes domestic violence
20 pursuant to NRS 33.018 who needs an assessment, a psychological
21 evaluation or psychological counseling for emotional trauma
22 suffered by the minor as a result of the battery.
23 (e) To a member of the victim’s household or immediate family
24 for psychological counseling for emotional trauma suffered by the
25 member as a result of the crime of murder as defined in
26 NRS 200.010.
27 (f) To a county in whose jurisdiction a sexual assault was
28 committed for the reimbursement of costs associated with a forensic
29 medical examination of a victim of sexual assault that are paid by
30 the county pursuant to NRS 217.300. A county may be reimbursed
31 pursuant to this paragraph in an amount equal to the cost of 10
32 forensic medical examinations or $10,000, whichever is less, each
33 fiscal year.
34 (g) To a victim described in paragraph (k) of subsection 1 of
35 NRS 217.070 for the remediation of property owned by the victim
36 that was damaged in the commission of the crime, in an amount
37 not to exceed $1,000 for each incident.
38 2. As used in this section:
39 (a) “Battery” has the meaning ascribed to it in paragraph (a) of
40 subsection 1 of NRS 200.481.
41 (b) “Forensic medical examination” has the meaning ascribed to
42 it in NRS 217.300.
43 (c) “Household” means an association of persons who live in the
44 same home or dwelling and who:
45 (1) Have significant personal ties to the victim; or
-
*SB62_R2*
– 4 –
1 (2) Are related by blood, adoption or marriage, within the
2 first degree of consanguinity or affinity.
3 (d) “Immediate family” means persons who are related by
4 blood, adoption or marriage, within the first degree of consanguinity
5 or affinity.
6 (e) “Victim of sexual assault” has the meaning ascribed to it in
7 NRS 217.280.
8 Sec. 5. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
9 General Fund to the Fund for the Compensation of Victims of
10 Crime created by NRS 217.260 for the purposes described in
11 subsection 2 the following sums:
12 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 .................................... $30,000
13 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 .................................... $30,000
14 2. The money appropriated by subsection 1 must be used to
15 provide monetary aid to victims of hate crimes as described in
16 chapter 217 of NRS.
17 3. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
18 the Office of the Attorney General for the purposes described in
19 subsection 4 the following sums:
20 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 .................................... $20,000
21 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 .................................... $20,000
22 4. The money appropriated by subsection 3 must be used to
23 provide support and resources to survivors of crimes prosecuted by
24 the Office of the Attorney General.
25 5. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
26 the Account for Victims of Human Trafficking created by NRS
27 217.530 the sum of $200,000.
28 6. As used in this section, “hate crime” means a crime for
29 which an:
30 (a) Additional penalty is imposed pursuant to NRS 193.1675; or
31 (b) Enhanced penalty is imposed pursuant to NRS 207.185.
32 Sec. 6. (Deleted by amendment.)
