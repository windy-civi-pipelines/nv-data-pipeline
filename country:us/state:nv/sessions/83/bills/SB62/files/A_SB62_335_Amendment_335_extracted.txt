EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to crimes; [providing that] expanding the definition of “victim”
for the purposes of eligibility for compensation from the Fund for
the Compensation of Victims of Crime to include a person [who
commits certain acts] targeted because of certain actual or perceived
characteristics of [a person is guilty of a bias crime; creating a cause of
action for a person who has suffered injury as a result of a bias crime;
requiring the Central Repository for Nevada Records of Criminal
History to make certain data relating to bias crimes available to the
public; creating the Account for Survivors of Crime and prescribing
the use of money in the Account; making an appropriation to the
Account; eliminating certain duplicative definitions; providing a
penalty;] the person; authorizing such a person to receive
compensation from the Fund for the remediation of property
owned by the person under certain circumstances; making
appropriations; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 [Section 1 of this bill provides that a person is guilty of a bias crime if the person, because
2 of the actual or perceived race, color, religion, national origin, physical or mental disability,
3 sexual orientation or gender identity or expression of a person or group of persons,
4 intentionally: (1) tampers or interferes with the property of a person with the intent to
5 substantially annoy or inconvenience the person; (2) subjects a person to offensive physical
6 contact; or (3) subjects a person to alarm by making certain threats. Section 1 makes a bias
7 crime punishable as a misdemeanor.
8 Existing law authorizes a person who has suffered injury as the proximate result of the
9 commission of certain crimes by a perpetrator who was motivated by certain characteristics of
10 the injured person to bring a civil action to recover his or her actual damages and punitive
11 damages. (NRS 41.690) Section 2 of this bill additionally authorizes a person who has
Senate Amendment No. 335 to Senate Bill No. 62 Page 4
12 suffered injury as the proximate result of a violation of section 1 to bring a civil action to
13 recover his or her actual damages and punitive damages.
14 Existing law requires the Central Repository for Nevada Records of Criminal History to
15 make available to the public data regarding the prosecution of certain crimes that manifest
16 evidence of prejudice. (NRS 179A.175) Section 3 of this bill additionally requires the Central
17 Repository to make data regarding any prosecution of a violation of section 1 available to the
18 public.
19 Section 4 of this bill creates the Account for Survivors of Crime in the State General
20 Fund to be administered by the Attorney General. Section 4 requires money in the Account to
21 be used to provide support and resources to survivors of: (1) hate crimes for which certain
22 additional or enhanced penalties are imposed; (2) bias crimes pursuant to section 1; and (3)
23 any other crime prosecuted by the Office of the Attorney General.] Existing law authorizes
24 certain victims of crime to receive compensation from the Fund for the Compensation of
25 Victims of Crime. (NRS 217.100, 217.260) Section 4.3 of this bill expands the definition
26 of “victim” for the purposes of eligibility for compensation from the Fund to include a
27 person targeted because of certain characteristics. Section 4.7 of this bill authorizes such
28 a victim to receive an amount not to exceed $1,000 per incident from the Fund for the
29 remediation of certain property damaged in the commission of the crime perpetrated
30 against him or her. Section 5 of this bill makes an appropriation to the [Account created by
31 section 4] : (1) Fund for the purposes [described in section 4.
32 Existing law defines the term “gender identity or expression” and makes that definition
33 applicable to the Nevada Revised Statutes as a whole. (NRS 0.034) Section 6 of this bill
34 repeals a duplicative definition of the term “gender identity or expression.” (NRS 193.0148)
35 Sections 2 and 3 make conforming changes to eliminate references to the definition repealed
36 by section 6.] of providing aid to certain victims; and (2) Office of the Attorney General
37 for the purposes of providing support and resources to survivors of crimes prosecuted
38 by the Office of the Attorney General.
39 Existing law creates the Account for Victims of Human Trafficking in the State
40 General Fund and requires a recipient of an allocation of money from the Account to use
41 the money only for establishing or providing programs or services to victims of human
42 trafficking. (NRS 217.530, 217.540) Section 5 makes an appropriation to the Account.
