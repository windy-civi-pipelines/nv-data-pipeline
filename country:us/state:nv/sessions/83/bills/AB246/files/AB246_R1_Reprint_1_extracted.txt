FEBRUARY 17, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to offenders.
(BDR 16-686)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to offenders; requiring the Department of
Corrections, to the extent money is available, to establish
vocational farm programs; authorizing the Department to
engage the services of certain registered dietitians;
authorizing the Department to adopt regulations relating
to such dietitians; requiring the Department to issue a
request for information and conduct a study on food
services during the 2025-2026 interim; limiting the term
of renewals of food service contracts during that period;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes the Director of the Department of Corrections, with the
2 approval of the Board of State Prison Commissioners, to allow offenders to
3 participate in certain educational, civic and charitable programs deemed beneficial
4 to the community. (NRS 209.471) Section 1.1 of this bill requires the Department,
5 to the extent money is available, to establish vocational farm programs in such
6 institutions or facilities operated by the Department as the Director deems practical.
7 Section 1.1 also prescribes certain requirements applicable to any such vocational
8 farm program.
9 Under existing law, each offender in an institution or facility of the Department
10 must be provided a healthful diet. (NRS 209.381) Section 1.3 of this bill authorizes
11 the Department to contract for the services of, or accept the volunteer services of, a
12 registered dietitian who is not employed by the Department and is not affiliated
13 with a food service vendor that has a contract with the Department. Section 1.3
14 also: (1) authorizes the Department to adopt regulations concerning the roles and
15 responsibilities of any such registered dietitian; and (2) requires those regulations to
16 be adopted in accordance with the Nevada Administrative Procedure Act. (Chapter
17 233B of NRS)
-

– 2 –
18 Section 1.7 of this bill requires the Department to issue a request for
19 information regarding food service contracts during the 2025-2026 interim and
20 outlines specific requirements that must be included in the request.
21 Section 1.9 of this bill: (1) requires the Department to conduct a comprehensive
22 1-year study during the 2025–2026 interim to evaluate the food services provided
23 in institutions and facilities of the Department; (2) establishes specific requirements
24 for the study; and (3) requires the Department to publish the results of the study on
25 the Internet website of the Department. Additionally, section 1.9 provides that if
26 the Department renews a contract with a food service provider during the period in
27 which the study is conducted, the term of the renewal must not exceed 2 years.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 209 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 1.1 and 1.3 of this act.
3 Sec. 1.1. 1. To the extent money is available for this
4 purpose, the Department shall establish vocational farm programs
5 in such institutions or facilities as the Director deems practical.
6 2. If a vocational farm program is established pursuant to
7 subsection 1:
8 (a) Participation in the program must be voluntary; and
9 (b) The program must:
10 (1) Comply with fair labor standards appropriate for
11 voluntary programs within correctional institutions;
12 (2) Implement sustainable agricultural practices suited to
13 the climate in this State; and
14 (3) Serve an educational and therapeutic purpose.
15 3. To the greatest extent practicable:
16 (a) Food produced through a vocational farm program
17 established pursuant to subsection 1 must be used in meals served
18 to offenders; and
19 (b) Any surplus of such food must be donated to charitable
20 organizations in the surrounding community.
21 Sec. 1.3. 1. The Department may contract for the services
22 of, or accept the volunteer services of, a registered dietitian who is
23 not employed by the Department and is not affiliated with a food
24 service vendor that has a contract with the Department.
25 2. The Department may adopt regulations concerning the
26 roles and responsibilities of a registered dietitian authorized to
27 provide services pursuant to subsection 1. Any such regulations
28 must be adopted in accordance with the provisions of chapter
29 233B of NRS.
30 3. As used in this section, “registered dietitian” has the
31 meaning ascribed to it in NRS 640E.080.

22 comprehensive study to evaluate the food services in institutions and
23 facilities of the Department. The study must:
24 (a) Provide actionable insights to inform future policy decisions

26 (b) Be completed within a 1-year period during the 2025-2026
27 interim.
28 2. In conducting the study required by subsection 1, the
29 Department shall:
30 (a) Use various data collection methods, including, without
31 limitation, surveys, interviews and focus groups, involving
32 Department staff and offenders; and

34 supported by a robust evidence base.

36 limitation:
37 (a) A comprehensive cost analysis of the:
38 (1) Actual food service expenditures during the period in
39 which the study is conducted; and
40 (2) Projected costs for improving food quality, including any
41 anticipated increases in expenditures;
42 (b) A comparison of the nutritional value of current food service
43 offerings with a higher-quality alternative;
44 (c) An evaluation of food safety protocols and kitchen practices
45 in institutions or facilities of the Department;

2 each institution or facility of the Department, including both the

6 conducted;
7 (f) A 90-day pilot program offering a “Pick Your Own Menu”
8 option, measuring food waste levels before, during and after the test
9 period;
10 (g) An evaluation of the impact of food services on the physical

18 by subsection 1 on the Internet website of the Department.
19 5. If the Department renews a contract with a food service

21 term of the renewal must not exceed 2 years.
