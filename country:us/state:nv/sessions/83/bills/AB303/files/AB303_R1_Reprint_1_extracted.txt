Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT A.B. 303
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 303–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS
KARRIS; AND JACKSON
FEBRUARY 27, 2025
____________
JOINT SPONSOR: SENATOR HANSEN
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions governing public works.
(BDR 28-866)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public works; requiring a contractor or
subcontractor engaged on certain public works that are
projects for the development, deployment or improvement
of broadband, telecommunication or fiber optic services
to submit certain records to the Board of Directors of the
Nevada State Infrastructure Bank; specifying that the
provisions governing public works do not apply to certain
contracts; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law sets forth various requirements for public works, including,
2 without limitation, requirements for: (1) procedures for the awarding of contracts
3 by a public body; (2) the prevailing wage to be paid to certain workers employed in
4 connection with the public work; (3) the retention of records relating to workers
5 employed in connection with the public work and the wages and benefits paid to
6 such workers; and (4) a contractor on a public work to establish certain fair
7 employment practices. (Chapter 338 of NRS)
8 Section 2 of this bill provides that the provisions of existing law governing
9 public works do not apply to any contract awarded for the development,
10 deployment or improvement of privately owned broadband, telecommunication or
11 fiber optic services.
12 Existing law requires a contractor or subcontractor engaged on a public work to
13 maintain certain records concerning workers employed by the contractor or
14 subcontractor in connection with the public work and the wages and benefits paid

– 2 –
15 to such workers. Existing law requires a contractor or subcontractor to ensure that a
16 copy of each such record for each calendar month is received by the public body
17 awarding the contract not later than 15 days after the end of each month. (NRS
18 338.070) Section 1 of this bill requires a contractor or subcontractor engaged on a
19 public work that is a project for the development, deployment or improvement of
20 broadband, telecommunication or fiber optic services who submits to the public
21 body awarding the contract a copy of such a record to simultaneously submit a
22 copy of the record to the Board of Directors of the Nevada State Infrastructure
23 Bank.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 338 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 A contractor or subcontractor engaged on a public work that is
4 a project for the development, deployment or improvement of
5 broadband, telecommunication or fiber optic services who,
6 pursuant to subsection 6 of NRS 338.070, submits to the public
7 body awarding the contract a copy of a record required pursuant
8 to subsection 5 of NRS 338.070 shall simultaneously submit a
9 copy of the record to the Board of Directors of the Nevada State
10 Infrastructure Bank.
11 Sec. 2. NRS 338.011 is hereby amended to read as follows:
12 338.011 The requirements of this chapter do not apply to a
13 contract:
14 1. Awarded in compliance with chapter 332 or 333 of NRS
15 which is directly related to the normal operation of the public body
16 or the normal maintenance of its property.
17 2. Awarded to meet an emergency which results from a natural
18 or artificially created disaster and which threatens the health, safety
19 or welfare of the public. If the public body or its authorized
20 representative determines that an emergency exists, a contract or
21 contracts necessary to contend with the emergency may be let
22 without complying with the requirements of this chapter. If such
23 emergency action was taken by the authorized representative, the
24 authorized representative shall report the contract or contracts to the
25 public body at the next regularly scheduled meeting of the public
26 body.
27 3. Awarded for the development, deployment or improvement
28 of privately owned broadband, telecommunication or fiber optic
29 services.
