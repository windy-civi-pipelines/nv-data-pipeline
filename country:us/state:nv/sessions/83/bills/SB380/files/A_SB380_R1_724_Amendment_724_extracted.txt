EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to autism; requiring peace officers to complete certain training
concerning [autism spectrum disorders;] developmental disabilities;
authorizing certain training concerning autism spectrum disorders
to be included as part of the training of peace officers in
interacting with persons with developmental disabilities;
authorizing an applicant for registration as a registered behavior
technician to engage in the supervised practice of applied behavior
analysis under certain conditions; requiring the Legislative Auditor
to conduct an audit of the Board of Applied Behavior Analysis; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires a peace officer, as a condition of certification, to complete training
2 concerning identifying and interacting with persons with developmental disabilities,
3 including, without limitation, autism. (NRS 289.605) Section 3 of this bill [requires]
4 authorizes such training to include [at least 4 hours of in-person] instruction in certain
5 specific topics relating to autism spectrum disorders. Section 2 of this bill requires continuing
6 education for peace officers to include training in interactions with persons with
7 developmental disabilities, [including] which may include training on recognizing and
8 responding to persons with an autism spectrum disorder. Section 1 of this bill defines the term
9 “autism spectrum disorder” for the purposes of sections 2 and 3.
10 Existing law provides for the registration of registered behavior technicians who provide
11 applied behavior analysis services under the supervision of a licensed behavior analyst or
12 assistant behavior analyst to persons with autism spectrum disorder in this State. (NRS
13 641D.300, 641D.310, 641D.600, 641D.610) Existing law requires the Board of Applied
14 Behavior Analysis to issue a determination to an applicant for registration as a registered
15 behavior technician not more than 120 days after receiving his or her application for
16 registration. (NRS 641D.310) Section 4 of this bill authorizes an applicant for such
17 registration to engage in the practice of applied behavior analysis under the direct supervision
18 of a licensed behavior analyst [until] pending the completion of the registration process by
19 the Board . [issues a determination if, after a background investigation, the President or
20 Secretary-Treasurer of the Board, or an employee of the Board designated by the Board
Senate Amendment No. 724 to Senate Bill No. 380 First Reprint Page 4
21 determines that the applicant has not been convicted of any crime that may disqualify him or
22 her from licensure.]
23 Section 4.5 of this bill requires the Legislative Auditor to conduct an audit of the
24 process of the Board of Applied Behavior Analysis for licensure of behavior analysts and
25 assistant behavior analysts and registration of registered behavior technicians,
26 including, without limitation, the effectiveness, efficiency and timeliness of the Board in
27 processing applications. Section 4.5 requires the Legislative Auditor to present a final
28 written report of the audit to the Audit Subcommittee of the Legislative Commission.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN

1 Section 1. NRS 289.010 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 289.010 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise requires:
3 1. “Administrative file” means any file of a peace officer containing
4 information, comments or documents about the peace officer. The term does not
5 include any file relating to an investigation conducted pursuant to NRS 289.057 or
6 a criminal investigation of a peace officer.
7 2. “Adult use of cannabis” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 678A.075.
8 3. “Autism spectrum disorder” means a condition that meets the diagnostic
9 criteria for autism spectrum disorder published in the current edition of the
10 Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders published by the
11 American Psychiatric Association or the edition thereof that was in effect at the
12 time the condition was diagnosed or determined.
13 4. “Law enforcement agency” means any agency, office, bureau, department,
14 unit or division created by any statute, ordinance or rule which:
15 (a) Has a duty to enforce the law; and
16 (b) Employs any person upon whom some or all of the powers of a peace
17 officer are conferred pursuant to NRS 289.150 to 289.360, inclusive.
18 [4.] 5. “Medical use of cannabis” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS
19 678A.215.
20 [5.] 6. “Peace officer” means any person upon whom some or all of the
21 powers of a peace officer are conferred pursuant to NRS 289.150 to 289.360,
22 inclusive.
23 [6.] 7. “Punitive action” means any action which may lead to dismissal,
24 demotion, suspension, reduction in salary, written reprimand or transfer of a peace
25 officer for purposes of punishment.
26 [7.] 8. “Screening test” means a test of a person’s blood, urine, hair or saliva
27 to detect the general presence of a controlled substance or other drug.
28 Sec. 2. NRS 289.510 is hereby amended to read as follows:
29 289.510 1. The Commission:
30 (a) Shall meet at the call of the Chair, who must be elected by a majority vote
31 of the members of the Commission.
32 (b) Shall provide for and encourage the training and education of persons
33 whose primary duty is law enforcement to ensure the safety of the residents of and
34 visitors to this State.
35 (c) May make necessary inquiries to determine whether the agencies of this
36 State and of the local governments are complying with standards set forth in the
37 regulations adopted pursuant to subsection 2.
38 (d) Shall carry out the duties required of the Commission pursuant to NRS
39 432B.610 and 432B.620.

2 functions of the Commission as set forth in NRS 289.450 to 289.680, inclusive.
3 (f) May enter into an interlocal agreement with an Indian tribe to provide
4 training to and certification of persons employed as police officers by that Indian
5 tribe.
6 (g) Shall develop and approve a standard curriculum of certified training
7 programs in crisis intervention, which may be made available in an electronic
8 format, and which address specialized responses to persons with mental illness and
9 train peace officers to identify the signs and symptoms of mental illness, to de-
10 escalate situations involving persons who appear to be experiencing a behavioral
11 health crisis and, if appropriate, to connect such persons to treatment. A peace
12 officer who completes any program developed pursuant to this paragraph must be
13 issued a certificate of completion.
14 2. The Commission shall adopt regulations establishing minimum standards
15 for:
16 (a) The certification and decertification, recruitment, selection and training of
17 peace officers. The standards adopted pursuant to this paragraph must:
18 (1) Establish requirements for evaluations to be conducted during the
19 recruitment and selection of peace officers, which must identify implicit bias on the
20 part of a peace officer on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, physical
21 or mental disability, sexual orientation or gender identity or expression;
22 (2) Establish requirements for basic training for category I, category II and
23 category III peace officers and reserve peace officers;
24 (3) Establish standards for programs for the continuing education of peace

26 attendance, which must require that all peace officers annually complete not less
27 than 12 hours of continuing education in courses that address:
28 (I) Racial profiling;

30 (III) The well-being of officers;
31 (IV) Implicit bias recognition;
32 (V) De-escalation;
33 (VI) Human trafficking; [and]
34 (VII) Firearms; and
35 (VIII) Interactions with persons with developmental disabilities
36 which [must] may include, without limitation, training on recognizing and
37 responding to persons with an autism spectrum disorder;
38 (4) Establish qualifications for instructors of peace officers;
39 (5) Establish requirements for the certification of a course of training;
40 (6) Require all peace officers to receive training in the handling of cases
41 involving abuse or neglect of children or missing children;
42 (7) Require all peace officers to receive training in the handling of cases
43 involving abuse, neglect, exploitation, isolation and abandonment of older persons
44 or vulnerable persons;
45 (8) Not prohibit the certification of an applicant solely on the basis that the
46 applicant has engaged in the adult use of cannabis or the medical use of cannabis;
47 (9) Not require the decertification of a peace officer solely on the basis that
48 the peace officer has engaged in the adult use of cannabis or the medical use of
49 cannabis; and
50 (10) Require the decertification of a peace officer upon a determination by
51 the Commission that the peace officer knowingly provided false or misleading
52 information in his or her application for certification.

1 (b) An annual behavioral wellness visit for peace officers to aid in preserving
2 the emotional and mental health of the peace officer and assessing any conditions

4 (c) The reciprocity of a person who has been certified as a category III peace
5 officer or its equivalent by the certifying authority of another state or who has

16 (a) A condition precedent to employment; or
17 (b) A condition for continued employment.
18 Sec. 3. NRS 289.605 is hereby amended to read as follows:
19 289.605 1. The Commission shall require, as a condition of the certification
20 of each peace officer, the completion of training concerning identifying and
21 interacting with persons with developmental disabilities. Such training [must] may
22 include, without limitation, [at least 4 hours of in-person] training concerning:
23 (a) The nature and manifestation of autism spectrum disorders;
24 (b) Appropriate techniques for interviewing or interrogating a person with
25 an autism spectrum disorder;
26 (c) Techniques for locating a person with an autism spectrum disorder who
27 is missing and in danger, with as little stress as possible for the person with an
28 autism spectrum disorder;
29 (d) Techniques for recognizing the agency of a person with an autism
30 spectrum disorder while identifying potentially abusive or coercive situations;
31 (e) Techniques for deescalating a potentially dangerous situation involving a
32 person with an autism spectrum disorder to protect the safety of the peace officer
33 and the person with an autism spectrum disorder;
34 (f) Techniques for differentiating between a person with an autism spectrum
35 disorder and a person who does not have an autism spectrum disorder but is
36 acting belligerently, uncooperatively or otherwise similarly to a person with an
37 autism spectrum disorder;
38 (g) Procedures to identify and address challenges related to the safety of
39 persons with an autism spectrum disorder in correctional facilities; and
40 (h) The impact of interacting with peace officers on persons with autism
41 spectrum disorders.
42 2. Training completed pursuant to this section also satisfies the requirement
43 for such training prescribed by NRS 450B.160 or 450B.180, if applicable.
44 3. As used in this section, “developmental disability” has the meaning
45 ascribed to it in NRS 435.007.

49 applied behavior analysis under the direct supervision of a licensed behavior
50 analyst as part of:

52 in which he or she is enrolled; or
53 (b) An internship or fellowship.
