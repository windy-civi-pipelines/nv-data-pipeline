SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to autism spectrum disorders.
(BDR 23-118)

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to autism; requiring peace officers to complete certain training
concerning developmental disabilities; authorizing certain training
concerning autism spectrum disorders to be included as part of the
training of peace officers in interacting with persons with
developmental disabilities; [authorizing an applicant for registration as
a registered behavior technician to engage in the supervised practice of
applied behavior analysis under certain conditions;] requiring the
Legislative Auditor to conduct an audit of the Board of Applied
Behavior Analysis; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires a peace officer, as a condition of certification, to complete training
2 concerning identifying and interacting with persons with developmental disabilities,
3 including, without limitation, autism. (NRS 289.605) Section 3 of this bill authorizes such
4 training to include instruction in certain specific topics relating to autism spectrum disorders.
5 Section 2 of this bill requires continuing education for peace officers to include training in
6 interactions with persons with developmental disabilities, which may include training on
7 recognizing and responding to persons with an autism spectrum disorder. Section 1 of this bill
8 defines the term “autism spectrum disorder” for the purposes of sections 2 and 3.
9 Existing law provides for the registration of registered behavior technicians who provide
10 applied behavior analysis services under the supervision of a licensed behavior analyst or
11 assistant behavior analyst to persons with autism spectrum disorder in this State. (NRS
12 641D.300, 641D.310, 641D.600, 641D.610) [Existing law requires the Board of Applied
13 Behavior Analysis to issue a determination to an applicant for registration as a registered
14 behavior technician not more than 120 days after receiving his or her application for
15 registration. (NRS 641D.310) Section 4 of this bill authorizes an applicant for such
16 registration to engage in the practice of applied behavior analysis under the direct supervision
17 of a licensed behavior analyst pending the completion of the registration process by the
18 Board.]
19 Section 4.5 of this bill requires the Legislative Auditor to conduct an audit of the process
20 of the Board of Applied Behavior Analysis for licensure of behavior analysts and assistant
21 behavior analysts and registration of registered behavior technicians, including, without
Assembly Amendment No. 894 to Senate Bill No. 380 Second Reprint Page 4
22 limitation, the effectiveness, efficiency and timeliness of the Board in processing applications.
23 Section 4.5 requires the Legislative Auditor to present a final written report of the audit to the
24 Audit Subcommittee of the Legislative Commission.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 289.010 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 289.010 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise requires:
3 1. “Administrative file” means any file of a peace officer containing
4 information, comments or documents about the peace officer. The term does not
5 include any file relating to an investigation conducted pursuant to NRS 289.057 or
6 a criminal investigation of a peace officer.
7 2. “Adult use of cannabis” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 678A.075.
8 3. “Autism spectrum disorder” means a condition that meets the diagnostic
9 criteria for autism spectrum disorder published in the current edition of the
10 Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders published by the
11 American Psychiatric Association or the edition thereof that was in effect at the
12 time the condition was diagnosed or determined.
13 4. “Law enforcement agency” means any agency, office, bureau, department,
14 unit or division created by any statute, ordinance or rule which:
15 (a) Has a duty to enforce the law; and
16 (b) Employs any person upon whom some or all of the powers of a peace
17 officer are conferred pursuant to NRS 289.150 to 289.360, inclusive.
18 [4.] 5. “Medical use of cannabis” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS
19 678A.215.
20 [5.] 6. “Peace officer” means any person upon whom some or all of the
21 powers of a peace officer are conferred pursuant to NRS 289.150 to 289.360,
22 inclusive.
23 [6.] 7. “Punitive action” means any action which may lead to dismissal,
24 demotion, suspension, reduction in salary, written reprimand or transfer of a peace
25 officer for purposes of punishment.
26 [7.] 8. “Screening test” means a test of a person’s blood, urine, hair or saliva
27 to detect the general presence of a controlled substance or other drug.
28 Sec. 2. NRS 289.510 is hereby amended to read as follows:
29 289.510 1. The Commission:
30 (a) Shall meet at the call of the Chair, who must be elected by a majority vote
31 of the members of the Commission.
32 (b) Shall provide for and encourage the training and education of persons
33 whose primary duty is law enforcement to ensure the safety of the residents of and
34 visitors to this State.
35 (c) May make necessary inquiries to determine whether the agencies of this
36 State and of the local governments are complying with standards set forth in the
37 regulations adopted pursuant to subsection 2.
38 (d) Shall carry out the duties required of the Commission pursuant to NRS
39 432B.610 and 432B.620.
40 (e) May perform any other acts that may be necessary and appropriate to the
41 functions of the Commission as set forth in NRS 289.450 to 289.680, inclusive.
42 (f) May enter into an interlocal agreement with an Indian tribe to provide
43 training to and certification of persons employed as police officers by that Indian
44 tribe.

2 programs in crisis intervention, which may be made available in an electronic
3 format, and which address specialized responses to persons with mental illness and
4 train peace officers to identify the signs and symptoms of mental illness, to de-
5 escalate situations involving persons who appear to be experiencing a behavioral
6 health crisis and, if appropriate, to connect such persons to treatment. A peace
7 officer who completes any program developed pursuant to this paragraph must be
8 issued a certificate of completion.

10 for:
11 (a) The certification and decertification, recruitment, selection and training of
12 peace officers. The standards adopted pursuant to this paragraph must:
13 (1) Establish requirements for evaluations to be conducted during the
14 recruitment and selection of peace officers, which must identify implicit bias on the
15 part of a peace officer on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, physical
16 or mental disability, sexual orientation or gender identity or expression;
17 (2) Establish requirements for basic training for category I, category II and
18 category III peace officers and reserve peace officers;
19 (3) Establish standards for programs for the continuing education of peace
20 officers, including minimum courses of study and requirements concerning
21 attendance, which must require that all peace officers annually complete not less
22 than 12 hours of continuing education in courses that address:
23 (I) Racial profiling;
24 (II) Mental health, including, without limitation, crisis intervention;
25 (III) The well-being of officers;
26 (IV) Implicit bias recognition;
27 (V) De-escalation;
28 (VI) Human trafficking; [and]
29 (VII) Firearms; and
30 (VIII) Interactions with persons with developmental disabilities
31 which may include, without limitation, training on recognizing and responding to
32 persons with an autism spectrum disorder;
33 (4) Establish qualifications for instructors of peace officers;
34 (5) Establish requirements for the certification of a course of training;
35 (6) Require all peace officers to receive training in the handling of cases
36 involving abuse or neglect of children or missing children;
37 (7) Require all peace officers to receive training in the handling of cases
38 involving abuse, neglect, exploitation, isolation and abandonment of older persons
39 or vulnerable persons;
40 (8) Not prohibit the certification of an applicant solely on the basis that the
41 applicant has engaged in the adult use of cannabis or the medical use of cannabis;
42 (9) Not require the decertification of a peace officer solely on the basis that
43 the peace officer has engaged in the adult use of cannabis or the medical use of
44 cannabis; and
45 (10) Require the decertification of a peace officer upon a determination by
46 the Commission that the peace officer knowingly provided false or misleading
47 information in his or her application for certification.
48 (b) An annual behavioral wellness visit for peace officers to aid in preserving
49 the emotional and mental health of the peace officer and assessing any conditions
50 that may affect the performance of duties by the peace officer.
51 (c) The reciprocity of a person who has been certified as a category III peace
52 officer or its equivalent by the certifying authority of another state or who has

1 successfully completed a federal law enforcement training program that is
2 equivalent to a category III peace officer in this State.
3 3. The regulations adopted by the Commission pursuant to subsection 2:
4 (a) Apply to all agencies of this State and of local governments in this State
5 that employ persons as peace officers; and
6 (b) May require that training be carried on at institutions which it approves in
7 those regulations.
8 4. Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit a law enforcement
9 agency from adopting a policy that requires a peace officer to submit to a screening
10 test as:
11 (a) A condition precedent to employment; or
12 (b) A condition for continued employment.
13 Sec. 3. NRS 289.605 is hereby amended to read as follows:
14 289.605 1. The Commission shall require, as a condition of the certification
15 of each peace officer, the completion of training concerning identifying and
16 interacting with persons with developmental disabilities. Such training may
17 include, without limitation, training concerning:
18 (a) The nature and manifestation of autism spectrum disorders;
19 (b) Appropriate techniques for interviewing or interrogating a person with
20 an autism spectrum disorder;
21 (c) Techniques for locating a person with an autism spectrum disorder who
22 is missing and in danger, with as little stress as possible for the person with an
23 autism spectrum disorder;
24 (d) Techniques for recognizing the agency of a person with an autism
25 spectrum disorder while identifying potentially abusive or coercive situations;
26 (e) Techniques for deescalating a potentially dangerous situation involving a
27 person with an autism spectrum disorder to protect the safety of the peace officer
28 and the person with an autism spectrum disorder;
29 (f) Techniques for differentiating between a person with an autism spectrum
30 disorder and a person who does not have an autism spectrum disorder but is
31 acting belligerently, uncooperatively or otherwise similarly to a person with an
32 autism spectrum disorder;
33 (g) Procedures to identify and address challenges related to the safety of
34 persons with an autism spectrum disorder in correctional facilities; and
35 (h) The impact of interacting with peace officers on persons with autism
36 spectrum disorders.
37 2. Training completed pursuant to this section also satisfies the requirement
38 for such training prescribed by NRS 450B.160 or 450B.180, if applicable.
39 3. As used in this section, “developmental disability” has the meaning
40 ascribed to it in NRS 435.007.
