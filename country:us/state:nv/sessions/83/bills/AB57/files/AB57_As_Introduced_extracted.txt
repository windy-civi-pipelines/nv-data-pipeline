Section 1:
A.B. 57
ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS
(ON BEHALF OF THE DIVISION OF FORESTRY
OF THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF
CONSERVATION AND NATURAL RESOURCES)
PREFILED NOVEMBER 20, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the Nevada Intrastate
Mutual Aid System. (BDR 36-263)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to emergency management; revising provisions
governing the Nevada Intrastate Mutual Aid System to
require the State Forester Firewarden of the Division of
Forestry of the State Department of Conservation and
Natural Resources to administer the System as it relates to
wildfire suppression; requiring the State Forester
Firewarden to serve as Co-Chair of the Intrastate Mutual
Aid Committee and appoint committee members jointly
with the Chief of the Division of Emergency Management
of the Office of the Military; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes the Nevada Intrastate Mutual Aid System within the
2 Division of Emergency Management of the Office of the Military to coordinate
3 requests for intrastate mutual aid among various public agencies and certain Indian
4 tribes and nations in response to an emergency or disaster in this State. Under
5 existing law, the Chief of the Division of Emergency Management is required to
6 administer the System and adopt regulations relating to the administration of the
7 System. (NRS 414A.100) Section 4 of this bill transfers to the State Forester
8 Firewarden of the Division of Forestry of the State Department of Conservation and
9 Natural Resources the authority to: (1) coordinate the provision of mutual aid
10 during the response to and recovery from wildfire suppression; (2) administer the
-
*AB57*
– 2 –
11 System as it relates to wildfire suppression; and (3) adopt regulations relating to the
12 administration of the System as it relates to wildfire suppression.
13 Under existing law, the Chief of the Division of Emergency Management
14 serves as the Chair of the Intrastate Mutual Aid Committee and appoints the
15 committee members, who advise the Chief on issues related to emergency
16 management and intrastate mutual aid. (NRS 414A.110) Under section 5 of this
17 bill, the State Forester Firewarden serves as Co-Chair of the Committee with the
18 Chief of the Division of Emergency Management and committee members must be
19 jointly appointed by the Chief of the Division of Emergency Management and the
20 State Forester Firewarden.
21 Under existing law, a public agency that participates in the System may elect to
22 withdraw from participation in the System, and any federally recognized Indian
23 tribe or nation within the boundaries of the State may become a participant in the
24 System, by adopting a resolution declaring the same and providing a copy of the
25 resolution to the Division of Emergency Management and the Governor. (NRS
26 414A.120) Section 6 of this bill requires that a copy of such a resolution also be
27 provided to the Division of Forestry.
28 Under existing law, a participant may request intrastate mutual aid through the
29 Division of Emergency Management, or directly to a participant if an urgent
30 response is needed, and each request must be documented and forwarded to the
31 Division of Emergency Management within 24 hours. (NRS 414A.130) Section 7
32 of this bill provides that a participant may request intrastate mutual aid through the
33 Division of Forestry for aid relating to wildfire suppression and through the
34 Division of Emergency Management for aid relating to all other emergencies or
35 disasters. Section 7 further requires that requests for aid be documented and
36 reported to the Division of Forestry or Division of Emergency Management, as
37 appropriate.
38 Under existing law, the Division of Emergency Management: (1) may facilitate
39 certain System participants in obtaining reimbursement for intrastate mutual aid;
40 and (2) is not liable for any claim relating to reimbursement. (NRS 414A.150)
41 Section 8 of this bill authorizes the Division of Forestry to also facilitate
42 reimbursement of System participants and provides immunity from liability to the
43 Division of Forestry for any claim relating to reimbursement.
44 Sections 1 and 3 of this bill define “Division of Forestry” and “Division of
45 Emergency Management,” respectively. Section 2 of this bill applies these
46 definitions to the provisions of existing law relating to the System.
