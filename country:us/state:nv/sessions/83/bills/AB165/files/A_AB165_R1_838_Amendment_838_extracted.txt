ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 165–COMMITTEE ON NATURAL RESOURCES
(ON BEHALF OF THE OUTDOOR EDUCATION
ADVISORY WORKING GROUP)
PREFILED JANUARY 31, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Natural Resources
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to outdoor education. (BDR 35-633)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to outdoor education; establishing the Outdoor Education Council
within the Division of Outdoor Recreation of the State Department of
Conservation and Natural Resources; prescribing the membership and
duties of the Council; requiring the Council to establish and administer
a program to award designations relating to outdoor education;
authorizing the Council to adopt certain regulations relating to the
program; making an appropriation; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 During the 2023 Legislative Session, the Legislature enacted Assembly Bill No. 164,
2 which required the Division of Outdoor Recreation of the State Department of Conservation
3 and Natural Resources to establish an Outdoor Education Advisory Working Group during the
4 2023-2024 interim to study approaches to incorporate outdoor recreation into the curriculum
5 of the public education system of this State and authorized the Working Group to request the
6 drafting of not more than 1 legislative measure which relates to matters within the scope of the
7 Working Group. (Section 1.7 of chapter 77, Statutes of Nevada 2023, at page 393)
8 Section 3 of this bill establishes the Outdoor Education Council within the Division and
9 prescribes the membership of the Council. Section 4 of this bill sets forth the duties of the
10 Council, including a requirement for the Council to establish and administer a program to
11 award a designation to: (1) schools in this State which provide to pupils outdoor education
12 programs that meet certain standards developed by the Council pursuant to section 5 of this
13 bill; and (2) a teacher in this State as Outdoor Educator of the Year. Section 5 requires the
14 Council, in administering such a program, to provide a nonmonetary award to a teacher
15 recognized as Outdoor Educator of the Year.
16 Section 4 requires the Division, to the extent possible, to provide the Council with such
17 administrative support as is necessary to assist the Council in carrying out its duties and
18 authorizes the Division to accept gifts, grants and donations from any source to support the
19 work of the Council.
20 Section 6 of this bill authorizes the Council to adopt regulations necessary to carry out
21 the provisions of sections 4 and 5. Section 7 of this bill applies definitions in existing law
Assembly Amendment No. 838 to Assembly Bill No. 165 First Reprint Page 4
22 relating to the Division and the definition of “Council” set forth in section 2 of this bill to the
23 provisions of sections 2-6.
24 Section 8 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Division for travel expenses to
25 provide administrative support to the Council.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 407A of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 2 to 6, inclusive, of this act.
3 Sec. 2. “Council” means the Outdoor Education Council created by section
4 3 of this act.
5 Sec. 3. 1. The Outdoor Education Council is hereby created in the
6 Division. The Council consists of:
7 (a) The following ten voting members:
8 (1) One member who is a representative of the Division appointed by the
9 Administrator;
10 (2) One member who is a representative of the Department of Education
11 appointed by the Superintendent of Public Instruction;
12 (3) One member who is a representative of the Department of Wildlife
13 appointed by the Director of the Department of Wildlife;
14 (4) One superintendent of a school district appointed by the Nevada
15 Association of School Superintendents;
16 (5) Two members who are licensed teachers appointed by the Governor;
17 (6) Two members who are providers of nature-based education, one of
18 whom must primarily serve a historically underserved community, appointed by
19 the Governor;
20 (7) One member of the Senate appointed by the Majority Leader of the
21 Senate; and
22 (8) One member of the Assembly appointed by the Speaker of the
23 Assembly; and
24 (b) One nonvoting member who is a pupil enrolled in a public school in this
25 State appointed by the Governor.
26 2. Each member of the Council serves a term of 3 years. Members may be
27 reappointed for an additional term of 3 years in the same manner as the original
28 appointment. Any vacancy occurring in the membership of the Council must be
29 filled in the same manner as the original appointment for the remainder of the
30 unexpired term.
31 3. A majority of the members of the Council constitutes a quorum for the
32 transaction of business, and a majority of a quorum present at any meeting is
33 sufficient for any official action taken by the Council.
34 4. At the first meeting of the Council and annually thereafter, the members
35 of the Council shall elect a Chair and Vice Chair from among the members of the
36 Council.
37 5. Each member of the Council serves without compensation.
38 6. Any member of the Council who is an officer or employee of the State or
39 a political subdivision of this State must be relieved from his or her duties without
40 loss of regular compensation to prepare for and attend meetings of the Council
41 and perform any work necessary to carry out the duties of the Council in the most
42 timely manner practicable. A state agency or political subdivision of this State
43 shall not require an officer or employee who is a member of the Council to:
Assembly Amendment No. 838 to Assembly Bill No. 165 First Reprint Page 5
1 (a) Make up the time he or she is absent from work to carry out his or her
2 duties as a member of the Council; or
3 (b) Take annual leave or compensatory time for the absence.
4 7. As used in this section, “historically underserved community” has the
5 meaning ascribed to it in NRS 704.78343.
6 Sec. 4. 1. The Council shall:
7 (a) Establish and administer a program to award a designation to:
8 (1) Schools in this State which provide to pupils outdoor education
9 programs that meet the standards developed pursuant to section 5 of this act; and
10 (2) A teacher in this State as Outdoor Educator of the Year.
11 (b) Provide opportunities for educators to receive training concerning
12 outdoor education by developing such training or identifying and making
13 available to schools information concerning opportunities for such training.
14 (c) Provide technical assistance to support schools and school districts
15 interested in developing initiatives for outdoor education. In providing such
16 assistance, the Council shall prioritize schools with the least access to nature.
17 (d) Identify sources of federal money and grants to support outdoor
18 education in this State.
19 2. The Division:
20 (a) Shall, to the extent possible, provide the Council with such administrative
21 support as is necessary to assist the Council in carrying out its duties; and
22 (b) May accept gifts, grants and donations from any source to support the
23 work of the Council.
24 Sec. 5. 1. In administering the program established pursuant to section 4
25 of this act, the Council shall:
26 (a) Develop voluntary standards for outdoor education programs provided to
27 pupils by schools, including, without limitation, standards:
28 (1) Which provide for an interdisciplinary approach for a curriculum of
29 outdoor education; and
30 (2) Concerning the development and use of living schoolyards which are
31 climate-resilient; and
32 (b) Provide a nonmonetary award to a teacher recognized pursuant to section
33 4 of this act as Outdoor Educator of the Year.
34 2. For the purposes of this section, “living schoolyard” means an outdoor
35 environment at a school that strengthens local ecological systems and serves as a
36 resource for hands-on education, including, without limitation, trees, gardens,
37 outdoor meeting areas and other areas designed for such purposes.
38 Sec. 6. The Council may adopt such regulations as are necessary to carry
39 out the provisions of sections 4 and 5 of this act.
40 Sec. 7. NRS 407A.500 is hereby amended to read as follows:
41 407A.500 As used in NRS 407A.500 to 407A.620, inclusive, and sections 2
42 to 6, inclusive, of this act, unless the context otherwise requires, the words and
43 terms defined in NRS 407A.510, 407A.515 and 407A.525 and section 2 of this act
44 have the meanings ascribed to them in those sections.
45 Sec. 8. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
46 the Division of Outdoor Recreation of the State Department of Conservation
47 and Natural Resources for travel expenses to provide administrative support
48 to the Outdoor Education Council created by section 3 of this act the following
49 sums:
50 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ......................................................... $6,120
51 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ......................................................... $6,120
52 2. Any balance of the sums appropriated by subsection 1 remaining at
53 the end of the respective fiscal years must not be committed for expenditure
Assembly Amendment No. 838 to Assembly Bill No. 165 First Reprint Page 6
1 after June 30 of the respective fiscal years by the entity to which the
2 appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
3 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the
4 appropriated money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
5 September 18, 2026, and September 17, 2027, respectively, by either the entity
6 to which the money was appropriated or the entity to which the money was
7 subsequently granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State
8 General Fund on or before September 18, 2026, and September 17, 2027,
9 respectively.
10 Sec. 9. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and approval.
11 2. Section 8 of this act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
12 3. Sections 1 to 7, inclusive, of this act become effective on October 1,
13 2025.
