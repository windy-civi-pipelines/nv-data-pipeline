Section 1:
S.B. 366
ELLISON, PAZINA AND SCHEIBLE
MARCH 17, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to Medicaid.
(BDR S-1040)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; requiring the Department of Health
and Human Services to seek an increase to certain
reimbursement rates under Medicaid; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services to
2 administer the Medicaid program. (NRS 422.270) This bill requires the Department
3 to submit to the United States Secretary of Health and Human Service a request to
4 amend Medicaid to increase the rate of reimbursement for each service provided by
5 certain providers of mental health care, physical therapists, occupational therapists,
6 speech-language pathologists and practitioners of respiratory care. This bill requires
7 the Department to request to increase the rates of reimbursement under Medicaid
8 by an additional amount for services provided: (1) in a community setting; (2) in
9 person in a county whose population is less than 100,000 (currently all counties
10 other than Clark and Washoe Counties); and (3) in person in an area with a shortage
11 of the type of provider of health care who provides that service.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. On or before October 1, 2025, the Department
2 of Health and Human Services shall determine the areas in this State
3 that have a shortage of type 34 providers.
4 2. On or before October 1, 2025, the Department of Health and
5 Human Services shall submit to the United States Secretary of
-
*SB366*
– 2 –
1 Health and Human Services a request to amend the Medicaid
2 program to increase the rate of reimbursement under Medicaid for:
3 (a) Each service provided by a type 14 provider or a type 34
4 provider by at least 3 percent of the rate of reimbursement for that
5 service on the effective date of this act;
6 (b) Each service described in paragraph (a) that is provided in a
7 community setting by at least an additional 3 percent of the rate of
8 reimbursement for that service on the effective date of this act;
9 (c) Each service described in paragraph (a) that is provided in
10 person in a county whose population is less than 100,000 by at least
11 an additional 3 percent of the rate of reimbursement for that service
12 on the effective date of this act;
13 (d) Each service that is provided by a type 14 provider in person
14 in a health professional shortage area for mental health by at least an
15 additional 3 percent of the rate of reimbursement for that service
16 provided on the effective date of this act; and
17 (e) Each service that is provided by a type 34 provider in person
18 in an area determined pursuant to subsection 1 to have a shortage of
19 type 34 providers by at least an additional 3 percent of the rate of
20 reimbursement for that service on the effective date of this act.
21 3. The request submitted pursuant to subsection 2 must be
22 supported using methods for determining reimbursement rates
23 accepted by the Secretary.
24 4. As used in this section:
25 (a) “Community setting” includes a home, school, preschool,
26 daycare center for children or community center.
27 (b) “Health professional shortage area for mental health” means
28 an area that has been designated by the United States Secretary of
29 Health and Human Services as a health professional shortage area
30 for mental health pursuant to 42 U.S.C. § 254e.
31 (c) “Provider of health care” has the meaning ascribed to it in
32 NRS 629.031.
33 (d) “Type 14 provider” means a provider of health care who is
34 designated as provider type 14 in the “Provider Enrollment
35 Information Booklet” for Nevada Medicaid and Nevada Check Up
36 published by the Division of Health Care Financing and Policy of
37 the Department of Health and Human Services.
38 (e) “Type 34 provider” means a provider of health care who is
39 designated as provider type 34 in the “Provider Enrollment
40 Information Booklet” for Nevada Medicaid and Nevada Check Up
41 published by the Division of Health Care Financing and Policy of
42 the Department of Health and Human Services.
43 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
