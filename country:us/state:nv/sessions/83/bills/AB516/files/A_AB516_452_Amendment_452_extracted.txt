ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 516–COMMITTEE ON

(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING COMMITTEE

MARCH 24, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to Medicaid coverage for certain health
services provided to children at schools. (BDR 38-348)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included in
Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; requiring the Director of the Department of Health
and Human Services to take certain measures to facilitate the provision
of health services to certain children who are recipients of Medicaid;
establishing the School Health Access Resource Center in the Division
of Health Care Financing and Policy of the Department for the purpose
of providing certain assistance relating to participation in Medicaid as
a provider; providing for the increase of certain rates of reimbursement
under Medicaid; requiring the Director to apply for any federal
authority necessary to simplify and streamline the methodology for
reimbursing under Medicaid certain services provided by a school-
based health center; making appropriations; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services, through the
2 Division of Health Care Financing and Policy of the Department, to administer Medicaid.
3 (NRS 422.2357, 422.270) Section 2 of this bill requires the Director of the Department to: (1)
4 take any action necessary to ensure that local [and state] educational agencies and the
5 Department of Education are able to receive reimbursement for health services covered by
6 Medicaid when provided on the premises of a school to certain children who are recipients of
7 Medicaid; and (2) establish incentives for certain providers to enter into an agreement with a
8 [school district, a charter school] local educational agency or the Department of Education to
9 provide health services to such children who are enrolled in a public school. Section 3 of this
10 bill establishes the School Health Access Resource Center within the Division for the purpose
11 of assisting providers of services under Medicaid and persons and entities who wish to
12 provide services to recipients of Medicaid on the premises of a school to evaluate and utilize
13 different methods of participating in and billing Medicaid. Section 4 of this bill makes a
14 conforming change to require the Director to administer the provisions of sections 2 and 3 in
Assembly Amendment No. 452 to Assembly Bill No. 516 Page 4
15 the same manner as the provisions of existing law governing Medicaid. Section 5 of this bill
16 makes appropriations to the Division to cover certain costs of operating the Center.
17 Section 6 of this bill requires the Director to apply for any federal authority that is
18 necessary to increase by at least 5 percent the rates of reimbursement for services covered by
19 Medicaid when provided on the premises of a school by an employee or independent
20 contractor of: (1) a [school district or charter school;] local educational agency; or (2) the
21 Department of Education. Section 6 also requires the Director to apply for any federal
22 authority that is necessary to: (1) simplify and streamline the methodology for reimbursing
23 primary care and preventative services covered by Medicaid when such services are provided
24 by a school-based health center; and (2) increase by at least 10 percent the rates of
25 reimbursement under Medicaid for such services provided by a school-based health center.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 422 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. 1. The Director shall:
4 (a) Take any action necessary to ensure that [school districts, charter
5 schools] local educational agencies and the Department of Education are able to
6 receive reimbursement for health services covered by Medicaid when such
7 services are provided on the premises of a school to children who are recipients of
8 Medicaid.
9 (b) Establish a program of incentive payments that increase the effective
10 rates of reimbursement provided under Medicaid to a certified community
11 behavioral health clinic or federally-qualified health center that enters into an
12 agreement with a [school district, a charter school] local educational agency or
13 the Department of Education to provide services covered by Medicaid to children
14 who are:
15 (1) Enrolled in a public school; and
16 (2) Recipients of Medicaid.
17 2. The incentives provided pursuant to paragraph (b) of subsection 1 may
18 be provided through increased rates of reimbursement or value-based payment
19 models.
20 3. The Department shall apply to the Secretary of Health and Human
21 Services for any necessary waiver, amendment of the State Plan for Medicaid or
22 other federal authority that authorizes the Department to receive federal funding
23 to carry out the provisions of this section. The Department shall fully cooperate
24 in good faith with the Federal Government during the application process to
25 satisfy the requirements of the Federal Government for obtaining a waiver,
26 amendment or other federal authority pursuant to this section.
27 4. As used in this section:
28 (a) “Certified community behavioral health clinic” means a community
29 behavioral health clinic certified in accordance with section 223 of the Protecting
30 Access to Medicare Act of 2014, Public Law No. 113-93.
31 (b) “Federally-qualified health center” has the meaning ascribed to it in 42
32 U.S.C. § 1396d(l)(2)(B).
33 (c) “Local educational agency” has the meaning ascribed to it in 20 U.S.C. §
34 7801(30)(A).
35 (d) “Value-based payment model” means a method of payment for health
36 care services that provides incentives based on the quality of care.
Assembly Amendment No. 452 to Assembly Bill No. 516 Page 5
1 Sec. 3. 1. The School Health Access Resource Center is hereby
2 established within the Division.
3 2. The School Health Access Resource Center shall assist providers of
4 services under Medicaid and persons and entities that wish to provide services to
5 recipients of Medicaid on the premises of a school to:
6 (a) Evaluate the benefits and drawbacks of various methods of participating
7 in and billing Medicaid that are available to the provider, person or entity,
8 including, without limitation, different provider types under which the provider,
9 person or entity may be authorized to participate in and bill Medicaid; and
10 (b) Meet the requirements and carry out the procedures necessary to
11 participate in and bill Medicaid using the methods chosen by the provider, person
12 or entity, as applicable.
13 3. The Division shall provide the necessary facilities, equipment, staff,
14 supplies and other usual operating expenses necessary to enable the School
15 Health Access Resource Center to carry out its functions.
16 Sec. 4. NRS 232.320 is hereby amended to read as follows:
17 232.320 1. The Director:
18 (a) Shall appoint, with the consent of the Governor, administrators of the
19 divisions of the Department, who are respectively designated as follows:
20 (1) The Administrator of the Aging and Disability Services Division;

22 (3) The Administrator of the Division of Child and Family Services;
23 (4) The Administrator of the Division of Health Care Financing and
24 Policy; and
25 (5) The Administrator of the Division of Public and Behavioral Health.

31 445A.055, inclusive, and all other provisions of law relating to the functions of the
32 divisions of the Department, but is not responsible for the clinical activities of the
33 Division of Public and Behavioral Health or the professional line activities of the
34 other divisions.
35 (c) Shall administer any state program for persons with developmental
36 disabilities established pursuant to the Developmental Disabilities Assistance and
37 Bill of Rights Act of 2000, 42 U.S.C. §§ 15001 et seq.
38 (d) Shall, after considering advice from agencies of local governments and
39 nonprofit organizations which provide social services, adopt a master plan for the
40 provision of human services in this State. The Director shall revise the plan
41 biennially and deliver a copy of the plan to the Governor and the Legislature at the
42 beginning of each regular session. The plan must:
43 (1) Identify and assess the plans and programs of the Department for the
44 provision of human services, and any duplication of those services by federal, state
45 and local agencies;
46 (2) Set forth priorities for the provision of those services;
47 (3) Provide for communication and the coordination of those services
48 among nonprofit organizations, agencies of local government, the State and the
49 Federal Government;
50 (4) Identify the sources of funding for services provided by the Department
51 and the allocation of that funding;

1 (5) Set forth sufficient information to assist the Department in providing
2 those services and in the planning and budgeting for the future provision of those
3 services; and
4 (6) Contain any other information necessary for the Department to
5 communicate effectively with the Federal Government concerning demographic
6 trends, formulas for the distribution of federal money and any need for the
7 modification of programs administered by the Department.
8 (e) May, by regulation, require nonprofit organizations and state and local
9 governmental agencies to provide information regarding the programs of those
10 organizations and agencies, excluding detailed information relating to their budgets

15 designee, is responsible for appointing and removing subordinate officers and
16 employees of the Department.
17 Sec. 5. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to the
18 Division of Health Care Financing and Policy of the Department of Health and
19 Human Services:
20 (a) The sum of $600,000 to enter into contracts with such persons or entities as
21 are necessary to conceptualize and establish the School Health Access Resource
22 Center pursuant to section 3 of this act.
23 (b) The sum of $224,000 for the purposes of hiring staff for and covering the
24 costs of operating the School Health Access Resource Center.
25 (c) The sum of $5,000 to acquire such equipment as is necessary to operate the
26 School Health Access Resource Center.
27 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriations made by subsection 1 must
28 not be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to which the
29 appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
30 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
31 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after September 17, 2027, by
32 either the entity to which the money was appropriated or the entity to which the
33 money was subsequently granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State
34 General Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
35 Sec. 6. 1. On or before October 1, 2025, the Director of the Department of
36 Health and Human Services shall submit to the United States Secretary of Health
37 and Human Services a request to amend the State Plan for Medicaid or for any
38 other necessary federal authority to:
39 (a) Increase by at least 5 percent the rates of reimbursement for services
40 covered by Medicaid when such services are provided:
41 (1) On the premises of a school; and
42 (2) By an employee or independent contractor of a [school district, a
43 charter school] local educational agency, as defined in 20 U.S.C. § 7801(30)(A),
44 or the Department of Education;
45 (b) Simplify and streamline the methodology for reimbursing primary care and
46 preventative services covered by Medicaid when such care and services are
47 provided by a school-based health center; and
48 (c) Increase by at least 10 percent the rates of reimbursement under Medicaid
49 for services described in paragraph (b) when such care and services are provided by
50 a school-based health center.
51 2. As used in this section, “school-based health center” means a health center
52 located on or in school grounds, property, buildings or any other school district
53 facilities for the purpose of rendering care or services to any person.
