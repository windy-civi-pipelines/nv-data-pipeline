EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to juvenile justice; expanding the scope of a program established
by the juvenile court for the treatment of children diagnosed with or
suspected to have autism spectrum disorders to include children
diagnosed with or suspected to have certain other neurobehavioral
disorders; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes the juvenile court to establish an appropriate program for the
2 treatment of children diagnosed with or suspected to have autism spectrum disorders and
3 prescribes the criteria for eligibility to participate in such a program. (NRS 62B.700) This bill:
4 (1) expands the scope of the program to include children diagnosed with or suspected to have
5 neurobehavioral disorders associated with prenatal exposure to alcohol or a controlled
6 substance; and (2) authorizes the juvenile court to assign a child who is diagnosed with or
7 suspected to have any such neurobehavioral disorder to the program under certain
8 circumstances.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 62B.700 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 62B.700 1. The juvenile court may establish an appropriate program for the
3 treatment of children diagnosed with or suspected to have autism spectrum
4 disorders or neurobehavioral disorders associated with prenatal exposure to
5 alcohol or a controlled substance to which it may assign a child who is alleged or
6 adjudicated to have committed a delinquent act if the child:
7 (a) Is diagnosed with, including, without limitation, through the use of a
8 standardized assessment, or suspected to have an autism spectrum disorder [;] or
Assembly Amendment No. 660 to Senate Bill No. 140 Page 4
1 neurobehavioral disorder associated with prenatal exposure to alcohol or a
2 controlled substance;
3 (b) Would benefit from assignment to the program; and
4 (c) Is not ineligible for assignment to the program pursuant to any other
5 provision of law.
6 2. The assignment of a child who is alleged or adjudicated to have committed
7 a delinquent act to a program pursuant to this section must:
8 (a) Include:
9 (1) The terms and conditions for successful completion of the program;
10 and
11 (2) The terms and conditions of the informal supervision or probation of
12 the child, if applicable.
13 (b) Provide for progress reports at intervals set by the juvenile court to ensure
14 that the child is making satisfactory progress towards completion of the program.
15 3. As used in this section [, “autism] :
16 (a) “Autism spectrum disorder” means a condition that meets the diagnostic
17 criteria for autism spectrum disorder published in the current edition of the
18 Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders published by the American
19 Psychiatric Association or the edition thereof that was in effect at the time the
20 condition was diagnosed or determined.
21 (b) “Neurobehavioral disorder associated with prenatal exposure to alcohol
22 or a controlled substance” means a condition that meets the diagnostic criteria
23 for a neurobehavioral disorder associated with prenatal exposure to alcohol or
24 controlled substances published in the current edition of the Diagnostic and
25 Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders published by the American Psychiatric
26 Association or the edition thereof that was in effect at the time the condition was
27 diagnosed or determined. The term includes, without limitation, fetal alcohol
28 spectrum disorders and neonatal abstinence syndrome.
