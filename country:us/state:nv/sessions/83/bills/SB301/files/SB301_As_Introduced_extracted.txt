Section 1:
S.B. 301
CRUZ-CRAWFORD, DALY, DOÑATE, FLORES, NGUYEN,
OHRENSCHALL AND PAZINA
MARCH 10, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to public employees.
(BDR 23-1024)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public employees; revising the definition of
“employee,” for purposes of collective bargaining for
state employees, to include category I, II and III peace
officers in the unclassified service of the State; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes certain groups of employees in the classified service of
2 the Executive Department of the State Government to engage in collective
3 bargaining with the Executive Department concerning wages, hours and other terms
4 and conditions of employment for such employees. (NRS 288.400-288.630) This
5 bill authorizes an additional group of employees, category I, II or III peace officers
6 in the unclassified service of the State, to engage in collective bargaining with the
7 Executive Department.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 288.425 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 288.425 1. “Employee” means a person who:
3 (a) Is employed in the classified service of the State pursuant to
4 chapter 284 of NRS; [or]
5 (b) Is employed as a category I, II or III peace officer, as
6 defined in NRS 289.460, 289.470 and 289.480, respectively, in the
-

1 unclassified service of the State pursuant to chapter 284 of NRS;
2 or
3 (c) Is employed by the Nevada System of Higher Education in
4 the classified service of the State or is required to be paid in
5 accordance with the pay plan for the classified service of the State.
6 2. The term does not include:
7 (a) A managerial employee whose primary function, as
8 determined by the Board, is to administer and control the business
9 of any agency, board, bureau, commission, department, division,
10 elected officer or any other unit of the Executive Department and
11 who is vested with discretion and independent judgment with regard
12 to the general conduct and control of that agency, board, bureau,
13 commission, department, division, elected officer or unit;
14 (b) An elected official or any person appointed to fill a vacancy
15 in an elected office;
16 (c) A confidential employee;
17 (d) A temporary employee who is employed for a fixed period
18 of 4 months or less;
19 (e) A commissioned officer or an enlisted member of the
20 Nevada National Guard;
21 (f) Any person employed by the Nevada System of Higher
22 Education who is not in the classified service of the State or
23 required to be paid in accordance with the pay plan of the classified
24 service of the State; or
25 (g) Any person employed by the Public Employees’ Retirement
26 System who is required to be paid in accordance with the pay plan
27 of the classified service of the State.
