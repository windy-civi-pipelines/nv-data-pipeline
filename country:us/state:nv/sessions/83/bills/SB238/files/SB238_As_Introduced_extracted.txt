Section 1:
S.B. 238
FEBRUARY 26, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the crime of hazing.
(BDR 15-1043)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to crimes; increasing the penalty for engaging in
hazing on the property of a college or university; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prohibits engaging in hazing at all high schools, colleges and
2 universities in this State. Existing law sets forth that a person who engages in
3 hazing is guilty of: (1) a misdemeanor if there is no substantial bodily harm
4 resulting from the hazing; and (2) a gross misdemeanor if there is substantial bodily
5 harm resulting from the hazing. (NRS 200.605) This bill makes it a category E
6 felony to engage in the act of hazing on the property of a college or university in
7 this State.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 200.605 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 200.605 1. [A] Unless a greater penalty is provided
3 pursuant to subsection 2, a person who engages in hazing is guilty
4 of:
5 (a) A misdemeanor, if no substantial bodily harm results.
6 (b) A gross misdemeanor, if substantial bodily harm results.
7 2. A person who engages in hazing on the property of a
8 college or university in this State is guilty of a category E felony
9 and shall be punished as provided in NRS 193.130.
10 3. Consent of a victim of hazing is not a valid defense to a
11 prosecution conducted pursuant to this section.
-
*SB238*
– 2 –
1 [3.] 4. For the purposes of this section, an activity shall be
2 deemed to be “forced” if initiation into or affiliation with a student
3 organization, academic association or athletic team is directly or
4 indirectly conditioned upon participation in the activity.
5 [4.] 5. As used in this section, “hazing” means an activity in
6 which a person intentionally or recklessly endangers the physical
7 health of another person for the purpose of initiation into or
8 affiliation with a student organization, academic association or
9 athletic team at a high school, college or university in this State. The
10 term:
11 (a) Includes, without limitation, any physical brutality or brutal
12 treatment, including, without limitation, whipping, beating,
13 branding, forced calisthenics, exposure to the elements or forced
14 consumption of food, liquor, drugs or other substances.
15 (b) Does not include any athletic, curricular, extracurricular or
16 quasi-military practice, conditioning or competition that is
17 sponsored or approved by the high school, college or university.
