ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 326–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS
KOENIG; COLE AND TORRES-FOSSETT
MARCH 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Provides for a study on the treatment of trauma.
(BDR S-153)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; requiring the Department of Health
and Human Services to conduct a study on the treatment
of trauma in this State; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires that before operating a center for the treatment of trauma,
2 a hospital must obtain the approval of the Administrator of the Division of Public
3 and Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and Human Services and, if the
4 hospital is located in a county whose population is 700,000 or more (currently only
5 Clark County), the district board of health. (NRS 450B.236, 450B.237) Existing
6 law also requires the Administrator to verify that a hospital meets certain standards
7 and complete a comprehensive assessment of needs before approving a proposal to
8 designate a hospital as a center for the treatment of trauma. (NRS 450B.237)
9 Existing regulations authorize the Administrator to designate a center for the
10 treatment of trauma as level I, II or III. (NAC 450B.780-450B.875) Section 3.5 of
11 this bill requires: (1) the Department of Health and Human Services to study during
12 the 2025-2026 interim the current system for the treatment of trauma in this State
13 and the future needs of this State relating to the treatment of trauma; (2) the study
14 to include the solicitation of public comment on the subject matter of the study; and
15 (3) the Department to publish a report of the study on the Internet and submit the
16 report to the Governor and the Legislature.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)

– 2 –
1 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 3.5. 1. During the 2025-2026 interim, the Department
3 of Health and Human Services shall study the current system for the
4 treatment of trauma in the State and the future needs of the State
5 relating to the treatment of trauma. The study must include, without
6 limitation:
7 (a) Consideration of the need to develop new hospitals to be
8 designated as centers for the treatment of trauma or newly designate
9 existing hospitals as centers for the treatment of trauma;
10 (b) Consideration of any plans to develop new hospitals to be
11 designated as centers for the treatment of trauma or newly designate
12 existing hospitals as centers for the treatment of trauma; and
13 (c) An evaluation of the potential benefits of designating
14 hospitals as level IV centers for the treatment of trauma.
15 2. The study must:
16 (a) Consider the demographics of each county in this State;
17 (b) Consider the manner in which this State and each county in
18 this State may most effectively ensure the availability of treatment
19 of trauma for the residents of this State or county, as applicable; and
20 (c) Include an opportunity for the public to comment on the
21 subject matter of the study, including, without limitation:
22 (1) A publicized time period for the public to submit written
23 comments to the Department; and
24 (2) A public meeting held in compliance with chapter 241 of
25 the NRS to allow members of the public to provide in-person
26 comments.
27 3. The Department may apply for and receive gifts, grants,
28 donations and other money from any source for the purpose of
29 carrying out the provisions of this section and to defray costs
30 incurred by the Department for carrying out the provisions of this
31 section.
32 4. On or before January 1, 2027, the Department shall:
33 (a) Compile a written report of the study conducted pursuant to
34 this section which must include, without limitation:
35 (1) A conspicuous section disclosing the name of any person
36 who provided any gift, grant, donation or other money for the
37 purpose of carrying out the provisions of this section and the amount
38 of money provided by the person;
39 (2) All written comments received pursuant to subparagraph
40 (1) of paragraph (c) of subsection 2;
41 (3) The minutes of the public meeting held pursuant to
42 subparagraph (2) of paragraph (c) of subsection 2; and
43 (4) A list of recommendations resulting from the study;
44 (b) Publish the report on an Internet website maintained by the
45 Department; and
-

1 (c) Submit the report to the Governor and the Director of the
2 Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the 84th Session of the
3 Legislature.
4 5. As used in this section, “Department” means the Department
5 of Health and Human Services.
6 Sec. 4. 1. This section and sections 3 and 3.5 of this act
7 become effective upon passage and approval.
8 2. Sections 1 and 2 of this act become effective:
9 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
10 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
11 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
12 (b) On January 1, 2026, for all other purposes.
