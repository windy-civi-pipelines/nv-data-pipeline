ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 439–ASSEMBLYMEMBER COLE

Referred to Committee on Growth and Infrastructure
SUMMARY—Revises provisions concerning the purchase or acquisition of certain
unmanned aerial vehicles or other related equipment and
services under certain circumstances. (BDR 44-896)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to unmanned aerial vehicles; revising provisions concerning the
purchase or acquisition of certain unmanned aerial vehicles or other
related equipment or services under certain circumstances; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law, in relevant part, requires the Department of Public Safety to adopt
2 regulations to establish a list of countries, businesses and entities from which a public agency
3 or law enforcement agency is prohibited from purchasing or acquiring any unmanned aerial
4 vehicle or other equipment or service relating to the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle.
5 (NRS 493.118) This bill provides that a public agency or law enforcement agency may
6 purchase or acquire any unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to the
7 operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle from a business or entity that would otherwise be
8 contained on the list if: (1) any photograph, image, recording or other information collected by
9 the business or entity is stored and maintained exclusively within the United States and is not
10 accessible to [any] certain third [party; and] parties; (2) before the purchase or acquisition,
11 the Department determines that the business or entity complies with the information storage
12 and access requirements [, unless the determination is waived by the Department.] ; and (3)
13 the purchase or acquisition complies with federal law.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 493.118 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 493.118 1. The Department shall, to the extent that money is available for
3 this purpose, establish and maintain a registry of unmanned aerial vehicles that are
4 operated by public agencies in this State. The Department shall include on its
5 Internet website the information that is maintained in the registry.
6 2. A public agency shall, for each unmanned aerial vehicle the public agency
7 intends to operate, submit to the Department, on a form provided by the
8 Department, for inclusion in the registry:
Assembly Amendment No. 411 to Assembly Bill No. 439 Page 4
1 (a) The name of the public agency;
2 (b) The name and contact information of each operator of the unmanned aerial
3 vehicle;
4 (c) Sufficient information to identify the unmanned aerial vehicle; and
5 (d) A statement describing the use of the unmanned aerial vehicle by the public
6 agency.
7 3. The Department shall, on or before February 1 of each year, prepare and
8 submit to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for submission to the
9 Legislature, or to the Legislative Commission when the Legislature is not in regular
10 session, a report outlining the activities of public agencies with respect to the
11 operation of unmanned aerial vehicles in this State.
12 4. The Department shall adopt regulations prescribing the public purposes for
13 which a public agency may operate an unmanned aerial vehicle that is registered
14 with the Department pursuant to this section, including, without limitation:
15 (a) The provision of fire services.
16 (b) The provision of emergency medical services.
17 (c) The protection of a critical facility that is public property.
18 (d) Search and rescue operations conducted for persons and property in
19 distress.
20 5. The regulations adopted by the Department pursuant to subsection 4 must
21 include provisions that:
22 (a) Authorize, as a public purpose, a public agency to operate an unmanned
23 aerial vehicle that is registered with the Department in order to conduct a scheduled
24 inspection to ensure compliance with building or fire codes or laws, ordinances,
25 regulations or rules adopting or establishing building or fire codes that are enforced
26 by the public agency.
27 (b) Prohibit a public agency from collecting any photograph, image or
28 recording through the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle during a scheduled
29 inspection described in paragraph (a). If any photograph, image or recording is
30 collected in violation of such a regulation or if any other information is collected
31 through the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle during such a scheduled
32 inspection, the photograph, image, recording or other information:
33 (1) Is not admissible and must not be disclosed in any judicial,
34 administrative or other adjudicatory proceeding other than a proceeding relating to
35 the purpose of the scheduled inspection; and
36 (2) May not be used to establish reasonable suspicion or probable cause as
37 the basis for the investigation or prosecution of a crime or other offense.
38 6. In addition to the regulations adopted pursuant to subsection 4, the
39 Department shall adopt regulations to establish:
40 (a) A list of countries, businesses and entities from which a public agency or
41 law enforcement agency , except as otherwise provided in subsection 8, shall not
42 purchase or acquire any unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service
43 relating to the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle; and
44 (b) A list of unmanned aerial vehicles and other related equipment or services
45 that a public agency or law enforcement agency shall not operate, purchase or
46 acquire.
47 7. The lists established pursuant to subsection 6 must include : [, without
48 limitation:]
49 (a) Any [country,] business or entity identified by the Secretary of Defense of
50 the United States Department of Defense pursuant to Section 1260H of the William
51 M. (Mac) Thornberry National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2021,
52 Public Law 116-283, any amendments thereto or any subsequent federal law
53 establishing such a list;
Assembly Amendment No. 411 to Assembly Bill No. 439 Page 5
1 (b) Any unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to the
2 operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle sold, manufactured or distributed by an
3 entity identified by the Secretary of Defense of the United States Department of
4 Defense pursuant to Section 1260H of the William M. (Mac) Thornberry National
5 Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2021, Public Law 116-283, any
6 amendments thereto or any subsequent federal law establishing such a list;
7 (c) Any hardware, software, vendor or service prohibited from being used by a
8 state agency in Nevada by a regulation, guideline or policy adopted by the Office of
9 the Chief Information Officer within the Office of the Governor pursuant to NRS
10 242.111 and 242.115; and
11 (d) Any other unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to
12 the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle, as determined by the Department.
13 8. A public agency or law enforcement agency may purchase or acquire any
14 unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to the operation
15 of an unmanned aerial vehicle from a business or entity that is identified on the
16 list established pursuant to paragraph (a) of subsection 6 if:
17 (a) Any photograph, image, recording or other information collected by the
18 business or entity from the purchase, acquisition or operation of the unmanned
19 aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to the operation of the
20 unmanned aerial vehicle:
21 (1) Is stored and maintained exclusively within the United States; and
22 (2) Is not accessible to any third party; [and]
23 (b) Before the purchase or acquisition of the unmanned aerial vehicle or
24 other equipment or service relating to the operation of the unmanned aerial
25 vehicle, the Department has determined that the policies of the business or entity
26 comply with paragraph (a) [, unless the determination is waived by the
27 Department.] ; and
28 (c) The purchase or acquisition complies with all requirements of federal
29 law, including, without limitation, any restrictions on the use of federal funds.
30 9. The provisions of subsection 8 shall not be construed to discourage the
31 purchase or acquisition of any unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or
32 service relating to the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle that is
33 manufactured in the United States. To the extent authorized by chapter 332 or
34 333 of NRS, a public agency or law enforcement agency is encouraged to
35 prioritize the purchase or acquisition of any unmanned aerial vehicle or other
36 equipment or service related to the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle that
37 is manufactured in the United States.
38 10. As used in this section, “third party” includes, without limitation, a
39 person that is domiciled in a foreign country or has its principal place of business
40 in, or is organized under the laws of, a foreign country. The term does not
41 include a governmental entity of the United States.
