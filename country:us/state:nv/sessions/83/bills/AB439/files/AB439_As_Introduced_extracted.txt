Section 1:
A.B. 439
MARCH 17, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Growth and Infrastructure
SUMMARY—Revises provisions concerning the purchase or
acquisition of certain unmanned aerial vehicles or
other related equipment and services under certain
circumstances. (BDR 44-896)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to unmanned aerial vehicles; revising provisions
concerning the purchase or acquisition of certain
unmanned aerial vehicles or other related equipment or
services under certain circumstances; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law, in relevant part, requires the Department of Public Safety to adopt
2 regulations to establish a list of countries, businesses and entities from which a
3 public agency or law enforcement agency is prohibited from purchasing or
4 acquiring any unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to the
5 operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle. (NRS 493.118) This bill provides that a
6 public agency or law enforcement agency may purchase or acquire any unmanned
7 aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to the operation of an
8 unmanned aerial vehicle from a business or entity that would otherwise be
9 contained on the list if: (1) any photograph, image, recording or other information
10 collected by the business or entity is stored and maintained exclusively within the
11 United States and is not accessible to any third party; and (2) before the purchase or
12 acquisition, the Department determines that the business or entity complies with the
13 information storage and access requirements, unless the determination is waived by
14 the Department.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 493.118 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 493.118 1. The Department shall, to the extent that money is
3 available for this purpose, establish and maintain a registry of
4 unmanned aerial vehicles that are operated by public agencies in this
5 State. The Department shall include on its Internet website the
6 information that is maintained in the registry.
7 2. A public agency shall, for each unmanned aerial vehicle the
8 public agency intends to operate, submit to the Department, on a
9 form provided by the Department, for inclusion in the registry:
10 (a) The name of the public agency;
11 (b) The name and contact information of each operator of the
12 unmanned aerial vehicle;
13 (c) Sufficient information to identify the unmanned aerial
14 vehicle; and
15 (d) A statement describing the use of the unmanned aerial
16 vehicle by the public agency.
17 3. The Department shall, on or before February 1 of each year,
18 prepare and submit to the Director of the Legislative Counsel
19 Bureau for submission to the Legislature, or to the Legislative
20 Commission when the Legislature is not in regular session, a report
21 outlining the activities of public agencies with respect to the
22 operation of unmanned aerial vehicles in this State.
23 4. The Department shall adopt regulations prescribing the
24 public purposes for which a public agency may operate an
25 unmanned aerial vehicle that is registered with the Department
26 pursuant to this section, including, without limitation:
27 (a) The provision of fire services.
28 (b) The provision of emergency medical services.
29 (c) The protection of a critical facility that is public property.
30 (d) Search and rescue operations conducted for persons and
31 property in distress.
32 5. The regulations adopted by the Department pursuant to
33 subsection 4 must include provisions that:
34 (a) Authorize, as a public purpose, a public agency to operate an
35 unmanned aerial vehicle that is registered with the Department in
36 order to conduct a scheduled inspection to ensure compliance with
37 building or fire codes or laws, ordinances, regulations or rules
38 adopting or establishing building or fire codes that are enforced by
39 the public agency.
40 (b) Prohibit a public agency from collecting any photograph,
41 image or recording through the operation of an unmanned aerial
42 vehicle during a scheduled inspection described in paragraph (a). If
-
*AB439*
– 3 –
1 any photograph, image or recording is collected in violation of such
2 a regulation or if any other information is collected through the
3 operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle during such a scheduled
4 inspection, the photograph, image, recording or other information:
5 (1) Is not admissible and must not be disclosed in any
6 judicial, administrative or other adjudicatory proceeding other than a
7 proceeding relating to the purpose of the scheduled inspection; and
8 (2) May not be used to establish reasonable suspicion or
9 probable cause as the basis for the investigation or prosecution of a
10 crime or other offense.
11 6. In addition to the regulations adopted pursuant to subsection
12 4, the Department shall adopt regulations to establish:
13 (a) A list of countries, businesses and entities from which a
14 public agency or law enforcement agency , except as otherwise
15 provided in subsection 8, shall not purchase or acquire any
16 unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service relating to
17 the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle; and
18 (b) A list of unmanned aerial vehicles and other related
19 equipment or services that a public agency or law enforcement
20 agency shall not operate, purchase or acquire.
21 7. The lists established pursuant to subsection 6 must include :
22 [, without limitation:]
23 (a) Any [country,] business or entity identified by the Secretary
24 of Defense of the United States Department of Defense pursuant to
25 Section 1260H of the William M. (Mac) Thornberry National
26 Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2021, Public Law 116-
27 283, any amendments thereto or any subsequent federal law
28 establishing such a list;
29 (b) Any unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or service
30 relating to the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle sold,
31 manufactured or distributed by an entity identified by the Secretary
32 of Defense of the United States Department of Defense pursuant to
33 Section 1260H of the William M. (Mac) Thornberry National
34 Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2021, Public Law 116-
35 283, any amendments thereto or any subsequent federal law
36 establishing such a list;
37 (c) Any hardware, software, vendor or service prohibited from
38 being used by a state agency in Nevada by a regulation, guideline or
39 policy adopted by the Office of the Chief Information Officer within
40 the Office of the Governor pursuant to NRS 242.111 and 242.115;
41 and
42 (d) Any other unmanned aerial vehicle or other equipment or
43 service relating to the operation of an unmanned aerial vehicle, as
44 determined by the Department.
