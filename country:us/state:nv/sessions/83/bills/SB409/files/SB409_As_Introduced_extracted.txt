Section 1:
S.B. 409
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING COMMITTEE
ON HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES)
MARCH 20, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the reporting and
investigation of child abuse or neglect.
(BDR 38-357)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to child welfare; revising the actions relating to a
report, investigation or legal intervention concerning the
abuse or neglect of a child for which a person is granted
immunity from liability; authorizing a court to award
reasonable attorney’s fees and costs to certain persons
who prevail in a civil action for performing such actions;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing federal law establishes a program to award grants to states to support
2 programs to prevent the abuse or neglect of children and to treat children who have
3 been abused or neglected. Existing federal law requires states applying for such a
4 grant to submit a plan that includes assurances that the state has in effect provisions
5 of law granting immunity from civil or criminal liability to a person who: (1) makes
6 a good faith report of suspected or known instances of child abuse or neglect; or (2)
7 otherwise provides information or assistance in connection with a report,
8 investigation or legal intervention pursuant to a report. (42 U.S.C. § 5106a)
9 Existing state law sets forth certain specific actions relating to such a report,
10 investigation or legal intervention for which a person is granted immunity from
11 civil or criminal liability. (NRS 432B.160)
12 This bill adds to the list of actions for which a person is granted immunity
13 reviewing or providing consultation or a medical opinion concerning photographs,
14 x-rays and other medical tests taken by a person investigating a report of abuse or
15 neglect of a child. This bill also authorizes a court to award reasonable attorney’s
16 fees and costs to a person who is required to report suspected or known abuse or
-

18 her for performing such an act.

12 (d) Causes a medical test to be performed pursuant to

15 concerning a medical test performed pursuant to NRS 432B.270;
16 (f) Provides a record, or a copy thereof, of a medical test
17 performed pursuant to NRS 432B.270 to an agency which provides
18 child welfare services to the child, a law enforcement agency that
19 participated in the investigation of the report made pursuant to NRS
20 432B.220 or the prosecuting attorney’s office;
21 [(f)] (g) Holds a child pursuant to NRS 432B.400, takes
22 possession of a child pursuant to NRS 432B.630 or places a child in
23 protective custody pursuant to any provision of this chapter;
24 [(g)] (h) Performs any act pursuant to subsection 2 of

26 [(h)] (i) Refers a case or recommends the filing of a petition
27 pursuant to NRS 432B.380; or
28 [(i)] (j) Participates in a judicial proceeding resulting from a
29 referral or recommendation.
30 2. The provisions of subsection 1 do not confer any immunity
31 from liability for the negligent performance of any act pursuant to
32 paragraph (b) of subsection 2 of NRS 432B.630.
33 3. In any proceeding to impose liability against a person for:
34 (a) Making a report pursuant to NRS 432B.220; or
35 (b) Performing any act set forth in paragraphs (b) to [(i),] (j),
36 inclusive, of subsection 1,

39 of a child pursuant to NRS 432B.220 prevails in a civil action
