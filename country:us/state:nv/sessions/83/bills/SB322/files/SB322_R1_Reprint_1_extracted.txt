Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 322
SENATE BILL NO. 322–SENATOR TAYLOR
MARCH 11, 2025
____________
JOINT SPONSOR: ASSEMBLYMEMBER HANSEN
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the Board of Regents
of the University of Nevada. (BDR 34-764)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to higher education; requiring each member of the
Board of Regents of the University of Nevada to complete
certain training for professional development; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 The Nevada Constitution requires the Legislature to provide for: (1) the
2 establishment of a State University controlled by a Board of Regents, whose duties
3 are to be prescribed by law; and (2) the election of the members of the Board of
4 Regents of Nevada. (Nev. Const. Art. 11, §§ 4, 7; NRS 396.040) Existing law
5 establishes the Board of Regents of the University of Nevada and imposes upon the
6 Board, and the members of the Board, certain duties. (Chapter 396 of NRS)
7 In addition, existing law requires each member elected or appointed to serve on
8 the board of trustees of a school district to complete a certain amount of training for
9 professional development during their term, which must contain instruction on
10 certain topics relating to the administration of public schools. (NRS 386.327)
11 Section 1 of this bill requires each member of the Board of Regents to
12 complete 6 hours of training for professional development during the first and third
13 year of each term that the member serves on the Board of Regents. Section 1
14 provides that the training may include instruction covering various matters relating
15 to the administration of a public system for higher education, including instruction
16 concerning the duties of the Board of Regents and other persons involved in higher
17 education, laws on ethics, laws on public records and meetings, laws governing
18 employment, financial matters and the fiduciary duties of the members of the
19 Board. Section 1 requires each member of the Board to complete each year that the
20 member is required to complete training, as part of the training requirement,
21 training relating to: (1) the Open Meeting Law (Chapter 241 of NRS); and (2) the
22 elimination of bias and discrimination in higher education.
-
*SB322_R1*
– 2 –
23 Existing law authorizes the Board of Regents to employ a Secretary of the
24 Board and requires the Secretary to perform certain duties. (NRS 396.090, 396.120,
25 396.853) Section 1 requires the Secretary to: (1) assist the members of the Board
26 with completing the training for professional development; (2) post notice of any
27 failure of a member of the Board to complete this training on an Internet website
28 maintained by the Board; and (3) provide written notice of such failure of a
29 member to the other members of the Board.
30 Existing law entitles each member of the Board of Regents to receive a certain
31 salary for each meeting of the Board that he or she attends. (NRS 396.070) Section
32 1 provides that each member of the Board is entitled to receive compensation for
33 each 3 hours of training for professional development that the member completes
34 outside of a meeting of the Board, subject to a maximum limit of 18 hours for each
35 calendar year, in an amount that is equal to the salary that the member is entitled to
36 receive for each meeting of the Board that the member attends.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 396 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. Each person who is elected or appointed to serve as a
4 member of the Board of Regents shall complete training for
5 professional development during the first and third year of each
6 term of the member, which must consist of not less than 6 hours of
7 instruction during each year that such training is required. Except
8 as otherwise provided in subsection 2, the instruction required
9 pursuant to this subsection may cover any of the following topics:
10 (a) The Open Meeting Law, including, without limitation, the
11 provisions of chapter 241 of NRS;
12 (b) The elimination of bias and discrimination in higher
13 education;
14 (c) Laws relating to public records, including, without
15 limitation, the provisions of chapter 239 of NRS;
16 (d) Strategies for the improvement of campus safety;
17 (e) Laws and standards relating to the ethical obligations of
18 public officers and employees, including, without limitation, the
19 provisions of chapter 281A of NRS;
20 (f) The system of public higher education in this State,
21 including, without limitation, the provisions of this chapter and
22 any regulations adopted pursuant thereto;
23 (g) The duties and responsibilities of the members of the Board
24 of Regents, the officers and staff of the Board of Regents, the
25 Chancellor, the staff of the Chancellor and the presidents of the
26 institutions within the System, including, without limitation,
27 circumstances under which the Board of Regents is required to
28 consult with such persons;
-
*SB322_R1*
– 3 –
1 (h) The existing relationship and interaction between the
2 Board of Regents and the System with the Legislative, Executive
3 and Judicial branches of the government of this State;
4 (i) Financial management, including, without limitation,
5 information concerning oversight, accountability and audits;
6 (j) Information relating to institutional mission differentiation
7 and accreditation standards in higher education;
8 (k) The fiduciary duties of the members of the Board of
9 Regents, including, without limitation, the provisions of NRS
10 396.122 and other applicable provisions of law;
11 (l) Laws and regulations relating to employment and
12 contracts;
13 (m) Parliamentary procedures applicable to meetings of the
14 Board of Regents;
15 (n) The promotion of academic freedom and shared
16 governance in institutions of higher education;
17 (o) Best practices relating to searches for executive officers in
18 higher education; or
19 (p) Other topics of instruction not described in paragraphs (a)
20 to (o), inclusive, that relate to the duties of the Board of Regents.
21 2. Each member of the Board of Regents is required to
22 complete, as part of the training for professional development
23 required pursuant to subsection 1, instruction covering the topics
24 described in paragraphs (a) and (b) of subsection 1 each year the
25 member is required to complete such training pursuant to
26 subsection 1.
27 3. The Secretary of the Board shall assist each member of the
28 Board of Regents as necessary to complete the training for
29 professional development required pursuant to subsection 1,
30 including, without limitation, arranging for experts on the topics
31 described in paragraphs (a) to (p), inclusive, of subsection 1 to
32 facilitate or provide the required instruction. Such an expert may
33 be a person who is employed by the System, or any state or local
34 agency, and whose duties relate to the training that the person has
35 agreed to provide.
36 4. Upon completing the training for professional development
37 required pursuant to subsection 1, a member of the Board of
38 Regents must provide evidence of the completion of the training to
39 the Secretary of the Board. If a member fails to complete the
40 training required pursuant to subsection 1, or fails to provide
41 evidence of the completion of the training to the Secretary in
42 accordance with this subsection, the Secretary shall:
43 (a) Publish a notice of such noncompliance in a conspicuous
44 manner on an Internet website maintained by the Board of
45 Regents; and
-
*SB322_R1*
– 4 –
1 (b) Provide written notice of the noncompliance to the other
2 members of the Board of Regents.
3 5. Each member of the Board of Regents is entitled to receive
4 compensation for each 3 hours of training for professional
5 development that the member completes pursuant to subsection 1
6 outside of a meeting of the Board. The amount of compensation
7 for each 3 hours of training must be equal to the salary that each
8 member of the Board of Regents is entitled to receive pursuant to
9 subsection 1 of NRS 396.070 for each meeting of the Board that
10 the member attends. A member may not receive compensation
11 pursuant to this subsection for more than 18 hours of training in a
12 calendar year.
13 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
14 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on January 1, 2026.
