EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to cannabis; requiring the Cannabis Compliance Board to adopt
regulations governing certain agreements to share information and data
maintained by the Board; making appropriations; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law deems certain information and data maintained by the Cannabis Compliance
2 Board to be confidential and sets forth the persons to whom and the circumstances under
3 which the Board may disclose such information and data. (NRS 678A.470) Section 1 of this
4 bill requires the Board to adopt regulations establishing procedures pursuant to which the
5 Board may enter into an agreement with a research institution to share information and data
6 maintained by the Board for the purposes of research relating to cannabis and the cannabis
7 industry. Section 1 defines the term “research institution” to mean a university, college or
8 other organization which has as one of its principal purposes the conducting of medical,
9 scientific or academic research. Section 2 of this bill authorizes the Board to disclose
10 confidential information and data maintained by the Board to a research institution in
11 accordance with the regulations adopted by the Board pursuant to section 1.
12 Sections 3 and 4 of this bill appropriate money to the Cannabis Policy Institute at the
13 University of Nevada, Las Vegas , for personnel, operating and travel expenses and for direct
14 costs relating to research conducted by the Institute.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 678A.450 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 678A.450 1. The Board may adopt regulations necessary or convenient to
3 carry out the provisions of this title. Such regulations may include, without
4 limitation:
5 (a) Financial requirements for licensees.
6 (b) Establishing such education, outreach, investigative and enforcement
7 mechanisms as the Board deems necessary to ensure the compliance of a licensee
Assembly Amendment No. 136 to Assembly Bill No. 149 Page 4
1 or registrant with the provisions of this title. Such mechanisms must include,
2 without limitation:
3 (1) A system to educate, train and certify employees of the Board which:
4 (I) Each employee must complete before he or she may engage in
5 inspections, investigations or audits; and
6 (II) At a minimum, includes training that is the same or substantially
7 similar to any training that is required by the Board by regulation to be completed
8 by a cannabis establishment agent before he or she may be employed by, volunteer
9 at or provide labor to a cannabis establishment;
10 (2) A system to educate and advise licensees and registrants on compliance
11 with the provisions of this title which may serve as an alternative to disciplinary
12 action; and
13 (3) Establishing specific grounds for disciplinary action against a licensee
14 or registrant who knowingly violates the law or engages in grossly negligent,
15 unlawful or criminal conduct or an act or omission that poses an imminent threat to
16 the health or safety of the public.
17 (c) Requirements for licensees or registrants relating to the cultivation,
18 processing, manufacture, transport, distribution, testing, study, advertising and sale
19 of cannabis and cannabis products.
20 (d) Policies and procedures to ensure that the cannabis industry in this State is
21 economically competitive, inclusive of racial minorities, women and persons and
22 communities that have been adversely affected by cannabis prohibition and
23 accessible to persons of low-income seeking to start a business.
24 (e) Policies and procedures relating to the disclosure of the identities of the
25 shareholders and the annual report of a cannabis establishment that is a publicly
26 traded company.
27 (f) Reasonable restrictions on the signage, marketing, display and advertising
28 of cannabis establishments. Such a restriction must not require a cannabis
29 establishment to obtain the approval of the Board before using a logo, sign or
30 advertisement.
31 (g) Provisions governing the sales of products and commodities made from
32 hemp, as defined in NRS 557.160, or containing cannabidiol by cannabis
33 establishments.
34 (h) Requirements relating to the packaging and labeling of cannabis and
35 cannabis products.
36 2. The Board shall adopt regulations providing for the gathering and
37 maintenance of comprehensive demographic information, including, without
38 limitation, information regarding race, ethnicity, age and gender, concerning each:
39 (a) Owner and manager of a cannabis establishment.
40 (b) Holder of a cannabis establishment agent registration card.
41 3. The Board shall adopt regulations providing for the investigation of
42 unlicensed cannabis activities and the imposition of penalties against persons who
43 engage in such activities. Such regulations must, without limitation:
44 (a) Establish penalties to be imposed for unlicensed cannabis activities, which
45 may include, without limitation, the issuance of a cease and desist order or citation,
46 the imposition of an administrative fine or civil penalty and other similar penalties.
47 (b) Set forth the procedures by which the Board may impose a penalty against
48 a person for engaging in unlicensed cannabis activities.
49 (c) Set forth the circumstances under which the Board is required to refer
50 matters concerning unlicensed cannabis activities to an appropriate state or local
51 law enforcement agency.

1 4. The Board shall transmit the information gathered and maintained pursuant
2 to subsection 2 to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmission
3 to the Legislature on or before January 1 of each odd-numbered year.
4 5. The Board shall, by regulation, establish a pilot program for identifying
5 opportunities for an emerging small cannabis business to participate in the cannabis
6 industry. As used in this subsection, “emerging small cannabis business” means a
7 cannabis-related business that:
8 (a) Is in existence, operational and operated for a profit;
9 (b) Maintains its principal place of business in this State; and
10 (c) Satisfies requirements for the number of employees and annual gross
11 revenue established by the Board by regulation.
12 6. The Board shall adopt regulations establishing procedures pursuant to
13 which the Board may enter into an agreement with a research institution to share
14 information and data maintained by the Board, including, without limitation,
15 information and data classified as confidential pursuant to NRS 678A.470, for
16 the purposes of research relating to cannabis and the cannabis industry. As used
17 in this subsection, “research institution” means a university, college or other
18 organization which has as one of its principal purposes the conducting of
19 medical, scientific or academic research.
20 Sec. 2. NRS 678A.470 is hereby amended to read as follows:
21 678A.470 1. The Board shall cause to be made and kept a record of all
22 proceedings at regular and special meetings of the Board. These records are open to
23 public inspection.
24 2. Any and all information and data prepared or obtained by the Board or by
25 an agent or employee of the Board relating to a holder of or an applicant for a
26 medical cannabis establishment license pursuant to NRS 678B.210, other than the
27 name of a licensee and each owner, officer and board member of the licensee and
28 information relating to the scoring and ranking of applications and the imposition of
29 disciplinary action, are confidential and may be revealed in whole or in part only in
30 the course of the necessary administration of this title or upon the lawful order of a
31 court of competent jurisdiction. The Board may reveal such information and data to
32 an authorized agent of any agency of the United States Government, any state , [or]
33 any political subdivision of a state or the government of any foreign country [.] or a
34 research institution pursuant to an agreement entered into in accordance with
35 the regulations adopted by the Board pursuant to subsection 6 of NRS 678A.450.
36 Notwithstanding any other provision of state law, such information and data may
37 not be otherwise revealed without specific authorization by the Board pursuant to
38 the regulations of the Board.
39 3. Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, any information and data
40 included in an application for an adult-use cannabis establishment license or a
41 registration card is confidential and may be revealed in whole or in part only in the
42 course of the necessary administration of this title or upon the lawful order of a
43 court of competent jurisdiction. The name of the holder of an adult-use cannabis
44 establishment license and each owner, officer and board member of the licensee
45 and information relating to the scoring and ranking of applications and the
46 imposition of disciplinary action are not confidential. The Board may reveal such
47 information and data to an authorized agent of any agency of the United States
48 Government, any state or any political subdivision of a state , [or] the government
49 of any foreign country [.] or a research institution pursuant to an agreement
50 entered into in accordance with the regulations adopted by the Board pursuant to
51 subsection 6 of NRS 678A.450. Notwithstanding any other provision of state law,
52 such information and data may not be otherwise revealed without specific
53 authorization by the Board pursuant to the regulations of the Board.

4 administration of this title.
5 5. As used in this section, “information and data” means all information and

9 document, including, without limitation, any document containing self-evaluative
10 assessments, self-critical analysis or self-appraisals of an applicant’s or licensee’s
11 compliance with statutory or regulatory requirements.
12 Sec. 3. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to the
13 Cannabis Policy Institute at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, for personnel,
14 operating and travel expenses the following sums:
15 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ........................................................ $657,241

31 research, but not including indirect costs or overhead expenses, the following
32 sums:
33 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ..................................................... $1,000,000
34 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ..................................................... $1,000,000

36 Any remaining balance of those sums must not be committed for expenditure after

43 17, 2027.
