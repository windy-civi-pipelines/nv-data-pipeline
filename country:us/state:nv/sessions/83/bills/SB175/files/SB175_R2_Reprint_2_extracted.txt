Section 1:
EXEMPT
SECOND REPRINT S.B. 175
SENATE BILL NO. 175–SENATORS ROGICH; AND CRUZ-CRAWFORD
PREFILED FEBRUARY 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions governing the treatment of
certain military credentials in the licensure and
hiring of educational personnel. (BDR 34-663)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to educational personnel; requiring the
Commission on Professional Standards in Education to
adopt regulations authorizing a member or veteran of the
Armed Forces of the United States who has received
certain types of certification as an instructor in the Junior
Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program to receive an
endorsement to teach pupils enrolled in the Junior
Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program; requiring
school districts to consider certification as an instructor in
the Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program to
satisfy qualifications for certain positions of employment
applied for by a member or veteran of the Armed Forces
of the United States; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Commission on Professional Standards in Education
2 to adopt regulations relating to the qualifications to obtain: (1) a license to teach in
3 this State; and (2) an endorsement to teach in a field of specialization. (NRS
4 391.019) Section 2.5 of this bill requires the Commission to adopt regulations
5 authorizing a member or veteran of the Armed Forces of the United States who has
6 received certification, including, without limitation, a temporary, provisional or
7 advanced certification, as an instructor in the Junior Reserve Officers’ Training
8 Corps program to receive an endorsement to teach pupils enrolled in the Junior
9 Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program.
10 Existing law requires school districts to consider the Joint Services Transcript
11 or a similar document of a veteran as credit towards such training, experience or
12 licensure required for certain skilled positions of employment in that school district.
-

– 2 –
13 (NRS 391.115) Section 3 of this bill applies these provisions to a person who is a
14 member of the Armed Forces of the United States in addition to veterans. Section 3
15 also adds certification as an instructor in the Junior Reserve Officers’ Training
16 Corps program as a specific certification on a Joint Services Transcript or a similar
17 document for which credit must be provided by a school district.

1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 2.5. NRS 391.019 is hereby amended to read as follows:
4 391.019 1. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.027,
5 the Commission shall adopt regulations:
6 (a) Prescribing the qualifications for licensing teachers and other
7 educational personnel and the procedures for the issuance and
8 renewal of those licenses. The regulations:
9 (1) Must include, without limitation, the qualifications for
10 licensing teachers and administrators pursuant to an alternative route
11 to licensure which provides that the required education and training
12 may be provided by any qualified provider which has been approved
13 by the Commission, including, without limitation, institutions of
14 higher education and other providers that operate independently of
15 an institution of higher education. The regulations adopted pursuant
16 to this subparagraph must:
17 (I) Establish the requirements for approval as a qualified
18 provider;
19 (II) Require a qualified provider to be selective in its
20 acceptance of students;
21 (III) Require a qualified provider to provide in-person or
22 virtual supervised, school-based experiences and ongoing support
23 for its students, such as mentoring and coaching;
24 (IV) Significantly limit the amount of course work
25 required or provide for the waiver of required course work for
26 students who achieve certain scores on tests;
27 (V) Allow for the completion in 2 years or less of the
28 education and training required under the alternative route to
29 licensure;
30 (VI) Provide that a person who has completed the
31 education and training required under the alternative route to
32 licensure and who has satisfied all other requirements for licensure
33 may apply for a regular license pursuant to sub-subparagraph (VII)
34 regardless of whether the person has received an offer of
35 employment from a school district, charter school or private school;
36 and

– 3 –
1 (VII) Upon the completion by a person of the education
2 and training required under the alternative route to licensure and the
3 satisfaction of all other requirements for licensure, provide for the
4 issuance of a regular license to the person pursuant to the provisions
5 of this chapter and the regulations adopted pursuant to this chapter.
6 (2) Must require an applicant for a license to teach middle
7 school or junior high school education or secondary education to
8 demonstrate proficiency in a field of specialization or area of
9 concentration by successfully completing course work prescribed by
10 the Department or completing a subject matter competency
11 examination prescribed by the Department with a score deemed
12 satisfactory.
13 (3) Must not prescribe qualifications which are more
14 stringent than the qualifications set forth in NRS 391.0315 for a
15 licensed teacher who applies for an additional license in accordance
16 with that section.
17 (b) Identifying fields of specialization in teaching which require
18 the specialized training of teachers.
19 (c) Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.125, requiring
20 teachers to obtain from the Department an endorsement in a field of
21 specialization to be eligible to teach in that field of specialization.
22 (d) Setting forth the educational requirements a teacher must
23 satisfy to qualify for an endorsement in each field of specialization.
24 (e) Setting forth the qualifications and requirements for
25 obtaining a license or endorsement to teach American Sign
26 Language, including, without limitation, being registered with the
27 Aging and Disability Services Division of the Department of Health
28 and Human Services pursuant to NRS 656A.100 to engage in the
29 practice of sign language interpreting in a primary or secondary
30 educational setting.
31 (f) Requiring teachers and other educational personnel to be
32 registered with the Aging and Disability Services Division pursuant
33 to NRS 656A.100 to engage in the practice of sign language
34 interpreting in a primary or secondary educational setting if they:
35 (1) Provide instruction or other educational services; and
36 (2) Concurrently engage in the practice of sign language
37 interpreting, as defined in NRS 656A.060.
38 (g) Prescribing course work on parental involvement and family
39 engagement. The Commission shall:
40 (1) Work in cooperation with the Office of Parental
41 Involvement and Family Engagement created by NRS 385.630 in
42 developing the regulations required by this paragraph.
43 (2) Establish standards for professional development training
44 which may be used to satisfy any course work requirement
45 prescribed pursuant to this paragraph.

1 (h) Establishing the requirements for obtaining an endorsement
2 on the license of a teacher, administrator or other educational
3 personnel in cultural competency.
4 (i) Authorizing the Superintendent of Public Instruction to issue
5 a license by endorsement to an applicant who holds an equivalent
6 license or authorization issued by a governmental entity in another
7 country if the Superintendent determines that the qualifications for
8 the equivalent license or authorization are substantially similar to
9 those prescribed pursuant to paragraph (a).
10 (j) Establishing the requirements for obtaining an endorsement
11 on the license of a teacher, administrator or other educational
12 personnel in teaching courses relating to financial literacy.
13 (k) Authorizing a person who is employed as a paraprofessional
14 and enrolled in a program to become a teacher to complete an
15 accelerated program of student teaching in the same or a
16 substantially similar area in which the person is employed as a
17 paraprofessional while remaining employed as a paraprofessional.
18 (l) Requiring the Department to accept a program of student
19 teaching or other teaching experience completed in another state or
20 foreign country by an applicant for a license if the Department
21 determines that the program or experience substantially fulfills the
22 standards of a program of student teaching in this State.
23 (m) Authorizing a person who is employed by a public school to
24 provide support or other services relating to school psychology, if
25 the person does not hold a license or endorsement as a school
26 psychologist but is enrolled in a program that would allow the
27 person to obtain such a license or endorsement, to complete a
28 program of internship in school psychology while remaining
29 employed in such a position.
30 (n) To carry out the provisions of NRS 391B.010.
31 (o) Authorizing a person who is a member of the Armed
32 Forces of the United States or a veteran of the Armed Forces of
33 the United States who has received certification as an instructor in
34 the Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program, as defined
35 in NRS 391.115, to receive an endorsement to teach pupils
36 enrolled in the Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program.
37 2. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.027, the
38 Commission may adopt such other regulations as it deems necessary
39 for its own government or to carry out its duties.
40 3. Any regulation which increases the amount of education,
41 training or experience required for licensing:
42 (a) Must, in addition to the requirements for publication in
43 chapter 233B of NRS, be publicized before its adoption in a manner
44 reasonably calculated to inform those persons affected by the
45 change.

1 (b) Must not become effective until at least 1 year after the date
2 it is adopted by the Commission.
3 (c) Is not applicable to a license in effect on the date the
4 regulation becomes effective.
5 Sec. 3. NRS 391.115 is hereby amended to read as follows:
6 391.115 1. If a person who is a member of the Armed
7 Forces of the United States or a veteran of the Armed Forces of the
8 United States submits an application for employment in a school
9 district for a position that requires certain training, experience ,
10 certification or licensure in a skilled trade, the school district must
11 consider any military education, training or occupational experience
12 listed on a Joint Services Transcript or a similar document ,
13 including, without limitation, certification as an instructor in the
14 Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program, as credit
15 towards any such required training, experience , certification or
16 licensure.
17 2. As used in this section, “certification as an instructor in
18 the Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps program” means any
19 form of certification as an instructor in the Junior Reserve
20 Officers’ Training Corps program set forth in 10 U.S.C. §§ 2031 et
21 seq. issued by any department of the Armed Forces of the United
22 States, including, without limitation, a temporary, provisional or
23 advanced certification.
24 Sec. 4. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
25 approval.
26 2. Sections 1 to 3, inclusive, of this act become effective:
27 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
28 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
29 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
30 (b) On July 1, 2026, for all other purposes.
