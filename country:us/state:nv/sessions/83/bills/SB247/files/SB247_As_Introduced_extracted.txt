Section 1:
S.B. 247
LEGISLATIVE OPERATIONS AND ELECTIONS
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON COMMERCE AND LABOR)
FEBRUARY 26, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Provides for health notes to be requested and
prepared for certain legislative measures by the
Research Division of the Legislative Counsel
Bureau. (BDR 17-512)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§ 6)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to governmental administration; requiring, under
certain circumstances, the Research Division of the
Legislative Counsel Bureau to prepare health notes for
bills and joint resolutions; requiring that certain
governmental entities and officers provide the Research
Division with assistance in preparing health notes;
providing that information obtained by the Research
Division relating to the preparation of health notes is
confidential and privileged and is not subject to discovery
or subpoena; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Research Division of the Legislative Counsel Bureau
2 to provide the Legislature and its members and committees with research,
3 information and assistance concerning public policy. (NRS 218F.810) Section 5 of
4 this bill requires the Research Division, upon the request of any legislator, to
5 prepare a health note for a bill or joint resolution that determines whether the bill or
6 joint resolution is likely to disproportionately impact the social determinants of
-
*SB247*
– 2 –
7 health of: (1) members of any racial or ethnic minority; (2) members of a low-
8 income or underserved community; (3) senior citizens; or (4) children. Sections 3
9 and 4 of this bill, respectively, define the terms “health note” and “social
10 determinants of health.” Section 2 of this bill applies these definitions to the
11 provisions of sections 5 and 6 of this bill.
12 Section 6 requires each agency, board, commission, department, officer,
13 employee or agent of this State or a local government to provide the Research
14 Division such assistance as is necessary for the Research Division to prepare a
15 health note. Section 7 of this bill provides that any information obtained by the
16 Research Division for the preparation of a health note is confidential and privileged
17 and is not subject to discovery or subpoena.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 218D of NRS is hereby amended by
2 adding thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 to 6, inclusive,
3 of this act.
4 Sec. 2. As used in sections 2 to 6, inclusive, of this act, unless
5 the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in
6 sections 3 and 4 of this act have the meanings ascribed to them in
7 those sections.
8 Sec. 3. “Health note” means a nonpartisan policy brief
9 prepared by the Research Division that analyzes how a bill or joint
10 resolution may impact the social determinants of health.
11 Sec. 4. “Social determinants of health” means the
12 nonmedical factors that influence health outcomes, including,
13 without limitation, education, environment, employment, housing,
14 food security and access to health care.
15 Sec. 5. 1. After a bill or joint resolution is prefiled or
16 introduced, any legislator may request that the Research Division
17 prepare a health note for the bill or joint resolution that
18 determines whether the bill or joint resolution is likely to
19 disproportionately impact the social determinants of health of any
20 of the following persons in this State:
21 (a) Members of any racial or ethnic minority;
22 (b) Members of a low-income or underserved community;
23 (c) Senior citizens; or
24 (d) Children.
25 2. Upon receipt of a request pursuant to subsection 1, the
26 Research Division shall prepare a health note for the bill or joint
27 resolution. A health note shall not contain any information that is
28 confidential pursuant to federal or state law.
29 3. Any health note prepared by the Research Division
30 pursuant to this section shall be posted on the Internet website of
31 the Legislature.
-
*SB247*
– 3 –
1 4. Nothing in this section prohibits the Legislature or a
2 committee thereof from taking action on a bill or joint resolution
3 before the Research Division completes a health note for a bill or
4 joint resolution.
5 Sec. 6. Every agency, board, commission, department,
6 officer, employee or agent of this State or a political subdivision of
7 this State shall provide the Research Division such assistance as is
8 necessary for the Research Division to prepare a health note
9 required pursuant to section 5 of this act.
10 Sec. 7. NRS 218F.150 is hereby amended to read as follows:
11 218F.150 1. The Director and other officers and employees
12 of the Legislative Counsel Bureau shall not:
13 (a) Oppose or urge legislation, except as the duties of the
14 Director, the Legislative Auditor, the Legislative Counsel, the
15 General Counsel, the Research Director and the Fiscal Analysts
16 require them to make recommendations to the Legislature.
17 (b) Except as otherwise provided in this section, NRS 218D.130,
18 218D.135, 218D.250 and 353.211, disclose to any person outside
19 the Legislative Counsel Bureau the nature or content of any matter
20 entrusted to the Legislative Counsel Bureau, and such matter is
21 confidential and privileged and is not subject to discovery or
22 subpoena, unless the person entrusting the matter to the Legislative
23 Counsel Bureau requests or consents to the disclosure.
24 2. [The] Except as otherwise provided in this section, the
25 nature or content of any work produced by the officers and
26 employees of the Research Division may be disclosed if or to the
27 extent that the disclosure does not reveal the identity of the person
28 who requested it or include any matter submitted by the requester
29 which has not been published or publicly disclosed. Any
30 information obtained by the Research Division for purposes of
31 preparing a health note pursuant to section 5 of this act is
32 confidential and privileged and is not subject to discovery or
33 subpoena.
34 3. The nature and content of any work produced by the officers
35 and employees of the Legal Division and the Fiscal Analysis
36 Division and any matter entrusted to those officers and employees to
37 produce such work are confidential and privileged and are not
38 subject to discovery or subpoena.
39 4. The provisions of subsections 1, 2 and 3 apply to any matter
40 or work in any form, including, without limitation, in any oral,
41 written, audio, visual, digital or electronic form, and such matter or
42 work includes, without limitation, any communications,
43 information, answers, advice, opinions, recommendations, drafts,
44 documents, records, questions, inquiries or requests in any such
45 form.
-
*SB247*
– 4 –
1 5. When a statute has been enacted or a resolution adopted, the
2 Legislative Counsel shall upon request disclose to any person the
3 state or other jurisdiction from whose law it appears to have been
4 adopted.
5 6. The records of the travel expenses of Legislators and officers
6 and employees of the Legislature and the Legislative Counsel
7 Bureau are available for public inspection at such reasonable hours
8 and under such other conditions as the Legislative Commission
9 prescribes.
10 Sec. 8. The provisions of NRS 354.599 do not apply to any
11 additional expenses of a local government that are related to the
12 provisions of this act.
