Section 1:
A.B. 182
PREFILED FEBRUARY 2, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Creates the Legislative Committee on the Reduction
of Nonessential State Expenditures. (BDR 17-929)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to legislative affairs; creating the Legislative
Committee on the Reduction of Nonessential State
Expenditures; prescribing the powers and duties of the
Committee; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates various committees consisting of Legislators to study
2 specified issues and perform other activities during the interim between legislative
3 sessions. (See, e.g., NRS 218E.150-218E.815) Section 5 of this bill creates the
4 Legislative Committee on the Reduction of Nonessential State Expenditures as a
5 statutory interim committee and provides for its membership. Section 6 of this bill
6 provides for the meetings of the Committee, prescribes the manner in which
7 meetings must be conducted and provides for the compensation of the members of
8 the Committee.
9 Section 7 of this bill prescribes the duties of the Committee, including the
10 review and evaluation of issues relating to state governmental spending and
11 efficiency. Specifically, section 7 requires the Committee to: (1) identify areas in
12 which state expenditures may be eliminated or avoided; (2) identify areas in which
13 state expenditures may be reduced; (3) investigate alleged instances within state
14 agencies of abuse, fraud or waste with respect to public money; and (4) make
15 recommendations for corrective action by such an agency relating to such an
16 investigation. Section 7 also requires a state agency to collaborate with the
17 Committee in carrying out its duties and to provide the Committee with certain
18 information upon request.
19 Section 8 of this bill authorizes the Committee to: (1) conduct investigations
20 and hold hearings; (2) request that the Legislative Counsel Bureau assist in the
21 research, investigations, hearings and reviews of the Committee; and (3)
22 recommend legislation concerning state governmental spending and efficiency.
23 Section 8 also requires the Committee to submit to the Legislature a biennial report
24 describing its activities. Section 8 also authorizes the Committee to submit such
-
*AB182*
– 2 –
25 additional reports to the Interim Finance Committee or the Legislature as it deems
26 appropriate.
27 Sections 2, 3 and 4 of this bill define certain terms for the purposes of this bill.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 218E of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 to 8, inclusive, of this
3 act.
4 Sec. 2. As used in sections 2 to 8, inclusive, of this act, unless
5 the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in
6 sections 3 and 4 of this act have the meanings ascribed to them in
7 those sections.
8 Sec. 3. “Agency” means an agency, bureau, board,
9 commission, department, division or any other unit of the State
10 Government.
11 Sec. 4. “Committee” means the Legislative Committee on the
12 Reduction of Nonessential State Expenditures created by section 5
13 of this act.
14 Sec. 5. 1. The Legislative Committee on the Reduction of
15 Nonessential State Expenditures is hereby created. The
16 membership of the Committee consists of:
17 (a) Two members of the Senate appointed by the Majority
18 Leader of the Senate;
19 (b) Two members of the Assembly appointed by the Speaker of
20 the Assembly;
21 (c) One member of the Senate appointed by the Minority
22 Leader of the Senate; and
23 (d) One member of the Assembly appointed by the Minority
24 Leader of the Assembly.
25 2. The Legislative Commission shall review and approve the
26 budget and work program for the Committee and any changes to
27 the budget or work program.
28 3. The Legislative Commission shall select a Chair and Vice
29 Chair of the Committee from among the members of the
30 Committee. After the initial selection, each Chair and Vice Chair
31 holds office for a term of 2 years commencing on July 1 of each
32 odd-numbered year. The office of Chair of the Committee must
33 alternate each biennium between the Houses. If a vacancy occurs
34 in the office of the Chair or Vice Chair, the vacancy must be filled
35 in the same manner as the original selection for the remainder of
36 the unexpired term.
37 4. A member of the Committee who is not a candidate for
38 reelection or who is defeated for reelection continues to serve after
-
*AB182*
– 3 –
1 the general election until the next regular or special session of the
2 Legislature convenes.
3 5. A vacancy on the Committee must be filled in the same
4 manner as the original appointment for the remainder of the
5 unexpired term.
6 Sec. 6. 1. Except as otherwise ordered by the Legislative
7 Commission, the members of the Committee shall meet not earlier
8 than September 1 of each odd-numbered year and not later than
9 August 31 of the following even-numbered year at the times and
10 places specified by a call of the Chair or a majority of the
11 Committee.
12 2. The Director or the Director’s designee shall act as the
13 nonvoting recording Secretary of the Committee.
14 3. Four members of the Committee constitute a quorum, and
15 a quorum may exercise all the power and authority conferred on
16 the Committee.
17 4. Except during a regular or special session of the
18 Legislature, for each day or portion of a day during which a
19 member of the Committee attends a meeting of the Committee or is
20 otherwise engaged in the business of the Committee, the member
21 is entitled to receive the:
22 (a) Compensation provided for a majority of the members of
23 the Legislature during the first 60 days of the preceding regular
24 session;
25 (b) Per diem allowance provided for state officers and
26 employees generally; and
27 (c) Travel expenses provided pursuant to NRS 218A.655.
28 5. All such compensation, per diem allowances and travel
29 expenses must be paid from the Legislative Fund.
30 Sec. 7. 1. The Committee shall review and evaluate issues
31 relating to state governmental spending and efficiency. The
32 Committee shall:
33 (a) Identify areas in which state expenditures may be
34 eliminated or avoided, including, without limitation, by identifying
35 programs or services provided by agencies that the Committee
36 determines are duplicative of other programs or services,
37 ineffective or nonessential for the operation of the State
38 Government.
39 (b) Identify areas in which the costs to an agency of providing
40 services that the Committee determines are essential for the
41 operation of the State Government may be reduced, including,
42 without limitation, areas in which increased efficiencies may be
43 found.
44 (c) Upon concluding that an agency:
-
*AB182*
– 4 –
1 (1) May be engaging in abuse, fraud or waste with respect
2 to public money, investigate the agency.
3 (2) Is engaged in abuse, fraud or waste with respect to
4 public money, provide written notice to the agency of that
5 determination. Such notice must include, without limitation, any
6 recommendations for corrective action by the agency.
7 2. Each agency shall:
8 (a) Collaborate with the Committee in carrying out the duties
9 prescribed in this section; and
10 (b) Provide the Committee with any information requested by
11 the Committee as soon as is reasonably practicable after receiving
12 the request.
13 3. An agency that receives notice of a determination by the
14 Committee that the agency is engaged in abuse, fraud or waste
15 pursuant to subparagraph (2) of paragraph (c) of subsection 1
16 shall, upon the request of the Committee, provide the Committee
17 with a written report regarding any corrective actions taken by the
18 agency to implement the recommendations made by the Committee
19 pursuant to that subparagraph.
20 Sec. 8. 1. The Committee may:
21 (a) Conduct investigations and hold hearings in connection
22 with its duties pursuant to section 7 of this act and exercise any of
23 the investigative powers set forth in NRS 218E.105 to 218E.140,
24 inclusive.
25 (b) Request that the Legislative Counsel Bureau assist in the
26 research, investigations, hearings and reviews of the Committee.
27 (c) Recommend to the Legislature, as a result of its review and
28 evaluation, any appropriate legislation concerning state
29 governmental spending and efficiency.
30 2. On or before October 1 of each even-numbered year, the
31 Committee shall:
32 (a) Compile a report describing the activities of the Committee
33 during the legislative interim. The report must include, without
34 limitation:
35 (1) Any recommendations for legislation considered by the
36 Committee. The report must indicate, for each such
37 recommendation, whether the recommendation was approved by a
38 majority vote of all of the members of the Committee.
39 (2) A summary of the areas identified by the Committee in
40 which state expenditures may be eliminated or avoided pursuant to
41 paragraph (a) of subsection 1 of section 7 of this act.
42 (3) A summary of the areas identified by the Committee in
43 which state expenditures may be reduced pursuant to paragraph
44 (b) of subsection 1 of section 7 of this act.
-
*AB182*
– 5 –
1 (4) A summary of any investigation conducted pursuant to
2 paragraph (c) of subsection 1 of section 7 of this act and any
3 recommendations for corrective action made by the Committee.
4 (5) A summary of any corrective action taken by an agency
5 to implement any recommendations made by the Committee of
6 which the Committee is aware.
7 (b) Submit the report to the Director for transmittal to the
8 Legislature.
9 3. The Committee may submit to the Director for transmittal
10 to the Interim Finance Committee or the Legislature any such
11 additional reports as it deems appropriate.
12 Sec. 9. The provisions of subsection 1 of NRS 218D.380 do
13 not apply to any provision of this act which adds or revises a
14 requirement to submit a report to the Legislature.
15 Sec. 10. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
