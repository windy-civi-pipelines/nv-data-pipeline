Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT A.B. 256
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 256–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS LA RUE HATCH,
GONZÁLEZ, ROTH, MOORE, ANDERSON; BROWN-MAY,
COLE, CONSIDINE, FLANAGAN, GOULDING, HUNT,
JACKSON, NGUYEN AND ORENTLICHER
FEBRUARY 18, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Directs the Legislative Commission to appoint a
Regional Rail Transit Advisory Working Group to
conduct an interim study relating to regional rail
transit. (BDR S-614)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to rail transit; directing the Legislative
Commission to appoint a Regional Rail Transit Advisory
Working Group to conduct an interim study relating to
regional rail transit in regional communities; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill directs the Legislative Commission to appoint a Regional Rail Transit
2 Advisory Working Group to conduct an interim study concerning the development
3 of regional rail transit in regional communities across the State. This bill requires
4 the Advisory Working Group to: (1) study the current state of regional rail transit
5 and public transit, the need for regional rail transit, the sources of money to support
6 the development of regional rail transit and public and private entities that should
7 be involved in the development of regional rail transit; and (2) submit an actionable
8 plan for the development of regional rail transit in regional communities across the
9 State and a written report of its findings and recommendations to the Joint Interim
10 Standing Committee on Growth and Infrastructure.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. The Legislative Commission shall appoint a
2 Regional Rail Transit Advisory Working Group to conduct a study
-
*AB256_R1*
– 2 –
1 during the 2025-2026 interim concerning the development of
2 regional rail transit in regional communities across the State.
3 2. The Advisory Working Group must be composed of the
4 following voting members appointed by the Legislative
5 Commission:
6 (a) One member who represents a labor union representing rail
7 or public transit employees in Clark County;
8 (b) One member who represents a labor union representing rail
9 or public transit employees in Washoe County;
10 (c) One member who represents the Regional Transportation
11 Commission of Southern Nevada;
12 (d) One member who represents the Regional Transportation
13 Commission of Washoe County;
14 (e) One member who represents a chamber of commerce or
15 trade association;
16 (f) Two members who are Legislators;
17 (g) One member who represents Clark County or another local
18 government in Clark County;
19 (h) One member who represents Washoe County or another
20 local government in Washoe County;
21 (i) One member who is a resident of Clark County who has an
22 interest or expertise in rail transit;
23 (j) One member who is a resident of Washoe County who has an
24 interest or expertise in rail transit;
25 (k) One member who is a resident of a county other than Clark
26 County or Washoe County, who has an interest or expertise in rail
27 transit; and
28 (l) One member who represents a county other than Clark
29 County or Washoe County or a local government in such a county
30 other than Clark County or Washoe County or who is a
31 representative of an organization representing multiple such
32 counties.
33 3. In addition to the members appointed pursuant to subsection
34 2, the Legislative Commission may appoint any other nonvoting
35 members to advise or assist the Advisory Working Group.
36 4. During the 2025-2026 interim, the Advisory Working Group
37 shall study:
38 (a) The current state of regional rail and public transit in
39 regional communities across the State, including, without limitation,
40 any current or future rail or public transit projects that are currently
41 in the planning stages or under development;
42 (b) The need for regional rail transit in regional communities
43 across the State;
-
*AB256_R1*
– 3 –
1 (c) Potential sources of federal, state, private or nonprofit money
2 to support the development of regional rail transit in regional
3 communities across the State; and
4 (d) Any public or private entities which should be involved or
5 oversee the planning, development and implementation of regional
6 rail transit in regional communities across the State.
7 5. The Advisory Working Group shall, at its first meeting, elect
8 a Chair and Vice Chair from among its voting members.
9 6. The Advisory Working Group shall meet at the call of the
10 Chair at least once each month. A majority of the Advisory Working
11 Group constitutes a quorum for the transaction of business, and a
12 majority of those voting members present at any meeting is
13 sufficient for any official action taken by the Advisory Working
14 Group.
15 7. The members of the Advisory Working Group serve without
16 compensation and are not entitled to the per diem and travel
17 expenses provided for state officers and employees generally.
18 8. On or before July 1, 2026, the Advisory Working Group
19 shall prepare and submit to the Director of the Legislative Counsel
20 Bureau for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
21 Growth and Infrastructure:
22 (a) An actionable plan to develop regional rail transit in regional
23 communities across the State; and
24 (b) A written report describing the findings and
25 recommendations of the Advisory Working Group, including any
26 recommendations for legislation.
27 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
