SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to the use of diacritical marks in
certain governmental records. (BDR 40-43)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to governmental records; [requiring] authorizing records of the
State Registrar of Vital Statistics, each county health officer and the
Department of Motor Vehicles to include a diacritical mark used in the
name of a person; [requiring] authorizing licenses, identification cards
and driver authorization cards issued by the Department of Motor
Vehicles to display a diacritical mark used in the full legal name of a
person; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law charges the State Registrar of Vital Statistics with maintaining and
2 preserving and each county health officer with collecting certain vital statistics, which is
3 defined as records of births, deaths, fetal deaths, marriages, annulments, divorce and related
4 information. (NRS 440.080, 440.110-440.260) Section 1 of this bill [requires] authorizes the
5 State Registrar and each county health officer to [ensure that] record a diacritical mark used
6 in the name of a person [is properly recorded] in the vital statistics of the State Registrar and
7 county health officer.
8 Existing law requires the Department of Motor Vehicles to issue: (1) licenses to operate a
9 motor vehicle or motorcycle, including, without limitation, instruction permits, commercial
10 driver’s licenses and motorcycle driver’s licenses; (2) identification cards; and (3) driver
11 authorization cards upon application, which must include, among other requirements, the full
12 legal name of the applicant. (NRS 483.2521, 483.270, 483.280, 483.290, 483.291, 483.340,
13 483.360, 483.375, 483.830, 483.850, 483.860, 483.8605, 483.908, 486.081) Sections 2 and 5
14 of this bill provide that if the full legal name of an applicant for such a license, identification
15 card or driver authorization card includes a diacritical mark, the Department [must ensure]
16 may record the diacritical mark [is properly recorded] in the records of the Department and
17 [displayed] display the mark on any license, identification card or driver authorization card
18 issued by the Department.
Senate Amendment No. 121 to Senate Bill No. 91 Page 4
19 Sections 1, 2 and 5 define “diacritical mark” as a mark in Latin script that is added or
20 attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or word.
21 Section 3 of this bill provides that the provisions of section 2 apply to commercial
22 driver’s licenses. Sections 4 and 6 of this bill apply the existing definitions in existing law to
23 the provisions of sections 1, 2 and 5.
24 Section 8 of this bill makes sections 2-7.5 of this bill effective upon the date on which
25 the United States Social Security Administration authorizes the recording and display of
26 diacritical marks on documents of the Social Security Administration or upon the date
27 on which the Director of the Department notifies the Governor and the Director of the
28 Legislative Counsel Bureau that the Department has sufficient funding to carry out the
29 provisions of this bill, whichever is later. Section 8 also makes the provisions of section 1
30 effective 6 months after sections 2-7.5 become effective.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 440 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. If the name of a person includes a diacritical mark, the State Registrar
4 and each county health officer [shall ensure that] may record the diacritical mark
5 [is properly recorded] in the vital statistics of the State Registrar or county health
6 officer, as applicable.
7 2. As used in this section, “diacritical mark” means a mark in Latin script
8 that is added or attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or
9 word. The term includes, without limitation, accents, tildes, graves, umlauts and
10 cedillas.
11 Sec. 2. Chapter 483 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
12 section to read as follows:
13 1. If the full legal name of an applicant for a license, a commercial driver’s
14 license, an identification card or a driver authorization card includes a diacritical
15 mark, the Department [shall ensure that] may record the diacritical mark [is
16 properly recorded] in the records of the Department and [displayed] display the
17 mark on any license, identification card or driver authorization card issued by the
18 Department.
19 2. As used in this section, “diacritical mark” means a mark in Latin script
20 that is added or attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or
21 word. The term includes, without limitation, accents, tildes, graves, umlauts and
22 cedillas.
23 Sec. 3. NRS 483.015 is hereby amended to read as follows:
24 483.015 Except as otherwise provided in NRS 483.330 [,] and section 2 of
25 this act, the provisions of NRS 483.010 to 483.630, inclusive, and section 2 of this
26 act, apply only with respect to noncommercial drivers’ licenses.
27 Sec. 4. NRS 483.020 is hereby amended to read as follows:
28 483.020 As used in NRS 483.010 to 483.630, inclusive, and section 2 of this
29 act, unless the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in NRS
30 483.030 to 483.190, inclusive, have the meanings ascribed to them in those
31 sections.
32 Sec. 5. Chapter 486 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
33 section to read as follows:
34 1. If the full legal name of an applicant for a motorcycle driver’s license
35 includes a diacritical mark, the Department [shall ensure that] may record the
36 diacritical mark [is properly recorded] in the records of the Department and
Senate Amendment No. 121 to Senate Bill No. 91 Page 5
1 [displayed] display the mark on any motorcycle driver’s license issued by the
2 Department.
3 2. As used in this section, “diacritical mark” means a mark in Latin script
4 that is added or attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or
5 word. The term includes, without limitation, accents, tildes, graves, umlauts and
6 cedillas.
7 Sec. 6. NRS 486.011 is hereby amended to read as follows:
8 486.011 As used in NRS 486.011 to 486.381, inclusive, and section 5 of this
9 act, unless the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in NRS
10 486.015 to 486.057, inclusive, have the meanings ascribed to them in those
11 sections.
12 Sec. 7. [The provisions of NRS 354.599 do not apply to any additional
13 expenses of a local government that are related to the provisions of this act.]
14 (Deleted by amendment.)
15 Sec. 7.5. As soon as practicable after determining that the Department of
16 Motor Vehicles has sufficient resources to carry out the provisions of sections 2
17 and 5 of this act, the Director of the Department shall notify the Governor and
18 the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau of the fact and shall publish
19 notice to the public of the fact on the Internet website of the Department.
20 Sec. 8. 1. This [act] section becomes effective [on July 1, 2025.] upon
21 passage and approval.
22 2. Sections 2 to 7.5, inclusive, of this act become effective on the later of:
23 (a) The date on which the United States Social Security Administration
24 authorizes the recording and display of diacritical marks on documents of the
25 Social Security Administration; or
26 (b) The date on which the Director of the Department of Motor
27 Vehicles notifies the Governor and the Director of the Legislative Counsel
28 Bureau that the Department has sufficient funding to carry out the provisions
29 of sections 2 and 5 of this act.
30 3. Section 1 of this act becomes effective 6 months after the date on which
31 sections 2 to 7.5, inclusive, of this act become effective pursuant to subsection
32 2.
