certain governmental records. (BDR 40-43)

Referred to Committee on Growth and Infrastructure
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to the use of diacritical marks in
certain governmental records. (BDR 40-43)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to governmental records; authorizing records of the State
Registrar of Vital Statistics, each county health officer and the
Department of Motor Vehicles to include a diacritical mark used in the
name of a person; authorizing licenses, identification cards and driver
authorization cards issued by the Department of Motor Vehicles to
display a diacritical mark used in the full legal name of a person; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law charges the State Registrar of Vital Statistics with maintaining and
2 preserving and each county health officer with collecting certain vital statistics, which is
3 defined as records of births, deaths, fetal deaths, marriages, annulments, divorce and related
4 information. (NRS 440.080, 440.110-440.260) Section 1 of this bill authorizes the State
5 Registrar and each county health officer to record a diacritical mark used in the name of a
6 person in the vital statistics of the State Registrar and county health officer.
7 Existing law requires the Department of Motor Vehicles to issue: (1) licenses to operate a
8 motor vehicle or motorcycle, including, without limitation, instruction permits, commercial
9 driver’s licenses and motorcycle driver’s licenses; (2) identification cards; and (3) driver
10 authorization cards upon application, which must include, among other requirements, the full
11 legal name of the applicant. (NRS 483.2521, 483.270, 483.280, 483.290, 483.291, 483.340,
12 483.360, 483.375, 483.830, 483.850, 483.860, 483.8605, 483.908, 486.081) Sections 2 and 5
13 of this bill provide that if the full legal name of an applicant for such a license, identification
14 card or driver authorization card includes a diacritical mark, the Department may record the
15 diacritical mark in the records of the Department and display the mark on any license,
16 identification card or driver authorization card issued by the Department.
17 Sections 1, 2 and 5 define “diacritical mark” as a mark in Latin script that is added or
18 attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or word.
19 Section 3 of this bill provides that the provisions of section 2 apply to commercial
20 driver’s licenses. Sections 4 and 6 of this bill apply the existing definitions in existing law to
21 the provisions of sections 1, 2 and 5.
Assembly Amendment No. 709 to Senate Bill No. 91 First Reprint Page 4
22 Section 8 of this bill makes sections 2-7.5 of this bill effective upon the date on which
23 the United States Social Security Administration authorizes the recording and display of
24 diacritical marks on documents of the Social Security Administration or upon the date on
25 which the Director of the Department notifies the Governor and the Director of the Legislative
26 Counsel Bureau that the Department has sufficient funding from the Federal Government to
27 carry out the provisions of this bill, whichever is later. Section 8 also makes the provisions of
28 section 1 effective 6 months after sections 2-7.5 become effective.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 440 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. If the name of a person includes a diacritical mark, the State Registrar
4 and each county health officer may record the diacritical mark in the vital
5 statistics of the State Registrar or county health officer, as applicable.
6 2. As used in this section, “diacritical mark” means a mark in Latin script
7 that is added or attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or
8 word. The term includes, without limitation, accents, tildes, graves, umlauts and
9 cedillas.
10 Sec. 2. Chapter 483 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
11 section to read as follows:
12 1. If the full legal name of an applicant for a license, a commercial driver’s
13 license, an identification card or a driver authorization card includes a diacritical
14 mark, the Department may record the diacritical mark in the records of the
15 Department and display the mark on any license, identification card or driver
16 authorization card issued by the Department.
17 2. As used in this section, “diacritical mark” means a mark in Latin script
18 that is added or attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or
19 word. The term includes, without limitation, accents, tildes, graves, umlauts and
20 cedillas.
21 Sec. 3. NRS 483.015 is hereby amended to read as follows:
22 483.015 Except as otherwise provided in NRS 483.330 [,] and section 2 of
23 this act, the provisions of NRS 483.010 to 483.630, inclusive, and section 2 of this
24 act, apply only with respect to noncommercial drivers’ licenses.
25 Sec. 4. NRS 483.020 is hereby amended to read as follows:
26 483.020 As used in NRS 483.010 to 483.630, inclusive, and section 2 of this
27 act, unless the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in NRS
28 483.030 to 483.190, inclusive, have the meanings ascribed to them in those
29 sections.
30 Sec. 5. Chapter 486 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
31 section to read as follows:
32 1. If the full legal name of an applicant for a motorcycle driver’s license
33 includes a diacritical mark, the Department may record the diacritical mark in
34 the records of the Department and display the mark on any motorcycle driver’s
35 license issued by the Department.
36 2. As used in this section, “diacritical mark” means a mark in Latin script
37 that is added or attached to a letter to indicate the pronunciation of the letter or
38 word. The term includes, without limitation, accents, tildes, graves, umlauts and
39 cedillas.

22 3. Section 1 of this act becomes effective 6 months after the date on which
23 sections 2 to 7.5, inclusive, of this act become effective pursuant to subsection 2.
