EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to time; exempting the State of Nevada from the provisions of
federal law requiring daylight saving time; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing federal law requires the annual advancement of the standard time for each time
2 zone by 1 hour commencing at 2 a.m. on the second Sunday of March of each year and ending
3 at 2 a.m. on the first Sunday of November of each year, commonly referred to as daylight
4 saving time. (15 U.S.C. § 260a(a)) Under existing federal law, a state may by state law
5 exempt itself and its political subdivisions from the provisions requiring the advancement of
6 time and observe the prescribed standard time for the entire calendar year. (15 U.S.C. § 260a)
7 This bill exempts Nevada from the federal provisions requiring the observance of
8 daylight saving time. Nevada would remain on Pacific Standard Time throughout the entire
9 year.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 234 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. The State of Nevada exempts itself from the provisions of 15 U.S.C. §
4 260a(a) requiring the advancement of time during the period commencing at 2
5 a.m. on the second Sunday of March of each year and ending at 2 a.m. on the
6 first Sunday of November of each year, commonly known as daylight saving time.
Assembly Amendment No. 68 to Assembly Bill No. 81 Page 4
1 2. During the period described in subsection 1, the State of Nevada and all
2 political subdivisions thereof [,] shall observe the standard time otherwise
3 applicable for the time zone in which the State is situated in accordance with the
4 provisions of 15 U.S.C. §§ 260 et seq., the solar time of the 120th meridian west
5 of Greenwich, commonly known as Pacific Standard Time.
6 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on [July] January 1, [2025.] 2026.
