Section 1:
A.B. 294
YUREK AND MARZOLA
FEBRUARY 25, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the use of the Internet
by minors. (BDR 52-567)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to the protection of minors; requiring a person that
operates an online platform, Internet website or other
Internet-based service that, as its primary business,
publishes or makes available material that is harmful to
minors to establish an age verification system to verify
the age of prospective users; prohibiting such persons
from allowing a prospective user to access material that is
harmful to minors under certain circumstances;
establishing civil penalties; authorizing the Attorney
General to bring a civil action to recover such penalties;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prohibits a person from engaging in certain conduct relating to the
2 exhibition and sale to minors of obscene material. (NRS 201.265) Section 1 of this
3 bill requires a person that operates an online platform, Internet website or other
4 Internet-based service that, as its primary business, publishes or makes available
5 material that is harmful to minors to establish an age verification system to
6 determine whether a prospective user is a minor before allowing the prospective
7 user to access material that is harmful to minors. Section 1 also: (1) prescribes
8 certain requirements relating to the security and privacy of any personal
9 information collected under section 1; (2) authorizes the Attorney General to
10 recover a civil penalty of not more than $10,000 for each violation of section 1; and
11 (3) authorizes the parent or guardian of a minor to bring a civil action for damages
12 against a person who violates section 1. Section 2 of this bill makes certain
13 definitions prescribed by existing law applicable to section 1.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 603 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. A person that operates an online platform, Internet website
4 or other Internet-based service that, as its primary business,
5 publishes or makes available material that is harmful to minors:
6 (a) Shall establish an age verification system to determine
7 whether a prospective user of the platform, website or service is a
8 minor before allowing the prospective user to access any material
9 that is harmful to minors published or made available on the
10 platform, website or service; and
11 (b) Shall not authorize a prospective user of the platform,
12 website or service to access any material that is harmful to minors
13 published or made available on the platform, website or service if
14 the person knows or reasonably believes the prospective user to be
15 a minor based on a determination made by the age verification
16 system established pursuant to paragraph (a).
17 2. A person shall be deemed to be in compliance with the
18 requirements of paragraph (a) of subsection 1 if the person
19 establishes an age verification system that verifies that a user is 18
20 years of age or older by:
21 (a) Requiring the user to upload a copy of a government-
22 issued identification card or submit an electronic version of a
23 government-issued identification card;
24 (b) Using any commercially reasonable method that relies on
25 public or private transactional data to verify the age of the user; or
26 (c) Using a third-party age verification service to verify the age
27 of the user.
28 3. A person that operates an online platform, Internet website
29 or other Internet-based service that publishes or makes available
30 material that is harmful to minors as its primary business:
31 (a) Is subject to the obligations and liabilities of chapter 603A
32 of NRS with regard to the security and privacy of any personal
33 information collected under this section; and
34 (b) Shall not use or retain any personal information collected
35 under this section for any purpose except to:
36 (1) Verify the age of a prospective user; or
37 (2) Prove that the person has complied with the
38 requirements of this section.
39 4. A person who willfully violates this section is subject to a
40 civil penalty of not more than $10,000 for each violation. The
41 Attorney General may recover the penalty in a civil action brought
42 in the name of the State of Nevada in any court of competent
-
*AB294*
– 3 –
1 jurisdiction. Any civil penalty collected pursuant to this section
2 must be used for the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this
3 section.
4 5. The parent or guardian of a minor may bring a civil action
5 for damages against a person who violates this section. Upon
6 prevailing in a civil action brought pursuant to this section, the
7 parent or guardian may recover compensatory damages,
8 reasonable attorney’s fees and any punitive damages that the facts
9 may warrant.
10 6. The provisions of this section do not apply to an Internet
11 service provider, search engine or cloud service provider that
12 provides access or connection to or from an online platform,
13 Internet website or other Internet-based service that, as its primary
14 business, publishes or makes available material that is harmful to
15 minors.
16 7. As used in this section:
17 (a) “Government-issued identification card” includes, without
18 limitation, a passport, driver’s license, permanent resident card or
19 tribal identification card.
20 (b) “Harmful to minors” has the meaning ascribed to it in
21 NRS 201.257.
22 Sec. 2. NRS 603.010 is hereby amended to read as follows:
23 603.010 As used in NRS 603.010 to 603.090, inclusive, and
24 section 1 of this act, unless the context otherwise requires, the
25 words and terms defined in NRS 603.020 and 603.030 have the
26 meanings ascribed to them in those sections.
27 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on January 1, 2026.
