SECOND REPRINT A.B. 539
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 539–COMMITTEE ON REVENUE
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON REVENUE)
MARCH 24, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Revenue
SUMMARY—Authorizes the Department of Employment, Training
and Rehabilitation to establish a career coaching
program for certain pupils in certain circumstances.
(BDR 18-418)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to workforce development; authorizing, to the
extent that money is available, the Department of
Employment, Training and Rehabilitation to establish and
carry out a program to place career coaches in middle
schools and junior high schools; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Department of Employment, Training and
2 Rehabilitation and provides that the purpose of the Department is to plan,
3 coordinate and carry out various services and activities designed to achieve and
4 support employment and economic independence for certain residents of this State.
5 (NRS 232.910) Section 1 of this bill authorizes, to the extent that money is
6 available, the Department to establish and carry out a program to place career
7 coaches in middle schools and junior high schools. Section 1 requires that the
8 program be designed to provide pupils enrolled in middle school or junior high
9 school with information and guidance, inside or outside the classroom, for the
10 exploration of career pathways and the development of knowledge, training and
11 skills required for career readiness. Section 1 further: (1) requires the Department
12 to collaborate with any appropriate persons or entities to establish and carry out the
13 program and, to the extent that money is available, prioritize the placement of such
14 career coaches in middle schools and junior high schools that are located in zip
15 codes with high levels of unemployment; and (2) authorizes the Department to
16 apply for and accept gifts, donations, grants or other sources of money and, within
17 the limits of available money, to enter into contracts and award grants related to the
-
*AB539_R2*
– 2 –
18 program. Section 2 of this bill makes certain definitions applicable to the
19 provisions of section 1.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 232 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. To the extent that money is available, the Department may
4 establish and carry out a program for the placement of career
5 coaches in middle schools and junior high schools.
6 2. The program established pursuant to subsection 1 must be
7 designed to provide pupils enrolled in middle school or junior high
8 school with information and guidance, inside or outside the
9 classroom, for:
10 (a) The exploration of career pathways; and
11 (b) The development of knowledge, training and skills required
12 for career readiness, including, without limitation, financial
13 literacy, and the opportunities available for such development in
14 high school.
15 3. For the purpose of establishing and carrying out the
16 program described in subsection 1, the Department:
17 (a) Shall:
18 (1) Collaborate with any appropriate persons or entities,
19 including, without limitation, a state agency, school district or
20 other local governmental agency, chamber of commerce,
21 nonprofit organization, trade association and regional
22 development authority, as defined in NRS 231.009; and
23 (2) Prioritize the placement of career coaches in middle
24 schools and junior high schools that are located in zip codes with
25 high levels of unemployment; and
26 (b) May:
27 (1) Apply for and accept any gift, donation, grant or other
28 source of money; and
29 (2) Within the limits of available money, enter into
30 contracts with or award grants to persons or entities that have the
31 appropriate expertise to provide any services or resources
32 necessary to assist the Department in establishing or carrying out
33 the program.
34 Sec. 2. NRS 232.900 is hereby amended to read as follows:
35 232.900 As used in NRS 232.900 to 232.990, inclusive, and
36 section 1 of this act, unless the context otherwise requires:
37 1. “Department” means the Department of Employment,
38 Training and Rehabilitation.
