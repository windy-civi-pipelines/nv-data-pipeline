Section 1:
EXEMPT
FIRST REPRINT A.B. 131
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 131–ASSEMBLYMEMBER JACKSON
PREFILED JANUARY 28, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Provides an exemption from property taxation for
accessory dwelling units rented or leased to certain
tenants. (BDR 32-694)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to housing; providing an exemption from property
taxation for accessory dwelling units rented or leased to
tenants receiving certain tenant-based housing assistance;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing federal law authorizes the Secretary of the United States Department
2 of Housing and Urban Development to provide assistance to public housing
3 agencies for tenant-based assistance through the Housing Choice Voucher Program.
4 (Section 8 of the United States Housing Act of 1937, 42 U.S.C. § 1437f) Section 6
5 of this bill provides an exemption from property taxes for an accessory dwelling
6 unit if the accessory dwelling unit: (1) is rented or leased to a tenant receiving
7 assistance under the Program pursuant to a lease which is for a period of not less
8 than 12 consecutive months; (2) is located on the same parcel as a single-family
9 residence which is the primary residence of the owner of the accessory dwelling
10 unit; (3) complies with all applicable local zoning laws; and (4) if the parcel is
11 located within a common-interest community, complies with all applicable
12 covenants, conditions and restrictions of the community. Section 6 requires a
13 person claiming the exemption to annually file with the county assessor: (1) certain
14 verifications from the appropriate public housing authority; and (2) a copy of the
15 lease. Section 6 also: (1) prohibits the exemption from being granted for more than
16 one accessory dwelling unit per parcel; and (2) requires the county assessor to
17 determine the value of the accessory dwelling unit based on the cost of replacing
18 the accessory dwelling unit. Section 7 of this bill makes a conforming change to
19 indicate the proper placement of section 6 in the Nevada Revised Statutes.

6 Sec. 6. Chapter 361 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
7 thereto a new section to read as follows:
8 1. An accessory dwelling unit is exempt from taxation if, for
9 the year in which the exemption applies, the accessory dwelling
10 unit:
11 (a) Is rented or leased to a qualified tenant receiving
12 assistance under the Housing Choice Voucher Program pursuant
13 to a written lease which is for a period of not less than 12
14 consecutive months;
15 (b) Is located on the same parcel as a single-family residence
16 which is the primary residence of the owner of the accessory
17 dwelling unit;
18 (c) Complies with all applicable local zoning laws; and
19 (d) If the parcel is located within a common-interest
20 community, complies with all applicable covenants, conditions and
21 restrictions of the common-interest community.
22 2. Any person claiming an exemption pursuant to this section
23 must file annually with the county assessor:
24 (a) Verification from the appropriate public housing authority
25 that:
26 (1) The person has entered into a housing assistance
27 payment contract with the public housing authority to receive
28 tenant-based assistance payments under the Housing Choice
29 Voucher Program for the accessory dwelling unit;
30 (2) The qualified tenant to whom the accessory dwelling
31 unit is rented or leased is receiving assistance under the Housing
32 Choice Voucher Program pursuant to a written lease which is for
33 a period of not less than 12 consecutive months; and
34 (3) The accessory dwelling unit, at the time it was last
35 inspected, met the housing quality standards set forth in 42 U.S.C.
36 1437f(o); and
37 (b) A copy of the lease for the accessory dwelling unit, which
38 must set forth, without limitation, the total square footage of the
39 accessory dwelling unit.
40 3. The person may claim an exemption pursuant to this
41 section for one accessory dwelling unit per parcel.
-
*AB131_R1*
– 3 –
1 4. To determine the value of the accessory dwelling unit for
2 the purposes of an exemption claimed pursuant to this section, the
3 value is equal to the cost of replacing the accessory dwelling unit,
4 which must be calculated by the county assessor in accordance
5 with the regulations adopted by the Nevada Tax Commission
6 pursuant to subsection 6 of NRS 361.227.
7 5. A county assessor may prescribe forms and adopt
8 procedures for claiming the exemption pursuant to this section not
9 inconsistent with any regulations, forms and procedures
10 prescribed or adopted by the Department or the Nevada Tax
11 Commission.
12 6. As used in this section:
13 (a) “Accessory dwelling unit” means an ancillary or secondary
14 living unit that has a separate egress, kitchen, bathroom and
15 sleeping area existing on the same lot as a single-family residence.
16 (b) “Housing Choice Voucher Program” means the federal
17 tenant-based assistance program established under 42 U.S.C.
18 1437f(o).
19 (c) “Qualified tenant” means a person who is not employed by
20 or related to the owner of the property on which the accessory
21 dwelling unit is located by consanguinity or affinity within the
22 third degree.
23 Sec. 7. NRS 361.160 is hereby amended to read as follows:
24 361.160 1. Personal property in transit through this State is
25 personal property:
26 (a) Which is moving in interstate commerce through or over the
27 territory of the State of Nevada; or
28 (b) Which was consigned to a warehouse, public or private,
29 within the State of Nevada from outside the State of Nevada for
30 storage in transit to a final destination outside the State of Nevada,
31 whether specified when transportation begins or afterward.
32  Such property is deemed to have acquired no situs in Nevada for
33 purposes of taxation. Such property is not deprived of exemption
34 because while in the warehouse the property is assembled, bound,
35 joined, manufactured, processed, disassembled, divided, cut, broken
36 in bulk, relabeled or repackaged, or because the property is being
37 held for resale to customers outside the State of Nevada. The
38 exemption granted shall be liberally construed to effect the purposes
39 of NRS 361.160 to 361.185, inclusive.
40 2. Personal property within this State as mentioned in NRS
41 361.030 and 361.045 to 361.155, inclusive, and section 6 of this act
42 does not include personal property in transit through this State as
43 defined in this section.
44 Sec. 8. The Legislature hereby finds that the exemption
45 provided by this act from any ad valorem tax on property:
-

– 4 –
1 1. Will achieve a bona fide social or economic purpose and that
2 the benefits of the exemption are expected to exceed any adverse
3 effect of the exemption on the provision of services to the public by
4 the State or a local government that would otherwise receive
5 revenue from the tax from which the exemption would be granted;
6 and
7 2. Will not impair adversely the ability of the State or a local
8 government to pay, when due, all interest and principal on any
9 outstanding bonds or any other obligations for which revenue from
10 the tax from which the exemption would be granted was pledged.
11 Sec. 9. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
12 approval.
13 2. Sections 1 to 8, inclusive, of this act become effective:
14 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
15 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
16 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
17 (b) On July 1, 2026, for all other purposes.
