SENATE BILL NO. 442–COMMITTEE ON
GROWTH AND INFRASTRUCTURE
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING COMMITTEE
ON GROWTH AND INFRASTRUCTURE)
MARCH 24, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Growth and Infrastructure
SUMMARY—Requires the Public Utilities Commission of Nevada to establish
certain reporting requirements. (BDR 58-429)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public utilities; requiring the Public Utilities Commission of
Nevada to adopt regulations to require a public utility to [submit a
monthly report] make available to the public certain reports
containing [certain] information regarding terminations of utility
service to residential customers; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Public Utilities Commission of Nevada to adopt or amend
2 certain regulations relating to a public utility terminating the gas, water or electric utilities of a
3 residential customer. (NRS 704.1835) This bill [provides that] requires the Commission
4 [must] to adopt regulations [to] that require a public utility [to submit] make available to the
5 public a [monthly] quarterly report [to the Commission] concerning such terminations of
6 utility services. Specifically, this bill requires that the report [to] include : [, without
7 limitation:] (1) the total number of monthly terminations of utility services of residential
8 customers during the previous [month;] quarter; and (2) aggregated [demographic]
9 information concerning the residential customers whose utility service was terminated,
10 [including, without limitation,] which must include the zip code of such residences.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 704.1835 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 704.1835 1. For the purposes of protecting the health of residential
3 customers who receive gas, water or electricity from public utilities, the
4 Commission shall adopt or amend regulations that:
Senate Amendment No. 245 to Senate Bill No. 442 Page 4
1 (a) Establish the criteria that will be used to determine when a public utility is
2 required to postpone its termination of utility service to the residence of a
3 residential customer who has failed to pay for such service. Such criteria may be
4 based in part upon the residential customer’s ability to pay.
5 (b) Require a public utility to postpone its termination of utility service to the
6 residence of a residential customer who has failed to pay for such service if the
7 residential customer satisfies the criteria established by the Commission and
8 termination of the utility service is reasonably likely to threaten the health of an
9 occupant of the residence of the residential customer.
10 2. In addition to the regulations adopted pursuant to subsection 1, for the
11 purposes of regulating public utilities that provide gas, water or electricity to
12 landlords who pay for the utility service and who distribute or resell the gas, water
13 or electricity to one or more residential tenants, the Commission shall adopt or
14 amend regulations to require a public utility to use its best efforts to post, in a
15 conspicuous location, notice of the intent of the public utility to terminate utility
16 service because the landlord has failed to pay for such service. Such notice must
17 provide sufficient information to allow residential tenants or their occupants to
18 contact the public utility if termination of the utility service is reasonably likely to
19 threaten the health of an occupant of the residence of a residential tenant.
20 3. The Commission shall adopt regulations [to] that require a public utility ,
21 on or before the last day of the first month of each calendar quarter, to [submit]
22 make available to the public a [monthly] report [to the Commission] that includes
23 : [, without limitation:]
24 (a) The total number of monthly terminations of utility service to the
25 residences of residential customers during the previous [month;] quarter; and
26 (b) Aggregated [demographic] information concerning the residential
27 customers described in paragraph (a), [including , without limitation,] which
28 must include the zip code of such residences.
29 4. A public utility shall not terminate utility service for gas, water or
30 electricity without complying with the regulations adopted by the Commission
31 pursuant to this section.
32 [4.] 5. As used in this section:
33 (a) “Gas” includes, without limitation, liquefied petroleum gas and natural gas.
34 (b) “Landlord” means a landlord who is subject, in whole or in part, to the
35 provisions of chapter 118A or 118B of NRS.
