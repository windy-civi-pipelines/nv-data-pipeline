ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 19–COMMITTEE ON
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
(ON BEHALF OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL)
PREFILED NOVEMBER 6, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the Statewide
Substance Use Response Working Group.
(BDR 40-442)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to substance use disorders; revising the
membership of the Statewide Substance Use Response
Working Group; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Statewide Substance Use Response Working Group in
2 the Office of the Attorney General to leverage and expand efforts by state and local
3 governmental entities to reduce the use of substances which are associated with
4 substance use disorders. (NRS 458.460, 458.480) Existing law further provides that
5 the membership of the Working Group consists of 18 members, 12 of whom are
6 appointed by the Attorney General. (NRS 458.460) This bill provides that, for
7 purposes of membership of the Working Group, the designee of the Director of the
8 Department of Health and Human Services must be a person from within the
9 Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the Department. This bill also adds to
10 the membership of the Working Group: (1) the Executive Director of the
11 Department of Indigent Defense Services, or his or her designee; and (2) four
12 members appointed by the Attorney General.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 458.460 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 458.460 1. The Statewide Substance Use Response Working
3 Group is hereby created in the Office of the Attorney General.
4 2. The Working Group consists of the following members:
5 (a) The Attorney General or his or her designee;
6 (b) The Director of the Department of Health and Human
7 Services, or his or her designee [;] from within the Division;
8 (c) The Executive Director of the Department of Indigent
9 Defense Services, or his or her designee;
10 (d) One member of the Senate who is appointed by the Senate
11 Majority Leader;
12 [(d)] (e) One member of the Senate who is appointed by the
13 Senate Minority Leader;
14 [(e)] (f) One member of the Assembly who is appointed by the
15 Speaker of the Assembly;
16 [(f)] (g) One member of the Assembly who is appointed by the
17 Assembly Minority Leader; and
18 [(g)] (h) The following members, appointed by the Attorney
19 General:
20 (1) One representative of a local governmental entity that
21 provides or oversees the provision of human services in a county
22 whose population is 700,000 or more;
23 (2) One representative of a local governmental entity that
24 provides or oversees the provision of human services in a county
25 whose population is 100,000 or more but less than 700,000;
26 (3) One representative of a local governmental entity that
27 provides or oversees the provision of human services in a county
28 whose population is less than 100,000;
29 (4) One provider of health care with expertise in medicine
30 for the treatment of substance use disorders;
31 (5) One representative of the Nevada Sheriffs’ and Chiefs’
32 Association, or its successor organization;
33 (6) One advocate for persons who have substance use
34 disorders and family members of such persons;
35 (7) One person who is in recovery from a substance use
36 disorder;
37 (8) One person who provides services relating to the
38 treatment of substance use disorders;
39 (9) One representative of a substance use disorder prevention
40 coalition;
41 (10) One representative of a program to reduce the harm
42 caused by substance misuse;
-

1 (11) One representative of a hospital; [and]
2 (12) One representative of a school district [.] ;
3 (13) One member of the general public, with preference
4 given to a person who is fluent in more than one language and
5 resides in a household where more than one language is spoken;
6 (14) One person who is an emergency response employee;
7 (15) One representative of the Division of Child and Family
8 Services of the Department of Health and Human Services; and
9 (16) One representative of the Nevada District Attorneys
10 Association, or its successor organization.
11 3. After the initial terms, members of the Working Group serve
12 terms of 2 years and serve at the pleasure of the appointing
13 authority. Members may be reappointed for additional terms of 2
14 years in the same manner as the original appointments.
15 4. If a vacancy occurs during a member’s term, the appointing
16 authority shall appoint a replacement for the remainder of the
17 unexpired term. A vacancy must be filled in the same manner as the
18 original appointment.
19 5. Members of the Working Group serve without compensation
20 and are not entitled to receive the per diem allowance and travel
21 expenses provided for state officers and employees generally.
22 6. A member of the Working Group who is an officer or
23 employee of this State or a political subdivision of this State must be
24 relieved from his or her duties without loss of regular compensation
25 to prepare for and attend meetings of the Working Group and
26 perform any work necessary to carry out the duties of the Working
27 Group in the most timely manner practicable. A state agency or
28 political subdivision of this State shall not require an officer or
29 employee who is a member of the Working Group to:
30 (a) Make up the time he or she is absent from work to carry out
31 his or her duties as a member of the Working Group; or
32 (b) Take annual leave or compensatory time for the absence.
33 7. As used in this section [, “substance] :
34 (a) “Attendant” has the meaning ascribed to it in
35 NRS 450B.050.
36 (b) “Emergency response employee” means a firefighter,
37 attendant, volunteer attendant, emergency medical technician,
38 advanced emergency medical technician, emergency medical
39 dispatcher, paramedic, law enforcement officer, correctional
40 officer, other peace officer or person who is employed by an
41 agency of criminal justice, including, without limitation, a law
42 enforcement dispatcher, county coroner or medical examiner or
43 any of their employees, or any other person who, in the course of
44 his or her professional duties, responds to emergencies in this
45 State.

2 coalition of persons and entities who possess knowledge and
3 experience related to the prevention of substance misuse and
4 substance use disorders in a region of this State.
5 Sec. 2. As soon as practicable after October 1, 2025, the
6 Attorney General shall appoint to the Statewide Substance Use
7 Response Working Group:
8 1. The members described in subparagraphs (13) and (14) of
9 paragraph (h) of subsection 2 of NRS 458.460, as amended by

11 and
12 2. The members described in subparagraphs (15) and (16) of
13 paragraph (h) of subsection 2 of NRS 458.460, as amended by
14 section 1 of this act, to initial terms that expire on January 1, 2028.
