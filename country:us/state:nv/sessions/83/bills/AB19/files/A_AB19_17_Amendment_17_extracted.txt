Working Group. (BDR 40-442)

ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 19–COMMITTEE ON
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
(ON BEHALF OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL)
PREFILED NOVEMBER 6, 2024
_______________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the Statewide Substance Use
Response Working Group. (BDR 40-442)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to substance use disorders; [adding members to] revising the
membership of the Statewide Substance Use Response Working
Group; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Statewide Substance Use Response Working Group in the Office
2 of the Attorney General to leverage and expand efforts by state and local governmental
3 entities to reduce the use of substances which are associated with substance use disorders.
4 (NRS 458.460, 458.480) Existing law further provides that the membership of the Working
5 Group consists of 18 members, 12 of whom are appointed by the Attorney General. (NRS
6 458.460) This bill provides that, for purposes of membership of the Working Group, the
7 designee of the Director of the Department of Health and Human Services must be a
8 person from within the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the Department.
9 This bill also adds to the membership of the Working Group: (1) the Executive Director of the
10 Department of Indigent Defense Services, or his or her designee; and (2) four members
11 appointed by the Attorney General.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 458.460 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 458.460 1. The Statewide Substance Use Response Working Group is
3 hereby created in the Office of the Attorney General.
4 2. The Working Group consists of the following members:
5 (a) The Attorney General or his or her designee;
6 (b) The Director of the Department of Health and Human Services, or his or
7 her designee [;] from within the Division;
8 (c) The Executive Director of the Department of Indigent Defense Services,
9 or his or her designee;
Assembly Amendment No. 17 to Assembly Bill No. 19 Page 4
1 (d) One member of the Senate who is appointed by the Senate Majority
2 Leader;
3 [(d)] (e) One member of the Senate who is appointed by the Senate Minority
4 Leader;
5 [(e)] (f) One member of the Assembly who is appointed by the Speaker of the
6 Assembly;
7 [(f)] (g) One member of the Assembly who is appointed by the Assembly
8 Minority Leader; and
9 [(g)] (h) The following members, appointed by the Attorney General:
10 (1) One representative of a local governmental entity that provides or
11 oversees the provision of human services in a county whose population is 700,000
12 or more;
13 (2) One representative of a local governmental entity that provides or
14 oversees the provision of human services in a county whose population is 100,000
15 or more but less than 700,000;
16 (3) One representative of a local governmental entity that provides or
17 oversees the provision of human services in a county whose population is less than
18 100,000;
19 (4) One provider of health care with expertise in medicine for the treatment
20 of substance use disorders;
21 (5) One representative of the Nevada Sheriffs’ and Chiefs’ Association, or
22 its successor organization;
23 (6) One advocate for persons who have substance use disorders and family
24 members of such persons;
25 (7) One person who is in recovery from a substance use disorder;
26 (8) One person who provides services relating to the treatment of substance
27 use disorders;
28 (9) One representative of a substance use disorder prevention coalition;
29 (10) One representative of a program to reduce the harm caused by
30 substance misuse;
31 (11) One representative of a hospital; [and]
32 (12) One representative of a school district [.] ;
33 (13) One member of the general public [;] , with preference given to a
34 person who is fluent in more than one language and resides in a household
35 where more than one language is spoken;
36 (14) One person who is an emergency response employee;
37 (15) One representative of the Division of Child and Family Services of
38 the Department of Health and Human Services; and
39 (16) One representative of the Nevada District Attorneys Association, or
40 its successor organization.
41 3. After the initial terms, members of the Working Group serve terms of 2
42 years and serve at the pleasure of the appointing authority. Members may be
43 reappointed for additional terms of 2 years in the same manner as the original
44 appointments.
45 4. If a vacancy occurs during a member’s term, the appointing authority shall
46 appoint a replacement for the remainder of the unexpired term. A vacancy must be
47 filled in the same manner as the original appointment.
48 5. Members of the Working Group serve without compensation and are not
49 entitled to receive the per diem allowance and travel expenses provided for state
50 officers and employees generally.
51 6. A member of the Working Group who is an officer or employee of this
52 State or a political subdivision of this State must be relieved from his or her duties
53 without loss of regular compensation to prepare for and attend meetings of the
Assembly Amendment No. 17 to Assembly Bill No. 19 Page 5
1 Working Group and perform any work necessary to carry out the duties of the
2 Working Group in the most timely manner practicable. A state agency or political
3 subdivision of this State shall not require an officer or employee who is a member
4 of the Working Group to:
5 (a) Make up the time he or she is absent from work to carry out his or her
6 duties as a member of the Working Group; or
7 (b) Take annual leave or compensatory time for the absence.
8 7. As used in this section [, “substance] :
9 (a) “Attendant” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 450B.050.
10 (b) “Emergency response employee” means a firefighter, attendant,
11 volunteer attendant, emergency medical technician, advanced emergency medical
12 technician, emergency medical dispatcher, paramedic, law enforcement officer,
13 correctional officer, other peace officer or person who is employed by an agency
14 of criminal justice, including, without limitation, a law enforcement dispatcher,
15 county coroner or medical examiner or any of their employees, or any other
16 person who, in the course of his or her professional duties, responds to
17 emergencies in this State.
18 (c) “Substance use disorder prevention coalition” means a coalition of persons
19 and entities who possess knowledge and experience related to the prevention of
20 substance misuse and substance use disorders in a region of this State.
21 Sec. 2. As soon as practicable after October 1, 2025, the Attorney General
22 shall appoint to the Statewide Substance Use Response Working Group:
23 1. The members described in subparagraphs (13) and (14) of paragraph (h) of
24 subsection 2 of NRS 458.460, as amended by section 1 of this act, to initial terms
25 that expire on January 1, 2027; and
26 2. The members described in subparagraphs (15) and (16) of paragraph (h) of
27 subsection 2 of NRS 458.460, as amended by section 1 of this act, to initial terms
28 that expire on January 1, 2028.
