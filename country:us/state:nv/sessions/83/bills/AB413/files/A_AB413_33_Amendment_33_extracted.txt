SUMMARY—Provides for the creation of the Southern Nevada Health Care
Workforce Apprenticeship Pilot Program. (BDR S-317)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included in
Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to workforce development; providing for the creation of the
Southern Nevada Health Care Workforce Apprenticeship Pilot
Program; making an appropriation; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides for the promotion, registration and approval of apprenticeship
2 programs in this State. (Chapter 610 of NRS) Section 1 of this bill requires [the Department
3 of Business and Industry] Workforce Connections to use the money appropriated by
4 section 2 of this bill to develop, create and administer the Southern Nevada Health Care
5 Workforce Apprenticeship Pilot Program for the purpose of addressing shortages in the
6 number of medical assistants and pharmaceutical technicians in this State through the
7 establishment of apprenticeship programs in Southern Nevada for the training of such persons.
8 Section 1 requires the Program to provide: (1) for the establishment of apprenticeship
9 programs in Southern Nevada for the training of medical assistants and pharmaceutical
10 technicians, which must be registered and approved by the State Apprenticeship Council; and
11 (2) money [to] for participants in the program to cover the costs of participating in the
12 program, including, without limitation, fees for professional certifications and registrations, as
13 applicable, for not more than 100 apprentices who are enrolled in apprenticeship programs
14 established under the Program.
15 Section 2 [of this bill] makes an appropriation to [the Department of Business and
16 Industry] Workforce Connections to carry out the Program.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. [The Department of Business and Industry] Workforce
2 Connections shall use the money appropriated by section 2 of this act to
3 develop, create and administer the Southern Nevada Health Care Workforce
4 Apprenticeship Pilot Program for the purpose of addressing shortages in the
5 number of medical assistants and pharmaceutical technicians in this State through
6 the establishment of apprenticeship programs for the training of such persons.
Assembly Amendment No. 33 to Assembly Bill No. 413 Page 4
1 2. The Program must provide:
2 (a) For the establishment of apprenticeship programs in Southern Nevada for
3 the training of medical assistants and pharmaceutical technicians. Any
4 apprenticeship program established under the Program must be registered and
5 approved by the State Apprenticeship Council pursuant to chapter 610 of NRS.
6 (b) Money [to] for participants in the Program to cover the costs of
7 participating in the Program, including, without limitation, fees for professional
8 certifications and registrations, as applicable, for not more than 100 apprentices
9 who are enrolled in apprenticeship programs established under the Program.
10 3. As used in this section:
11 (a) “Medical assistant” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 630.0129 and
12 633.075.
13 (b) “Pharmaceutical technician” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS
14 639.0113.
15 Sec. 2. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to [the
16 Department of Business and Industry] Workforce Connections the sum of
17 $300,000 to carry out the Southern Nevada Health Care Workforce Apprenticeship
18 Pilot Program established pursuant to section 1 of this act.
19 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1 must not
20 be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to which the
21 appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
22 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
23 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after September 17, 2027, by
24 either the entity to which the money was appropriated or the entity to which the
25 money was subsequently granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State
26 General Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
27 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
