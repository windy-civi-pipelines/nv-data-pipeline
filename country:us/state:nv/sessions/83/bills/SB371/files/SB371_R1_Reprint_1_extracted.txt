SENATE BILL NO. 371–SENATORS STEINBECK AND DOÑATE
MARCH 17, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to crimes. (BDR 15-908)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to crimes; revising provisions relating to warnings
against trespassing; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides that any person who, under circumstances not amounting
2 to a burglary, willfully goes or remains upon any land or in any building after
3 having been warned during the previous 24 months by the owner or occupant
4 thereof not to trespass is guilty of a misdemeanor. (NRS 207.200) Section 5 of this
5 bill extends the period relating to such a warning from 24 months to 36 months.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
4 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
5 Sec. 5. NRS 207.200 is hereby amended to read as follows:
6 207.200 1. Unless a greater penalty is provided pursuant to
7 NRS 200.603, any person who, under circumstances not amounting
8 to a burglary:
9 (a) Goes upon the land or into any building of another with
10 intent to vex or annoy the owner or occupant thereof, or to commit
11 any unlawful act; or
-
*SB371_R1*
– 2 –
1 (b) Willfully goes or remains upon any land or in any building
2 after having been warned during the previous [24] 36 months by the
3 owner or occupant thereof not to trespass,
4  is guilty of a misdemeanor. The meaning of this subsection is not
5 limited by subsections 2 and 4.
6 2. A sufficient warning against trespassing, within the meaning
7 of this section, is given by any of the following methods:
8 (a) Painting with fluorescent orange paint:
9 (1) Not less than 50 square inches of a structure or natural
10 object or the top 12 inches of a post, whether made of wood, metal
11 or other material, at:
12 (I) Intervals of such a distance as is necessary to ensure
13 that at least one such structure, natural object or post would be
14 within the direct line of sight of a person standing next to another
15 such structure, natural object or post, but at intervals of not more
16 than 1,000 feet; and
17 (II) Each corner of the land, upon or near the boundary;
18 and
19 (2) Each side of all gates, cattle guards and openings that are
20 designed to allow human ingress to the area;
21 (b) Fencing the area;
22 (c) Posting “no trespassing” signs or other notice of like
23 meaning at:
24 (1) Intervals of such a distance as is necessary to ensure that
25 at least one such sign would be within the direct line of sight of a
26 person standing next to another such sign, but at intervals of not
27 more than 500 feet; and
28 (2) Each corner of the land, upon or near the boundary;
29 (d) Using the area as cultivated land; or
30 (e) By the owner or occupant of the land or building making an
31 oral or written demand to any guest to vacate the land or building.
32 3. It is prima facie evidence of trespass for any person to be
33 found on private or public property which is posted or fenced as
34 provided in subsection 2 without lawful business with the owner or
35 occupant of the property.
36 4. An entryman on land under the laws of the United States is
37 an owner within the meaning of this section.
38 5. As used in this section:
39 (a) “Cultivated land” means land that has been cleared of its
40 natural vegetation and is presently planted with a crop.
41 (b) “Fence” means a barrier sufficient to indicate an intent to
42 restrict the area to human ingress, including, but not limited to, a
43 wall, hedge or chain link or wire mesh fence. The term does not
44 include a barrier made of barbed wire.
-

1 (c) “Guest” means any person entertained or to whom
2 hospitality is extended, including, but not limited to, any person
3 who stays overnight. The term does not include a tenant as defined
4 in NRS 118A.170.
