SENATE BILL NO. 444–COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION
MARCH 24, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions concerning policies governing the use of certain
electronic devices in schools. (BDR 34-855)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring a policy adopted by the board of trustees
of a school district concerning the use and possession by pupils of
certain electronic devices to include certain provisions governing the
discipline of pupils and exceptions to limitations prescribed as part of
such a policy; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) requires the board of trustees of each school district to adopt a policy
2 concerning the use and possession by pupils of certain electronic devices while on school
3 property or at a school activity; and (2) sets forth certain requirements concerning the policy.
4 (NRS 392.4637) This bill [: (1)] requires the policy to address the use and possession by
5 pupils of an electronic communication device . [; and (2) defines “electronic communication
6 device” to include, without limitation, a cellular phone, computer, laptop computer and tablet
7 device.] This bill additionally requires the policy to include: (1) measures for disciplining a
8 pupil using a progressive scale based on the number and seriousness of violations; (2)
9 limitations on the use of an electronic communication device during instruction; and (3)
10 certain exceptions for any limitations imposed as part of such a policy.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 392.4637 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 392.4637 1. The board of trustees of each school district shall adopt a policy
3 concerning the use and possession by pupils of [a pager, cellular telephone or any
4 other similar] an electronic communication device [used for communication] while
5 on the premises of a public school or while at an activity sponsored by a public
6 school.
7 2. The policy adopted pursuant to subsection 1 must:
8 (a) Prescribe appropriate measures for disciplining a pupil who violates the
9 policy [.] that include, without limitation, a system to rate violations of a policy
10 adopted pursuant to subsection 1 on a progressive scale based on the number and
11 seriousness of the violations.
Senate Amendment No. 177 to Senate Bill No. 444 Page 4
1 (b) Prescribe limitations on the use of an electronic communication device
2 during instruction.
3 (c) Include exceptions to any limitations on the use of an electronic
4 communication device prescribed as part of a policy adopted pursuant to
5 subsection 1, including, without limitation, exceptions:
6 (1) On the use of an electronic communication device during an
7 emergency or crisis or to manage the health of a pupil;
8 (2) If a teacher allows for the use of an electronic communication device
9 for instructional purposes; and
10 (3) If the use of an electronic communication device is included as part
11 of an individualized education program or a plan developed in accordance with
12 section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, 29 U.S.C. § 794.
13 (d) Be included within each copy of the rules of behavior for pupils that the
14 school district provides to pupils pursuant to NRS 392.463.
15 3. As used in this section:
16 (a) “Electronic communication device” means any electronic device that is
17 capable of transmitting any audio, written or pictorial information or messages to
18 another electronic device. [The term includes, without limitation, a cellular
19 phone, computer, laptop computer and tablet device.] The term does not include
20 an electronic device issued to a pupil by the school district or the school in which
21 the pupil is enrolled.
22 (b) “Individualized education program” has the meaning ascribed to it in 20
23 U.S.C. § 1414(d)(1)(A).
24 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective:
25 1. Upon passage and approval for the purpose of performing any
26 administrative tasks necessary to adopt the policy required by NRS 392.4637, as
27 amended by section 1 of this act, not later than June 30, 2026; and
28 2. On July 1, 2026, for all other purposes.
