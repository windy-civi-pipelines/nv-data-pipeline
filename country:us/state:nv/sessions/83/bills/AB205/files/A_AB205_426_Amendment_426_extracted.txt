SUMMARY—Revises provisions governing sexual education. (BDR 34-597)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; revising provisions governing consent to participate
in a course or unit of instruction concerning the human
immunodeficiency virus, the human reproductive system, related
communicable diseases and sexual responsibility; revising
requirements for forms used to refuse authorization for a pupil to
participate in such a course or unit of a course; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the board of trustees of each school district to establish a course or
2 unit of a course of instruction concerning the human immunodeficiency virus, the human
3 reproductive system, related communicable diseases and sexual responsibility. Existing law
4 further requires notice to be given to a parent or guardian of a pupil to whom such a course or
5 unit of instruction will be offered and requires the school district to provide a form for the
6 parent or guardian of the pupil to provide consent for the pupil to participate in the course or
7 unit. (NRS 389.036) This bill instead requires the school district to provide a form for the
8 parent or guardian of a pupil to refuse to authorize the pupil to participate in the course or
9 unit. This bill requires the school district to furnish the notice containing the form to the
10 parent or guardian not later than 4 weeks after the start of the school year or 4 weeks
11 before the course or unit is to commence, whichever is earlier. This bill additionally
12 authorizes a school district to make the form available on the secure Internet website of the
13 school district and requires that the form include [an option for] a notice that a parent or
14 guardian of a pupil [to] may refuse permission for the pupil to participate in a course or unit
15 of sexuality education [: (1) for that school year only; or (2) for] at any time during the
16 school year . [in which the pupil is enrolled in the school district.]
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 389.036 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 389.036 1. The board of trustees of a school district shall establish a course
3 or unit of a course of:
4 (a) Factual instruction concerning the human immunodeficiency virus; and
Assembly Amendment No. 426 to Assembly Bill No. 205 Page 4
1 (b) Instruction on the human reproductive system, related communicable
2 diseases and sexual responsibility.
3 2. Each board of trustees shall appoint an advisory committee consisting of:
4 (a) Five parents of children who attend schools in the district; and
5 (b) Four representatives, one from each of four of the following professions or
6 occupations:
7 (1) Medicine or nursing;
8 (2) Counseling;
9 (3) Religion;
10 (4) Pupils who attend schools in the district; or
11 (5) Teaching.
12  This committee shall advise the district concerning the content of and materials
13 to be used in a course [of instruction] or unit established pursuant to this section,
14 and the recommended ages of the pupils to whom the course or unit is offered. The
15 final decision on these matters must be that of the board of trustees.
16 3. The subjects of the courses or units may be taught only by a teacher or
17 school nurse whose qualifications have been previously approved by the board of
18 trustees.
19 4. The parent or guardian of each pupil to whom a course or unit is offered
20 must [first] be furnished written notice , not later than 4 weeks after the start of
21 the school year or 4 weeks before the first date on which the course or unit will
22 commence, whichever is earlier, that the course or unit will be offered. The notice
23 must be given in the usual manner used by the local district to transmit written
24 material to parents, and must contain a form for the signature of the parent or
25 guardian of the pupil [consenting] refusing to authorize the pupil’s attendance [.]
26 in the course or unit. Upon receipt of the written [consent] refusal of the parent or
27 guardian, the pupil [may attend the course. If the written consent of the parent or
28 guardian is not received, the pupil] must be excused from [such attendance]
29 attending the course or unit without any penalty as to credits or academic standing.
30 If the written refusal of the parent or guardian is not received, the pupil must
31 attend each lesson of the course or unit. Any course or unit offered pursuant to
32 this section is not a requirement for graduation.
33 5. The form required to be provided to a parent or guardian of a pupil
34 pursuant to subsection 4:
35 (a) May be made available on the secure Internet website of the school
36 district for the electronic signature of the parent or guardian and may be
37 included with any online registration to register a pupil with a school.
38 (b) [Must allow the parent or guardian to refuse to authorize the pupil to
39 attend a course or unit established pursuant to subsection 1 during:
40 (1) The school year in which the authorization is refused; or
41 (2) Any school year in which the pupil is enrolled in the school district.
42 (c)] Must include a notification that [authorization] the parent or guardian
43 of a pupil may , [be refused] at any time [and, regardless of how authorization is
44 refused, the parent or guardian will receive written notice pursuant to subsection
45 4 when a course or unit of a course of instruction established pursuant to
46 subsection 1 will be offered.] during the school year, refuse to authorize or
47 withdraw authorization for the pupil to attend the course or unit.
48 6. All instructional materials to be used in a course or unit must be available
49 for inspection by parents or guardians of pupils at reasonable times and locations
50 before the course or unit is taught, and appropriate written notice of the availability
51 of the material must be furnished to all parents and guardians.
52 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
