Section 1:
A.B. 171
YEAGER AND WATTS
PREFILED JANUARY 31, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Authorizes the State Quarantine Officer to order the
temporary suspension of certain prohibitions relating
to the sale, offer or exposing for sale or transport for
sale of eggs produced by egg-laying hens in certain
enclosures under certain circumstances.
(BDR 51-985)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to eggs; authorizing the State Quarantine Officer
to order the temporary suspension of certain requirements
relating to the sale, offer or exposing for sale or transport
for sale of egg products or shell eggs under certain
circumstances; authorizing the State Quarantine Officer to
adopt regulations relating to an order to temporarily
suspend such requirements; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prohibits an egg product or shell egg from being sold or offered,
2 exposed or transported for sale within this State if the egg product or shell egg was
3 produced by an egg-laying hen confined in certain enclosures that are not cage-free.
4 (NRS 583.211-583.251) Section 1 of this bill authorizes the State Quarantine
5 Officer to order the temporary suspension of such requirements during an ongoing
6 event that negatively impacts the national supply chain for egg products or shell
7 eggs which may not exceed 120 consecutive days per suspension. Section 1
8 additionally: (1) prohibits the State Quarantine Officer from ordering more than
9 two temporary suspensions in 1 calendar year; and (2) requires the State Quarantine
10 Officer to provide notice to the public when the order for the temporary suspension
11 will be released.
12 Section 8 of this bill authorizes the State Quarantine Officer to adopt
13 regulations governing the circumstances in which a temporary suspension will be
14 ordered and any requirements for such a temporary suspension.
-
*AB171*
– 2 –
15 Sections 4-7 and 9 of this bill provide that the provisions of section 1 are an
16 exception to the existing prohibitions, enforcement and penalties relating to egg
17 products and shell eggs produced by egg-laying hens confined in certain enclosures
18 that are not cage-free. Section 3 of this bill provides that certain exemptions to
19 these prohibitions also apply to the provisions of section 1.
20 Section 2 of this bill applies certain definitions relating to cage-free eggs to the
21 provisions of section 1.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 583 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The State Quarantine Officer may, by order, temporarily
4 suspend the provisions of this section and NRS 583.211 to
5 583.251, inclusive, for a period of time which may not exceed 120
6 consecutive days per suspension during any ongoing event that
7 negatively impacts the national supply chain for egg products or
8 shell eggs, including, without limitation:
9 (a) A foreign animal disease identified by the Animal and
10 Plant Health Inspection Service of the United States Department
11 of Agriculture; or
12 (b) A federally declared disease emergency or natural disaster.
13 2. If the State Quarantine Officer orders the temporary
14 suspension of the provisions of this section and NRS 583.211 to
15 583.251, inclusive, pursuant to subsection 1:
16 (a) Subject to the maximum duration specified in subsection 1,
17 the temporary suspension remains in effect until the State
18 Quarantine Officer orders the release of the temporary
19 suspension; and
20 (b) Not less than 14 days before the State Quarantine Officer
21 orders the release of the temporary suspension, the State
22 Quarantine Officer must provide notice to the public that the order
23 temporarily suspending the provisions of this section and NRS
24 583.211 to 583.251, inclusive, will be released.
25 3. The State Quarantine Officer may not order more than two
26 temporary suspensions in 1 calendar year.
27 Sec. 2. NRS 583.211 is hereby amended to read as follows:
28 583.211 As used in NRS 583.211 to 583.251, inclusive, and
29 section 1 of this act, unless the context otherwise requires, the
30 words and terms defined in NRS 583.212 to 583.224, inclusive, and
31 section 1 of this act have the meanings ascribed to them in those
32 sections.
