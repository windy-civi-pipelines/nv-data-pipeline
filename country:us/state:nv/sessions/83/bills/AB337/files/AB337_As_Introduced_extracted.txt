Section 1:
A.B. 337
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
(ON BEHALF OF THE LEGISLATIVE COMMITTEE ON SENIOR
CITIZENS, VETERANS AND ADULTS WITH SPECIAL NEEDS)
MARCH 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Creates programs related to dementia.
(BDR 38-456)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to dementia; requiring the Aging and Disability
Services Division of the Department of Health and
Human Services to establish and administer a dementia
care specialist program; setting forth certain requirements
for the program; providing for the establishment of the
Nevada Memory Network for the diagnosis of dementia
and the care of patients with dementia; setting forth
certain requirements for the Nevada Memory Network;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Aging and Disability Services Division of the
2 Department of Health and Human Services to establish and administer a program to
3 provide the community-based services necessary to enable a frail elderly person to
4 remain in his or her home and avoid being placed in a facility for long-term care.
5 (NRS 427A.250) Existing law also authorizes the Division to establish and
6 administer a program of all-inclusive care for the elderly. (NRS 427A.255)
7 Section 1 of this bill requires the Division to establish and administer a
8 program for dementia care specialists in this State. Section 1 requires the goals of
9 the program to include: (1) developing and providing certain crisis intervention
10 services for persons with dementia and their families and caregivers; (2) proactively
11 supporting persons with dementia and their families and caregivers; and (3)
12 providing access to the program through translation and interpretation services for
13 persons with dementia and their families and caregivers where such persons have
14 limited English proficiency. Within the limits of legislative appropriations and any
-
*AB337*
– 2 –
15 other money available for this purpose, section 1 requires the Division to: (1)
16 prepare requests for proposals for the provision of services by dementia care
17 specialists employed by local governmental agencies or nonprofit organizations to
18 carry out the program; and (2) partner or contract with an appropriate educational
19 organization to provide training to dementia care specialists and evaluate the
20 program.
21 Existing law creates the Task Force on Alzheimer’s Disease within the
22 Department of Health and Human Services and requires the Task Force to develop
23 a state plan to address Alzheimer’s disease. (NRS 439.508-439.5085) Section 2 of
24 this bill authorizes the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, School of Medicine and
25 the University of Nevada, Reno, School of Medicine, in collaboration with the
26 Department, to establish the Nevada Memory Network as a system for the early
27 detection and diagnosis of dementia and the ongoing management of care for
28 patients with dementia. If the Network is established, section 2 requires the
29 Universities and the Department to establish clinics, or contract with clinics who
30 meet certain criteria, in order to: (1) train providers of primary care in screening for
31 and treating dementia; (2) diagnose dementia in patients referred to such clinics;
32 and (3) create plans of care for patients with dementia, using telehealth when
33 appropriate, in order to provide such services. Section 2 also requires the Network,
34 if established, to employ or contract with community health workers who specialize
35 in dementia care for the provision of certain services.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 427A of NRS is hereby amended by
2 adding thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Division shall establish and administer a program for
4 dementia care specialists in this State.
5 2. The program established pursuant to this section must
6 have as its goals, without limitation:
7 (a) To develop and provide crisis intervention services for
8 persons with dementia and their families and caregivers,
9 including, without limitation, services that provide an initial crisis
10 response, crisis stabilization and long-term care for persons with
11 dementia who exhibit behavioral problems.
12 (b) To proactively support persons with dementia and their
13 families and caregivers to ensure the highest quality of life
14 possible and to enable persons with dementia to remain in their
15 own homes or with family and avoid placement in a facility for
16 long-term care by, without limitation:
17 (1) Providing information, assistance, education and
18 support to persons with dementia and their families and
19 caregivers;
20 (2) Facilitating the development of communities in this
21 State where persons with dementia may remain safe and active;
-
*AB337*
– 3 –
1 (3) Providing training to governmental agencies and
2 nonprofit organizations that provide services to persons with
3 dementia; and
4 (4) Raising awareness of and developing community
5 support for persons with dementia and their families and
6 caregivers.
7 (c) To assist persons with dementia and their families and
8 caregivers with limited English proficiency by providing access to
9 the program through, without limitation, translation and
10 interpretation services.
11 3. Within the limits of legislative appropriations and any
12 other money available for this purpose, the Division shall:
13 (a) Prepare requests for proposals for the provision of services
14 by dementia care specialists employed by local governmental
15 agencies or nonprofit organizations to carry out the program
16 established pursuant to this section. The Division shall establish
17 such requirements for the requests for proposals as it determines
18 necessary.
19 (b) Partner or contract with an appropriate educational
20 organization to:
21 (1) Provide training to dementia care specialists.
22 (2) Evaluate the program established pursuant to this
23 section.
24 4. The Division may adopt regulations necessary to establish
25 and administer the program established pursuant to this section.
26 5. As used in this section, “limited English proficiency”
27 means a person who reads, writes or speaks a language other than
28 English and who cannot readily understand or communicate in
29 the English language in written or spoken form, as applicable,
30 based on the manner in which information is being
31 communicated.
32 Sec. 2. Chapter 439 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
33 thereto a new section to read as follows:
34 1. The University of Nevada, Las Vegas, School of Medicine
35 and the University of Nevada, Reno, School of Medicine, in
36 collaboration with the Department, may establish the Nevada
37 Memory Network in accordance with this section. If the Nevada
38 Memory Network is established, the University of Nevada, Las
39 Vegas, School of Medicine, the University of Nevada, Reno,
40 School of Medicine and the Department shall:
41 (a) Establish or contract with clinics that employ or contract
42 with physicians who specialize in neurology, neuropsychology and
43 geriatrics to:
44 (1) Train providers of primary care in screening for and
45 treating dementia;
-
*AB337*
– 4 –
1 (2) Diagnose dementia in patients referred to the clinics by
2 providers of primary care;
3 (3) Create plans of care for patients with dementia; and
4 (4) Use telehealth where necessary or convenient to carry
5 out the duties prescribed in subparagraphs (1), (2) and (3).
6 (b) Employ or contract with community health workers who
7 specialize in dementia care. The community health workers shall:
8 (1) Coordinate care and provide referrals to community-
9 based services and in-home care for patients diagnosed with
10 dementia pursuant to subparagraph (2) of paragraph (a); and
11 (2) Monitor the well-being of and provide support to
12 persons who provide care to patients diagnosed with dementia
13 pursuant to subparagraph (2) of paragraph (a), including, without
14 limitation, providers of respite care to such patients.
15 2. The University of Nevada, Las Vegas, School of Medicine,
16 the University of Nevada, Reno, School of Medicine and the
17 Department may collaborate or contract with any additional
18 persons or entities as necessary to carry out the provisions of this
19 section.
20 3. As used in this section:
21 (a) “Community health worker” has the meaning ascribed to it
22 in NRS 449.0027.
23 (b) “Telehealth” has the meaning ascribed to it in
24 NRS 629.515.
25 Sec. 3. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
26 approval.
27 2. Sections 1 and 2 of this act become effective:
28 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
29 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
30 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
31 (b) On July 1, 2025, for all other purposes.
