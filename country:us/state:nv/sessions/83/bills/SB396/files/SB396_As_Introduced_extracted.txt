EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring the Department of
Education to establish a program to provide certain
professional development relating to literacy instruction;
requiring the submission of certain reports concerning
literacy and the academic growth of pupils identified as
having a reading disability; prohibiting various classes
and services for pupils with certain disabilities from
exceeding certain size limitations; setting forth certain
provisions governing pupils with disabilities who require
educational stability; revising provisions governing the
safety of pupils in schools; revising certain definitions
concerning pupils with disabilities; revising provisions
governing instruction and services provided to certain
pupils with disabilities; revising provisions governing
early literacy screening assessments; revising provisions
governing pupils with dyslexia; revising provisions
governing due process hearings; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.

28 complaint relating to the identification of a pupil as a pupil with or without a
29 disability or the sufficiency of services provided to such a pupil. (20 U.S.C. § 1415)
30 Existing law: (1) provides for the selection of a hearing officer; (2) requires the
31 local educational agency or governing body of a charter school involved in the
32 complaint to pay the cost of the hearing; and (3) authorizes an aggrieved party to
33 appeal the decision of a hearing officer to the Department. (NRS 388.463) Section

35 process hearing; (2) requires the Department to appoint a State Review Officer to
36 make a determination concerning appeals submitted after a due process hearing has
37 concluded; and (3) provides for a party to a due process hearing to request a
38 temporary order of relief.
39 Existing law requires the board of trustees of each school district and the
40 governing body of each charter school that serves pupils in kindergarten or grade 1,

42 located in the school district or the charter school, respectively. (NRS 388.439)
43 Existing law additionally requires: (1) the board of trustees of each school district
44 and the governing body of each charter school to administer screenings for dyslexia
45 to certain pupils in certain grade levels; (2) the board of trustees and governing
46 body, as applicable, to address the needs of a pupil if the screening confirms that
47 the pupil has indicators for dyslexia; and (3) if the response to scientific, research-
48 based intervention system of instruction determines that a pupil needs additional
49 screening, the board of trustees and governing body, as applicable, to perform
50 certain evaluations of the pupil. (NRS 388.441) Section 10 of this bill: (1) requires
51 the board of trustees of each school district and the governing body of each charter

53 requirements; (2) requires such entities to administer the screening to pupils
54 enrolled in kindergarten and grades 1 to 6, inclusive, at least twice each school
55 year; and (3) sets forth certain requirements governing the results of the screening.
56 Section 11 sets forth the requirements for: (1) a pupil to receive additional
57 screening after taking the universal literacy screening; (2) the person who
58 administers the screening; and (3) certain meetings with administrators concerning
59 the results of the screening.

79 emergencies; and (2) a plan to inform local law enforcement agencies or public
80 safety organizations of the specific needs of each pupil with a disability.
81 Under existing law, the Legislature declares that members of the board of
82 trustees of each school district, the governing body of each charter school and all
83 administrators and teachers have a duty to create and provide a safe and respectful
84 learning environment for all pupils. (NRS 388.1321) Section 6 of this bill
85 additionally requires a safe and respectful learning environment to be provided for
86 all staff members of a school.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring the Department of
Education to establish a program to provide certain
professional development relating to literacy instruction;
requiring the submission of certain reports concerning
literacy and the academic growth of pupils identified as
having a reading disability; prohibiting various classes
and services for pupils with certain disabilities from
exceeding certain size limitations; setting forth certain
provisions governing pupils with disabilities who require
educational stability; revising provisions governing the
safety of pupils in schools; revising certain definitions
concerning pupils with disabilities; revising provisions
governing instruction and services provided to certain
pupils with disabilities; revising provisions governing
early literacy screening assessments; revising provisions
governing pupils with dyslexia; revising provisions
governing due process hearings; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
