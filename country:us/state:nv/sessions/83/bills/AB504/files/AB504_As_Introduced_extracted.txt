Section 1:
A.B. 504
MARCH 24, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Establishes certain deceptive trade practices relating
to the sale of or an offer to sell certain hemp
products. (BDR 52-867)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to deceptive trade practices; requiring a person
who does not hold a cannabis establishment license and
who sells or offers to sell certain hemp products to display
a sign containing certain information; prohibiting such a
person from making certain false, misleading or deceptive
statements in advertising; making it a deceptive trade
practice to violate those requirements or prohibitions; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law defines activities that constitute deceptive trade practices and
2 provides for the imposition of civil and criminal penalties against persons who
3 engage in deceptive trade practices. (Chapter 598 of NRS) This bill requires a
4 person who does not hold a cannabis establishment license and who sells or offers
5 to sell a hemp product which is intended for human consumption to display at all
6 times at the location at which the sale or offer is made a sign notifying customers
7 that the person does not hold a cannabis establishment license and the location is
8 not the premises of a cannabis establishment. This bill additionally prohibits such a
9 person from advertising or permitting to be advertised in any manner any false,
10 misleading or deceptive statement or representation with regard to: (1) whether the
11 person holds a cannabis establishment license or the location at which the sale or
12 offer is made is the premises of a cannabis establishment; or (2) the nature of any
13 hemp product which is intended for human consumption that is sold or offered for
14 sale by the person. Under this bill, any violation of the requirements or prohibitions
15 set forth in this bill constitutes a deceptive trade practice.
-
*AB504*
– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 598 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. A person who does not hold a cannabis establishment
4 license and who sells or offers to sell a hemp product which is
5 intended for human consumption:
6 (a) Shall display at all times in a conspicuous place at the
7 location at which the sale or offer is made a legible and
8 typewritten sign, in a type and font that is easy to read, which
9 notifies customers that the person does not hold a cannabis
10 establishment license and the location is not the premises of a
11 cannabis establishment; and
12 (b) Shall not advertise or permit to be advertised in any
13 manner any false, misleading or deceptive statement or
14 representation with regard to:
15 (1) Whether the person holds a cannabis establishment
16 license or the location at which the sale or offer is made is the
17 premises of a cannabis establishment; or
18 (2) The nature of any hemp product which is intended for
19 human consumption that is sold or offered for sale by the person.
20 2. The remedies, penalties, duties and prohibitions set forth in
21 this section are not exclusive and are in addition to any other
22 remedies, penalties, duties and prohibitions provided by law.
23 3. Any violation of this section constitutes a deceptive trade
24 practice for the purposes of NRS 598.0903 to 598.0999, inclusive.
25 4. As used in this section:
26 (a) “Approved hemp component” has the meaning ascribed to
27 it in NRS 446.844.
28 (b) “Cannabis establishment” has the meaning ascribed to it
29 in NRS 678A.095.
30 (c) “Cannabis establishment license” has the meaning
31 ascribed to “license” in NRS 678A.160.
32 (d) “Hemp” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 557.160.
33 (e) “Hemp product” means a commodity or product containing
34 hemp or any other commodity or product that purports to contain
35 cannabidiol. The term does not include a commodity or product
36 that contains only an approved hemp component and no other
37 hemp.
38 (f) “Intended for human consumption” has the meaning
39 ascribed to it in NRS 439.532.
