A.J.R. 10
ASSEMBLY JOINT RESOLUTION NO. 10–
ASSEMBLYMEMBER JAUREGUI
MARCH 4, 2025
____________
SUMMARY—Urges the Federal Government to release federally
managed land in Nevada for housing.
(BDR R-1097)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
ASSEMBLY JOINT RESOLUTION—Urging the Federal
Government to release federally managed land in Nevada
for housing.
1 WHEREAS, The State of Nevada is experiencing a significant
2 housing crisis characterized by a shortage of affordable housing and
3 insufficient amount of land for residential development; and
4 WHEREAS, Approximately 85 percent of land in Nevada is
5 owned and managed by the Federal Government with the United
6 States Bureau of Land Management overseeing the majority of such
7 land; and
8 WHEREAS, The limited availability of developable land is a key
9 factor in the future of affordable housing in Nevada in order to meet
10 the needs of the growing population of the State; and
11 WHEREAS, The State of Nevada has a history of bipartisan
12 efforts that sought to make certain lands available for housing while
13 protecting other public lands for future generations; and
14 WHEREAS, Releasing certain federally managed lands to the
15 State or local governments would allow for a flexible and targeted
16 approach to address the State’s long-term housing needs, economic
17 development and community planning initiatives; and
18 WHEREAS, The State of Nevada is committed to managing land
19 in a responsible and sustainable manner to balance economic
20 development, environmental stewardship and public access; and
-
*AJR10*
– 2 –
1 WHEREAS, Transferring ownership of certain federal lands to
2 the State or local governments for the purpose of addressing the
3 housing crisis would align with the goals of the Federal Government
4 to support local economic development and foster community
5 resilience; and
6 WHEREAS, Collaboration between federal, state and local
7 governments is essential to ensuring that land use decisions are
8 made in the best interest of the residents of Nevada while respecting
9 environmental and cultural considerations; now, therefore, be it
10 RESOLVED BY THE ASSEMBLY AND SENATE OF THE STATE OF
11 NEVADA, JOINTLY, That the members of the 83rd Session of the
12 Nevada Legislature hereby urge the President of the United States
13 and the Congress of the United States to prioritize the passage and
14 implementation of the Southern Nevada Economic Development
15 and Conservation Act which releases certain parcels of federally
16 managed land to the State of Nevada and the local governments of
17 the State for the purposes of addressing and stabilizing the State’s
18 housing crisis, while protecting public lands, conservation and
19 wildlife; and be it further
20 RESOLVED, That such transfers of land should be conducted in a
21 manner that ensures comprehensive planning and public input,
22 promotes sustainable development and protects the environmental
23 and cultural values of the affected areas; and be it further
24 RESOLVED, That the Chief Clerk of the Assembly prepare and
25 transmit a copy of this resolution to the President of the United
26 Sates, the Vice President of the United States as the presiding
27 officer of the United States Senate, the Speaker of the United States
28 House of Representatives and each member of the Nevada
29 Congressional Delegation; and be it further
30 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon
31 passage.
