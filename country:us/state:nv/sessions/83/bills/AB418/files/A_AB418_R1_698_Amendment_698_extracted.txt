SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to animals. (BDR 20-849)

Effect on the State: No.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§§ 1, 2)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to animals; requiring each governing body of a county or
incorporated city to adopt an ordinance requiring, with certain
exceptions, the owner or operator of a kennel, an animal rescue
organization that handles pets or a commercial establishment engaged
in the business of handling pets to complete a training course on
handling pets prescribed by the governing body and renew the training
biennially; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law grants a governing body of a county or incorporated city all powers
2 necessary and proper to address matters of local concern. (NRS 244.146, 268.0035) Sections
3 1 and 2 of this bill require each governing body of a county or incorporated city to adopt an
4 ordinance requiring, with certain exceptions, the owner or operator of a kennel, an animal
5 rescue organization that handles pets or a commercial establishment engaged in the business
6 of handling pets to complete a training course on handling pets prescribed by the governing
7 body. Sections 1 and 2 also require the ordinance adopted by the governing body of the
8 county or incorporated city to prescribe the manner in which a kennel, an animal rescue
9 organization that handles pets or a commercial establishment engaged in the business of
10 handling pets: (1) completes the required training course on handling pets; and (2) renews the
11 training biennially. Sections 1 and 2 define the term “pet” to mean a domestic cat or dog
12 commonly kept for pleasure.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 244 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 3, each board of county
4 commissioners shall adopt an ordinance requiring the owner or operator of a

1 kennel, an animal rescue organization that handles pets or a commercial
2 establishment engaged in the business of handling pets, including, without
3 limitation, a breeder, a cattery, a retailer, a pet groomer or a dealer, to complete a
4 training course on handling pets in a manner prescribed by the board of county
5 commissioners.
6 2. Each owner or operator who is required to complete a training course on
7 handling pets pursuant to subsection 1 shall renew the training biennially in a
8 manner prescribed by the board of county commissioners.
9 3. The ordinance adopted by a board of county commissioners pursuant to
10 subsection 1 does not apply to:
11 (a) A veterinarian;

13 the supervision of the veterinarian;
14 (c) An animal shelter; or
15 [(c)] (d) A nonprofit organization whose primary purpose is to shelter the
16 pets of victims of domestic violence.

29 bathes, brushes or clips a pet.
30 (j) “Retailer” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 574.320.
31 (k) “Veterinarian” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 574.330.

40 the city council or other governing body of the incorporated city.
