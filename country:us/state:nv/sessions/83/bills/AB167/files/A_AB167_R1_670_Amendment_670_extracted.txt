A.B. No. 167—Revises provisions relating to the use of corrective room restriction.

Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the use of corrective room restriction.
(BDR 5-769)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to juvenile justice; revising provisions related to the review of
corrective room restriction status for a child detained in a state, local or
regional facility for the detention of children; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) authorizes the use of corrective room restriction on a child who is
2 detained in a state, local or regional facility for the detention of children; and (2) specifies
3 certain actions that must be taken with respect to a child who is subjected to corrective room
4 restriction. Under existing law, a child who is subjected to corrective room restriction for
5 more than 24 hours must be provided a review of the corrective room restriction status at least
6 once every 24 hours. (NRS 62B.215, 63.505) Sections 1 and 2 of this bill require that the
7 review be conducted by a member of the staff of the facility and include [an assessment] a
8 review of whether a referral for a mental health screening, evaluation or treatment is
9 appropriate.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 62B.215 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 62B.215 1. A child who is detained in a local facility for the detention of
3 children or committed to a regional facility for the treatment and rehabilitation of
4 children may be subjected to corrective room restriction only if all other less-
5 restrictive options have been exhausted and only for the purpose of:
6 (a) Modifying the negative behavior of the child;
7 (b) Holding the child accountable for a violation of a rule of the facility; or
8 (c) Ensuring the safety of the child, staff or others or ensuring the security of
9 the facility.
10 2. Any action that results in corrective room restriction for more than 2 hours
11 must be documented in writing and approved by a supervisor.
12 3. A local facility for the detention of children or regional facility for the
13 treatment and rehabilitation of children shall conduct a safety and well-being check
Senate Amendment No. 670 to Assembly Bill No. 167 First Reprint Page 4
1 on a child subjected to corrective room restriction at least once every 10 minutes
2 while the child is subjected to corrective room restriction.
3 4. A child may be subjected to corrective room restriction only for the
4 minimum time required to address the negative behavior, rule violation or threat to
5 the safety of the child, staff or others or to the security of the facility, and the child
6 must be returned to the general population of the facility as soon as reasonably
7 possible.
8 5. A child who is subjected to corrective room restriction for more than 24
9 hours must be provided:
10 (a) Not less than 1 hour of out-of-room, large muscle exercise each day,
11 including, without limitation, access to outdoor recreation if weather permits . [;]
12 (b) Access to the same meals and medical and mental health treatment, the
13 same access to contact with parents or legal guardians, and the same access to legal
14 assistance and educational services as is provided to children in the general
15 population of the facility . [; and]
16 (c) A review of the corrective room restriction status by a member of the staff
17 of the facility at least once every 24 hours. Any such review must include, without
18 limitation, [an assessment] a review of whether a referral for a mental health
19 screening, evaluation or treatment is appropriate. If, upon review, the corrective
20 room restriction is continued, the continuation must be documented in writing,
21 including, without limitation, an explanation as to why no other less-restrictive
22 option is available.
23 6. A local facility for the detention of children or regional facility for the
24 treatment and rehabilitation of children shall not subject a child to corrective room
25 restriction for more than 72 consecutive hours.
26 7. Each local facility for the detention of children and regional facility for the
27 treatment and rehabilitation of children shall report monthly to the Juvenile Justice
28 Programs Office of the Division of Child and Family Services the number of
29 children who were subjected to corrective room restriction during that month and
30 the length of time that each child was in corrective room restriction. Any incident
31 that resulted in the use of corrective room restriction for 72 consecutive hours must
32 be addressed in the monthly report, and the report must include the reason or
33 reasons any attempt to return the child to the general population of the facility was
34 unsuccessful.
35 8. As used in this section, “corrective room restriction” means the
36 confinement of a child to his or her room as a disciplinary or protective action and
37 includes, without limitation:
38 (a) Administrative seclusion;
39 (b) Behavioral room confinement;
40 (c) Corrective room rest; and
41 (d) Room confinement.

48 (c) Ensuring the safety of the child, staff or others or ensuring the security of
49 the facility.

51 must be documented in writing and approved by a supervisor.
