FIRST REPRINT A.B. 167
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 167–ASSEMBLYMEMBER HANSEN
PREFILED JANUARY 31, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the use of corrective
room restriction. (BDR 5-769)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to juvenile justice; revising provisions related to
the review of corrective room restriction status for a child
detained in a state, local or regional facility for the
detention of children; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) authorizes the use of corrective room restriction on a child
2 who is detained in a state, local or regional facility for the detention of children;
3 and (2) specifies certain actions that must be taken with respect to a child who is
4 subjected to corrective room restriction. Under existing law, a child who is
5 subjected to corrective room restriction for more than 24 hours must be provided a
6 review of the corrective room restriction status at least once every 24 hours. (NRS
7 62B.215, 63.505) Sections 1 and 2 of this bill require that the review be conducted
8 by a member of the staff of the facility and include an assessment of whether a
9 referral for a mental health screening, evaluation or treatment is appropriate.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 62B.215 is hereby amended to read as
2 follows:
3 62B.215 1. A child who is detained in a local facility for the
4 detention of children or committed to a regional facility for the
5 treatment and rehabilitation of children may be subjected to
6 corrective room restriction only if all other less-restrictive options
7 have been exhausted and only for the purpose of:
8 (a) Modifying the negative behavior of the child;
-

– 2 –
1 (b) Holding the child accountable for a violation of a rule of the
2 facility; or
3 (c) Ensuring the safety of the child, staff or others or ensuring
4 the security of the facility.
5 2. Any action that results in corrective room restriction for
6 more than 2 hours must be documented in writing and approved by
7 a supervisor.
8 3. A local facility for the detention of children or regional
9 facility for the treatment and rehabilitation of children shall conduct
10 a safety and well-being check on a child subjected to corrective
11 room restriction at least once every 10 minutes while the child is
12 subjected to corrective room restriction.
13 4. A child may be subjected to corrective room restriction only
14 for the minimum time required to address the negative behavior,
15 rule violation or threat to the safety of the child, staff or others or to
16 the security of the facility, and the child must be returned to the
17 general population of the facility as soon as reasonably possible.
18 5. A child who is subjected to corrective room restriction for
19 more than 24 hours must be provided:
20 (a) Not less than 1 hour of out-of-room, large muscle exercise
21 each day, including, without limitation, access to outdoor recreation
22 if weather permits . [;]
23 (b) Access to the same meals and medical and mental health
24 treatment, the same access to contact with parents or legal
25 guardians, and the same access to legal assistance and educational
26 services as is provided to children in the general population of the
27 facility . [; and]
28 (c) A review of the corrective room restriction status by a
29 member of the staff of the facility at least once every 24 hours. Any
30 such review must include, without limitation, an assessment of
31 whether a referral for a mental health screening, evaluation or
32 treatment is appropriate. If, upon review, the corrective room
33 restriction is continued, the continuation must be documented in
34 writing, including, without limitation, an explanation as to why no
35 other less-restrictive option is available.
36 6. A local facility for the detention of children or regional
37 facility for the treatment and rehabilitation of children shall not
38 subject a child to corrective room restriction for more than 72
39 consecutive hours.
40 7. Each local facility for the detention of children and regional
41 facility for the treatment and rehabilitation of children shall report
42 monthly to the Juvenile Justice Programs Office of the Division of
43 Child and Family Services the number of children who were
44 subjected to corrective room restriction during that month and the
45 length of time that each child was in corrective room restriction.
