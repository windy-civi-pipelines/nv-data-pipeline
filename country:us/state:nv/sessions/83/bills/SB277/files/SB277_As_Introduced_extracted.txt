SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to education.

Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring each public school to
employ a school social worker to the extent that money is
available for that purpose; requiring the Commission on
Professional Standards in Education to adopt regulations
relating to the licensure of school counselors and school
social workers; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes various requirements governing educational personnel,
2 including requirements governing the licensing, qualifications, employment,
3 powers, duties, supervision and evaluation of such personnel. (Chapter 391 of
4 NRS) Under existing law, the Commission on Professional Standards in Education
5 is required to adopt regulations relating to the qualifications for licensure of
6 teachers and other educational personnel, including: (1) regulations relating to the
7 qualifications for licensure of school counselors and school social workers; and (2)
8 regulations that allow a person who is currently employed by a public school in a
9 position to provide support or other services relating to school psychology to
10 simultaneously complete a program of internship in psychology. (NRS 391.019,
11 391.0349) Section 2 of this bill requires the Commission to adopt regulations that
12 allow a person who is employed by a public school in a position relating to school
13 counseling or school social work, as applicable, and is enrolled in a program that
14 would allow the person to obtain a license or endorsement as a school counselor or
15 school social worker, as applicable, to simultaneously complete any required
16 internship or practicum hours for the purpose of obtaining such a license or
17 endorsement.
-
*SB277*
– 2 –
18 Existing law establishes the powers and duties of a school counselor and school
19 social worker. (NRS 391.293, 391.296) Existing law requires each public school,
20 including each charter school, to the extent that money is available for that purpose,
21 to employ a school counselor at the school on a full-time basis. (NRS 388.055)
22 Section 1 of this bill additionally requires each public school, to the extent that
23 money is available for that purpose, to employ a school social worker at the school
24 on a full-time basis. Section 1 also prohibits, insofar as is practicable, each public
25 school from entering into a contract with an independent contractor for the
26 provision of the services that a school social worker is authorized to perform.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 Each public school, including, without limitation, each charter
4 school:
5 1. Shall, to the extent that money is available for that
6 purpose, employ a school social worker at the school on a full-time
7 basis.
8 2. Shall not, insofar as is practicable, enter into a contract
9 with an independent contractor for the provision of the services
10 that a school social worker is authorized to perform pursuant to
11 NRS 391.296.
12 Sec. 2. NRS 391.019 is hereby amended to read as follows:
13 391.019 1. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.027,
14 the Commission shall adopt regulations:
15 (a) Prescribing the qualifications for licensing teachers and other
16 educational personnel and the procedures for the issuance and
17 renewal of those licenses. The regulations:
18 (1) Must include, without limitation, the qualifications for
19 licensing teachers and administrators pursuant to an alternative route
20 to licensure which provides that the required education and training
21 may be provided by any qualified provider which has been approved
22 by the Commission, including, without limitation, institutions of
23 higher education and other providers that operate independently of
24 an institution of higher education. The regulations adopted pursuant
25 to this subparagraph must:
26 (I) Establish the requirements for approval as a qualified
27 provider;
28 (II) Require a qualified provider to be selective in its
29 acceptance of students;
30 (III) Require a qualified provider to provide in-person or
31 virtual supervised, school-based experiences and ongoing support
32 for its students, such as mentoring and coaching;
-
*SB277*
– 3 –
1 (IV) Significantly limit the amount of course work
2 required or provide for the waiver of required course work for
3 students who achieve certain scores on tests;
4 (V) Allow for the completion in 2 years or less of the
5 education and training required under the alternative route to
6 licensure;
7 (VI) Provide that a person who has completed the
8 education and training required under the alternative route to
9 licensure and who has satisfied all other requirements for licensure
10 may apply for a regular license pursuant to sub-subparagraph (VII)
11 regardless of whether the person has received an offer of
12 employment from a school district, charter school or private school;
13 and
14 (VII) Upon the completion by a person of the education
15 and training required under the alternative route to licensure and the
16 satisfaction of all other requirements for licensure, provide for the
17 issuance of a regular license to the person pursuant to the provisions
18 of this chapter and the regulations adopted pursuant to this chapter.
19 (2) Must require an applicant for a license to teach middle
20 school or junior high school education or secondary education to
21 demonstrate proficiency in a field of specialization or area of
22 concentration by successfully completing course work prescribed by
23 the Department or completing a subject matter competency
24 examination prescribed by the Department with a score deemed
25 satisfactory.
26 (3) Must not prescribe qualifications which are more
27 stringent than the qualifications set forth in NRS 391.0315 for a
28 licensed teacher who applies for an additional license in accordance
29 with that section.
30 (b) Identifying fields of specialization in teaching which require
31 the specialized training of teachers.
32 (c) Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.125, requiring
33 teachers to obtain from the Department an endorsement in a field of
34 specialization to be eligible to teach in that field of specialization.
35 (d) Setting forth the educational requirements a teacher must
36 satisfy to qualify for an endorsement in each field of specialization.
37 (e) Setting forth the qualifications and requirements for
38 obtaining a license or endorsement to teach American Sign
39 Language, including, without limitation, being registered with the
40 Aging and Disability Services Division of the Department of Health
41 and Human Services pursuant to NRS 656A.100 to engage in the
42 practice of sign language interpreting in a primary or secondary
43 educational setting.
44 (f) Requiring teachers and other educational personnel to be
45 registered with the Aging and Disability Services Division pursuant
-
*SB277*
– 4 –
1 to NRS 656A.100 to engage in the practice of sign language
2 interpreting in a primary or secondary educational setting if they:
3 (1) Provide instruction or other educational services; and
4 (2) Concurrently engage in the practice of sign language
5 interpreting, as defined in NRS 656A.060.
6 (g) Prescribing course work on parental involvement and family
7 engagement. The Commission shall:
8 (1) Work in cooperation with the Office of Parental
9 Involvement and Family Engagement created by NRS 385.630 in
10 developing the regulations required by this paragraph.
11 (2) Establish standards for professional development training
12 which may be used to satisfy any course work requirement
13 prescribed pursuant to this paragraph.
14 (h) Establishing the requirements for obtaining an endorsement
15 on the license of a teacher, administrator or other educational
16 personnel in cultural competency.
17 (i) Authorizing the Superintendent of Public Instruction to issue
18 a license by endorsement to an applicant who holds an equivalent
19 license or authorization issued by a governmental entity in another
20 country if the Superintendent determines that the qualifications for
21 the equivalent license or authorization are substantially similar to
22 those prescribed pursuant to paragraph (a).
23 (j) Establishing the requirements for obtaining an endorsement
24 on the license of a teacher, administrator or other educational
25 personnel in teaching courses relating to financial literacy.
26 (k) Authorizing a person who is employed as a paraprofessional
27 and enrolled in a program to become a teacher to complete an
28 accelerated program of student teaching in the same or a
29 substantially similar area in which the person is employed as a
30 paraprofessional while remaining employed as a paraprofessional.
31 (l) Requiring the Department to accept a program of student
32 teaching or other teaching experience completed in another state or
33 foreign country by an applicant for a license if the Department
34 determines that the program or experience substantially fulfills the
35 standards of a program of student teaching in this State.
36 (m) Authorizing a person who is employed by a public school in
37 a position to provide support or other services relating to [school] :
38 (1) School psychology, if the person does not hold a license
39 or endorsement as a school psychologist but is enrolled in a program
40 that would allow the person to obtain such a license or endorsement,
41 to complete a program of internship in school psychology while
42 remaining employed in such a position.
43 (2) School counseling, if the person does not hold a license
44 or endorsement as a school counselor but is enrolled in a program
45 that would allow the person to obtain such a license or
-
*SB277*
– 5 –
1 endorsement, to complete any required internship or practicum
2 hours for the purpose of obtaining a license or endorsement as a
3 school counselor while remaining employed in such a position.
4 (3) School social work, if the person does not hold a license
5 or endorsement as a school social worker but is enrolled in a
6 program that would allow the person to obtain such a license or
7 endorsement, to complete any required internship or practicum
8 hours for the purpose of obtaining a license or endorsement as a
9 school social worker while remaining employed in such a position.
10 (n) To carry out the provisions of NRS 391B.010.
11 2. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.027, the
12 Commission may adopt such other regulations as it deems necessary
13 for its own government or to carry out its duties.
14 3. Any regulation which increases the amount of education,
15 training or experience required for licensing:
16 (a) Must, in addition to the requirements for publication in
17 chapter 233B of NRS, be publicized before its adoption in a manner
18 reasonably calculated to inform those persons affected by the
19 change.
20 (b) Must not become effective until at least 1 year after the date
21 it is adopted by the Commission.
22 (c) Is not applicable to a license in effect on the date the
23 regulation becomes effective.
