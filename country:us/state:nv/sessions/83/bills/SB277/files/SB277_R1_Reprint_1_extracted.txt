Section 1:
EXEMPT
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 277
SENATE BILL NO. 277–SENATORS CRUZ-CRAWFORD AND DOÑATE
MARCH 5, 2025
____________
JOINT SPONSORS: ASSEMBLYMEMBERS
JACKSON; ANDERSON AND D’SILVA
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to education.
(BDR 34-786)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring certain public schools to
employ a school social worker to the extent that money is
available for that purpose; requiring the Commission on
Professional Standards in Education to adopt regulations
relating to the licensure of school psychologists, school
counselors and school social workers; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes various requirements governing educational personnel,
2 including requirements governing the licensing, qualifications, employment,
3 powers, duties, supervision and evaluation of such personnel. (Chapter 391 of
4 NRS) Under existing law, the Commission on Professional Standards in Education
5 is required to adopt regulations relating to the qualifications for licensure of
6 teachers and other educational personnel, including: (1) regulations relating to the
7 qualifications for licensure of school counselors and school social workers; and (2)
8 regulations that allow a person who is currently employed by a public school in a
9 position to provide support or other services relating to school psychology to
10 simultaneously complete a program of internship in psychology. (NRS 391.019,
11 391.0349) Section 2 of this bill requires the Commission to adopt regulations that
12 allow a person who is employed by a public school in a position relating to school
13 counseling or school social work, as applicable, and is enrolled in a program that
14 would allow the person to obtain a license or endorsement as a school counselor or
15 school social worker, as applicable, to simultaneously complete any required
16 internship or practicum hours for the purpose of obtaining such a license or
17 endorsement. Section 2 further requires the Commission to adopt regulations that
-
*SB277_R1*
– 2 –
18 allow a person who is employed by a public school in a position relating to school
19 psychology and is enrolled in a program that would allow the person to obtain a
20 license or endorsement as a school psychologist to simultaneously complete any
21 required practicum hours.
22 Existing law establishes the powers and duties of a school counselor and school
23 social worker. (NRS 391.293, 391.296) Existing law requires each public school,
24 including each charter school, to the extent that money is available for that purpose,
25 to employ a school counselor at the school on a full-time basis. (NRS 388.055)
26 Section 1 of this bill additionally requires each public school in a county whose
27 population is 700,000 or more (currently only Clark County), to the extent that
28 money is available for that purpose, to employ a school social worker at the school
29 on a full-time basis. Section 1 also prohibits, insofar as is practicable, each public
30 school from entering into a contract with an independent contractor for the
31 provision of the services that a school social worker is authorized to perform.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 Each public school, including, without limitation, each charter
4 school, in a county whose population is 700,000 or more:
5 1. Shall, to the extent that money is available for that
6 purpose, employ a school social worker at the school on a full-time
7 basis.
8 2. Shall not, insofar as is practicable, enter into a contract
9 with an independent contractor for the provision of the services
10 that a school social worker is authorized to perform pursuant to
11 NRS 391.296.
12 Sec. 2. NRS 391.019 is hereby amended to read as follows:
13 391.019 1. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.027,
14 the Commission shall adopt regulations:
15 (a) Prescribing the qualifications for licensing teachers and other
16 educational personnel and the procedures for the issuance and
17 renewal of those licenses. The regulations:
18 (1) Must include, without limitation, the qualifications for
19 licensing teachers and administrators pursuant to an alternative route
20 to licensure which provides that the required education and training
21 may be provided by any qualified provider which has been approved
22 by the Commission, including, without limitation, institutions of
23 higher education and other providers that operate independently of
24 an institution of higher education. The regulations adopted pursuant
25 to this subparagraph must:
26 (I) Establish the requirements for approval as a qualified
27 provider;
28 (II) Require a qualified provider to be selective in its
29 acceptance of students;
-
*SB277_R1*
– 3 –
1 (III) Require a qualified provider to provide in-person or
2 virtual supervised, school-based experiences and ongoing support
3 for its students, such as mentoring and coaching;
4 (IV) Significantly limit the amount of course work
5 required or provide for the waiver of required course work for
6 students who achieve certain scores on tests;
7 (V) Allow for the completion in 2 years or less of the
8 education and training required under the alternative route to
9 licensure;
10 (VI) Provide that a person who has completed the
11 education and training required under the alternative route to
12 licensure and who has satisfied all other requirements for licensure
13 may apply for a regular license pursuant to sub-subparagraph (VII)
14 regardless of whether the person has received an offer of
15 employment from a school district, charter school or private school;
16 and
17 (VII) Upon the completion by a person of the education
18 and training required under the alternative route to licensure and the
19 satisfaction of all other requirements for licensure, provide for the
20 issuance of a regular license to the person pursuant to the provisions
21 of this chapter and the regulations adopted pursuant to this chapter.
22 (2) Must require an applicant for a license to teach middle
23 school or junior high school education or secondary education to
24 demonstrate proficiency in a field of specialization or area of
25 concentration by successfully completing course work prescribed by
26 the Department or completing a subject matter competency
27 examination prescribed by the Department with a score deemed
28 satisfactory.
29 (3) Must not prescribe qualifications which are more
30 stringent than the qualifications set forth in NRS 391.0315 for a
31 licensed teacher who applies for an additional license in accordance
32 with that section.
33 (b) Identifying fields of specialization in teaching which require
34 the specialized training of teachers.
35 (c) Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.125, requiring
36 teachers to obtain from the Department an endorsement in a field of
37 specialization to be eligible to teach in that field of specialization.
38 (d) Setting forth the educational requirements a teacher must
39 satisfy to qualify for an endorsement in each field of specialization.
40 (e) Setting forth the qualifications and requirements for
41 obtaining a license or endorsement to teach American Sign
42 Language, including, without limitation, being registered with the
43 Aging and Disability Services Division of the Department of Health
44 and Human Services pursuant to NRS 656A.100 to engage in the
-
*SB277_R1*
– 4 –
1 practice of sign language interpreting in a primary or secondary
2 educational setting.
3 (f) Requiring teachers and other educational personnel to be
4 registered with the Aging and Disability Services Division pursuant
5 to NRS 656A.100 to engage in the practice of sign language
6 interpreting in a primary or secondary educational setting if they:
7 (1) Provide instruction or other educational services; and
8 (2) Concurrently engage in the practice of sign language
9 interpreting, as defined in NRS 656A.060.
10 (g) Prescribing course work on parental involvement and family
11 engagement. The Commission shall:
12 (1) Work in cooperation with the Office of Parental
13 Involvement and Family Engagement created by NRS 385.630 in
14 developing the regulations required by this paragraph.
15 (2) Establish standards for professional development training
16 which may be used to satisfy any course work requirement
17 prescribed pursuant to this paragraph.
18 (h) Establishing the requirements for obtaining an endorsement
19 on the license of a teacher, administrator or other educational
20 personnel in cultural competency.
21 (i) Authorizing the Superintendent of Public Instruction to issue
22 a license by endorsement to an applicant who holds an equivalent
23 license or authorization issued by a governmental entity in another
24 country if the Superintendent determines that the qualifications for
25 the equivalent license or authorization are substantially similar to
26 those prescribed pursuant to paragraph (a).
27 (j) Establishing the requirements for obtaining an endorsement
28 on the license of a teacher, administrator or other educational
29 personnel in teaching courses relating to financial literacy.
30 (k) Authorizing a person who is employed as a paraprofessional
31 and enrolled in a program to become a teacher to complete an
32 accelerated program of student teaching in the same or a
33 substantially similar area in which the person is employed as a
34 paraprofessional while remaining employed as a paraprofessional.
35 (l) Requiring the Department to accept a program of student
36 teaching or other teaching experience completed in another state or
37 foreign country by an applicant for a license if the Department
38 determines that the program or experience substantially fulfills the
39 standards of a program of student teaching in this State.
40 (m) Authorizing a person who is employed by a public school in
41 a position to provide support or other services relating to [school] :
42 (1) School psychology, if the person does not hold a license
43 or endorsement as a school psychologist but is enrolled in a program
44 that would allow the person to obtain such a license or endorsement,
45 to complete a program of internship in school psychology or any
-
*SB277_R1*
– 5 –
1 required practicum hours for the purpose of obtaining a license or
2 endorsement as a school psychologist while remaining employed in
3 such a position.
4 (2) School counseling, if the person does not hold a license
5 or endorsement as a school counselor but is enrolled in a program
6 that would allow the person to obtain such a license or
7 endorsement, to complete any required internship or practicum
8 hours for the purpose of obtaining a license or endorsement as a
9 school counselor while remaining employed in such a position.
10 (3) School social work, if the person does not hold a license
11 or endorsement as a school social worker but is enrolled in a
12 program that would allow the person to obtain such a license or
13 endorsement, to complete any required internship or practicum
14 hours for the purpose of obtaining a license or endorsement as a
15 school social worker while remaining employed in such a position.
16 (n) To carry out the provisions of NRS 391B.010.
17 2. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 391.027, the
18 Commission may adopt such other regulations as it deems necessary
19 for its own government or to carry out its duties.
20 3. Any regulation which increases the amount of education,
21 training or experience required for licensing:
22 (a) Must, in addition to the requirements for publication in
23 chapter 233B of NRS, be publicized before its adoption in a manner
24 reasonably calculated to inform those persons affected by the
25 change.
26 (b) Must not become effective until at least 1 year after the date
27 it is adopted by the Commission.
28 (c) Is not applicable to a license in effect on the date the
29 regulation becomes effective.
