SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to certain licenses issued by the Division

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; revising the expiration date of certain licenses
issued by the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the
Department of Health and Human Services; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires a medical facility or a facility for the dependent to obtain a license
2 from the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and Human
3 Services. (NRS 449.030) Under existing law, each such license expires on December 31 of
4 each year and may be renewed annually. (NRS 449.089) Section 1 of this bill instead provides
5 that any such license: (1) expires 1 year after the date on which the license is issued, rather
6 than on December 31; and (2) may be renewed annually. [Section 2 of this bill provides that a
7 license which is valid on December 30, 2025, remains valid until the anniversary date in 2026
8 of the initial issuance of the license, at which time the license may be renewed in accordance
9 with section 1. Section 2 also requires the Division, upon the request of a licensee, to assist the
10 licensee in determining the anniversary date in 2026 of the initial issuance of the license
11 issued by the Division.]
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 449.089 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 449.089 1. Each license issued pursuant to NRS 449.029 to 449.2428,
3 inclusive, expires 1 year after the date on [December 31 following its issuance and
4 is renewable for 1 year] which it was issued. Such a license may be renewed
5 annually upon reapplication and payment of all fees required pursuant to
6 subsection 4 and NRS 449.050, as applicable, unless the Division finds, after an
7 investigation, that the facility has not:
Assembly Amendment No. 795 to Assembly Bill No. 544 Page 4
1 (a) Satisfactorily complied with the provisions of NRS 449.029 to 449.2428,
2 inclusive, or the standards and regulations adopted by the Board;
3 (b) Obtained the approval of the Director of the Department of Health and
4 Human Services before undertaking a project, if such approval is required by NRS
5 439A.100 or 439A.102; or
6 (c) Conformed to all applicable local zoning regulations.
7 2. Each reapplication for an agency to provide personal care services in the
8 home, an agency to provide nursing in the home, a community health worker pool,
9 a facility for intermediate care, a facility for skilled nursing, a provider of
10 community-based living arrangement services, a hospital described in 42 U.S.C. §
11 1395ww(d)(1)(B)(iv), a psychiatric hospital that provides inpatient services to
12 children, a psychiatric residential treatment facility, a residential facility for groups,
13 a program of hospice care, a home for individual residential care, a facility for the
14 care of adults during the day, a facility for hospice care, a nursing pool, the distinct
15 part of a hospital which meets the requirements of a skilled nursing facility or
16 nursing facility pursuant to 42 C.F.R. § 483.5, a hospital that provides swing-bed
17 services as described in 42 C.F.R. § 482.58 or, if residential services are provided
18 to children, a medical facility or facility for the treatment of alcohol or other
19 substance use disorders must include, without limitation, a statement that the
20 facility, hospital, agency, program, pool or home is in compliance with the
21 provisions of NRS 449.115 to 449.125, inclusive, and 449.174.
22 3. Each reapplication for an agency to provide personal care services in the
23 home, a community health worker pool, a facility for intermediate care, a facility
24 for skilled nursing, a facility for the care of adults during the day, a residential
25 facility for groups or a home for individual residential care must include, without
26 limitation, a statement that the holder of the license to operate, and the
27 administrator or other person in charge and employees of, the facility, agency, pool
28 or home are in compliance with the provisions of NRS 449.093.
29 4. Each reapplication for a surgical center for ambulatory patients, facility for
30 the treatment of irreversible renal disease, facility for hospice care, program of
31 hospice care, hospital, facility for intermediate care, facility for skilled nursing,
32 agency to provide personal care services in the home or rural clinic must be
33 accompanied by the fee prescribed by the State Board of Health pursuant to NRS
34 457.240, in addition to the fees imposed pursuant to NRS 449.050.
35 Sec. 2. [1. Any valid license issued pursuant to NRS 449.029 to 449.2428,
36 inclusive, which is held by a licensee on December 30, 2025, remains valid until
37 the anniversary date in 2026 of the initial issuance of the license. Such a license
38 may be renewed in 2026 in accordance with NRS 449.089, as amended by section 1
39 of this act.
40 2. Upon request, the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the
41 Department of Health and Human Services shall assist a medical facility or a
42 facility for the dependent in determining the anniversary date in 2026 on which the
43 license issued to the medical facility or facility for the dependent expires pursuant
44 to subsection 1.
45 3. As used in this section:
46 (a) “Facility for the dependent” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS
47 449.0045.
48 (b) “Medical facility” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 449.029.]
49 (Deleted by amendment.)
50 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on [December 30, 2025.] January 1,
51 2026.
