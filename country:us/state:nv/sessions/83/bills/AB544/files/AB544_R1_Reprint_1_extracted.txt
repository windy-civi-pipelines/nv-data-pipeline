FIRST REPRINT A.B. 544
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 544–COMMITTEE ON WAYS AND MEANS
(ON BEHALF OF THE OFFICE OF FINANCE
IN THE OFFICE OF THE GOVERNOR)
MARCH 31, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Ways and Means
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to certain licenses issued
by the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of
the Department of Health and Human Services.
(BDR 40-1118)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; revising the expiration date of
certain licenses issued by the Division of Public and
Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and
Human Services; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires a medical facility or a facility for the dependent to obtain
2 a license from the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the Department of
3 Health and Human Services. (NRS 449.030) Under existing law, each such license
4 expires on December 31 of each year and may be renewed annually. (NRS
5 449.089) Section 1 of this bill instead provides that any such license: (1) expires 1
6 year after the date on which the license is issued, rather than on December 31; and
7 (2) may be renewed annually.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
8 Section 1. NRS 449.089 is hereby amended to read as follows:
9 449.089 1. Each license issued pursuant to NRS 449.029 to
10 449.2428, inclusive, expires 1 year after the date on [December 31
11 following its issuance and is renewable for 1 year] which it was
-

– 2 –
1 issued. Such a license may be renewed annually upon reapplication
2 and payment of all fees required pursuant to subsection 4 and NRS
3 449.050, as applicable, unless the Division finds, after an
4 investigation, that the facility has not:
5 (a) Satisfactorily complied with the provisions of NRS 449.029
6 to 449.2428, inclusive, or the standards and regulations adopted by
7 the Board;
8 (b) Obtained the approval of the Director of the Department of
9 Health and Human Services before undertaking a project, if such
10 approval is required by NRS 439A.100 or 439A.102; or
11 (c) Conformed to all applicable local zoning regulations.
12 2. Each reapplication for an agency to provide personal care
13 services in the home, an agency to provide nursing in the home, a
14 community health worker pool, a facility for intermediate care, a
15 facility for skilled nursing, a provider of community-based living
16 arrangement services, a hospital described in 42 U.S.C. §
17 1395ww(d)(1)(B)(iv), a psychiatric hospital that provides inpatient
18 services to children, a psychiatric residential treatment facility, a
19 residential facility for groups, a program of hospice care, a home for
20 individual residential care, a facility for the care of adults during the
21 day, a facility for hospice care, a nursing pool, the distinct part of a
22 hospital which meets the requirements of a skilled nursing facility or
23 nursing facility pursuant to 42 C.F.R. § 483.5, a hospital that
24 provides swing-bed services as described in 42 C.F.R. § 482.58 or,
25 if residential services are provided to children, a medical facility or
26 facility for the treatment of alcohol or other substance use disorders
27 must include, without limitation, a statement that the facility,
28 hospital, agency, program, pool or home is in compliance with the
29 provisions of NRS 449.115 to 449.125, inclusive, and 449.174.
30 3. Each reapplication for an agency to provide personal care
31 services in the home, a community health worker pool, a facility for
32 intermediate care, a facility for skilled nursing, a facility for the care
33 of adults during the day, a residential facility for groups or a home
34 for individual residential care must include, without limitation, a
35 statement that the holder of the license to operate, and the
36 administrator or other person in charge and employees of, the
37 facility, agency, pool or home are in compliance with the provisions
38 of NRS 449.093.
39 4. Each reapplication for a surgical center for ambulatory
40 patients, facility for the treatment of irreversible renal disease,
41 facility for hospice care, program of hospice care, hospital, facility
42 for intermediate care, facility for skilled nursing, agency to provide
43 personal care services in the home or rural clinic must be
44 accompanied by the fee prescribed by the State Board of Health

1 pursuant to NRS 457.240, in addition to the fees imposed pursuant
2 to NRS 449.050.
3 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
4 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on January 1, 2026.
