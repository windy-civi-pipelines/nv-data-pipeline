EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to homelessness; requiring the board of county commissioners of
certain counties to [establish] notify the Department of
Transportation before conducting a clean-up of an encampment of
homeless persons near certain rights-of-way; authorizing the
Department to consult with a homeless outreach [teams ; establishing
the duties of each] team and participate in the clean-up of an
encampment of homeless persons conducted by the homeless
outreach team; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 [Existing law authorizes a board of county commissioners, with certain exceptions, to
2 exercise all powers: (1) expressly granted to the board; (2) necessarily or fairly implied in or
3 incident to the powers expressly granted to the board; and (3) necessary or proper to address
4 any matter that primarily affects matters of local concern for the effective operation of the
5 county government. (NRS 244.146) Under existing law, a board of county commissioners
6 may by ordinance create a regional transportation commission if a streets and highway plan
7 has been adopted as a part of the master plan by the county or regional planning commission.
8 (NRS 277A.170) The Regional Transportation Commission Act establishes various duties and
9 general and special powers relating to a commission, including authorizing a commission to
10 exercise powers as are necessary. (NRS 277A.200-277A.380) Existing law establishes the
11 Nevada Interagency Advisory Council on Homelessness to Housing and requires the Council,
12 among certain other duties, to: (1) collaborate with state and local agencies on their responses
13 to homelessness and promote cooperation among federal, state and local agencies to address
14 homelessness; and (2) develop a strategic plan for addressing homelessness in this State.
15 (NRS 232.4983)
16 This] Section 1 of this bill requires the board of county commissioners of each county
17 whose population is 700,000 or more (currently only Clark County) to [establish homeless
18 outreach teams to provide support to] notify the Department of Transportation before
19 conducting a clean-up of an encampment of homeless persons near [schools, parks and
20 senior housing in areas of the county in which
Assembly Amendment No. 74 to Assembly Bill No. 263 Page 4
21 pervasive poverty, unemployment or economic distress exist. This bill also requires each] a
22 right-of-way that is controlled by the Department. Section 3 of this bill authorizes the
23 Department to: (1) consult with a homeless outreach team ; [to develop a plan in
24 collaboration with] and [request assistance from the regional transportation commission for
25 outreach and clean-ups related to] (2) approve and otherwise participate in the clean-up of
26 an encampment of homeless persons [for public highways or public rights-of-way located in
27 areas of the county in which pervasive poverty, unemployment or economic distress exist.
28 Finally, this bill requires each] that is conducted by the homeless outreach team [to
29 collaborate and consult with the Nevada Interagency Advisory Council on Homelessness to
30 Housing.] in a right-of-way.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 244 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. The board of county commissioners of each county whose population is
4 700,000 or more shall [establish homeless outreach teams.
5 2. Each homeless outreach team shall:
6 (a) Provide support to] notify the Department of Transportation before
7 conducting a clean-up of an encampment of homeless persons near [schools,
8 parks and senior housing in areas of the county in which pervasive poverty,
9 unemployment or economic distress exist.
10 (b) Develop a plan in collaboration with and request assistance from the
11 regional transportation commission for outreach and clean-ups related to
12 homeless persons for public highways or public rights-of-way located in areas of
13 the county in which pervasive poverty, unemployment or economic distress exist.
14 (c) Collaborate and consult with the Nevada Interagency Advisory Council
15 on Homelessness to Housing created by NRS 232.4981.] a right-of-way that is
16 controlled by the Department of Transportation.
17 [3.] 2. As used in this section, [“public highway”] “right-of-way” has the
18 meaning ascribed to it in NRS [277A.110.] 408.55014.
19 Sec. 2. [The provisions of NRS 354.599 do not apply to any additional
20 expenses of a local government that are related to the provisions of this act.]
21 (Deleted by amendment.)
22 Sec. 3. Chapter 408 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
23 section to read as follows:
24 1. The Department may:
25 (a) Consult with a homeless outreach team; and
26 (b) Approve and otherwise participate in the clean-up of an encampment of
27 homeless persons that is conducted by the homeless outreach team in a right-of-
28 way, as defined in NRS 408.55014.
29 2. As used in this section, “homeless outreach team” means a homeless
30 outreach team established or designated by a board of county commissioners for
31 the purposes of providing outreach and clean-ups related to homeless persons.
