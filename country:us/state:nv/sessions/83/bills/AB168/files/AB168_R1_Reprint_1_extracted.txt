FIRST REPRINT A.B. 168
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 168–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS GOULDING;
BROWN-MAY, DICKMAN AND WATTS
PREFILED JANUARY 31, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Growth and Infrastructure
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to bicycles.
(BDR 43-598)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public safety; revising provisions governing the
operation of a bicycle on a roadway; providing a civil
penalty; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law grants certain rights to a person operating a bicycle upon a
2 roadway and subjects any such person to certain duties and responsibilities while
3 operating the bicycle on a roadway. (NRS 484B.760-484B.785) Section 1 of this
4 bill: (1) requires a person operating a bicycle to stop in obedience to a stop sign or
5 signal displaying a steady red light; and (2) creates an exception to the requirement
6 to stop in obedience to a stop sign if the person can safely proceed through the
7 intersection without stopping and certain other requirements are met. Section 1 also
8 authorizes a person operating a bicycle to proceed cautiously through an
9 intersection where the signal is a steady red signal or make certain turns if the
10 person stops before entering the intersection and certain other requirements are met.
11 Section 2 of this bill provides that a violation of the provisions of section 1 is a
12 civil infraction. Section 3 of this bill makes a conforming change to indicate the
13 applicability of the requirements prescribed by section 1.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 484B of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 2, a person
4 operating a bicycle upon a roadway shall stop in obedience to a
5 stop sign or traffic-control signal displaying a steady red signal.
6 2. A person operating a bicycle approaching an intersection
7 controlled by a stop sign may proceed cautiously through the
8 intersection without stopping if the person:
9 (a) Ascertains that such movement can be made safely;
10 (b) Decreases the speed of the bicycle and confirms that the
11 intersection is clear before entering the intersection; and
12 (c) Yields the right-of-way to pedestrians and all vehicles
13 whose proximity constitutes an immediate hazard.
14 3. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 4, a person
15 operating a bicycle upon a roadway may proceed cautiously
16 through an intersection where the signal is a steady red signal or
17 make a turn through any such intersection if the person:
18 (a) Stops before entering the intersection as required by
19 subsection 1;
20 (b) Ascertains that such movement can be made safely; and
21 (c) Yields the right-of-way to pedestrians and all vehicles
22 whose proximity constitutes an immediate hazard.
23 4. A person operating a bicycle upon a roadway shall not turn
24 left through an intersection where the signal is a steady red signal
25 unless the roadway onto which the person is turning is a one-way
26 roadway.
27 Sec. 2. NRS 484B.760 is hereby amended to read as follows:
28 484B.760 1. It is a civil infraction punishable pursuant to
29 NRS 484A.703 to 484A.705, inclusive, for any person to do any act
30 forbidden or fail to perform any act required in NRS 484B.768 to
31 484B.790, inclusive [.] , and section 1 of this act.
32 2. The parent of any child and the guardian of any ward shall
33 not authorize or knowingly permit the child or ward to violate any
34 of the provisions of chapters 484A to 484E, inclusive, of NRS.
35 3. The provisions applicable to bicycles, electric bicycles and
36 electric scooters apply whenever a bicycle, an electric bicycle or an
37 electric scooter is operated upon any highway or upon any path set
38 aside for the exclusive use of bicycles, electric bicycles and electric
39 scooters subject to those exceptions stated herein.
40 Sec. 3. NRS 484B.763 is hereby amended to read as follows:
41 484B.763 Every person riding a bicycle, an electric bicycle or
42 an electric scooter upon a roadway has all of the rights and is subject
-

1 to all of the duties applicable to the driver of a vehicle except as
2 otherwise provided in NRS 484B.767 to 484B.790, inclusive, and
3 section 1 of this act, and except as to those provisions of chapters
4 484A to 484E, inclusive, of NRS which by their nature can have no
5 application.
