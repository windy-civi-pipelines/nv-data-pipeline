Section 1:
REQUIRES TWO-THIRDS MAJORITY VOTE EXEMPT
(§ 1.5 & NRS 533.345)
FIRST REPRINT A.B. 419
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 419–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS DELONG,
COLE, GURR; DICKMAN AND O’NEILL
MARCH 13, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Natural Resources
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to water. (BDR 48-736)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to water; requiring the State Engineer to prepare
and submit a biennial report to the Legislature relating to
certain applications pending a final decision by the State
Engineer; requiring the State Engineer to adopt certain
regulations relating to hearings and to establish a pre-
application review process to meet with prospective
applicants; making the State Engineer subject to the
Nevada Administrative Procedure Act for purposes of
adopting regulations and certain provisions governing the
adjudication of contested cases; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 The State Engineer, an appointee of the Director of the State Department of
2 Conservation and Natural Resources, manages the appropriation of water in this
3 State. (Title 48 of NRS) Section 1 of this bill requires the State Engineer to prepare
4 and submit a biennial report to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for
5 transmittal to the Legislative Commission relating to certain applications to
6 appropriate water that have been pending a final decision by the State Engineer for
7 2 or more years.
8 Under existing law, the State Engineer is authorized to make such reasonable
9 rules and regulations as may be necessary for the proper and orderly execution of
10 the powers conferred upon him or her by law, including regulations governing the
11 practice and procedure in all contests before the Office of the State Engineer. (NRS
12 532.120) Section 1.5 of this bill requires the State Engineer to adopt regulations:
13 (1) governing hearings before the Office, which must include rules governing
14 expert testimony; and (2) to establish a pre-application process for purposes of the
15 provisions governing underground water and wells, the adjudication of vested water
16 rights and the appropriation of public water, which must include procedures for a
-
*AB419_R1*
– 2 –
17 prospective applicant to meet with the State Engineer or his or her designee at the
18 request of the potential applicant to review the prospective application and identify
19 any potential barriers to approval of the application. (Chapters 533 and 534 of
20 NRS)
21 Under existing law the State Engineer is exempt from the Nevada
22 Administrative Procedure Act, which establishes procedural requirements for the
23 adoption of regulations and the adjudication of contested cases. (Chapter 233B of
24 NRS) Section 11 of this bill makes the State Engineer subject to the Nevada
25 Administrative Procedure Act for purposes of adopting regulations and certain
26 provisions governing the adjudication of contested cases.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 532 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 On or before September 15 of each even-numbered year, the
4 State Engineer shall prepare and submit to the Director of the
5 Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Legislative
6 Commission a report of all applications submitted pursuant to
7 chapters 533 and 534 of NRS that are pending a final decision by
8 the State Engineer as of the date of the report. The report must
9 include, without limitation:
10 1. The number of applications pending a final decision that
11 have been pending for 2 or more years; and
12 2. The name of the applicant for each application pending a
13 final decision that has been pending for 2 or more years, the
14 primary reason the application is pending a final decision and the
15 anticipated date a final decision will be made on the application, if
16 any.
17 Sec. 1.5. NRS 532.120 is hereby amended to read as follows:
18 532.120 1. The State Engineer may make such reasonable
19 rules and regulations as may be necessary for the proper and orderly
20 execution of the powers conferred by law.
21 2. The State Engineer may adopt regulations, not in conflict
22 with law, governing the practice and procedure in all contests and
23 hearings before the Office of the State Engineer, to ensure the
24 proper and orderly exercise of the powers granted by law, and the
25 speedy accomplishment of the purposes of chapters 533, 534, 535
26 and 536 of NRS. Such rules of practice and procedure must be
27 furnished to any person upon application therefor [.] and must
28 include, without limitation, rules:
29 (a) Limiting the evidence presented in a hearing to the subject
30 matter contained in an application or a protest, as applicable; and
31 (b) Requiring expert testimony presented in a hearing to
32 comply with the provisions of NRS 50.275 and 50.285.
