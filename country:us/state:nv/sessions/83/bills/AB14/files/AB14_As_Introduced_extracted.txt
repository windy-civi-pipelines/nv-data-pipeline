Section 1:
A.B. 14
ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS
(ON BEHALF OF THE NEVADA ASSOCIATION OF COUNTIES)
PREFILED OCTOBER 31, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to certain public officers.
(BDR 20-453)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to counties; revising qualifications for certain
county officers to require such persons to be registered to
vote in this State; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires a person to be a qualified elector to be eligible for a
2 public office in this State. (Nev. Const. Art. 15, § 3) Existing law further sets forth
3 additional qualifications for certain county offices and provides for the election of
4 such officers, including the office of: (1) county commissioner; (2) county clerk;
5 (3) county recorder; (4) sheriff; (5) county treasurer; (6) county assessor; (7) district
6 attorney; (8) public administrator; and (9) constable. (NRS 244.020, 246.010,
7 247.010, 248.005, 248.010, 249.010, 250.010, 252.010, 253.010, 258.005, 258.010)
8 Finally, existing law sets forth the qualifications for a supervisor of the Board of
9 Supervisors of Carson City and provides for the election of Supervisors of the
10 Board. (Carson City Charter § 2.010) Sections 1-10 of this bill include as a
11 qualification for these offices a requirement for these officers to be registered to
12 vote in this State. Section 11 of this bill provides that the requirement for these
13 officers to be registered to vote in this State do not apply to a person who, on
14 July 1, 2025, holds such office.

2 244.020 1. County commissioners must [be] :

6 county commissioner.

8 246.010 1. County clerks shall be elected by the qualified
9 electors of their respective counties.
10 2. County clerks must be registered to vote in this State.
11 3. County clerks shall be chosen by the electors of their
12 respective counties at the general election in 1922, and at the
13 general election every 4 years thereafter, and shall enter upon the
14 duties of their respective offices on the first Monday of January
15 subsequent to their election.
16 Sec. 3. NRS 247.010 is hereby amended to read as follows:
17 247.010 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 3 or
18 as altered pursuant to the mechanism set forth in NRS 244.1507,
19 county recorders must be [elected] :
20 (a) Registered to vote in this State; and
21 (b) Elected by the qualified electors of their respective counties.
22 2. County recorders must be chosen by the electors of their
23 respective counties at the general election in 1922, and at the
24 general election every 4 years thereafter, and shall enter upon the
25 duties of their respective offices on the first Monday of January
26 subsequent to their election.
27 3. The Clerk of Carson City is ex officio the Recorder of
28 Carson City.

31 the person:
32 (a) Will have attained the age of 21 years on the date he or she
33 would take office if so elected;

35 (c) Is registered to vote in this State; and

37 forth in subsection 2 or 3, as applicable.

39 a candidate for the office of sheriff in a county whose population is
40 30,000 or more, the person must meet the following requirements at
41 the time he or she files his or her declaration of candidacy for the
42 office:
43 (a) He or she has a history of at least 5 consecutive years of
44 employment or service:
45 (1) As a peace officer;

2 States;

6 subparagraphs (1), (2) and (3); and
7 (b) He or she has:
8 (1) Been certified as a category I peace officer by the
9 Commission;
10 (2) Been certified as a category I peace officer or its
11 equivalent by the certifying authority of another state that, as
12 determined by the Commission, imposes requirements for
13 certification as a category I peace officer in this State; or
14 (3) Successfully completed a federal law enforcement
15 training program approved by the Commission.

23 earn certification by the Commission as a category I peace officer,
