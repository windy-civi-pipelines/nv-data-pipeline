SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the employment of offenders.

Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to correctional institutions; requiring the Director of the
Department of Corrections to submit an annual report to the Director of
the Legislative Counsel Bureau relating to the employment of
offenders; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill requires the Director of the Department of Corrections to submit a report relating
2 to the employment of offenders on or before March 1 of each year to the Director of the
3 Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Senate Standing Committee on Finance and
4 the Assembly Standing Committee on Ways and Means during a regular or special session of
5 the Legislature, or the Interim Finance Committee when the Legislature is not in session. The
6 report must include, without limitation, the following information relating to the previous
7 year: (1) the number of offenders who were eligible for employment; (2) the number of
8 offenders who were employed; (3) the number of offenders who refused employment; (4) the
9 pay of the offenders who were employed; (5) the educational attainments of the offenders
10 who were employed; (6) the amount of the deductions from the accounts of offenders who
11 were employed; and [(6)] (7) of the offenders who were employed and who were subsequently
12 released, the percentage of offenders who found employment following release with the
13 industries in which they were employed.
