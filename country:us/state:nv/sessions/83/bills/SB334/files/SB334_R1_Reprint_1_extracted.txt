Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 334
SENATE BILL NO. 334–SENATOR NEAL
MARCH 11, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the employment of
offenders. (BDR 16-1115)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to correctional institutions; requiring the Director
of the Department of Corrections to submit an annual
report to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau
relating to the employment of offenders; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill requires the Director of the Department of Corrections to submit a
2 report relating to the employment of offenders on or before March 1 of each year to
3 the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Senate
4 Standing Committee on Finance and the Assembly Standing Committee on Ways
5 and Means during a regular or special session of the Legislature, or the Interim
6 Finance Committee when the Legislature is not in session. The report must include,
7 without limitation, the following information relating to the previous year: (1) the
8 number of offenders who were eligible for employment; (2) the number of
9 offenders who were employed; (3) the number of offenders who refused
10 employment; (4) the pay of the offenders who were employed; (5) the amount of
11 the deductions from the accounts of offenders who were employed; and (6) of the
12 offenders who were employed and who were subsequently released, the percentage
13 of offenders who found employment following release with the industries in which
14 they were employed.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
4 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
