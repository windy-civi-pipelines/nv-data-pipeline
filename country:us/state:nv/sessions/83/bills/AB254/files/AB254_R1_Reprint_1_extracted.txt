ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 254–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS MOSCA,
MONROE-MORENO; GONZÁLEZ AND KASAMA
FEBRUARY 18, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to grants. (BDR 18-691)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to grants; creating the Community Project Grant
Program to provide grants for certain community projects;
creating the Account for the Community Project Grant
Program in the State General Fund; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Office of Federal Assistance within the Office of the
2 Governor for the purpose of obtaining and maximizing any money, equipment,
3 material or services available from the Federal Government. (NRS 223.464,
4 223.472) The Office of Federal Assistance is required, as part of its duties, to
5 administer the Nevada Grant Matching Program, which provides grants to state
6 agencies, local governments, tribal governments and nonprofit organizations for the
7 purpose of satisfying the matching requirement for a federal grant. (NRS
8 223.488-223.498)
9 Section 2 of this bill creates the Community Project Grant Program for the
10 purpose of awarding grants to specified nonprofit corporations to conduct projects
11 that benefit communities in Nevada. Section 2 requires the Director of the Office of
12 Federal Assistance to adopt regulations to carry out the Grant Program, including
13 regulations that prescribe: (1) the criteria for eligibility for such a grant and the
14 procedures for the submission and review of applications for and the awarding of
15 such a grant; and (2) requirements for reporting expenditures of money from such a
16 grant.
17 Section 3 of this bill creates the Account for the Community Project Grant
18 Program in the State General Fund. Section 3 requires that money in the Account
19 be used only to carry out the Community Project Grant Program and provides that
20 any money remaining in the Account at the end of a fiscal year does not revert to
21 the State General Fund.
22 Sections 2 and 3: (1) require the Director of the Office of Federal Assistance to
23 administer the Grant Program and the Account and to prepare a biennial report
24 regarding the Grant Program for submission to the Legislature; and (2) authorize
-

– 2 –
25 the Office to apply for and accept grants, gifts and other sources of money for
26 deposit in the Account.
27 Section 3.3 of this bill makes a conforming change to have existing definitions
28 that apply to the Office of Federal Assistance also apply to sections 2 and 3 of this
29 act.
30 Section 3.7 of this bill makes a conforming change to the general authority of
31 the Office to apply for and receive any gift, grant, contribution or other source
32 money to carry out the provisions of law relating to the Office to account for the
33 authority of the Office in section 3 to apply for gifts, grants, bequests, donations or
34 other sources of money specifically for the Community Project Grant Program.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 223 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. 1. The Community Project Grant Program is hereby
4 created for the purpose of awarding grants to nonprofit
5 corporations to conduct projects that benefit communities in this
6 State.
7 2. The Director shall administer the Grant Program.
8 3. The Director shall adopt regulations necessary to carry out
9 the Grant Program, including, without limitation, regulations that
10 prescribe:
11 (a) The criteria for eligibility for such a grant and the
12 procedures for the submission and review of applications for and
13 the awarding of such a grant, including, without limitation, a
14 separate process with criteria and procedures suitable for a
15 nonprofit corporation that:
16 (1) Has an annual revenue that does not exceed
17 $1,000,000; or
18 (2) Has been in existence for less than 5 years after the
19 effective date of its recognition as tax-exempt by the Internal
20 Revenue Service; and
21 (b) Requirements for reporting expenditures of the money
22 from such a grant.
23 4. On or before October 1 of each odd-numbered year, the
24 Director shall prepare and transmit a written report to the
25 Legislature concerning the applications submitted for grants
26 under the Grant Program during the immediately preceding
27 biennium.
28 5. As used in this section, “nonprofit corporation” means a
29 corporation that:
30 (a) Operates exclusively for any exempt purpose under section
31 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. §501(c)(3); and
-
*AB254_R1*
– 3 –
1 (b) Is recognized as exempt by the Internal Revenue Service
2 pursuant to section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, 26
3 U.S.C. §501(c)(3).
4 Sec. 3. 1. The Account for the Community Project Grant
5 Program is hereby created in the State General Fund.
6 2. The Director shall administer the Account.
7 3. In addition to any direct legislative appropriation, the
8 Office may apply for and accept any gift, grant, bequest, donation
9 or other source of money. Any money so received must be
10 deposited in the Account.
11 4. The money in the Account must be used only to carry out
12 the Community Project Grant Program created by section 2 of this
13 act.
14 5. Any interest and income earned on money in the Account,
15 after deducting any applicable charges, must be credited to the
16 Account.
17 6. All claims against the Account must be paid as other
18 claims against the State are paid.
19 7. Any money remaining in the Account at the end of a fiscal
20 year does not revert to the State General Fund, and the balance in
21 the Account must be carried forward to the next fiscal year.
22 Sec. 3.3. NRS 223.460 is hereby amended to read as follows:
23 223.460 As used in NRS 223.460 to 223.498, inclusive, and
24 sections 2 and 3 of this act, unless the context otherwise requires,
25 the words and terms defined in NRS 223.462 to 223.470, inclusive,
26 have the meanings ascribed to them in those sections.
27 Sec. 3.7. NRS 223.482 is hereby amended to read as follows:
28 223.482 [The] Except as otherwise provided in section 3 of
29 this act, the Office may apply for and receive any gift, grant,
30 contribution or other money from any source to carry out the
31 provisions of NRS 223.460 to 223.498, inclusive [.] , and sections 2
32 and 3 of this act.
33 Sec. 4. The provisions of NRS 218D.380 do not apply to any
34 provision of this act which adds or revises a requirement to submit a
35 report to the Legislature.
36 Sec. 5. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
37 approval.
38 2. Sections 1 to 4, inclusive, of this act become effective:
39 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
40 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
41 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
42 (b) On July 1, 2025, for all other purposes.
