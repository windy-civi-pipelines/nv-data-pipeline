SECOND REPRINT A.B. 249
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 249–COMMITTEE ON WAYS AND MEANS
FEBRUARY 18, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to fiscal notes.
(BDR 17-932)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to fiscal notes; revising the information
concerning fiscal effect that must be included in the
summary of each bill or joint resolution introduced in the
Legislature; revising provisions relating to the preparation
of a fiscal note by a local government; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the summary of a bill or joint resolution introduced in the
2 Legislature to include certain statements relating to the fiscal effect on the State and
3 local government. (NRS 218D.415) Section 1 of this bill amends the summary of a
4 bill or joint resolution relating to the fiscal effect on the State from “Effect on the
5 State: Yes” to “Effect on the State: May have Fiscal Impact.” Section 1 further
6 requires the summary of a bill or resolution to include certain statements relating to
7 the fiscal effect of the bill or joint resolution on the State Highway Fund.
8 Existing law provides that, if a bill or joint resolution is submitted to a local
9 government for a fiscal note and the local government determines the bill or joint
10 resolution reduces the revenues or increases the expenditures of the local
11 government, the local government is required to prepare a fiscal note and return it
12 to the Fiscal Analysis Division of the Legislative Counsel Bureau within 8 working
13 days. (NRS 218D.475) Section 1.5 of this bill authorizes the Fiscal Analysis
14 Division to extend the period of time to return the fiscal note by not more than 7
15 additional days if the matter requires extended research.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 218D.415 is hereby amended to read as
2 follows:
3 218D.415 1. The summary of each bill or joint resolution
4 introduced in the Legislature must include the statement:
5 (a) “Fiscal Note: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal
6 Impact,”
7 “Fiscal Note: Effect on Local Government: No,” or
8 “Fiscal Note: Effect on Local Government: Increases or
9 Newly Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City Jail or
10 Detention Facility,”
11  whichever is appropriate . [; and]
12 (b) “Effect on the State: [Yes,”] May have Fiscal Impact,”
13 “Effect on the State: No,”
14 “Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation from the State
15 General Fund included in Executive Budget,”
16 “Effect on the State: Executive Budget,” or
17 “Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation from the State
18 General Fund not included in Executive Budget,”
19  whichever is appropriate.
20 (c) “Effect on the State Highway Fund: May have Fiscal
21 Impact,”
22 “Effect on the State Highway Fund: No,”
23 “Effect on the State Highway Fund: Contains
24 Appropriation from the State Highway Fund included in
25 Executive Budget,” or
26 “Effect on the State Highway Fund: Contains
27 Appropriation from the State Highway Fund not included in
28 Executive Budget,”
29  whichever is appropriate.
30 2. The Legislative Counsel shall consult the Fiscal Analysis
31 Division to secure the appropriate information for summaries of
32 bills and joint resolutions.
33 3. If an amendment adds an appropriation to a bill that
34 previously did not include an appropriation or removes all
35 appropriations from a bill that previously included one or more
36 appropriations, the Legislative Counsel shall change the summary of
37 the bill to reflect the inclusion or removal.
38 Sec. 1.5. NRS 218D.475 is hereby amended to read as
39 follows:
40 218D.475 1. Whenever a bill or joint resolution is submitted
41 to an agency for a fiscal note, the agency shall prepare the fiscal
42 note and return it to the Fiscal Analysis Division within 5 working
-

1 days. The Fiscal Analysis Division may extend the period for not
2 more than 10 additional working days if the matter requires
3 extended research.
4 2. Whenever a bill or joint resolution is submitted to a local
5 government for a fiscal note, the local government shall:
6 (a) Review the provisions of the bill or joint resolution to
7 determine whether the bill or joint resolution reduces the revenues
8 or increases the expenditures of the local government; and
9 (b) If the local government determines that the bill or joint
10 resolution reduces the revenues or increases the expenditures of the
11 local government, prepare a fiscal note for that bill or joint
12 resolution and return it to the Fiscal Analysis Division within 8
13 working days. The Fiscal Analysis Division may extend the period
14 for not more than 7 additional working days if the matter requires
15 extended research.
16 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
17 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
