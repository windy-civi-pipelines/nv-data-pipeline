Referred to Committee on Ways and Means
SUMMARY—Makes appropriations to Clark County , [and] the City of Reno and
the Nevada Rural Housing Authority for eviction diversion
programs. (BDR S-1094)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included in
Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT making appropriations to Clark County , [and] the City of Reno and the
Nevada Rural Housing Authority for eviction diversion programs;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
2 Clark County the sum of [$18,000,000] $15,000,000 for eviction diversion
3 programs.
4 2. Money appropriated by subsection 1 may be:
5 (a) Awarded as a grant to another entity to carry out an eviction diversion
6 program.
7 (b) Used for administrative costs to carry out an eviction diversion program,
8 except not more than 10 percent of the money appropriated by subsection 1 may be
9 used for such administrative costs by Clark County or any entity to which a grant is
10 awarded pursuant to paragraph (a).
11 3. Upon acceptance of the money appropriated by subsection 1, the County
12 Manager of Clark County agrees to:
13 (a) Prepare and transmit a report to the Interim Finance Committee on or
14 before December 18, 2026, that describes each expenditure made from the money
15 appropriated by subsection 1 from the date on which the money was received by
16 Clark County through December 1, 2026;
17 (b) Prepare and transmit a final report to the Interim Finance Committee on or
18 before September 17, 2027, that describes each expenditure made from the money
19 appropriated by subsection 1 from the date on which the money was received by
20 Clark County through June 30, 2027; and
21 (c) Upon request of the Legislative Commission, make available to the
22 Legislative Auditor any of the books, accounts, claims, reports, vouchers or other
23 records of information, confidential or otherwise, of Clark County, regardless of
Assembly Amendment No. 837 to Assembly Bill No. 475 Page 4
1 their form or location, that the Legislative Auditor deems necessary to conduct an
2 audit of the use of the money appropriated by subsection 1.
3 4. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1 must not
4 be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to which the
5 appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
6 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
7 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after September 17, 2027, by
8 either the entity to which the money was appropriated or the entity to which the
9 money was subsequently granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State
10 General Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
11 Sec. 2. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to the
12 City of Reno the sum of [$7,000,000] $5,250,000 for eviction diversion programs.
13 2. Money appropriated by subsection 1 may be:
14 (a) Awarded as a grant to another entity to carry out an eviction diversion
15 program.
16 (b) Used for administrative costs to carry out an eviction diversion program,
17 except not more than 10 percent of the money appropriated by subsection 1 may be
18 used for such administrative costs by the City of Reno or any entity to which a
19 grant is awarded pursuant to paragraph (a).
20 3. Money appropriated by subsection 1 may not be used to fund or be
21 awarded as a grant to another entity to carry out an eviction diversion
22 program that:
23 (a) Prohibits residents of unincorporated Washoe County or of the City of
24 Sparks from participating in the program.
25 (b) Establishes program eligibility requirements for residents of
26 unincorporated Washoe County or of the City of Sparks that are more
27 restrictive than any requirements established for participants who are
28 residents of the City of Reno.
29 4. Upon acceptance of the money appropriated by subsection 1, the City
30 Manager of the City of Reno agrees to:
31 (a) Prepare and transmit a report to the Interim Finance Committee on or
32 before December 18, 2026, that describes each expenditure made from the money
33 appropriated by subsection 1 from the date on which the money was received by the
34 City of Reno through December 1, 2026;
35 (b) Prepare and transmit a final report to the Interim Finance Committee on or
36 before September 17, 2027, that describes each expenditure made from the money
37 appropriated by subsection 1 from the date on which the money was received by the
38 City of Reno through June 30, 2027; and
39 (c) Upon request of the Legislative Commission, make available to the
40 Legislative Auditor any of the books, accounts, claims, reports, vouchers or other
41 records of information, confidential or otherwise, of the City of Reno, regardless of
42 their form or location, that the Legislative Auditor deems necessary to conduct an
43 audit of the use of the money appropriated by subsection 1.
44 [4.] 5. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1
45 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to which
46 the appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
47 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
48 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after September 17, 2027, by
49 either the entity to which the money was appropriated or the entity to which the
50 money was subsequently granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State
51 General Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
