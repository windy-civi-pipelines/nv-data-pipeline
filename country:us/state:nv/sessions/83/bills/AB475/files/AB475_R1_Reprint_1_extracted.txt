ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 475–COMMITTEE ON WAYS AND MEANS
MARCH 19, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Ways and Means
SUMMARY—Makes appropriations to Clark County, the City of
Reno and the Nevada Rural Housing Authority for
eviction diversion programs. (BDR S-1094)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT making appropriations to Clark County, the City of Reno
and the Nevada Rural Housing Authority for eviction
diversion programs; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
2 General Fund to Clark County the sum of $15,000,000 for eviction
3 diversion programs.
4 2. Money appropriated by subsection 1 may be:
5 (a) Awarded as a grant to another entity to carry out an eviction
6 diversion program.
7 (b) Used for administrative costs to carry out an eviction
8 diversion program, except not more than 10 percent of the money
9 appropriated by subsection 1 may be used for such administrative
10 costs by Clark County or any entity to which a grant is awarded
11 pursuant to paragraph (a).
12 3. Upon acceptance of the money appropriated by subsection 1,
13 the County Manager of Clark County agrees to:
14 (a) Prepare and transmit a report to the Interim Finance
15 Committee on or before December 18, 2026, that describes each
16 expenditure made from the money appropriated by subsection 1
-

– 2 –
1 from the date on which the money was received by Clark County
2 through December 1, 2026;
3 (b) Prepare and transmit a final report to the Interim Finance
4 Committee on or before September 17, 2027, that describes each
5 expenditure made from the money appropriated by subsection 1
6 from the date on which the money was received by Clark County
7 through June 30, 2027; and
8 (c) Upon request of the Legislative Commission, make available
9 to the Legislative Auditor any of the books, accounts, claims,
10 reports, vouchers or other records of information, confidential or
11 otherwise, of Clark County, regardless of their form or location, that
12 the Legislative Auditor deems necessary to conduct an audit of the
13 use of the money appropriated by subsection 1.
14 4. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
15 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
16 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
17 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
18 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
19 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
20 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
21 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
22 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
23 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
24 Sec. 2. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
25 General Fund to the City of Reno the sum of $5,250,000 for eviction
26 diversion programs.
27 2. Money appropriated by subsection 1 may be:
28 (a) Awarded as a grant to another entity to carry out an eviction
29 diversion program.
30 (b) Used for administrative costs to carry out an eviction
31 diversion program, except not more than 10 percent of the money
32 appropriated by subsection 1 may be used for such administrative
33 costs by the City of Reno or any entity to which a grant is awarded
34 pursuant to paragraph (a).
35 3. Money appropriated by subsection 1 may not be used to
36 fund or be awarded as a grant to another entity to carry out an
37 eviction diversion program that:
38 (a) Prohibits residents of unincorporated Washoe County or of
39 the City of Sparks from participating in the program.
40 (b) Establishes program eligibility requirements for residents of
41 unincorporated Washoe County or of the City of Sparks that are
42 more restrictive than any requirements established for participants
43 who are residents of the City of Reno.
44 4. Upon acceptance of the money appropriated by subsection 1,
45 the City Manager of the City of Reno agrees to:
