Section 1:
S.B. 248
TITUS, KRASNER, HANSEN; AND STEINBECK
FEBRUARY 26, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to education.
(BDR 34-638)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§ 1)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring each public school to
establish and maintain a publicly available list in an
electronic format cataloguing all print and digital
materials available in the school library and all
instructional materials used as a part of the school
curriculum; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the board of trustees of a school district to purchase all
2 new library books, textbooks and supplementary schoolbooks which are necessary
3 and have been approved by the State Board of Education to carry out the mandates
4 of the school curriculum to be used by pupils of the school district. (NRS 393.170)
5 Section 1 of this bill requires each public school to establish and maintain in an
6 electronic format a list of all print and digital materials available in the school
7 library and all instructional materials used as a part of the school curriculum. The
8 list must: (1) be posted in a publicly accessible location on an Internet website
9 maintained by the school; (2) identify each item by International Standard Book
10 Number, title and author; and (3) be maintained and updated as necessary to
11 accurately reflect all print and digital materials available in the school library and
12 any instructional materials used as a part of the school curriculum.
-
*SB248*
– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. Each public school shall establish and maintain in an
4 electronic format a list of:
5 (a) All print and digital materials available in the school
6 library at the public school; and
7 (b) All instructional materials used as a part of the school
8 curriculum.
9 2. The list required pursuant to subsection 1 must:
10 (a) Be posted in a publicly accessible location on an Internet
11 website maintained by the public school in a searchable and
12 downloadable format;
13 (b) Identify each item by title, author, International Standard
14 Book Number and any such other identifying features or
15 information that the school deems appropriate; and
16 (c) Be maintained and updated as necessary to accurately
17 reflect all print and digital materials available in the school library
18 and any instructional materials used as a part of the school
19 curriculum.
20 3. As used in this section:
21 (a) “Instructional material” means any print or digital
22 material that is provided to a pupil or otherwise used in the
23 classroom as a part of the school curriculum, including, without
24 limitation, the curriculum of any course or program of instruction
25 provided or offered to pupils enrolled in the school. The term does
26 not include academic tests or assessments or audiovisual
27 materials.
28 (b) “International Standard Book Number” means the
29 numeric code which is unique to each publication of a book and
30 assigned to a publication of a book by the International ISBN
31 Agency.
32 (c) “Print material” means any book, graphic novel, pamphlet,
33 magazine, newspaper, periodical or other written material or
34 document that is available in a print or digital format. The term
35 includes, without limitation, any such material that is composed of
36 or includes drawings, paintings, photographs or other visual
37 depictions.
38 (d) “School library” means any area inside a public school
39 containing library books and other materials, including, without
40 limitation, paperback and hardback books.
-
*SB248*
– 3 –
1 Sec. 2. The provisions of NRS 354.599 do not apply to any
2 additional expenses of a local government that are related to the
3 provisions of this act.
