ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 281–COMMITTEE

Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the state militia. (BDR 36-398)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to the state militia; [revising the benefits and compensation]
clarifying for purposes of industrial insurance that a member of the
state militia [and the dependents of the member are entitled to receive
if the member is wounded, injured, disabled or killed while] is on state
active duty [;] for 24 hours a day while on state active duty; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes that the militia of the State is composed of the Nevada National
2 Guard and, when called into active service by the Governor, reservists to the Nevada National
3 Guard and any volunteer military organizations licensed by the Governor. (NRS 412.026)
4 Existing law entitles, with certain exceptions, any member of the militia of the State or his or her
5 dependents to certain compensation pursuant to the Nevada Industrial Insurance Act or the
6 Nevada Occupational Diseases Act if the member is wounded, injured, disabled or killed while in
7 the line of duty in the service of the State. (NRS 412.142) Existing law further requires that
8 certain compensation and benefits: (1) must be determined based on the member’s average
9 income from all sources during the year immediately preceding his or her injury or death or the
10 commencement of his or her disability; and (2) must not exceed the maximum amount prescribed
11 by the Nevada Industrial Insurance Act or the Nevada Occupational Diseases Act. (NRS
12 412.142) [Section] Sections 1 and 2 of this bill [provides instead that the member or the
13 dependents of the member are entitled to receive compensation and benefits that are
14 equivalent to the federal compensation or benefits that the member would be entitled to
15 receive if the wound, injury, disability or death arose from military duties performed pursuant
16 to Title 10 or Title 32 of the United States Code.] clarify that for purposes of industrial
17 insurance a member of the militia of the State who is on state active duty is on state
18 active duty 24 hours a day for each day of state active duty.
Assembly Amendment No. 198 to Assembly Bill No. 281 Page 4
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 412.142 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 412.142 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection [2:] 3:
3 (a) In [, in] all cases in which any member of the militia of the State is
4 wounded, injured, disabled or killed while [in the line of] on state active duty [in
5 the service of the State,] or if that wound, injury or disability is aggravated or
6 recurs while the member is on state active duty, the member or the dependents of
7 the member are entitled to receive compensation [and benefits] from the State of
8 Nevada , in accordance with the provisions of chapters 616A to 616D, inclusive, or
9 chapter 617 of NRS. If that wound, injury or disability is aggravated or recurs while
10 the member is in the line of duty in the service of the State, the member or the
11 member’s dependents are also entitled to receive such compensation.
12 (b) In all cases, the member who has a disability or is deceased shall be
13 deemed to be an employee of the State of Nevada. The compensation to be awarded
14 to the member or to the dependents of the member must be determined upon the
15 basis of the member’s average income from all sources during the year immediately
16 preceding the date of his or her injury or death or the commencement of his or her
17 disability, but the compensation must not exceed the maximum prescribed in
18 chapters 616A to 616D, inclusive, or chapter 617 of NRS. [that are equivalent to
19 the federal compensation or benefits that the member would be entitled to receive
20 if the injury, wound, illness, disability or death arose from military duties
21 performed pursuant to Title 10 or Title 32 of the United States Code.]
22 2. For the purposes of subsection 1, a member of the militia of the State
23 who is on state active duty shall be deemed to be on state active duty 24 hours a
24 day for each day of state active duty.
25 3. The provisions of this section do not apply to a member of the militia of the
26 State or any dependents of the member who is receiving or is entitled to receive
27 compensation or benefits for an injury, wound, illness, disability or death described
28 in this section pursuant to any law or regulation of the Federal Government, if:
29 (a) The federal compensation or benefits arise from military duties performed
30 pursuant to Title 10 or Title 32 of the United States Code; and
31 (b) The wound, injury, illness or disability is not an aggravation or recurrence
32 of a wound, injury, illness or disability that arose from previous duties performed
33 pursuant to Title 10 or Title 32 of the United States Code.
34 Sec. 2. Chapter 616A of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a
35 new section to read as follows:
36 1. Any member of the Nevada National Guard shall be deemed an employee
37 of the State of Nevada for purposes of chapters 616A to 616D, inclusive, of NRS
38 while on state active duty.
39 2. For the purposes of subsection 1, a member of the Nevada National
40 Guard shall be deemed to be on state active duty 24 hours a day for each day of
41 state active duty.
42 Sec. 3. NRS 616A.025 is hereby amended to read as follows:
43 616A.025 As used in chapters 616A to 616D, inclusive, of NRS, unless the
44 context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in NRS 616A.030 to
45 616A.360, inclusive, and section 2 of this act, have the meanings ascribed to them
46 in those sections.
