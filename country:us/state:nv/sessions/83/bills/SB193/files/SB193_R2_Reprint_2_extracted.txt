Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Establishes a pilot program to reduce interest rates
on mortgage loans to certain eligible borrowers.
(BDR 25-42)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to housing; requiring the Housing Division of the
Department of Business and Industry to establish a pilot
program to buy down the interest rate on mortgage loans
for certain eligible borrowers; making an appropriation
for the pilot program; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes the Housing Division of the Department of Business
2 and Industry to enter into instruments, agreements and other transactions for certain
3 purposes, including: (1) reducing or modifying the amount or duration of any
4 payment, interest rate, spread or similar risk; and (2) lowering the cost of borrowing
5 when used in combination with the issuance or carrying of bonds or investments.
6 (NRS 319.167) Section 1 of this bill requires the Division to establish a pilot
7 program for certain eligible borrowers to buy down the interest rate of a 30-year
8 fixed-rate mortgage loan for an owner-occupied residence for such an eligible
9 borrower in this State. Section 1 requires the eligible borrower to: (1) be in the
10 process of obtaining his or her first mortgage loan on a residence for the eligible
11 borrower; (2) qualify as a first-time homebuyer; (3) with certain exceptions, have a
12 household income of not more than 160 percent of the median income for the
13 county in this State in which the residence is located; and (4) meet certain
14 underwriting criteria. Section 1 further authorizes the Division to establish a
15 process for an eligible borrower to obtain a waiver of the household income
16 requirement.

17 Section 2 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Division for the purpose of

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 319 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Division shall, subject to the availability of funds
4 appropriated for such a purpose, establish a pilot program for an
5 eligible borrower who meets the qualifications set forth in
6 subsection 3 to buy down the interest rate of a fixed-rate mortgage
7 loan with an amortization period of 30 years for an owner-
8 occupied residence in this State for the eligible borrower that is
9 obtained from a lending institution approved by the Division in
10 accordance with any regulations adopted pursuant to subsection 5.
11 2. The pilot program established pursuant to subsection 1
12 must, without limitation:
13 (a) Include a process for an eligible borrower to apply to
14 participate in the program; and
15 (b) Provide for a buy down for the mortgage loan of the
16 eligible borrower.
17 3. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 4, an eligible
18 borrower may receive assistance from the pilot program
19 established pursuant to subsection 1 if the eligible borrower:
20 (a) Is in the process of obtaining his or her first mortgage loan
21 from a lending institution approved by the Division for the
22 purchase of an owner-occupied residence.
23 (b) Qualifies as a first-time homebuyer. An eligible borrower
24 qualifies as a first-time homebuyer if:
25 (1) The eligible borrower and, except as otherwise provided
26 in subparagraphs (2) and (3), the spouse of the eligible borrower,
27 if applicable, have had no ownership interest in any residential
28 real property at any time during the 3-year period immediately
29 preceding the date on which a mortgage for the residence is or will
30 be executed;
31 (2) The eligible borrower is a single person who is a
32 displaced homemaker and, except for owning a home with his or
33 her spouse or residing in a home owned by his or her spouse,
34 meets the requirements of subparagraph (1); or
35 (3) The eligible borrower is a single person who is a single
36 parent and who, except for owning a home with his or her spouse
37 or residing in a home owned by his or her spouse while married,
38 meets the requirements of subparagraph (1).
-
*SB193_R2*
– 3 –
1 (c) Has a household income of not more than 160 percent of
2 the median income for the county in this State in which the
3 residence to be purchased is located.
4 (d) Meets any criteria for underwriting established by the
5 lending institution.
6 (e) Meets any other requirement prescribed by the Division by
7 regulation.
8 4. The Division may establish a process for an eligible
9 borrower who does not meet the household income requirement
10 set forth in paragraph (c) of subsection 3 to apply to the Division
11 for a waiver of that requirement.
12 5. The Division may adopt regulations to carry out the
13 provisions of this section, which may include, without limitation:
14 (a) The requirements for a lending institution to be approved
15 to participate in the program; and
16 (b) The procedures to apply for a waiver from the Division
17 pursuant to subsection 4.
18 6. As used in this section:
19 (a) “Buy down” means the permanent decrease of the interest
20 rate of a fixed-rate mortgage loan with an amortization period of
21 30 years.
22 (b) “Displaced homemaker” has the meaning ascribed to it in
23 42 U.S.C. § 12854.
24 (c) “Single parent” has the meaning ascribed to it in 42 U.S.C.
25 § 12854.
26 Sec. 2. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
27 General Fund to the Housing Division of the Department of
28 Business and Industry the sum of $10,000,000 for establishing and
29 carrying out the pilot program to buy down interest rates on
30 mortgage loans for eligible borrowers required by section 1 of this
31 act.
32 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
33 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
34 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
35 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
36 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
37 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
38 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
39 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
40 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
41 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
42 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
