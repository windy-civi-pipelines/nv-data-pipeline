A.B. No. 285—Establishes the Nevada State Service Corps within the Department
of Administration. (BDR 23-98)

ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 285–[ASSEMBLYMEMBER] ASSEMBLYMEMBERS MOSCA ;
D’SILVA, FLANAGAN, JACKSON, KASAMA AND NGUYEN
FEBRUARY 25, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Establishes the Nevada State Service Corps within the Department
of Administration. (BDR 23-98)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to employment; establishing the Nevada State Service Corps as a
program within the Department of Administration; requiring the
Department to develop and oversee the Nevada State Service Corps;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) requires the Human Resources Commission to adopt regulations for the
2 certification of qualified persons for temporary service with this State; and (2) authorizes an
3 agency to appoint persons temporarily for less than 160 cumulative hours, with certain
4 exceptions, during any calendar year without regard to such regulations. (NRS 284.325)
5 Section 1 of this bill establishes the Nevada State Service Corps as a program within the
6 Department of Administration and requires the Department to [: (1)] develop and oversee the
7 Nevada State Service Corps to provide for the placement and mentoring of recent graduates of
8 the Nevada System of Higher Education for [temporary] service in certain positions with the
9 Executive Department of the State Government . [; and (2) consult with the Nevada System of
10 Higher Education to develop and oversee the Nevada State Service Corps.] Section 1 requires
11 the Department to [: (1)] recruit certain students to participate in the Nevada State Service
12 Corps . [; (2) designate positions with the Executive Department of the State Government in
13 which participants may provide temporary service; (3) match and place participants with those
14 designated positions; (4) provide mentoring to the participants; and (5) promote the
15 opportunities to participants for full-time employment with the Executive Department of the
16 State Government.] Section 1 further [authorizes the Board of Regents of the University of
17 Nevada to request that] requires the Legislative Commission to appoint a legislator to serve
18 as an advisor to the participants, promote the benefits of state service and provide
19 encouragement to the participants.
20 Section 2 of this bill provides that a position in the classified service with the Nevada
21 State Service Corps may be filled by an appointing authority without competitive evaluation.
22 Section 3 of this bill provides that employment for [temporary] service with the Nevada
23 State Service Corps is an exception to the limitation on the number of hours an agency may
24 appoint a person for temporary service without regard to the regulations adopted by the
25 Human Resources Commission.
Assembly Amendment No. 129 to Assembly Bill No. 285 Page 4
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 284 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. The Nevada State Service Corps is hereby established as a program
4 within the Department of Administration.
5 2. The Department shall [:
6 (a) Develop] develop and oversee the Nevada State Service Corps to provide
7 for the placement and mentoring of recent graduates of the Nevada System of
8 Higher Education for [temporary] service in positions with the Executive
9 Department of the State Government . [; and
10 (b) Consult with the Nevada System of Higher Education to develop and
11 oversee the Nevada State Service Corps pursuant to paragraph (a).]
12 3. In overseeing the Nevada State Service Corps, the Department shall [:
13 (a) Recruit] recruit students who are graduating or graduated within the
14 immediately preceding year from the Nevada System of Higher Education to
15 participate in the Nevada State Service Corps . [;
16 (b) Designate positions with the Executive Department of the State
17 Government in which participants may provide temporary service;
18 (c) Match participants with the designated positions and place participants in
19 these positions;
20 (d) Provide mentoring to the participants; and
21 (e) Promote the opportunities to the participants for full-time employment
22 with the Executive Department of the State Government.]
23 4. The Director of the Department shall determine the number of students
24 who may participate in the Nevada State Service Corps in each year.
25 5. The [Board of Regents of the University of Nevada may request that the]
26 Legislative Commission shall appoint a legislator to serve as an advisor to
27 participants in the Nevada State Service Corps, promote the benefits of state
28 service and provide encouragement to the participants. A legislator appointed
29 pursuant to this subsection serves without compensation and is not entitled to the
30 per diem and travel expenses provided for state officers and employees generally.
31 6. The Director of the Department may:
32 (a) Apply for and receive gifts, grants, donations and bequests to carry out
33 the provisions of this section; and
34 (b) Adopt regulations to carry out the provisions of this section.
35 Sec. 2. NRS 284.305 is hereby amended to read as follows:
36 284.305 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 2, positions in the
37 classified service may be filled by an appointing authority without competitive
38 evaluation only as provided in NRS 284.155, 284.300, 284.307, 284.308, 284.309,
39 284.310, 284.315, 284.320, 284.325, 284.327, 284.330, 284.375 and 284.3775 [.]
40 and section 1 of this act.
41 2. The Commission may adopt regulations which provide for filling positions
42 in the classified service without competitive evaluation in cases involving:
43 (a) The appointment of a current employee with a disability to a position at or
44 below the grade of his or her position if the employee becomes unable to perform
45 the essential functions of his or her position with or without reasonable
46 accommodation;
47 (b) The demotion of a current employee;
48 (c) The reemployment of a current or former employee who was or will be
49 adversely affected by layoff, military service, reclassification or a permanent partial
Assembly Amendment No. 129 to Assembly Bill No. 285 Page 5
1 disability arising out of and in the course of the employment of the current or
2 former employee; or
3 (d) The reappointment of a current employee.
4 Sec. 3. NRS 284.325 is hereby amended to read as follows:
5 284.325 1. The Commission shall adopt regulations not inconsistent with
6 this section for the certification of qualified persons for temporary service.
7 2. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 3, temporary positions which
8 occur, terminate or recur periodically must be filled by certification in accordance
9 with the regulations adopted by the Commission.
10 3. An agency may appoint persons temporarily for less than 160 cumulative
11 hours during any calendar year without regard to the regulations adopted by the
12 Commission pursuant to subsection 1.
13 4. The limitation on hours set forth in subsection 3 does not apply to
14 temporary or part-time service by:
15 (a) A pupil attending the pupil’s last 2 years of high school;
16 (b) A student employed by the college or university the student attends;
17 (c) A person certified for temporary service in accordance with NRS 284.327;
18 (d) An employee of an events center, museum or research center of the Nevada
19 System of Higher Education;
20 (e) A person employed by the Nevada System of Higher Education in a
21 temporary position which recurs periodically for the registration of students; [or]
22 (f) A person employed by the Nevada System of Higher Education to provide
23 such assistance to a student with a disability or to a student with an identified
24 academic disadvantage as it determines is necessary for the academic success of the
25 student, including, without limitation, a person employed as a tutor, note taker,
26 reader, sign interpreter or test proctor [.] ; or
27 (g) A person employed for [temporary] service with the Nevada State Service
28 Corps established pursuant to section 1 of this act.
29 5. The acceptance or refusal by an eligible person of a temporary appointment
30 does not affect the person’s standing on the register for permanent employment, nor
31 may the period of temporary service be counted as part of the probationary period
32 in case of subsequent appointment to a permanent position.
33 6. Successive temporary appointments to the same position must not be made
34 under this section.
35 7. As used in this section, “student with an identified academic disadvantage”
36 includes, without limitation, a student who the Nevada System of Higher Education
37 has determined requires the services of a tutor for success in a course of study.
38 Sec. 4. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
