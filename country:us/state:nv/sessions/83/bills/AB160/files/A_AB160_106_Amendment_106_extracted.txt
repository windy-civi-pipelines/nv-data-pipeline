A.B. No. 160—Creates the Nevada-Ireland Trade Commission. (BDR 18-650)

Referred to Committee on Revenue
SUMMARY—Creates the Nevada-Ireland Trade Commission. (BDR 18-650)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to economic development; creating the Nevada-Ireland Trade
Commission; prescribing the membership, powers and duties of the
Commission; creating the Account for the Nevada-Ireland Trade
Commission; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Board of Economic Development, which is required to review
2 and make recommendations on various aspects of economic development in Nevada. (NRS
3 231.033, 231.037) Section 3 of this bill creates the Nevada-Ireland Trade Commission, which
4 consists of nine members. Section 4 of this bill: (1) provides for the officers of the
5 Commission; (2) prescribes requirements for meetings of the Commission; and (3) requires
6 the Office of Economic Development to provide necessary administrative support to the
7 Commission. Section 5 of this bill requires the Commission to study and make
8 recommendations to the Board of Economic Development regarding: (1) advancing bilateral
9 trade and investment between Nevada and Ireland; (2) initiating joint action on policy issues
10 of mutual interest to Nevada and Ireland; (3) promoting business and academic exchanges
11 between Nevada and Ireland; (4) encouraging mutual economic support between Nevada and
12 Ireland; (5) encouraging mutual investment in the infrastructure of Nevada and Ireland; and
13 (6) such other issues as determined by the Commission. Section 5 also requires the
14 Commission to prepare and submit an annual report of its activities and recommendations to
15 the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau and the Governor. Section 6 of this bill creates
16 the Account for the Nevada-Ireland Trade Commission in the State General Fund, which is
17 required to be administered by the Commission and only used to carry out the activities of the
18 Commission. Section 6 also: (1) authorizes the Commission to apply for grants and accept
19 gifts, grants, bequests and other contributions; and (2) requires that any such money received
20 by the Commission be deposited in the Account. Section 2 of this bill defines the term
21 “Commission” for purposes of sections 3-6.
1 WHEREAS, During the early mining boom in Nevada in the mid-1800s, Irish
2 immigrants were a significant part of this State’s population and workforce; and
3 WHEREAS, The rich Irish heritage of Nevada is reflected in the names of many
4 prominent Nevadans, including, McCarran, Mackay, Manogue and O’Callaghan,
5 who made important contributions to this State in the areas of religion, government,
6 development, business and gaming; and
Assembly Amendment No. 106 to Assembly Bill No. 160 Page 4
1 WHEREAS, There are significant and enduring connections between Nevada
2 and Ireland, and Nevada has historic and practical reasons to desire the building of
3 stronger economic, educational, governmental and cultural links with Ireland; and
4 WHEREAS, In light of the mutual economic interests of Nevada and Ireland and
5 the need to adapt to the evolving global economy, creation of a trade commission
6 demonstrates a shared commitment to fostering stronger commercial ties between
7 Nevada and Ireland to encourage investment between, leverage the resources of and
8 create mutually beneficial opportunities for both regions; and
9 WHEREAS, A trade commission will also serve as a platform for educational
10 and cultural exchanges and for building a lasting and collaborative relationship with
11 a global partner; now, therefore,
12
13 THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
14 SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
15
16 Section 1. Chapter 231 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
17 provisions set forth as sections 2 to 6, inclusive, of this act.
18 Sec. 2. As used in sections 2 to 6, inclusive, of this act, unless the context
19 otherwise requires, “Commission” means the Nevada-Ireland Trade Commission
20 created by section 3 of this act.
21 Sec. 3. 1. There is hereby created the Nevada-Ireland Trade Commission,
22 consisting of the following nine members:
23 (a) Two members of the Senate, appointed by the Majority Leader of the
24 Senate;
25 (b) Two members of the Assembly, appointed by the Speaker of the
26 Assembly; and
27 (c) Five members appointed by the Governor, including, without limitation:
28 (1) A representative of the Nevada System of Higher Education;
29 (2) A representative of a chamber of commerce or trade association; and
30 (3) A representative of the general public who represents Irish-American
31 communities or interests in this State.
32 2. After the initial terms, each member of the Commission serves a term of
33 2 years. A member of the Commission may be reappointed.
34 3. The membership of any member of the Commission who is a Legislator
35 and who is not a candidate for reelection or who is defeated for reelection
36 terminates on the day next after the general election.
37 4. Any vacancy occurring in the membership of the Commission must be
38 filled in the same manner as the original appointment. A member appointed to fill
39 a vacancy serves as a member of the Commission for the remainder of the
40 original term of appointment.
41 5. Each member of the Commission:
42 (a) Serves without compensation; and
43 (b) While engaged in the business of the Commission, is entitled to receive
44 the per diem allowance and travel expenses provided for state officers and
45 employees generally.
46 Sec. 4. 1. The members of the Commission shall elect a Chair and Vice
47 Chair from among its members. After the initial election, the Chair and Vice
48 Chair serve for a term of 1 year beginning on July 1 of each year. If the position
49 of Chair or Vice Chair becomes vacant, the members of the Commission shall
50 elect a Chair or Vice Chair, as appropriate, from among its members for the
51 remainder of the unexpired term.
52 2. The Commission shall meet at the call of the Chair or a majority of the
53 members of the Commission.
Assembly Amendment No. 106 to Assembly Bill No. 160 Page 5
1 3. A majority of the members of the Commission constitutes a quorum and
2 is required to transact any business of the Commission.
3 4. The Office shall provide the Commission with such administrative
4 support as is necessary to assist the Commission in carrying out the provisions of
5 sections 2 to 6, inclusive, of this act.
6 Sec. 5. 1. The Commission shall study and make recommendations to the
7 Board regarding:
8 (a) Advancing bilateral trade and investment between Nevada and Ireland;
9 (b) Initiating joint action on policy issues of mutual interest to Nevada and
10 Ireland;
11 (c) Promoting business and academic exchanges between Nevada and
12 Ireland;
13 (d) Encouraging mutual economic support between Nevada and Ireland;
14 (e) Encouraging mutual investment in the infrastructure of Nevada and
15 Ireland; and
16 (f) Such other issues as determined by the Commission.
17 2. On or before October 1 of each year, the Commission shall:
18 (a) Prepare a written report of its activities during the immediately preceding
19 fiscal year and any recommendations; and
20 (b) Submit a copy of the written report prepared pursuant to paragraph (a)
21 to:
22 (1) The Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to:
23 (I) If the report is prepared in an even-numbered year, the next
24 regular session of the Legislature; or
25 (II) If the report is prepared in an odd-numbered year, the
26 Legislative Commission.
27 (2) The Governor.
28 Sec. 6. 1. The Account for the Nevada-Ireland Trade Commission is
29 hereby created in the State General Fund.
30 2. The Commission shall administer the Account. The money in the
31 Account must be used only to carry out the provisions of sections 2 to 6, inclusive,
32 of this act, including, without limitation, for payment of administrative costs and
33 expenses relating to travel, meals and lodging.
34 3. The Commission may apply for any available grants and accept gifts,
35 grants, bequests and other contributions from any public or private source for
36 deposit in the Account.
37 4. The interest and income earned on the money in the Account, after
38 deducting applicable charges, must be credited to the Account.
39 5. Money in the Account must remain in the Account and must not revert to
40 the State General Fund at the end of any fiscal year.
41 Sec. 7. The provisions of NRS 218D.380 do not apply to any provision of
42 this act which adds or revises a requirement to submit a report to the Legislature.
43 Sec. 8. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
