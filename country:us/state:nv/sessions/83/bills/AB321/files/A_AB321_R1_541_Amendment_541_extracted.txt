SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to offenders. (BDR 16-1015)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to offenders; authorizing the Director of the Department of
Corrections to establish a transitional housing program for certain
offenders to develop skills and training relating to forestry
management and conservation; requiring the State Forester Firewarden
to establish a hiring program to appoint certain former offenders to
firefighting positions; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, the Director of the Department of Corrections is authorized to enter
2 into one or more contracts with one or more public or private entities to provide certain
3 services to offenders, including transitional housing. (NRS 209.4889) Section 4.5 of this bill
4 authorizes the Director to establish, in coordination with the Division of Forestry of the State
5 Department of Conservation and Natural Resources, a transitional housing program to
6 provide training in forestry management and conservation for certain offenders who [are
7 not more than 18 months from eligibility for parole to receive training in forestry management
8 and conservation.] : (1) are committed to the custody of the Department and eligible for
9 assignment to an institution or facility of minimum security; and (2) have previously
10 been denied parole solely due to a lack of other available transitional housing.
11 Under existing law, the State Forester Firewarden, in cooperation with the Department of
12 Corrections, is required to establish and carry out a program for operating conservation camps
13 in this State which may use offenders who meet certain requirements to perform certain work,
14 including firefighting. (NRS 209.457) Section 5 of this bill requires the State Forester
15 Firewarden to establish a hiring program to appoint former offenders who performed
16 firefighting work in such conservation camps to firefighting positions in the Division of
17 Forestry of the State Department of Conservation and Natural Resources.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)

1 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 4.5. Chapter 209 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
4 section to read as follows:
5 The Director may establish, in coordination with the Division of Forestry of
6 the State Department of Conservation and Natural Resources, a transitional
7 housing program to provide training in forestry management and conservation
8 for offenders who [are:
9 1. Not more than 18 months from eligibility for parole to receive training in
10 forestry management and conservation; and
11 2. Committed] :
12 1. Are committed to the custody of the Department and eligible for
13 assignment to an institution or facility of minimum security pursuant to the
14 provisions of NRS 209.481 [.] ; and
15 2. Have previously been denied parole solely due to a lack of other available
16 transitional housing.
17 Sec. 5. Chapter 472 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
18 section to read as follows:
19 1. The State Forester Firewarden shall establish a hiring program to
20 provide a pathway to hire for firefighting positions within this State. Under the
21 hiring program, the State Forester Firewarden may appoint a person to fill a
22 position in the classified service as a firefighter with the Division of Forestry of
23 the State Department of Conservation and Natural Resources if the person:
24 (a) Was a former offender assigned to work in a program for operating
25 conservation camps established pursuant to NRS 209.457 and performed work
26 relating to firefighting;
27 (b) Successfully completed the training necessary to prepare the offenders
28 assigned to the conservation camp to assist in firefighting pursuant to NRS
29 209.457;
30 (c) Has obtained any certifications required for employment as a firefighter
31 with the Division of Forestry; and
32 (d) Meets any other qualifications established by the State Forester
33 Firewarden.
34 2. The State Forester Firewarden may adopt regulations to carry out the
35 provisions of this section.
36 Sec. 6. (Deleted by amendment.)
