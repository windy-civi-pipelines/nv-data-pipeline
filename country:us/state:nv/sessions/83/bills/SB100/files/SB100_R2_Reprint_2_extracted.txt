SECOND REPRINT S.B. 100
SENATE BILL NO. 100–SENATOR DALY
PREFILED JANUARY 16, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to elections.
(BDR 24-197)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to elections; establishing provisions governing the
performance of certain election duties in a timely manner;
requiring the approval of the Secretary of State relating to
certain vendors of mechanical voting systems and
mechanical recording devices used in an election; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, a public officer or other person, upon whom any duty is
2 imposed under the provisions governing the election laws of this State, who
3 willfully neglects his or her duty or willfully performs the duty in such a way as to
4 hinder the objects and purposes of the election laws is guilty of a category E felony,
5 which is punishable by a minimum term of imprisonment of 1 year and a maximum
6 term of not more than 4 years and may be subject to a fine of not more than $5,000.
7 If the person is a public officer and is convicted of this offense, his or her office is
8 forfeited. (NRS 293.800)
9 Section 1 of this bill requires the Secretary of State to adopt regulations to
10 ensure certain duties relating to an election are completed and carried out in a
11 timely manner. Section 1 requires a public officer or other person to notify the
12 Secretary of State and the Attorney General whether he or she will be able to
13 perform such a duty by the deadline. Section 1 also provides that if a public officer
14 or other person who represents or is employed by a county or city, upon whom a
15 duty set forth in the regulations adopted by the Secretary of State, fails to perform
16 his or her duty in a timely manner by the deadline, the Secretary of State must: (1)
17 notify the governing body of the county or city that employs or oversees the public
18 officer or other person or the public officer, if the public officer is not employed or
19 overseen by the governing body of the county or city; and (2) offer to assist the
20 public officer or other person in carrying out the duty. Section 1 further provides
21 that after providing notice: (1) if the governing body or public officer fails to
22 initiate action, the Secretary of State is required to refer a complaint for prosecution
23 to the Attorney General; and (2) the Secretary of State may seek a writ of
-
*SB100_R2*
– 2 –
24 mandamus to compel the public officer or other person to perform the duty if the
25 public officer or other person fails to initiate action to perform the duty not more
26 than 72 hours after receiving notice from the Secretary of State.
27 Existing law requires: (1) all mechanical voting systems and mechanical
28 recording devices purchased or leased in or used in any election of this State to be
29 approved by the Secretary of State; and (2) a city or county that wishes to change or
30 improve a mechanical voting system or mechanical recording device that has been
31 approved by the Secretary of State to obtain the approval of the Secretary of State.
32 (NRS 293B.1045) Section 3 of this bill requires: (1) a vendor of a mechanical
33 voting system or mechanical recording device to also be approved by the Secretary
34 of State; and (2) a city or county that wishes to change the vendor of the
35 mechanical voting system or mechanical recording device to obtain the approval of
36 the Secretary of State.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 293 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Secretary of State shall adopt regulations to ensure
4 that election duties are completed and carried out in a timely
5 manner in accordance with any deadline set forth in this title or a
6 regulation adopted pursuant to this title. The regulations adopted
7 by the Secretary of State must, without limitation:
8 (a) Set forth the types of election duties that must be carried
9 out in a timely manner;
10 (b) Set forth any procedural steps necessary to carry out an
11 election duty set forth pursuant to paragraph (a) in a timely
12 manner;
13 (c) Establish the timelines for carrying out the election duties
14 and procedural steps set forth pursuant to paragraphs (a) and (b);
15 (d) Establish the circumstances that constitute performing an
16 election duty set forth pursuant to paragraph (a) in a timely
17 manner;
18 (e) Establish the circumstances or events that may cause an
19 unforeseen delay, through no fault of a public officer or other
20 person who represents or is employed by a county or city or agency
21 thereof, including, without limitation, a county or city clerk, in
22 performing an election duty set forth pursuant to paragraph (a)
23 resulting in a failure to carry out the election duty in a timely
24 manner;
25 (f) Establish an electronic method for a public officer or other
26 person who represents or is employed by a county or city or agency
27 thereof, to provide the notice required pursuant to subsection 2 to
28 the Secretary of State and the Attorney General; and
29 (g) Set forth any other duties relating to the policy of elections
30 in this State that must be completed in accordance with the
-
*SB100_R2*
– 3 –
1 provisions of this title or any regulation adopted pursuant to this
2 title but are not subject to the timelines established pursuant to
3 paragraph (c).
4 2. A public officer or other person who represents or is
5 employed by a county or city or agency thereof, including, without
6 limitation, a county or city clerk, shall notify the Secretary of State
7 and the Attorney General whether the public officer or other
8 person will be able to perform an election duty set forth in the
9 regulations adopted by the Secretary of State pursuant to
10 subsection 1 by the deadline established by this title or any
11 regulation adopted pursuant to this title.
12 3. If a public officer or other person who represents or is
13 employed by a county or city or agency thereof and upon whom
14 any election duty set forth in the regulations adopted by the
15 Secretary of State pursuant to subsection 1 is imposed fails to
16 carry out that election duty in a timely manner in accordance with
17 the applicable deadlines set forth in this title or any regulation
18 adopted pursuant to this title for the performance of such duties,
19 the Secretary of State shall immediately provide notice to:
20 (a) In the case of a public officer or other person that is
21 employed or overseen by a governing body of a county or city, the
22 governing body of the county or city that employs or oversees the
23 public officer or other person; and
24 (b) In the case of an elected public officer that is not employed
25 or overseen by a governing body of a county or city, the public
26 officer.
27 4. The notice provided pursuant to subsection 3 must:
28 (a) Request:
29 (1) If the governing body of a county or city employs or
30 oversees the public officer or other person, that the governing
31 body compel the public officer or other person to carry out the
32 election duty; or
33 (2) If a governing body of a county or city does not employ
34 or oversee the public officer, that the public officer carry out the
35 election duty.
36 (b) Include, without limitation:
37 (1) Sufficient detail regarding the election duty that is not
38 being performed by the applicable deadline; and
39 (2) An offer from the Secretary of State to assist the public
40 officer or other person in carrying out the election duty.
41 5. If, not more than 48 hours after sending the notice
42 required:
43 (a) Pursuant to paragraph (a) of subsection 3, the governing
44 body of the county or city fails to initiate action to compel the
45 public officer or other person to carry out an election duty, the
-
*SB100_R2*
– 4 –
1 Secretary of State shall refer a complaint against the members of
2 the governing body to the Attorney General for prosecution
3 pursuant to NRS 283.440 and 293.800.
4 (b) Pursuant to paragraph (b) of subsection 3, the public
5 officer fails to initiate action to carry out the election duty, the
6 Secretary of State shall immediately refer a complaint against the
7 public officer to the Attorney General for prosecution pursuant to
8 NRS 283.440 and 293.800.
9 6. The Attorney General shall initiate proceedings not more
10 than 30 days after a complaint is referred to the Attorney General
11 by the Secretary of State pursuant to subsection 5.
12 7. In addition to the remedies set forth in subsection 5, if, not
13 more than 72 hours after receiving notice pursuant to subsection
14 3, a public officer or other person fails to initiate action to perform
15 an election duty set forth in the regulations adopted by the
16 Secretary of State pursuant to subsection 1 in a timely manner in
17 accordance with the deadlines set forth in statute or regulation for
18 the performance of such duties, the Secretary of State may seek a
19 writ of mandamus to compel the public officer or other person to
20 perform the election duty.
21 8. As used in this section, “election duty” means a ministerial
22 duty relating to an election that is required to be carried out
23 pursuant to this title or a regulation adopted pursuant thereto.
24 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
25 Sec. 3. NRS 293B.1045 is hereby amended to read as follows:
26 293B.1045 1. No mechanical voting system or mechanical
27 recording device may be purchased or leased in or used in any
28 election of this State unless the mechanical voting system or
29 mechanical recording device and the vendor of the mechanical
30 voting system or mechanical recording device is approved by the
31 Secretary of State.
32 2. A [person who owns or has an interest in] vendor of a
33 mechanical voting system or mechanical recording device may
34 submit an application to the Secretary of State for approval of the
35 vendor and to have the system or device examined for approval for
36 use during the elections of this State. The Secretary of State shall
37 approve or disapprove the vendor and use of such a system or
38 device not later than 120 days after the application is submitted.
39 3. As a condition to approval, the [person] vendor shall have
40 the mechanical voting system or mechanical recording device
41 independently examined by a person approved by the Secretary of
42 State. The examiner shall:
43 (a) Review and analyze any electronic or computerized features
44 of the system or device; and
-
*SB100_R2*
– 5 –
1 (b) Prepare and submit to the Secretary of State a report of the
2 results of the examination which includes a statement of the opinion
3 of the examiner regarding the feasibility of using such a system or
4 device during the elections of this State, with consideration for the
5 safe and proper operation of the system or device under the
6 conditions prescribed by the applicable election laws.
7 4. Any cost for the independent examination of a mechanical
8 voting system or mechanical recording device conducted pursuant to
9 subsection 3 must be paid by the [person] vendor who submits an
10 application [to have the system or device approved] for approval by
11 the Secretary of State.
12 5. The Secretary of State shall approve a mechanical voting
13 system or mechanical recording device for use during the elections
14 of this State if:
15 (a) The report prepared pursuant to subsection 3 states that the
16 system or device can be used safely and properly in this State; and
17 (b) The Secretary of State determines after independently
18 examining the system or device that it can be used safely and
19 properly in this State.
20 6. Before a city or county may change a vendor or otherwise
21 change or improve a mechanical voting system or mechanical
22 recording device that has been approved by the Secretary of State
23 pursuant to this section, the city or county must obtain approval
24 from the Secretary of State. If any such change or improvement
25 does not comply with the requirements of this section, the Secretary
26 of State shall not approve the vendor or the use or sale of any
27 system or device that incorporates the change or improvement in
28 this State.
29 7. The Secretary of State may reexamine a mechanical voting
30 system or mechanical recording device or any part thereof at any
31 time for the purpose of approving a change or improvement
32 pursuant to subsection 6 or to ensure that the system or device
33 continues to comply with the election laws of this State.
34 8. The Secretary of State and any examiner of a mechanical
35 voting system or mechanical recording device must not have any
36 pecuniary interest in the system or device examined.
37 9. The Secretary of State may establish regulations to carry out
38 the provisions of this section.
39 10. As used in this section, “vendor” means a person who
40 owns or has an interest in a mechanical voting system or
41 mechanical recording device.
42 Sec. 4. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
43 approval.
44 2. Sections 1 and 2 of this act become effective:
-
*SB100_R2*
– 6 –
1 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
2 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
3 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
4 (b) On January 1, 2026, for all other purposes.
5 3. Section 3 of this act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
