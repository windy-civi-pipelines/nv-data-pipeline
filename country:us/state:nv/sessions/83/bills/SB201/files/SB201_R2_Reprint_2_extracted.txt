Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Prohibits certain restrictions on the display of
religious or cultural items under certain
circumstances. (BDR 10-122)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to religious and cultural expression; prohibiting a
unit-owners’ association, a unit’s owner who rents or
leases his or her unit or a landlord from imposing certain
restrictions on the display of religious or cultural items;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the unit-owners’ association of a common-interest
2 community to adopt bylaws and authorizes an association to amend bylaws and
3 adopt rules and regulations concerning the community. (NRS 116.3102) Section 1
4 of this bill restricts, with certain exceptions, an association or unit’s owner who
5 rents or leases his or her unit from prohibiting a unit’s owner or occupant of a unit
6 from engaging in the display of religious or cultural items. Sections 1.3 and 1.7 of
7 this bill impose a similar restriction on landlords.
8 For the purposes of these restrictions, sections 1 and 1.3 define “display of
9 religious or cultural items” to mean, in general, an item displayed or affixed in or
10 on a unit or dwelling, as applicable, because of sincerely held religious or cultural
11 beliefs, practices or traditions.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 116 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 2 or 3, the
4 executive board of an association or a unit’s owner who rents or
5 leases his or her unit shall not and the governing documents of an
6 association or rental agreement must not prohibit a unit’s owner
7 or an occupant of a unit from engaging in the display of religious
8 or cultural items within such physical portion of the common-
9 interest community as that owner or occupant has a right to
10 occupy and use exclusively.
11 2. The provisions of this section do not:
12 (a) Apply to a display of religious or cultural items that:
13 (1) Has a total size that is greater than 36 by 12 square
14 inches or that exceeds the size of the door on which or whose
15 frame on which the display of religious or cultural items is
16 displayed or affixed;
17 (2) Has a total size that is greater than 36 by 12 square
18 inches that is immediately adjacent or affixed to the entry of a
19 unit;
20 (3) Threatens the health, safety or welfare of the public;
21 (4) Hinders the opening or closing of any entry door;
22 (5) Violates any federal, state or local law;
23 (6) Promotes discriminatory behavior; or
24 (7) Contains graphics, language or any display that is
25 obscene or otherwise illegal.
26 (b) Preclude an association or a unit’s owner who rents or
27 leases his or her unit from adopting, and do not preclude the
28 governing documents of an association or a rental agreement
29 from setting forth, rules that reasonably restrict the placement and
30 manner of the display of religious or cultural items by a unit’s
31 owner or an occupant of a unit.
32 3. An association or a unit’s owner who is performing or
33 causing to be performed any maintenance, repair or replacement
34 of an entry door or doorframe of a unit may:
35 (a) Remove a display of religious or cultural items on the entry
36 door or doorframe during the time the work is being performed in
37 accordance with the provisions of this paragraph. An association
38 or unit’s owner shall, before temporarily removing a display of
39 religious or cultural items pursuant to this paragraph, provide
40 written notice at least 7 days before the work is performed, except
41 in an emergency, to the affected unit’s owner or occupant of the
42 unit. If, following receipt of such written notice, the unit’s owner

2 cultural items or fails, within a reasonable period, to respond to
3 the written notice, then the association or unit’s owner may
4 temporarily remove the display of religious or cultural items. The
5 association or unit’s owner shall store the item or items with
6 respect and in accordance with the appropriate religious or
7 cultural practice, tradition or custom and document compliance
8 with this paragraph. After the work is completed, the association
9 or unit’s owner shall return the display of religious or cultural
10 items to the entry door or doorframe within 72 hours after the
11 work is completed.
12 (b) Require the unit’s owner or an occupant of the unit to
13 remove a display of religious or cultural items on the entry door or
14 doorframe during the time the work is being performed in

16 or unit’s owner shall, before requiring a unit’s owner or occupant
17 of a unit to temporarily remove a display of religious or cultural

20 the affected unit’s owner or occupant. After the work is completed,
21 the unit’s owner or occupant must be permitted to return the
22 display of religious or cultural items to the entry door or
23 doorframe within 72 hours after the work is completed.
24 4. In any action commenced to enforce the provisions of this
25 section, the prevailing party is entitled to recover reasonable

30 religious or cultural beliefs, practices or traditions.
31 (b) “Rental agreement” means an oral or written agreement
32 between a unit’s owner and another person for the use and
33 occupancy of a unit.
