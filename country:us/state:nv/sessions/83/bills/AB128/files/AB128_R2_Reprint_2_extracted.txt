Section 1:
SECOND REPRINT A.B. 128
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 128–COMMITTEE
ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS)
PREFILED JANUARY 28, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Creates the Public Records Task Force.
(BDR S-400)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public records; creating the Public Records
Task Force; setting forth the membership and duties of
the Task Force; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides that, unless otherwise declared by law to be confidential,
2 all public books and records of a state or local governmental entity are required to
3 be open at all times during office hours for the public to inspect, copy or receive a
4 copy thereof. Existing law also authorizes a person to request a copy of a public
5 book or record in any medium in which the book or record is available. (NRS
6 239.010) This bill creates the Public Records Task Force to evaluate certain topics
7 relating to requests for public records and make certain recommendations. This bill
8 also: (1) prescribes the membership and duties of the Task Force; and (2) requires
9 the Task Force to submit a written report describing its work and recommendations
10 for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Government Affairs and
11 the Senate and Assembly Standing Committees on Government Affairs for the 84th
12 Session of the Legislature.

SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:

5 Sec. 7. 1. The Public Records Task Force is hereby created.
6 The Task Force consists of the following 10 members:
7 (a) One government representative and two transparency
8 advocates appointed by the Majority Leader of the Senate;
9 (b) Two government representatives and one transparency
10 advocate appointed by the Speaker of the Assembly;
11 (c) One government representative appointed by the Minority
12 Leader of the Senate;
13 (d) One transparency advocate appointed by the Minority
14 Leader of the Assembly; and
15 (e) One government representative and one transparency
16 advocate appointed by the Governor.
17 2. The Task Force shall:
18 (a) Evaluate:
19 (1) Current exemptions to chapter 239 of NRS;
20 (2) The policies relating to public records of any review
21 board created pursuant to NRS 289.380 or 289.383;
22 (3) The impact of broad requests for public records on
23 governmental entities;
24 (4) The cost, burden and time constraint on governmental
25 entities to redact confidential information;
26 (5) The financial burden on a person who requests a public
27 record;
28 (6) Mechanisms to resolve disputes involving requests for
29 public records, including, without limitation, mediation and other
30 types of alternative dispute resolution; and
31 (7) Methods for collecting and sharing data related to
32 requests for public records.
33 (b) Make recommendations, including, without limitation:
34 (1) To clarify the custodianship and control of public
35 records;
36 (2) To protect sensitive information while promoting
37 government transparency;
38 (3) To identify situations in which access to public records
39 should be granted or denied;
40 (4) For penalties to impose against governmental entities for
41 providing delayed or incomplete responses to a request for public
42 records;
43 (5) For protocols to protect personal information and
44 criminal investigations;
45 (6) For a standardized fee schedule; and
-
*AB128_R2*
– 3 –
1 (7) For mechanisms to protect governmental entities from
2 retaliatory litigation.
3 3. The members of the Task Force serve without
4 compensation.
5 4. Each member of the Task Force who is an officer or
6 employee of the State or local government must be relieved from his
7 or her duties without loss of regular compensation so that the
8 member may prepare for and attend meetings of the Task Force and
9 perform any work necessary to carry out the duties of the Task
10 Force in the most timely manner practicable. A state agency or local
11 government shall not require an officer or employee who is a
12 member of the Task Force to make up the time the member is absent
13 from work to carry out his or her duties as a member, and shall not
14 require the member to take annual, vacation or compensatory time
15 for the absence.
16 5. The Task Force shall, at its first meeting, elect a Chair from
17 the members who are transparency advocates and a Vice Chair from
18 the members who are government representatives.
19 6. The Task Force shall meet:
20 (a) Upon the call of the Chair or a majority of the members of
21 the Task Force; and
22 (b) At least four times.
23 7. A majority of the members of the Task Force constitutes a
24 quorum for the transaction of business, and a majority of those
25 members present at the meeting is sufficient for any official action
26 taken by the Task Force.
27 8. The Task Force may apply for and accept any gift, donation,
28 bequest, grant or other source of money to carry out the duties of the
29 Task Force.
30 9. On or before October 31, 2026, the Task Force shall prepare
31 and submit a written report to the Director of the Legislative
32 Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing
33 Committee on Government Affairs and the Senate and Assembly
34 Standing Committees on Government Affairs for the 84th Session
35 of the Legislature. The report must include, without limitation, a
36 summary of the work and recommendations of the Task Force.
37 10. As used in this section:
38 (a) “Governmental entity” has the meaning ascribed to it in
39 NRS 239.005.
40 (b) “Government representative” includes, without limitation, a
41 representative:
42 (1) From the Nevada Association of Counties or a county;
43 (2) From the Nevada League of Cities or a local government;
44 (3) Who is a district attorney or city attorney;
45 (4) From a law enforcement agency;
-
*AB128_R2*
– 4 –
1 (5) From a school district;
2 (6) From the Office of the Attorney General;
3 (7) From a public utility that is not regulated by the Public
4 Utilities Commission of Nevada; and
5 (8) From the Division of State Library, Archives and Public
6 Records of the Department of Administration.
7 (c) “Transparency advocate” includes, without limitation, a
8 representative:
9 (1) From the Nevada Broadcasters Association;
10 (2) From the Nevada Press Association;
11 (3) From the Nevada Open Government Coalition;
12 (4) From the American Civil Liberties Union;
13 (5) Who is an attorney that specializes in requests for public
14 records;
15 (6) From the Legal Aid Center of Southern Nevada; and
16 (7) Who is a member of the public and has experience related
17 to public records.
18 Sec. 8. The members of the Public Records Task Force
19 must be appointed pursuant to section 7 of this act not later than
20 October 31, 2025.
21 Sec. 9. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025, and expires
22 by limitation on June 30, 2027.
