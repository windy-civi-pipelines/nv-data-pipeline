ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 128–COMMITTEE
ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON GOVERNMENT AFFAIRS)
PREFILED JANUARY 28, 2025
_______________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Creates the Public Records Task Force. (BDR S-400)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public records; creating the Public Records Task Force; setting
forth the membership and duties of the Task Force; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides that, unless otherwise declared by law to be confidential, all public
2 books and records of a state or local governmental entity are required to be open at all times
3 during office hours for the public to inspect, copy or receive a copy thereof. Existing law also
4 authorizes a person to request a copy of a public book or record in any medium in which the
5 book or record is available. (NRS 239.010) This bill creates the Public Records Task Force to
6 evaluate certain topics relating to requests for public records and make certain
7 recommendations. This bill also: (1) prescribes the membership and duties of the Task Force;
8 and (2) requires the Task Force to submit a written report describing its work and
9 recommendations for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Government
10 Affairs and the Senate and Assembly Standing Committees on Government Affairs for the
11 84th Session of the Legislature.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
4 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
5 Sec. 5. (Deleted by amendment.)
6 Sec. 6. (Deleted by amendment.)
7 Sec. 7. 1. The Public Records Task Force is hereby created. The Task
8 Force consists of the following 10 members:
Senate Amendment No. 606 to Assembly Bill No. 128 First Reprint Page 4
1 (a) One government representative and two transparency advocates appointed
2 by the Majority Leader of the Senate;
3 (b) Two government representatives and one transparency advocate appointed
4 by the Speaker of the Assembly;
5 (c) One government representative appointed by the Minority Leader of the
6 Senate;
7 (d) One transparency advocate appointed by the Minority Leader of the
8 Assembly; and
9 (e) One government representative and one transparency advocate appointed
10 by the Governor.
11 2. The Task Force shall:
12 (a) Evaluate:
13 (1) Current exemptions to chapter 239 of NRS;
14 (2) The policies relating to public records of any review board created
15 pursuant to NRS 289.380 or 289.383;
16 (3) The impact of broad requests for public records on governmental
17 entities;
18 [(3)] (4) The cost, burden and time constraint on governmental entities to
19 redact confidential information;
20 [(4)] (5) The financial burden on a person who requests a public record;
21 [(5)] (6) Mechanisms to resolve disputes involving requests for public
22 records, including, without limitation, mediation and other types of alternative
23 dispute resolution; and
24 [(6)] (7) Methods for collecting and sharing data related to requests for
25 public records.
26 (b) Make recommendations, including, without limitation:
27 (1) To clarify the custodianship and control of public records;
28 (2) To protect sensitive information while promoting government
29 transparency;
30 (3) To identify situations in which access to public records should be
31 granted or denied;
32 (4) For penalties to impose against governmental entities for providing
33 delayed or incomplete responses to a request for public records;
34 (5) For protocols to protect personal information and criminal
35 investigations;
36 (6) For a standardized fee schedule; and
37 (7) For mechanisms to protect governmental entities from retaliatory
38 litigation.
39 3. The members of the Task Force serve without compensation.
40 4. Each member of the Task Force who is an officer or employee of the State
41 or local government must be relieved from his or her duties without loss of regular
42 compensation so that the member may prepare for and attend meetings of the Task
43 Force and perform any work necessary to carry out the duties of the Task Force in
44 the most timely manner practicable. A state agency or local government shall not
45 require an officer or employee who is a member of the Task Force to make up the
46 time the member is absent from work to carry out his or her duties as a member,
47 and shall not require the member to take annual, vacation or compensatory time for
48 the absence.
49 5. The Task Force shall, at its first meeting, elect a Chair from the members
50 who are transparency advocates and a Vice Chair from the members who are
51 government representatives.
52 6. The Task Force shall meet:
Senate Amendment No. 606 to Assembly Bill No. 128 First Reprint Page 5
1 (a) Upon the call of the Chair or a majority of the members of the Task Force;
2 and
3 (b) At least four times.
4 7. A majority of the members of the Task Force constitutes a quorum for the
5 transaction of business, and a majority of those members present at the meeting is
6 sufficient for any official action taken by the Task Force.
7 8. The Task Force may apply for and accept any gift, donation, bequest, grant
8 or other source of money to carry out the duties of the Task Force.
9 9. On or before October 31, 2026, the Task Force shall prepare and submit a
10 written report to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to
11 the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Government Affairs and the Senate and
12 Assembly Standing Committees on Government Affairs for the 84th Session of the
13 Legislature. The report must include, without limitation, a summary of the work
14 and recommendations of the Task Force.
15 10. As used in this section:
16 (a) “Governmental entity” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 239.005.
17 (b) “Government representative” includes, without limitation, a representative:
18 (1) From the Nevada Association of Counties or a county;
19 (2) From the Nevada League of Cities or a local government;
20 (3) Who is a district attorney or city attorney;
21 (4) From a law enforcement agency;
22 (5) From a school district;
23 (6) From the Office of the Attorney General;
24 (7) From a public utility that is not regulated by the Public Utilities
25 Commission of Nevada; and
26 (8) From the Division of State Library, Archives and Public Records of the
27 Department of Administration.
28 (c) “Transparency advocate” includes, without limitation, a representative:
29 (1) From the Nevada Broadcasters Association;
30 (2) From the Nevada Press Association;
31 (3) From the Nevada Open Government Coalition;
32 (4) From the American Civil Liberties Union;
33 (5) Who is an attorney that specializes in requests for public records;
34 (6) From the Legal Aid Center of Southern Nevada; and
35 (7) Who is a member of the public and has experience related to public
36 records.
37 Sec. 8. The members of the Public Records Task Force must be appointed
38 pursuant to section 7 of this act not later than October 31, 2025.
39 Sec. 9. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025, and expires by limitation
40 on June 30, 2027.
