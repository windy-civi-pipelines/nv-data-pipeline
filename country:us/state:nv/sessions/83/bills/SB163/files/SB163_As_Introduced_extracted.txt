S.B. 163
PREFILED FEBRUARY 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to bail. (BDR 14-725)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to criminal procedure; revising provisions
governing bail; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes a court to revoke the bail of a defendant upon a
2 showing that the defendant committed a felony during the period of release.
3 Existing law also authorizes a defendant to be held without bail pending the hearing
4 on the revocation. (NRS 178.487) Upon such a showing, section 1 of this bill
5 requires the bail of the defendant to be revoked and the defendant to be held
6 without further bail pending trial of the offense for which the bail was revoked.
7 Section 2 of this bill makes a conforming change by repealing certain
8 procedures that contemplate the pretrial release of a defendant who was arrested for
9 certain felonies while admitted to bail. (NRS 178.4855)
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 178.487 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 178.487 Every release on bail with or without security is
3 conditioned upon the defendant’s good behavior while so released,
4 and upon a showing that the proof is evident or the presumption
5 great that the defendant has committed a felony during the period of
6 release, the defendant’s bail [may] must be revoked, after a hearing,
7 by the magistrate who allowed it or by any judge of the court in
8 which the original charge is pending [. Pending such revocation,]
9 and the defendant [may] must be held without further bail [by order
10 of the magistrate before whom the defendant is brought after an

3 Sec. 2. NRS 178.4855 is hereby repealed.
