FIRST REPRINT A.B. 250
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 250–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS GOULDING;
ANDERSON, FLANAGAN, HUNT, LA RUE HATCH AND
NGUYEN
FEBRUARY 18, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to certain debt.
(BDR 52-599)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to debt; establishing coerced debt as an
affirmative defense in certain civil actions to collect a
consumer debt; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law governs the collection of debt by certain persons in this State,
2 including, without limitation, collection agencies and private debt collectors. (See,
3 e.g., chapters 353C and 649 of NRS) Section 1 of this bill establishes that in any
4 civil action to collect a consumer debt, a coerced debt may be used as an
5 affirmative defense. Section 1 also: (1) sets forth the requirements for establishing
6 a prima facie affirmative defense of coerced debt; and (2) defines “coerced debt” to
7 mean a consumer debt or any part thereof which is the proximate result of a person
8 having been a victim of domestic violence, human trafficking, abuse, coercion,
9 fraud, duress, threats, intimidation, manipulation or other unlawful conduct
10 intended to induce the person to incur debt against his or her own will.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 597 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. In any civil action to collect a consumer debt, it is an
4 affirmative defense that the debt is a coerced debt.
5 2. A debtor establishes a prima facie affirmative defense that
6 a debt is a coerced debt by proving that:
-
*AB250_R1*
– 2 –
1 (a) The debt is a consumer debt accrued in his or her name;
2 (b) The debt was accrued as the proximate result of the debtor
3 having been a victim of domestic violence, human trafficking,
4 abuse, coercion, fraud, duress, threats, intimidation, manipulation
5 or other unlawful conduct; and
6 (c) Being a victim of domestic violence, human trafficking,
7 abuse, coercion, fraud, duress, threats, intimidation, manipulation
8 or other unlawful conduct caused the debtor to incur the debt
9 against the debtor’s will.
10 3. As used in this section, “coerced debt” means a consumer
11 debt or any part thereof which is the proximate result of a person
12 having been a victim of domestic violence, human trafficking,
13 abuse, coercion, fraud, duress, threats, intimidation, manipulation
14 or other unlawful conduct intended to induce the person to incur
15 debt against his or her own will.
16 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
17 Sec. 3. The provisions of this act apply to any action filed on
18 or after October 1, 2025.
