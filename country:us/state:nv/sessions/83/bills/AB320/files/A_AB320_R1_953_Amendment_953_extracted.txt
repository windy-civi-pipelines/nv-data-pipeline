SUMMARY—Revises provisions related to the administration of justice.
(BDR 1-810)

Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to the administration of justice; prohibiting courts from
penalizing defendants who have been denied access to courtrooms
under certain circumstances related to attire; authorizing the Division
of Parole and Probation of the Department of Public Safety to establish
a pilot program relating to employment opportunities and training for
parolees and probationers; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes various provisions related to the courts of this State. (Title 1 of
2 NRS) Section 1.3 of this bill: (1) makes certain legislative declarations recognizing the
3 importance of access to the courts of this State and the interest of the courts in maintaining
4 order and decorum in the courtroom; and (2) provides that the Legislature urges the courts not
5 to create policies, rules or administrative orders that unduly restrict access to justice based on
6 the attire of the parties. Section 1.7 of this bill prohibits a court from imposing a penalty on a
7 defendant who is denied access to a courtroom solely because [of the attire of] the defendant
8 [.] is not wearing formal or professional attire.
9 Section 5 of this bill authorizes the Division of Parole and Probation of the Department of
10 Public Safety to establish a pilot program to identify and develop employment opportunities
11 for and provide training on employment readiness to parolees and probationers. Section 6 of
12 this bill applies definitions in existing law relating to parole to section 5.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 1 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 1.3 and 1.7 of this act.
3 Sec. 1.3. 1. The Legislature hereby finds and declares that access to
4 justice and the courts of this State is of great importance to the people of this
5 State, and the Legislature recognizes the interest of the courts in maintaining
6 order and decorum in the courtroom.
Senate Amendment No. 953 to Assembly Bill No. 320 First Reprint Page 4
1 2. The Legislature therefore urges the courts of this State, for the purpose
2 of recognizing the diverse socioeconomic backgrounds of the people who appear
3 before them, not to create policies, rules or administrative orders that unduly
4 restrict access to justice based on the attire of the parties, witnesses or public.
5 Sec. 1.7. 1. A court may not impose a penalty on a defendant who is
6 denied access to a courtroom solely because [of the attire of] the defendant [.] is
7 not wearing formal or professional attire.
8 2. As used in this section [, “penalty”] :
9 (a) “Formal or professional attire” includes, without limitation, suits,
10 trousers, slacks, long pants, dress shirts, long-sleeved shirts, suit jackets, blazers,
11 ties, dresses, skirts, pantsuits, dress shoes and closed-toed shoes.
12 (b) “Penalty” includes, without limitation:
13 [(a)] (1) A fine or fee;
14 [(b)] (2) Entering upon the minutes that a person failed to appear when
15 lawfully required in court; or
16 [(c)] (3) The issuance of a warrant for arrest.
17 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
18 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
19 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
20 Sec. 5. Chapter 213 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
21 section to read as follows:
22 1. The Division may, by regulation, establish a pilot program to identify and
23 develop employment opportunities for and provide training on employment
24 readiness to parolees and probationers.
25 2. In establishing the pilot program, the Division may collaborate with
26 nonprofit organizations in the community that provide services to parolees and
27 probationers.
28 Sec. 6. NRS 213.107 is hereby amended to read as follows:
29 213.107 As used in NRS 213.107 to 213.157, inclusive, and section 5 of this
30 act, unless the context otherwise requires:
31 1. “Board” means the State Board of Parole Commissioners.
32 2. “Chief” means the Chief Parole and Probation Officer.
33 3. “Division” means the Division of Parole and Probation of the Department
34 of Public Safety.
35 4. “Residential confinement” means the confinement of a person convicted of
36 a crime to his or her place of residence under the terms and conditions established
37 by the Board.
38 5. “Responsivity factors” means characteristics of a person that affect his or
39 her ability to respond favorably or unfavorably to any treatment goals.
40 6. “Risk and needs assessment” means a validated, standardized actuarial tool
41 that identifies risk factors that increase the likelihood of a person reoffending and
42 factors that, when properly addressed, can reduce the likelihood of a person
43 reoffending.
44 7. “Sex offender” means any person who has been or is convicted of a sexual
45 offense.
46 8. “Sexual offense” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 179D.097.
47 9. “Standards” means the objective standards for granting or revoking parole
48 or probation which are adopted by the Board or the Chief.
49 Sec. 7. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and approval.
50 2. Sections 1 to 6, inclusive, of this act become effective:
51 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any regulations or
52 performing any other preparatory administrative tasks that are necessary to carry
53 out the provisions of this act; and
Senate Amendment No. 953 to Assembly Bill No. 320 First Reprint Page 5
1 (b) On October 1, 2025, for all other purposes.
