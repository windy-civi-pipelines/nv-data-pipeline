Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 138
SENATE BILL NO. 138–SENATOR CANNIZZARO
PREFILED JANUARY 30, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Makes revisions governing health care for pregnant
women and newborn children. (BDR 40-580)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§§ 2.4, 2.6)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; requiring certain hospitals to enroll
as a qualified provider for determining whether a pregnant
woman is presumptively eligible for Medicaid; requiring
such hospitals to provide notices relating to Medicaid to
certain persons; prescribing certain rights for parents and
legal guardians of newborn children who are patients in a
neonatal intensive care unit of a hospital; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires certain hospitals to enter into agreements with the United
2 States Secretary of Health and Human Services to accept payment through
3 Medicare. (NRS 449.1821) Existing law also requires the Director of the
4 Department of Health and Human Services to authorize a pregnant woman who is
5 determined to be presumptively eligible for Medicaid to enroll in Medicaid for a
6 specified period of time without submitting an application for enrollment in
7 Medicaid which includes additional proof of eligibility. (NRS 422.27171) Section
8 2.4 of this bill requires a hospital that provides birthing services to: (1) enroll as a
9 qualified provider to determine whether a pregnant woman is presumptively
10 eligible for Medicaid; (2) determine whether each pregnant woman seeking
11 services from the hospital is presumptively eligible for Medicaid; and (3) notify a
12 pregnant woman and the parent or legal guardian of certain newborn children of
13 federal benefits that the woman or child, as applicable, may be eligible to receive.
14 Existing law establishes certain rights for patients of medical facilities and
15 facilities for the dependent. (NRS 449A.100-449A.124) Section 2.6 of this bill
16 establishes certain rights of a parent or legal guardian of a newborn child who is
17 receiving care in a neonatal intensive care unit of a hospital. Section 2.2 of this bill
-
*SB138_R1*
– 2 –
18 authorizes the suspension or revocation of the license of a hospital that fails to
19 honor such rights.

1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 2.2. NRS 449.160 is hereby amended to read as follows:
4 449.160 1. The Division may deny an application for a
5 license or may suspend or revoke any license issued under the
6 provisions of NRS 449.029 to 449.2428, inclusive, upon any of the
7 following grounds:
8 (a) Violation by the applicant or the licensee of any of the
9 provisions of NRS 439B.410, 449.029 to 449.245, inclusive, or
10 449A.100 to 449A.124, inclusive, and section 2.6 of this act, and
11 449A.270 to 449A.286, inclusive, or of any other law of this State
12 or of the standards, rules and regulations adopted thereunder.
13 (b) Aiding, abetting or permitting the commission of any illegal
14 act.
15 (c) Conduct inimical to the public health, morals, welfare and
16 safety of the people of the State of Nevada in the maintenance and
17 operation of the premises for which a license is issued.
18 (d) Conduct or practice detrimental to the health or safety of the
19 occupants or employees of the facility.
20 (e) Failure of the applicant to obtain written approval from the
21 Director of the Department of Health and Human Services as
22 required by NRS 439A.100 or 439A.102 or as provided in any
23 regulation adopted pursuant to NRS 449.001 to 449.430, inclusive,
24 and 449.435 to 449.531, inclusive, and chapter 449A of NRS if such
25 approval is required, including, without limitation, the closure or
26 conversion of any hospital in a county whose population is 100,000
27 or more that is owned by the licensee without approval pursuant to
28 NRS 439A.102.
29 (f) Failure to comply with the provisions of NRS 441A.315 and
30 any regulations adopted pursuant thereto or NRS 449.2486.
31 (g) Violation of the provisions of NRS 458.112.
32 (h) Failure to comply with the provisions of NRS 449A.170 to
33 449A.192, inclusive, and any regulation adopted pursuant thereto.
34 (i) Violation of the provisions of NRS 629.260.
35 2. In addition to the provisions of subsection 1, the Division
36 may revoke a license to operate a facility for the dependent if, with
37 respect to that facility, the licensee that operates the facility, or an
38 agent or employee of the licensee:
-

1 (a) Is convicted of violating any of the provisions of
2 NRS 202.470;
3 (b) Is ordered to but fails to abate a nuisance pursuant to NRS
4 244.360, 244.3603 or 268.4124; or
5 (c) Is ordered by the appropriate governmental agency to correct
6 a violation of a building, safety or health code or regulation but fails
7 to correct the violation.
8 3. The Division shall maintain a log of any complaints that it
9 receives relating to activities for which the Division may revoke the
10 license to operate a facility for the dependent pursuant to subsection
11 2. The Division shall provide to a facility for the care of adults
12 during the day:
13 (a) A summary of a complaint against the facility if the
14 investigation of the complaint by the Division either substantiates
15 the complaint or is inconclusive;
16 (b) A report of any investigation conducted with respect to the
17 complaint; and
18 (c) A report of any disciplinary action taken against the facility.
19  The facility shall make the information available to the public
20 pursuant to NRS 449.2486.
21 4. On or before February 1 of each odd-numbered year, the
22 Division shall submit to the Director of the Legislative Counsel
23 Bureau a written report setting forth, for the previous biennium:
24 (a) Any complaints included in the log maintained by the
25 Division pursuant to subsection 3; and
26 (b) Any disciplinary actions taken by the Division pursuant to
27 subsection 2.
28 Sec. 2.4. NRS 449.1821 is hereby amended to read as follows:
29 449.1821 1. A hospital, other than a psychiatric hospital,
30 critical access hospital or rural hospital, shall enter into an
31 agreement with the United States Secretary of Health and Human
32 Services pursuant to 42 U.S.C. § 1395cc to accept payment through
33 Medicare.
34 2. A hospital that provides birthing services shall:
35 (a) Enroll as a qualified provider to determine whether a
36 pregnant woman is presumptively eligible for Medicaid pursuant
37 to NRS 422.27171;
38 (b) Determine whether each pregnant woman seeking services
39 from the hospital is presumptively eligible for Medicaid; and
40 (c) If a child is born prematurely, with a low birth weight or
41 with any other condition that qualifies the child for benefits under
42 the Supplemental Security Income Program, ensure that the
43 parent or legal guardian of the child receives written notice that
44 the child may be eligible to receive benefits under the
45 Supplemental Security Income Program and Medicaid.
-
*SB138_R1*
– 4 –
1 3. If a qualified member of the staff of a hospital determines
2 that a pregnant woman is:
3 (a) Presumptively eligible for Medicaid, the hospital shall
4 provide the pregnant woman with written information concerning
5 Medicaid. The written information must include, without
6 limitation:
7 (1) The dates on which the presumptive eligibility period
8 begins and ends;
9 (2) A summary of the benefits provided by Medicaid; and
10 (3) Details on the process to apply for enrollment in
11 Medicaid beyond the presumptive eligibility period.
12 (b) Not presumptively eligible for Medicaid, the hospital shall
13 provide the pregnant woman with an explanation of the reason
14 she is not eligible and information on submitting an application to
15 enroll in Medicaid.
16 4. As used in this section:
17 (a) “Presumptive eligibility period” has the meaning ascribed
18 to it in 42 U.S.C. § 1396r-1(b)(1).
19 (b) “Qualified provider” has the meaning ascribed to it in 42
20 U.S.C. § 1396r-1(b)(2).
21 (c) “Supplemental Security Income Program” has the
22 meaning ascribed to it in NRS 422A.075.
23 Sec. 2.6. Chapter 449A of NRS is hereby amended by adding
24 thereto a new section to read as follows:
25 The parent or legal guardian of a newborn child who is
26 receiving care in a neonatal intensive care unit of a hospital has
27 the right to:
28 1. Receive clear, honest and timely updates concerning the
29 condition, treatment plan and prognosis of the newborn child in a
30 language that the parent or legal guardian understands.
31 2. Be near the newborn child, except in emergency situations
32 or if other safety concerns exist, and participate in the daily
33 rounds and decision making relating to the child.
34 3. Be informed of and provide consent to all treatments for
35 and procedures performed on the newborn child, except in
36 emergency situations.
37 4. As medically appropriate, hold and touch the newborn
38 child.
39 5. Access lactation support, receive information on
40 breastfeeding and the expression of breast milk and access options
41 for the safe storage of breast milk that are within the existing
42 capabilities of the hospital, if the parent or legal guardian chooses
43 to breastfeed the newborn child.
-
*SB138_R1*
– 5 –
1 6. Receive information concerning resources relating to the
2 mental health of the parent or legal guardian, including, without
3 limitation, support groups, counseling services or peer networks.
4 7. Ask questions and voice concerns to the medical staff of
5 the hospital.
6 8. Request and, when feasible, receive accommodations for
7 religious or cultural needs, including, without limitation, spiritual
8 support or dietary needs.
9 9. Be informed concerning the process for discharge from the
10 hospital and any potential long-term needs of the newborn child.
11 10. Receive guidance on applying for and receiving benefits
12 under private insurance, the Supplemental Security Income
13 Program, as defined in NRS 422A.075, and Medicaid, where
14 applicable.
15 11. An environment in the hospital that acknowledges the
16 role of the parent or legal guardian as a caregiver, including,
17 without limitation, reasonable accommodations for the comfort
18 and privacy of the parent or legal guardian and the ability of the
19 parent or legal guardian to communicate with the medical staff
20 and others.
21 Sec. 3. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
22 approval.
23 2. Sections 1 to 2.6, inclusive, of this act become effective:
24 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
25 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
26 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
27 (b) On January 1, 2026, for all other purposes.
