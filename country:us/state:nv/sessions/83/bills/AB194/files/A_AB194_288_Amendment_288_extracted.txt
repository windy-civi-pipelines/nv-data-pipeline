A.B. No. 194—Prohibits the release of certain balloons that are lighter than air
under certain circumstances. (BDR 15-338)

SUMMARY—Prohibits the release of certain balloons that are lighter than air
under certain circumstances. (BDR 15-338)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public safety; prohibiting the release of certain balloons that
are lighter than air under certain circumstances; providing for the
imposition of civil penalties; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prohibits certain acts concerning public safety. (NRS 202.500-202.595)
2 Currently, at least 10 other states prohibit the release of certain types of balloons that are
3 lighter than air under certain circumstances. (Cal. Penal Code § 653.1; Conn. Gen. Stat. § 26-
4 25c; Del. Code Ann. tit. 16, § 1604; Fla. Stat. § 379.233; Haw. Rev. Stat. § 339-12; Me. Rev.
5 Stat. Ann. tit. 17 § 2263-A; Md. Code Ann., Envir. § 9-2301; R.I. Gen. Laws § 23-96-1; Tenn.
6 Code Ann. § 68-101-108; Va. Code Ann. § 29.1-556.1) This bill: (1) prohibits intentionally
7 releasing, organizing the release of or intentionally causing the release of [10 or more certain
8 balloons] a balloon inflated with a gas that is lighter than air ; [within a 24-hour period;] (2)
9 provides for the imposition of civil penalties that may be recovered by the Attorney General
10 or a district or city attorney in a civil action; and (3) exempts certain balloons from the
11 prohibition such as hot air balloons, certain balloons used for research and balloons that are
12 released and remain indoors.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 202 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 1. No person shall intentionally release, organize the release of or
4 intentionally cause [to be released 10 or more balloons] the release of a balloon
5 inflated with a gas that is lighter than air . [within a 24-hour period.]
6 2. A person who violates the provisions of subsection 1 is subject to a civil
7 penalty not to exceed $250. [Each balloon released in a 24-hour period in excess
8 of the limit imposed in subsection 1 constitutes a separate violation.]
Assembly Amendment No. 288 to Assembly Bill No. 194 Page 4
1 3. A civil penalty imposed pursuant to subsection 2 must be recovered in a
2 civil action brought by the Attorney General or by the district attorney or city
3 attorney for the jurisdiction in which the violation occurred.
4 4. Any civil penalty collected pursuant to this section by:
5 (a) The Attorney General must be paid to the State Treasurer for credit to the
6 State General Fund.
7 (b) A district attorney or city attorney must be deposited in the county or city
8 treasury, as applicable.
9 5. The Attorney General or the district attorney or city attorney of a county
10 or city, as applicable, in which a person releases a balloon in violation of
11 subsection 1 may bring an action to enjoin the violation.
12 6. As used in this section, “balloon” means any balloon made of
13 nonbiodegradable material or biodegradable material that requires more than 5
14 minutes of contact with air or water to degrade. The term does not include:
15 (a) A hot air balloon that is recovered after launching;
16 (b) A balloon used in a governmental or scientific research project; or
17 (c) A balloon that is released and remains indoors.
