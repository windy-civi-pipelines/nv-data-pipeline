Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT A.B. 194
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 194–ASSEMBLYMEMBER JAUREGUI
PREFILED FEBRUARY 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Prohibits the release of certain balloons that are
lighter than air under certain circumstances.
(BDR 15-338)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public safety; prohibiting the release of certain
balloons that are lighter than air under certain
circumstances; providing for the imposition of civil
penalties; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prohibits certain acts concerning public safety. (NRS 202.500-
2 202.595) Currently, at least 10 other states prohibit the release of certain types of
3 balloons that are lighter than air under certain circumstances. (Cal. Penal Code §
4 653.1; Conn. Gen. Stat. § 26-25c; Del. Code Ann. tit. 16, § 1604; Fla. Stat. §
5 379.233; Haw. Rev. Stat. § 339-12; Me. Rev. Stat. Ann. tit. 17 § 2263-A; Md. Code
6 Ann., Envir. § 9-2301; R.I. Gen. Laws § 23-96-1; Tenn. Code Ann. § 68-101-108;
7 Va. Code Ann. § 29.1-556.1) This bill: (1) prohibits intentionally releasing,
8 organizing the release of or intentionally causing the release of a balloon inflated
9 with a gas that is lighter than air; (2) provides for the imposition of civil penalties
10 that may be recovered by the Attorney General or a district or city attorney in a
11 civil action; and (3) exempts certain balloons from the prohibition such as hot air
12 balloons, certain balloons used for research and balloons that are released and
13 remain indoors.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 202 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. No person shall intentionally release, organize the release
4 of or intentionally cause the release of a balloon inflated with a
5 gas that is lighter than air.
6 2. A person who violates the provisions of subsection 1 is
7 subject to a civil penalty not to exceed $250.
8 3. A civil penalty imposed pursuant to subsection 2 must be
9 recovered in a civil action brought by the Attorney General or by
10 the district attorney or city attorney for the jurisdiction in which
11 the violation occurred.
12 4. Any civil penalty collected pursuant to this section by:
13 (a) The Attorney General must be paid to the State Treasurer
14 for credit to the State General Fund.
15 (b) A district attorney or city attorney must be deposited in the
16 county or city treasury, as applicable.
17 5. The Attorney General or the district attorney or city
18 attorney of a county or city, as applicable, in which a person
19 releases a balloon in violation of subsection 1 may bring an action
20 to enjoin the violation.
21 6. As used in this section, “balloon” means any balloon made
22 of nonbiodegradable material or biodegradable material that
23 requires more than 5 minutes of contact with air or water to
24 degrade. The term does not include:
25 (a) A hot air balloon that is recovered after launching;
26 (b) A balloon used in a governmental or scientific research
27 project; or
28 (c) A balloon that is released and remains indoors.
