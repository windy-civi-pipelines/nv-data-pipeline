Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to gaming.
(BDR 41-146)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to gaming; requiring the disgorgement of any
profit, gain, gross receipt or other benefit related to
certain illegal gaming activities; increasing a penalty; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prohibits: (1) a person from engaging in certain activities relating
2 to gaming without procuring a state gaming license; (2) a person from engaging in
3 a fraudulent act or otherwise manipulating or cheating at a gambling game; and (3)
4 a person from accepting, receiving or allowing certain other persons to accept or
5 receive certain wagers through any medium of communication from another person
6 physically present within this State. (NRS 463.160, 465.070, 465.092) Sections 1-3
7 of this bill require, in addition to any existing penalty, a person to disgorge any
8 profit, gain, gross receipt or other benefit resulting from certain illegal gaming
9 activities and pay that amount to the State Treasurer for deposit in the State General
10 Fund. Section 3 also increases the penalty for accepting, receiving or allowing
11 certain persons to accept or receive certain wagers under certain circumstances
12 from a misdemeanor to a gross misdemeanor.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 463.360 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 463.360 1. Conviction by a court of competent jurisdiction of
3 a person for a violation of, an attempt to violate, or a conspiracy to
4 violate any of the provisions of this chapter or of chapter 463B, 464
5 or 465 of NRS may act as an immediate revocation of all licenses
6 which have been issued to the violator, and, in addition, the court
7 may, upon application of the district attorney of the county or of the
8 Commission, order that no new or additional license under this
9 chapter be issued to the violator, or be issued to any person for the
10 room or premises in which the violation occurred, for 1 year after
11 the date of the revocation.
12 2. A person who willfully fails to report, pay or truthfully
13 account for and pay over any license fee or tax imposed by the
14 provisions of this chapter, or willfully attempts in any manner to
15 evade or defeat any such license fee, tax or payment thereof is guilty
16 of a category C felony and shall be punished as provided in NRS
17 193.130. In addition to any other penalty, the court shall order the
18 person to pay restitution.
19 3. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 4, a person who
20 willfully violates, attempts to violate, or conspires to violate any of
21 the provisions of subsection 1 of NRS 463.160 is guilty of a
22 category B felony and shall be punished by imprisonment in the
23 state prison for a minimum term of not less than 1 year and a
24 maximum term of not more than 10 years, by a fine of not more than
25 $50,000, or by both fine and imprisonment. The court shall also
26 order any profits, gain, gross receipts or other benefit from the
27 violation to be disgorged and paid to the State Treasurer for
28 deposit in the State General Fund.
29 4. A licensee who puts additional games or slot machines into
30 play or displays additional games or slot machines in a public area
31 without first obtaining all required licenses and approval is subject
32 only to the penalties provided in NRS 463.270 and 463.310 and in
33 any applicable ordinance of the county, city or town.
34 5. A person who willfully violates any provision of a
35 regulation adopted pursuant to NRS 463.125 is guilty of a category
36 C felony and shall be punished as provided in NRS 193.130.
37 6. The violation of any of the provisions of this chapter, the
38 penalty for which is not specifically fixed in this chapter, is a gross
39 misdemeanor.
40 Sec. 2. NRS 465.088 is hereby amended to read as follows:
41 465.088 1. A person who violates any provision of NRS
42 465.070 to 465.086, inclusive:

1 (a) For the first offense, is guilty of a category C felony and
2 shall be punished as provided in NRS 193.130. The court shall also
3 order any profits, gain, gross receipts or other benefit from the
4 violation to be disgorged and paid to the State Treasurer for
5 deposit in the State General Fund.
6 (b) For a second or subsequent violation of any of these
7 provisions, is guilty of a category B felony and shall be punished by
8 imprisonment in the state prison for a minimum term of not less
9 than 1 year and a maximum term of not more than 6 years, and may
10 be further punished by a fine of not more than $10,000. The court
11 shall also order any profits, gain, gross receipts or other benefit
12 from the violation to be disgorged and paid to the State Treasurer
13 for deposit in the State General Fund.
14 2. A person who attempts, or two or more persons who
15 conspire, to violate any provision of NRS 465.070 to 465.086,
16 inclusive, each is guilty of a category C felony and shall be punished
17 by imposing the penalty provided in subsection 1 for the completed
18 crime, whether or not he or she personally played any gambling
19 game or used any prohibited device.
20 Sec. 3. NRS 465.092 is hereby amended to read as follows:
21 465.092 1. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 465.094, a
22 person, alone or with others, shall not knowingly, within or outside
23 of this state:
24 (a) Accept or receive, directly or indirectly, through any medium
25 of communication a wager from another person who is physically
26 present within this state; or
27 (b) Allow a lessee, agent or employee to accept or receive,
28 directly or indirectly, through any medium of communication a
29 wager from another person who is physically present within this
30 state.
31 2. If a person engages in conduct in violation of subsection 1
32 and the person is outside of this state at the time of the offense:
33 (a) The offense shall be deemed to commence outside of this
34 state;
35 (b) The offense shall be deemed to be consummated within this
36 state; and
37 (c) The person may be prosecuted within this state pursuant to
38 the provisions of NRS 171.015.
39 3. A person who violates the provisions of this section is guilty
40 of a gross misdemeanor [.] and the court shall order any profits,
41 gain, gross receipts or other benefit from the violation to be
42 disgorged and paid to the State Treasurer for deposit in the State
43 General Fund.
