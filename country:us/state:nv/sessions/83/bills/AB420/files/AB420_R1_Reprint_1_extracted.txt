ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 420–ASSEMBLYMEMBER GONZÁLEZ
MARCH 13, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to education.
(BDR 34-538)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring the board of trustees of
certain school districts and the governing body of certain
charter schools to prepare and publish an annual report
relating to the use of force by school police officers; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes: (1) the board of trustees of a school district to employ,
2 appoint or contract for the provision of school police officers; and (2) the governing
3 body of a charter school to contract with the board of trustees of the school district
4 in which the charter school is located to provide school police officers. (NRS
5 388A.384, 391.281)
6 Section 1 of this bill requires, on or before the first day of each school year, the
7 board of trustees of a school district that employs school police officers and the
8 governing body of a charter school that has entered into a contract for the provision
9 of school police officers to prepare and publish on the Internet website of the
10 school district or charter school, as applicable, a report relating to the use of force
11 by school police officers during the immediately preceding school year. Section 1
12 also sets forth the requirements for such a report.
13 Section 5.5 of this bill provides that the first report required pursuant to section
14 1: (1) is due on or before the first day of the 2026-2027 school year; and (2) must
15 contain the information required pursuant to section 1 for the 2025-2026 school
16 year.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 391 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. On or before the first day of each school year, the board of
4 trustees of a school district that employs school police officers and
5 the governing body of a charter school that has entered into a
6 contract pursuant to NRS 388A.384 for the provision of school
7 police officers must prepare and publish on the Internet website of
8 the school district or charter school, as applicable, a report
9 relating to the use of force by school police officers during the
10 immediately preceding school year.
11 2. The report required pursuant to subsection 1 must set
12 forth:
13 (a) The number of incidents involving the use of force
14 involving the use of:
15 (1) A baton;
16 (2) A chemical agent; or
17 (3) An electronic stun device.
18 (b) For each incident described in paragraph (a):
19 (1) The location of the incident, including, without
20 limitation, whether the incident occurred:
21 (I) On or in the grounds, property, buildings or any
22 other facility of the school district or charter school; or
23 (II) A location other than the grounds, property,
24 buildings or any other facility of the school district or charter
25 school;
26 (2) Whether the incident occurred during the school day or
27 at an activity or event sponsored by the school district or charter
28 school; and
29 (3) Except as otherwise provided in this subparagraph, the
30 rank of each school police officer who used such force and the
31 number of years that each such school police officer has been
32 employed. The provisions of this subparagraph only apply to the
33 report required of the board of trustees of a school district that
34 employs more than 75 school police officers.
35 (c) The demographic information, including, without
36 limitation, age, race, gender and ethnicity, of the persons upon
37 whom such force was used by school police. All information must
38 be reported in the aggregate and must not include any personally
39 identifiable information.
40 3. As used in this section:
-
*AB420_R1*
– 3 –
1 (a) “Baton” means a cylindrical instrument that is designed to
2 deliver a blunt force contact or a strike to a person in close
3 proximity.
4 (b) “Chemical agent” means any chemical which can rapidly
5 produce sensory irritation or disabling physical effects in humans,
6 which disappear within a short time following termination of
7 exposure. The term includes, without limitation, items commonly
8 referred to as tear gas, pepper spray, pepper balls and oleoresin
9 capsicum.
10 (c) “Electronic stun device” means a device that:
11 (1) Emits an electrical charge or current that is transmitted
12 by projectile, physical contact or other means; and
13 (2) Is designed to disable a person or animal temporarily or
14 permanently.
15 (d) “School police officer” includes a peace officer who is
16 employed by a metropolitan police department created pursuant to
17 chapter 280 of NRS or a sheriff’s office that has entered into a
18 contract with a school district to provide police services in public
19 schools pursuant to NRS 391.281.
20 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
21 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
22 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
23 Sec. 5. (Deleted by amendment.)
24 Sec. 5.5. The first report required pursuant to section 1 of this
25 act:
26 1. Is due on or before the first day of the 2026-2027 school
27 year; and
28 2. Must contain the information required pursuant to section 1
29 of this act for the 2025-2026 school year.
30 Sec. 6. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
