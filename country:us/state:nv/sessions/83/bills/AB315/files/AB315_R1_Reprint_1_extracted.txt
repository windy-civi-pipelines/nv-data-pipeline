Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT A.B. 315
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 315–ASSEMBLYMEMBER EDGEWORTH
FEBRUARY 27, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Requires applications to participate in Medicaid as a
provider to be accompanied by a certain verification.
(BDR 38-659)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; requiring an application to
participate in Medicaid as a provider to be accompanied
with a verification of the identity and signature of the
applicant; establishing the means by which the identity
and signature of an applicant may be established; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires an application to participate in Medicaid as a provider to
2 contain a statement that all matters stated within the application are true and
3 accurate. Existing law requires a natural person who is the applicant, or is
4 authorized to act on behalf of the applicant, to sign the statement under the pains
5 and penalties of perjury. Existing law provides that a person who signs such a
6 statement knowing that the application contains information which is false, whether
7 in whole or in part, and whether by commission or omission, is guilty of a category
8 D felony. (NRS 422.550) This bill additionally requires that an application
9 submitted to participate in Medicaid as a provider be accompanied by verification
10 of the identity and signature of the person who signed the statement. This bill
11 specifies that a person may verify his or her identity and signature for this purpose
12 by: (1) oath or affirmation before a notary public or other person authorized by law
13 to administer oaths or affirmations; or (2) through the use of an electronic identity
14 verification system.
-
*AB315_R1*
– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 422.550 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 422.550 1. Each application or report submitted to participate
3 as a provider, each report stating income or expense upon which
4 rates of payment are or may be based, and each invoice for payment
5 for goods or services provided to a recipient must contain a
6 statement that all matters stated therein are true and accurate, signed
7 by a natural person who is the provider or is authorized to act for the
8 provider, under the pains and penalties of perjury.
9 2. In addition to the requirements of subsection 1, each
10 application submitted to participate as a provider must be
11 accompanied by verification of the identity and signature of the
12 natural person who signed the statement pursuant to subsection 1:
13 (a) By oath or affirmation of the person administered by a
14 notary public or other person authorized by law to administer
15 oaths or affirmations; or
16 (b) Through an electronic identity verification system that is
17 used or approved by the Department for the purpose of verifying
18 the identification or signature of a person.
19 3. A person is guilty of perjury which is a category D felony
20 and shall be punished as provided in NRS 193.130 if the person
21 signs or submits, or causes to be signed or submitted, [such] a
22 statement described in subsection 1 , knowing that the application,
23 report or invoice contains information which is false, in whole or in
24 part, by commission or by omission.
25 [3.] 4. For the purposes of this section, a person who signs on
26 behalf of a provider is presumed to have the authorization of the
27 provider and to be acting at the direction of the provider.
28 5. As used in this section, “electronic identity verification
29 system” means an electronic system that is capable of verifying the
30 identity or signature of a person by:
31 (a) Using multifactor authentication;
32 (b) Verifying data contained within governmental databases or
33 appearing on government-issued identification cards, including,
34 without limitation, photographs and signatures;
35 (c) Utilizing biometric data or facial recognition technology;
36 (d) Utilizing a combination of any mechanism described in
37 paragraphs (a), (b) and (c); or
38 (e) Utilizing other secure and accurate means or procedures.
39 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
