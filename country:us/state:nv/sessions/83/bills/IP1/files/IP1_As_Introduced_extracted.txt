AN ACT relating to public employees; exempting certain teachers
and licensed educational personnel and their employee
organizations, bargaining agents and bargaining units
from the prohibition against public employees engaging
in a strike against a public employer; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 288 of NRS (Relations between
2 Governments and Public Employees) is hereby amended to read as
3 follows:
4 NRS 288.720 Prohibitions on strikes do not apply to certain
5 local government employees, employee organizations, bargaining
6 agents, and bargaining units.
7 1. Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, the provisions of
8 NRS 288.700 through NRS 288.715 and any other law prohibiting
9 strikes shall not apply to local government employees licensed as
10 teachers pursuant to Chapter 391 of the Nevada Revised Statutes
11 and their employee organizations, bargaining agents, and
12 bargaining units.
13 2. As used in this section: “teacher” means a person licensed
14 pursuant to chapter 391 of NRS who is classified by the board of
15 trustees of a school district:
16 (a) As a teacher and who spends at least 50 percent of his or
17 her work year providing instruction or discipline to pupils;
18 (b) As instructional support staff, who does not hold a
19 supervisory position and who spends not more than 50 percent of
20 his or her work year providing instruction to pupils. Such
-

1 instructional support staff includes, without limitation, librarians
2 and persons who provide instructional support; or
3 (c) As school counselors, school nurses and other employees
4 who spend at least 50 percent of their work year providing
5 emotional support, noninstructional guidance or medical support
6 to pupils.
7 Section 2. Severability. If any provision of this act, or the
8 application therefore to any person, thing or circumstance is held
9 invalid or unconstitutional by a court of competent jurisdiction, such
10 invalidity or unconstitutionality shall not affect the validity or
11 constitutionality of this act as a whole or any provision or

13 or unconstitutional provision or application, and to this end the
14 provisions of this act are declared to be severable.
15 Section 4. This act shall become effective if approved by the
16 legislature, or upon passage and approval by the voters.
