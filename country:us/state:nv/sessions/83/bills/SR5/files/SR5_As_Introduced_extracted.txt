S.R. 5
BUCK, CRUZ-CRAWFORD, DALY, DOÑATE, DONDERO
LOOP, ELLISON, FLORES, HANSEN, KRASNER, LANGE,
NEAL, NGUYEN, OHRENSCHALL, PAZINA, ROGICH,
SCHEIBLE, STEINBECK, STONE AND TAYLOR
MAY 7, 2025
____________
Read and Adopted
SUMMARY—Inducts Pete Goicoechea into the Senate Hall of
Fame. (BDR R-1169)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
SENATE RESOLUTION—Inducting Pete Goicoechea into the
Senate Hall of Fame.
1 WHEREAS, The Senate of the Legislature of the State of Nevada
2 has established a Senate Hall of Fame whose members are selected
3 by leadership from those past Senators who have served with
4 distinction and who have made exemplary contributions to the State
5 of Nevada; and
6 WHEREAS, Pete Goicoechea was born in Salt Lake City, Utah,
7 attended elementary school in Ely, Nevada, graduated from Eureka
8 County High School and attended college at Utah State University
9 in Logan, Utah; and
10 WHEREAS, Of Basque decent, Pete is a third-generation rancher
11 in Nevada whose cattle ranching operation extends throughout Elko,
12 Eureka and White Pine Counties and before returning to cattle
13 ranching on a full-time basis, he worked for Kennecott Copper
14 Mining, the Eureka County Road Department, Mt. Wheeler Power
15 and Windfall Mining; and
16 WHEREAS, As a volunteer firefighter and Director of the Eureka
17 County High School Rodeo Club, Pete served his local community
18 in Eureka County; and
19 WHEREAS, In 1987, Pete was first elected to the Eureka County
20 Board of County Commissioners on which he served for 16 years,
21 14 years of which as Chair of the Board; and
-
*SR5*
– 2 –
1 WHEREAS, In 2002, Pete was elected to the Nevada Assembly to
2 represent Assembly District 35, where he served for 10 years,
3 holding various leadership positions, including positions as Co-
4 Minority Whip and Assembly Minority Floor Leader and
5 participating as an integral member of the illustrious Cowboy
6 Caucus; and
7 WHEREAS, In 2012, Pete was elected to the Nevada Senate to
8 represent Senate District 19, where he served for 12 years and held
9 positions including Chair of the Senate Committee on Government
10 Affairs and Chair of the Legislative Commission’s Subcommittee to
11 Study Water; and
12 WHEREAS, Pete served as a member of the Assembly
13 Committee on Ways and Means for two legislative sessions
14 beginning in 2009, and then, after being elected to the Nevada
15 Senate, he spent his next six legislative sessions as a member of the
16 Senate Committee on Finance; and
17 WHEREAS, Throughout his time serving in the Nevada
18 Legislature, Pete was dedicated to supporting and strengthening
19 rural communities across this State by championing policies to
20 improve access to quality education and health care, advocating for
21 legislation to protect farmers and ranchers and their way of life and
22 promoting the responsible management of natural resources and
23 water systems; and
24 WHEREAS, After completing 22 years of public service in the
25 Nevada Legislature and just as Pete was preparing to retire to his
26 ranch, Governor Lombardo appointed Pete to represent District 8 on
27 the Board of Regents of the University of Nevada; and
28 WHEREAS, Pete has been married to his wife, Glady, for nearly
29 53 years and they had three children, Julian Joseph (“J.J.”), Jolene
30 and Raymond Baptista (“Tista”), and have two granddaughters,
31 Julianna and Amelia, who Pete affectionately refers to as “Punkie”
32 and “Meme”; now, therefore, be it
33 RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, That, for
34 his many years of devoted service to the State of Nevada and Eureka
35 County, Nevada, as a member of the Eureka County Board of
36 County Commissioners, a member of both the Nevada Assembly
37 and Senate and as a Regent of the University of Nevada, Pete
38 Goicoechea is hereby inducted into the Senate Hall of Fame of the
39 Legislature of the State of Nevada; and be it further
40 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon
41 adoption.
H
