EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public works; providing a declaration of legislative intent
regarding the payment of prevailing wages to workers employed by a
contractor or subcontractor to perform construction work on certain
projects awarded by a public utility; requiring, with certain exceptions,
a contractor or subcontractor who is awarded a contract for
construction work on certain projects by a public utility to comply with
prevailing wage provisions applicable to public works when the
construction work does not qualify as a public work; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law sets forth general provisions applicable to public works, including
2 provisions requiring, with certain exceptions, the payment of prevailing wages for public
3 works projects. (NRS 338.013-338.090) Section 2 of this bill makes certain declarations of
4 legislative intent relating to the payment of prevailing wages to workers employed by a
5 contractor or subcontractor to perform construction work on a significant operational or
6 capital requirement project awarded by a public utility. Section 3 of this bill requires, with
7 certain exceptions, a contractor or subcontractor who is awarded a contract for construction
8 work on a significant operational or capital requirement project by a public utility to comply
9 with prevailing wage provisions applicable to public works when the construction work does
10 not qualify as a public work. Section 3 also defines “electric utility,” “public utility” and
11 “significant operational or capital requirement project” for the purposes of that section.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 338 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. The Legislature hereby finds and declares that the payment of
4 prevailing wages to workers employed by a contractor or subcontractor to
5 perform construction work on a significant operational or capital requirement
6 project awarded by a public utility is essential to:
Assembly Amendment No. 746 to Senate Bill No. 443 Third Reprint Page 4
1 1. Increasing the number of skilled construction workers in this State;
2 2. Ensuring the highest quality of work on such projects;
3 3. Ensuring the safe and timely completion of such projects;
4 4. Ensuring that the residents of this State have access to safe, reliable and
5 affordable energy resources; and
6 5. Enhancing public safety.
7 Sec. 3. 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 2, the provisions of
8 NRS 338.013 to 338.090, inclusive, apply to any contract for construction work
9 awarded by a public utility to a contractor or subcontractor on a significant
10 operational or capital requirement project when the construction work does not
11 qualify as a public work as defined in NRS 338.010.
12 2. The provisions of subsection 1 do not apply to a contract awarded by a
13 public utility where the primary construction work will be performed by a
14 contractor with:
15 (a) A collective bargaining agreement;
16 (b) A project labor agreement; [or]
17 (c) A worksite agreement that provides for the construction of a significant
18 operational or capital requirement project, or for the maintenance of, or other
19 ongoing work to be performed upon completion of, a significant operational or
20 capital requirement project [.] ; or
21 (d) An existing labor agreement with a public utility, if the public utility is an
22 electric utility.
23 3. As used in this section:
24 (a) “Electric utility” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 704.7571.
25 (b) “Public utility” means a utility that purchases natural gas for retail [.
26 (b)] or an electric utility.
27 (c) “Significant operational or capital requirement project” means [the] :
28 (1) The replacement of a natural gas pipeline approved in a plan
29 submitted pursuant to NRS 704.991 [.] ; or
30 (2) The construction of a new generation electric utility facility of not
31 less than 50 megawatts and that is approved in a plan submitted pursuant to NRS
32 704.741.
33 Sec. 4. The provisions of section 3 of this act do not apply to any contract
34 entered into before October 1, 2025.
