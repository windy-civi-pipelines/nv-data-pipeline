Section 1:
FOURTH REPRINT S.B. 443
SENATE BILL NO. 443–COMMITTEE ON
GROWTH AND INFRASTRUCTURE
MARCH 24, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to public works.
(BDR 28-1112)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to public works; providing a declaration of
legislative intent regarding the payment of prevailing
wages to workers employed by a contractor or
subcontractor to perform construction work on certain
projects awarded by a public utility; requiring, with
certain exceptions, a contractor or subcontractor who is
awarded a contract for construction work on certain
projects by a public utility to comply with prevailing
wage provisions applicable to public works when the
construction work does not qualify as a public work; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law sets forth general provisions applicable to public works, including
2 provisions requiring, with certain exceptions, the payment of prevailing wages for
3 public works projects. (NRS 338.013-338.090) Section 2 of this bill makes certain
4 declarations of legislative intent relating to the payment of prevailing wages to
5 workers employed by a contractor or subcontractor to perform construction work
6 on a significant operational or capital requirement project awarded by a public
7 utility. Section 3 of this bill requires, with certain exceptions, a contractor or
8 subcontractor who is awarded a contract for construction work on a significant
9 operational or capital requirement project by a public utility to comply with
10 prevailing wage provisions applicable to public works when the construction work
11 does not qualify as a public work. Section 3 also defines “electric utility,” “public
12 utility” and “significant operational or capital requirement project” for the purposes
13 of that section.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 338 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. The Legislature hereby finds and declares that the
4 payment of prevailing wages to workers employed by a contractor
5 or subcontractor to perform construction work on a significant
6 operational or capital requirement project awarded by a public
7 utility is essential to:
8 1. Increasing the number of skilled construction workers in
9 this State;
10 2. Ensuring the highest quality of work on such projects;
11 3. Ensuring the safe and timely completion of such projects;
12 4. Ensuring that the residents of this State have access to
13 safe, reliable and affordable energy resources; and
14 5. Enhancing public safety.
15 Sec. 3. 1. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 2, the
16 provisions of NRS 338.013 to 338.090, inclusive, apply to any
17 contract for construction work awarded by a public utility to a
18 contractor or subcontractor on a significant operational or capital
19 requirement project when the construction work does not qualify
20 as a public work as defined in NRS 338.010.
21 2. The provisions of subsection 1 do not apply to a contract
22 awarded by an electric utility where the primary construction work
23 will be performed by a contractor with:
24 (a) A collective bargaining agreement;
25 (b) A project labor agreement; or
26 (c) A worksite agreement that provides for the construction of
27 a significant operational or capital requirement project, or for the
28 maintenance of, or other ongoing work to be performed upon
29 completion of, a significant operational or capital requirement
30 project.
31 3. As used in this section:
32 (a) “Electric utility” has the meaning ascribed to it in
33 NRS 704.7571.
34 (b) “Public utility” means a utility that purchases natural gas
35 for retail or an electric utility.
36 (c) “Significant operational or capital requirement project”
37 means:
38 (1) The replacement of a natural gas pipeline approved in a
39 plan submitted pursuant to NRS 704.991; or
40 (2) The construction of a new generation electric utility
41 facility of not less than 50 megawatts and that is approved in a
42 plan submitted pursuant to NRS 704.741.

– 3 –
1 Sec. 4. The provisions of section 3 of this act do not apply to
2 any contract entered into before October 1, 2025.
