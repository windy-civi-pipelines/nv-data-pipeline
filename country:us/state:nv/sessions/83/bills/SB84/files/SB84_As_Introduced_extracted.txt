Section 1:
S.B. 84
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING
COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY)
PREFILED JANUARY 7, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to domestic violence.
(BDR 40-488)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to domestic violence; revising provisions relating
to programs for the treatment of persons who commit
domestic violence; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of the
2 Department of Health and Human Services to: (1) adopt regulations governing the
3 evaluation, certification and monitoring of programs for the treatment of persons
4 who commit domestic violence; and (2) evaluate, certify and monitor programs for
5 the treatment of persons who commit domestic violence in accordance with those
6 regulations. (NRS 439.258) Under section 1 of this bill, the regulations adopted by
7 the Division must include provisions requiring that the curriculum and instructional
8 materials used in a program be evidence-based.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 439.258 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 439.258 1. The Division shall evaluate, certify and monitor
3 programs for the treatment of persons who commit domestic
4 violence in accordance with the regulations adopted pursuant to
5 subsection 2.
-
*SB84*
– 2 –
1 2. The Division shall adopt regulations governing the
2 evaluation, certification and monitoring of programs for the
3 treatment of persons who commit domestic violence.
4 3. The regulations adopted pursuant to subsection 2 must
5 include, without limitation, provisions:
6 (a) Requiring that a program:
7 (1) Include a module specific to victim safety; and
8 (2) Be based on:
9 (I) Evidence-based practices; and
10 (II) The assessment of a program participant by a
11 supervisor of treatment or provider of treatment; [and]
12 (b) Requiring that the curriculum and instructional materials
13 used in a program be evidence-based; and
14 (c) Allowing a program that is located in another state to
15 become certified in this State to provide treatment to persons who:
16 (1) Reside in this State; and
17 (2) Are ordered by a court in this State to participate in a
18 program for the treatment of persons who commit domestic
19 violence.
20 Sec. 2. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
21 approval.
22 2. Section 1 of this act becomes effective:
23 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
24 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
25 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
26 (b) On October 1, 2025, for all other purposes.
