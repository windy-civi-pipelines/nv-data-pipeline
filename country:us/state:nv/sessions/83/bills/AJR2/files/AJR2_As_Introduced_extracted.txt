Section 1:
A.J.R. 2
PREFILED JANUARY 24, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Proposes to amend the Nevada Constitution to
require that the language of a bill or joint resolution
be made available to the public for at least 72 hours
before a House may vote on the final passage of the
bill or joint resolution. (BDR C-20)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
ASSEMBLY JOINT RESOLUTION—Proposing to amend the
Nevada Constitution to require that the language of a bill
or joint resolution be made available to the public by
publication on the Internet website of the Legislature for
at least 72 hours before a House may vote on the final
passage of the bill or joint resolution.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 The Nevada Constitution imposes various procedural requirements relating to
2 the passage of bills and joint resolutions by the Legislature. (Nev. Const. Art. 4, §
3 18) This resolution proposes to amend the Nevada Constitution to require that the
4 language of a bill or joint resolution be made available to the public by publication
5 on the Internet website of the Legislature for at least 72 hours before a House may
6 vote on the final passage of the bill or joint resolution. The House may only waive
7 such requirement upon request of the Governor for an emergency.
1 RESOLVED BY THE ASSEMBLY AND SENATE OF THE STATE OF
2 NEVADA, JOINTLY, That Section 18 of Article 4 of the Nevada
3 Constitution be amended to read as follows:
4 Sec. 18. 1. Every bill, except a bill placed on a
5 consent calendar adopted as provided in subsection 4, must be
6 read by sections on three several days, in each House, unless
7 in case of emergency, two thirds of the House where such bill
8 is pending shall deem it expedient to dispense with this rule.
-
*AJR2*
– 2 –
1 The reading of a bill by sections, on its final passage, shall in
2 no case be dispensed with, and the vote on the final passage
3 of every bill or joint resolution shall be taken by yeas and
4 nays to be entered on the journals of each House. Except as
5 otherwise provided in subsection 2, a majority of all the
6 members elected to each House is necessary to pass every bill
7 or joint resolution, and all bills or joint resolutions so passed,
8 shall be signed by the presiding officers of the respective
9 Houses and by the Secretary of the Senate and Clerk of the
10 Assembly.
11 2. Except as otherwise provided in subsection 3, an
12 affirmative vote of not fewer than two-thirds of the members
13 elected to each House is necessary to pass a bill or joint
14 resolution which creates, generates, or increases any public
15 revenue in any form, including but not limited to taxes, fees,
16 assessments and rates, or changes in the computation bases
17 for taxes, fees, assessments and rates.
18 3. A majority of all of the members elected to each
19 House may refer any measure which creates, generates, or
20 increases any revenue in any form to the people of the State at
21 the next general election, and shall become effective and
22 enforced only if it has been approved by a majority of the
23 votes cast on the measure at such election.
24 4. Each House may provide by rule for the creation of a
25 consent calendar and establish the procedure for the passage
26 of uncontested bills.
27 5. Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, the
28 language of a bill or joint resolution must be made available
29 to the public by publication on the Internet website of the
30 Legislature for at least 72 hours before the House where
31 such bill or joint resolution is pending may vote on the final
32 passage of the bill or joint resolution. The House may only
33 waive such requirement upon request of the Governor for
34 an emergency.
35 And be it further
36 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon
37 passage.
H
