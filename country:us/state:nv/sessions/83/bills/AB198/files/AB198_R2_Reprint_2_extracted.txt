ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 198–ASSEMBLYMEMBER BROWN-MAY
PREFILED FEBRUARY 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to certain inflatable
devices. (BDR 52-49)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to trade regulations; setting forth certain
requirements and restrictions concerning certain inflatable
devices; requiring certain businesses that operate such
devices to meet certain requirements; setting forth
requirements for certain inspections of and warnings
relating to such devices; imposing certain restrictions on
the use of such devices under certain circumstances;
authorizing a county, city or town to enact certain
ordinances; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Sections 2-12 of this bill set forth certain requirements and restrictions
2 concerning inflatable devices and certain businesses that operate such devices.
3 Section 3 defines the term “inflatable device” to mean an air-filled device that: (1)
4 incorporates certain structural and mechanical elements to achieve its strength,
5 shape and stability by tensioning from internal air pressure; (2) is intended for use
6 by a person to bounce, play, slide, climb or otherwise interact for outdoor
7 recreation; and (3) includes a constant air inflatable bounce house, inflatable water
8 slide or similar device. Section 5 defines the term “operator business” to mean a
9 business entity which is engaged in the business of making inflatable devices
10 available for use in this State and which employs, controls, directs or hires as an
11 independent contractor an operator to act on behalf of the business entity. Section 4
12 defines the term “operator” to mean a person on-site at the location where an
13 inflatable device is used who sets up, operates, maintains or supervises the use of
14 the inflatable device on behalf of an operator business.
15 Section 6 prohibits a person from engaging in the business of an operator
16 business unless the person: (1) holds all applicable state and local business licenses;
-
*AB198_R2*
– 2 –
17 and (2) has obtained certain insurance or a surety bond in an amount of not less
18 than $1,000,000.
19 Section 7 requires an operator business to keep a logbook for any inflatable
20 device that is made available for use by the operator business, which must be kept
21 with the inflatable device and made available for inspection at the request of certain
22 persons and any state or local agency. Sections 7 and 8 require an operator, before
23 and during each use of an inflatable device, to conduct certain inspections of the
24 device and the area in which it will be used and to record and retain for not less
25 than 2 years the results of those inspections and certain other information in the
26 logbook. Section 8 prohibits an operator from allowing any person to use the
27 inflatable device if an inspection reveals or would have revealed a hazard or
28 potential hazard that would make use of the inflatable device unsafe according to
29 the requirements of this bill or the requirements or recommendations contained in
30 any user manual provided by the manufacturer of the inflatable device, any update
31 to such a user manual and any information relating to a recall of the inflatable
32 device. Section 9 prohibits an operator from allowing a person to use an inflatable
33 device if any label attached by the manufacturer of the device is missing or
34 illegible. Section 9 also prohibits an operator business from selling or transferring
35 an inflatable device if any such label is missing or illegible if any user manual
36 provided by the manufacturer, any update to such a user manual and any
37 information relating to a recall of the inflatable device is not provided to the
38 purchaser or transferee. Section 10 requires an operator to: (1) monitor wind speed
39 at any location where an inflatable device will be used at all times while the device
40 is in use; and (2) cease operation of the inflatable device if the wind speed exceeds
41 the recommendation of the manufacturer or 15 miles per hour, whichever is greater.
42 Section 11 prescribes certain requirements for anchoring the inflatable device to the
43 ground. Section 12 requires an operator to display a sign containing certain
44 information at all times during the use of an inflatable device.
45 Section 13.5 of this bill authorizes a county, city or town to enact an ordinance
46 which establishes requirements and restrictions which are the same as or in addition
47 to those set forth in this bill and which provides for the imposition of criminal or
48 civil penalties, or both, for violations of those requirements and restrictions.

8 unless the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined
9 in sections 3, 4 and 5 of this act have the meanings ascribed to
10 them in those sections.

12 1. Incorporates a structural and mechanical system and
13 employs a high-strength fabric or film that achieves its strength,
14 shape and stability by tensioning from internal air pressure;
15 2. Is intended for use by a person to bounce, play, slide, climb
16 or otherwise interact for outdoor recreation; and
17 3. Includes, without limitation, a constant air inflatable
18 bounce house, inflatable slide or water slide, obstacle course, pool
19 or enclosed inflatable trampoline.

21 where an inflatable device is used who sets up, operates, maintains
22 or supervises the use of the inflatable device on behalf of an
23 operator business.

25 including, without limitation, a sole proprietorship, that:
26 1. Is engaged in the business of making inflatable devices

28 2. Employs, controls, directs or hires as an independent
29 contractor an operator to act on behalf of the business entity.
30 Sec. 6. A person shall not engage in the business of an
31 operator business unless the person:
32 1. Holds all applicable state and local business licenses; and
33 2. Has obtained a liability insurance policy, or a certificate of
34 insurance thereof, or a bond of a surety company or other surety
35 in an amount not less than $1,000,000.
36 Sec. 7. 1. An operator business shall keep a logbook for
37 any inflatable device which is made available for use by the
38 operator business. The logbook may be written or electronic, but
39 must be available in a form that is kept with the inflatable device
40 at all times and must be available for inspection at the request of
41 any customer of the business, user of the inflatable device or any
42 state or local agency.
43 2. An operator shall record and retain for not less than 2
44 years the results of each inspection conducted pursuant to section
45 8 of this act in the logbook.
