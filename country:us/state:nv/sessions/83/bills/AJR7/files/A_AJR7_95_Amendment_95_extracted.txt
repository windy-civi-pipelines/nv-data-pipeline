Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Proposes to amend the Nevada Constitution to revise provisions
relating to the compensation of certain elected officers.
(BDR C-654)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
ASSEMBLY JOINT RESOLUTION—Proposing to amend the Nevada
Constitution to provide a citizens’ commission to establish the
compensation of certain elected officers.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This resolution proposes to revise the Nevada Constitution to reserve to a Citizens’
2 Commission on Compensation for Certain Elected Officers the power to establish salaries and
3 benefits for members of the Legislature, the Governor, the Lieutenant Governor, the Secretary
4 of State, the State Treasurer, the State Controller and the Attorney General. This resolution
5 also proposes to revise the Nevada Constitution to authorize the Commission to: (1) fix the
6 compensation of the members of the Legislature for each calendar day of service during any
7 regular or special session of the Legislature; and (2) provide for the compensation of members
8 of the Legislature for the performance of duties as a member of the Legislature outside of a
9 regular or special session. This resolution further proposes to repeal from the Nevada
10 Constitution the authority of the Legislature to provide by law, under certain circumstances,
11 for increasing or diminishing the salaries or compensation of constitutional officers.
1 RESOLVED BY THE ASSEMBLY AND SENATE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, JOINTLY,
2 That a new section, designated Section 33A, be added to Article 4 of the Nevada
3 Constitution to read as follows:
4 Sec. 33A. 1. The Legislature shall provide by law for a Citizens’
5 Commission on Compensation for Certain Elected Officers.
Assembly Amendment No. 95 to Assembly Joint Resolution No. 7 Page 4
1 2. The Commission must consist of seven members appointed by the
2 Governor who have diverse personal and professional interests and reside
3 in various geographical areas of this State, who must include:
4 (a) One member who has expertise in public compensation and is
5 recommended by the Public Employees’ Retirement Board or its
6 successor organization;
7 (b) One member who represents a nonprofit public interest
8 organization;
9 (c) One member who represents the general public;
10 (d) Two members who represent the interests of business in this
11 State; and
12 (e) Two members who have experience as officers or members of a
13 labor organization in this State and are recommended by a labor
14 organization in this State.
15 3. Each member of the Commission must be a resident of this State
16 and must not be a state officer, public employee or lobbyist, or a parent,
17 spouse, sibling, child or dependent of a state officer, public employee or
18 lobbyist.
19 4. Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, the term of office
20 of each member of the Commission is 4 years. The Governor shall
21 appoint three of the members first appointed by him or her for initial
22 terms of 2 years. If a vacancy occurs, the Governor shall fill the vacancy
23 for the unexpired term in the same manner as the original appointment
24 within 30 days after the vacancy occurs. A member of the Commission
25 may not serve more than two terms.
26 5. The Governor may remove a member of the Commission only for
27 cause of incapacity, incompetence, neglect of duty, malfeasance in office
28 or failure to meet a qualification set forth in subsections 2 and 3.
29 6. The Commission shall elect a Chair from among its members.
30 Except as otherwise provided in this section, the Commission shall adopt
31 rules of procedure for the conduct of its hearings and any other
32 procedural rules it deems necessary to carry out its duties. The
33 affirmative vote of a majority of all the members appointed to the
34 Commission is required to take action.
35 7. Members of the Commission are entitled to:
36 (a) The compensation provided by law for members of the
37 Commission on Judicial Discipline who are not judicial officers; and
38 (b) The per diem allowance and travel expenses provided by law for
39 state officers and employees generally.
40 8. The Commission shall:
41 (a) Study the relationship of salaries and benefits to the duties of the
42 members of the Legislature, the Governor, Lieutenant Governor,
43 Secretary of State, State Treasurer, State Controller and Attorney
44 General;
45 (b) Compare the salaries and benefits of the elected officers set forth
46 in paragraph (a) to the salaries and benefits of persons who are employed
47 by a public or private employer and who have similar qualifications and
48 responsibilities as those elected officers;
49 (c) Fix the salaries and benefits of the elected officers set forth in
50 paragraph (a); and
51 (d) Carry out any duties provided by law by the Legislature.
52 9. Except for the initial schedule of salaries and benefits for elected
53 officers filed pursuant to subsection 10, the Commission may not increase
Assembly Amendment No. 95 to Assembly Joint Resolution No. 7 Page 5
1 or decrease the salary of any elected officer set forth in paragraph (a) of
2 subsection 8 by more than 15 percent of the salary of that elected officer
3 provided in the immediately preceding schedule of salaries for elected
4 officers. The Commission may exercise any powers conferred by the
5 Legislature.
6 10. The Commission shall file its initial schedule of salaries and
7 benefits for elected officers with the Secretary of State not later than
8 January 1, [2029,] 2031, and shall file a schedule of salaries and benefits
9 not later than January 1 of each odd-numbered year thereafter. Each
10 schedule of salaries and benefits is effective:
11 (a) For members of the Legislature, for the period from the first
12 Monday of February immediately following the January 1 that the
13 schedule is due through the day before the first Monday of February of
14 the next odd-numbered year; and
15 (b) For all other elected officers set forth in paragraph (a) of
16 subsection 8, for the period from July 1 immediately following the
17 January 1 that the schedule is due through June 30 of the next odd-
18 numbered year.
19 11. The Legislature shall provide by law for setting apart from each
20 year’s revenues a sufficient amount of money to pay such salaries and
21 benefits.
22 12. Before the Commission may file a schedule of salaries and
23 benefits with the Secretary of State, the Commission shall hold at least
24 four meetings at various locations throughout the State to receive public
25 testimony on the schedule. At the last public hearing before the schedule
26 is filed with the Secretary of State, the Commission shall adopt the
27 schedule as originally proposed or as amended. All meetings of the
28 Commission are subject to the provisions of any open meeting laws made
29 applicable generally to other public bodies.
30 13. The Legislative Counsel Bureau shall include in the Nevada
31 Revised Statutes a copy of the most recent schedule of salaries and
32 benefits established by the Commission and filed with the Secretary of
33 State.
34 And be it further
35 RESOLVED, That Section 33 of Article 4 of the Nevada Constitution be amended
36 to read as follows:
37 Sec. 33. The members of the Legislature shall receive for their
38 services a compensation to be fixed by [law] the Citizens’ Commission on
39 Compensation for Certain Elected Officers pursuant to Section 33A of
40 this Article and paid out of the public treasury, for [not to exceed 60 days]
41 each calendar day of service during any regular session of the Legislature
42 and [not to exceed 20 days] during any special session [; but no increase of
43 such compensation shall take effect during the term for which the members
44 of either house shall have been elected; Provided, that an] . The Citizens’
45 Commission on Compensation for Certain Elected Officers may provide
46 for the compensation of members of the Legislature for the performance
47 of duties as a member of the Legislature outside of a regular or special
48 session. An appropriation may be made for the payment of such actual
49 expenses as members of the Legislature may incur for postage, express
50 charges, newspapers and stationery not exceeding the sum of Sixty dollars
51 for any general or special session to each member; and Furthermore
52 Provided, that the Speaker of the Assembly, and Lieutenant Governor, as
53 President of the Senate, shall each, during the time of their actual
Assembly Amendment No. 95 to Assembly Joint Resolution No. 7 Page 6
1 attendance as such presiding officers receive an additional allowance of two
2 dollars per diem.
3 And be it further
4 RESOLVED, That Section 9 of Article 15 of the Nevada Constitution is hereby
5 repealed.
6 And be it further
7 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon passage.

Sec: 9. Increase or decrease of compensation of officers whose
compensation fixed by constitution. The Legislature may, at any time,
provide by law for increasing or diminishing the salaries or compensation
of any of the Officers, whose salaries or compensation is fixed in this
Constitution; Provided, no such change of Salary or compensation shall
apply to any Officer during the term for which he may have been elected.
