AN ACT relating to health care; prescribing requirements governing the denial of
requests for prior authorization; requiring, in certain circumstances,
licensed physicians, physician assistants, advanced practice registered
nurses and osteopathic physicians to discuss with patients certain
information relating to stem cell treatment, storage and donation; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law authorizes certain health insurers to require an insured to obtain prior
2 authorization from the insurer before receiving certain care. (NRS 687B.225) Sections 1, 4 [,]
3 and 5 [and 8] of this bill prohibit certain health insurers, including [Medicaid, the Children’s
4 Health Insurance Program and] insurance for public employees, from using , employing or
5 relying upon an artificial intelligence system or an automated decision tool to: (1) deny a
6 request for prior authorization for medical or dental care; (2) modify a request for prior
7 authorization for such care submitted by a provider of health care; (3) reduce the scope of
8 services or amount of coverage for such care that is included within a request for prior
9 authorization; or (4) terminate, reduce or modify coverage for previously approved medical
10 or dental care. Sections 1, 4 [,] and 5 [and 8] also [prohibit] require such health insurers
11 [from denying or modifying] to approve a request for prior authorization [on the grounds that
12 the care requested is not medically necessary or is experimental or investigational,] for
13 medical or dental care that is covered by the insurer, unless a licensed health care
14 professional with the education, training and expertise necessary to evaluate the condition at
15 issue [first makes such a determination. Section 2 of this bill makes a conforming change to
16 require the Director of the Department of Health and Human Services to administer the
17 requirements set forth in section 8 in the same manner as other requirements governing
18 Medicaid and the Children’s Health Insurance Program.] determines that the care requested
19 is not medically necessary or is experimental or investigational. Sections 6 and 7 of this
20 bill make conforming changes to clarify that certain provisions generally applicable to
21 members of plans of self-insurance for employees of local governments and are applicable to
22 the provisions of sections 4 and 5, respectively.
23 Existing law requires a physician, physician assistant, advanced practice registered nurse
24 or an osteopathic physician to counsel and provide certain information to a patient who is
25 diagnosed with certain conditions. (NRS 630.3737, 632.2383, 633.6947) Sections 9-11 of this
Senate Amendment No. 301 to Senate Bill No. 128 Page 4
26 bill require a physician, physician assistant, advanced practice registered nurse or an
27 osteopathic physician to: (1) discuss with a patient, upon diagnosing the patient with arthritis,
28 osteoarthritis or any other condition that is regularly treated using stem cell therapy, the
29 potential use of stem cell therapy to treat the condition; and (2) when acting as a provider of
30 primary care during the first encounter with a new patient, inform the patient of options that
31 may be available to the patient for donating, banking or storing stem cells for future use by the
32 patient or a donee.

AN ACT relating to health care; prescribing requirements governing the denial of
requests for prior authorization; requiring, in certain circumstances,
licensed physicians, physician assistants, advanced practice registered
nurses and osteopathic physicians to discuss with patients certain
information relating to stem cell treatment, storage and donation; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
