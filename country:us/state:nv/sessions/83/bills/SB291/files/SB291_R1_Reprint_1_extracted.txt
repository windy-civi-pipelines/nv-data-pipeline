FIRST REPRINT S.B. 291
SENATE BILL NO. 291–SENATORS DOÑATE, FLORES AND TAYLOR
MARCH 6, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Requires the Department of Motor Vehicles to
establish procedures by which a victim of identity
theft may obtain a new driver’s license number.
(BDR 43-39)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: Increases or Newly
Provides for Term of Imprisonment in County or City
Jail or Detention Facility.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to drivers’ licenses; requiring the Department of
Motor Vehicles to establish procedures by which a victim
of identity theft may obtain a new driver’s license
number; prohibiting the Department from charging such a
person a fee to obtain a new driver’s license number; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides for the issuance of drivers’ licenses by the Department of
2 Motor Vehicles. (Chapter 483 of NRS) Section 12 of this bill requires the
3 Department to establish procedures by which a licensee who is a victim of identity
4 theft may request that the number of his or her driver’s license be changed to a new
5 unique number. Section 14 of this bill prohibits the Department from charging a fee
6 for making such a change. Section 13 of this bill makes a conforming change so
7 that the definitions applicable to the provisions of existing law governing drivers’
8 licenses apply to section 12.

8 Sec. 12. Chapter 483 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
9 thereto a new section to read as follows:
10 1. The Department shall establish procedures by which a
11 licensee who is a victim of identity theft may request that the
12 number of his or her driver’s license be changed to a new unique
13 number.
14 2. As used in this section, “identity theft” means a violation
15 of the provisions of NRS 205.463, 205.464 or 205.465.
16 Sec. 13. NRS 483.020 is hereby amended to read as follows:
17 483.020 As used in NRS 483.010 to 483.630, inclusive, and
18 section 12 of this act, unless the context otherwise requires, the
19 words and terms defined in NRS 483.030 to 483.190, inclusive,
20 have the meanings ascribed to them in those sections.

24 motorcycle driver’s license, issued and service performed, the
25 following fees must be charged:
26
27 An original or renewal license issued to a person

29 An original or renewal license issued to any
30 person less than 65 years of age which
31 expires on the eighth anniversary of the

35 expires on or before the fourth anniversary of
36 the licensee’s birthday ................................................ 18.50

38 NRS 483.330 for a noncommercial driver’s

40 Each readministration to the same person of the

1 484C.120, 484C.130 or 484C.430, or

3 Reinstatement of a license after revocation for a
4 violation of NRS 484C.110, 484C.120,
5 484C.130 or 484C.430, or pursuant to NRS
6 484C.210 and 484C.220 ........................................... 120.00
7 A new photograph, change of name, change of
8 other information, except address, or any
9 combination .................................................................. 5.00
10 A duplicate license .......................................................... 14.00
11
12 2. For every motorcycle endorsement to a driver’s license, a
13 fee of $5 must be charged.
14 3. If no other change is requested or required, the Department
15 shall not charge a fee to change the number of a driver’s license at
16 the request of a licensee who is a victim of identity theft pursuant
17 to section 12 of this act or to convert the number of a license from
18 the licensee’s social security number, or a number that was
19 formulated by using the licensee’s social security number as a basis
20 for the number, to a unique number that is not based on the
21 licensee’s social security number.
22 4. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 483.417, the increase
23 in fees authorized by NRS 483.347 and the fees charged pursuant to
24 NRS 483.415 must be paid in addition to the fees charged pursuant
25 to subsections 1 and 2.
26 5. A penalty of $10 must be paid by each person renewing a
27 license after it has expired for a period of 30 days or more as
28 provided in NRS 483.386 unless the person is exempt pursuant to
29 that section.
30 6. The Department may not charge a fee for the reinstatement
31 of a driver’s license that has been:
32 (a) Voluntarily surrendered for medical reasons; or
33 (b) Cancelled pursuant to NRS 483.310.
34 7. All fees and penalties are payable to the Administrator at the
35 time a license or a renewal license is issued.
36 8. Except as otherwise provided in NRS 483.340, subsection 3
37 of NRS 483.3485, NRS 483.415 and 483.840, and subsection 3 of
38 NRS 483.863, all money collected by the Department pursuant to
39 this chapter must be deposited in the State Treasury for credit to the
40 Motor Vehicle Fund.
