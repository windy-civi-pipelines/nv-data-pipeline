Section 1:
S.B. 203
FEBRUARY 12, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Provides immunity from civil liability for certain
agencies, entities and persons relating to the pari-
mutuel system of wagering. (BDR 41-608)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to gaming; providing immunity from civil liability
for certain agencies, entities and persons relating to the
pari-mutuel system of wagering; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Nevada Gaming Commission to administer provisions
2 governing the issuance of licenses and to perform various acts relating to the
3 regulation and control of the pari-mutuel system of wagering. (Chapter 464 of
4 NRS) This bill provides that the State of Nevada, the Nevada Gaming Commission,
5 the Nevada Gaming Control Board, the Off-Track Pari-Mutuel Wagering
6 Committee and certain other persons are immune from civil liability for any
7 decision or action taken in good faith and without malicious intent in carrying out
8 the provisions of law relating to the pari-mutuel system of wagering.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 464 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 In addition to any other rights, privileges and immunities
4 recognized by law, the State of Nevada, the Nevada Gaming
5 Commission and any of its members, employees, attorneys and
6 other personnel, the Nevada Gaming Control Board and any of its
7 members, employees, attorneys and other personnel, and the Off-
8 Track Pari-Mutuel Wagering Committee appointed pursuant to
-
*SB203*
– 2 –
1 NRS 464.020 and any of its members, employees, attorneys and
2 other personnel are immune from civil liability for any decision or
3 action taken in good faith and without malicious intent in carrying
4 out the provisions of this chapter.
5 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
