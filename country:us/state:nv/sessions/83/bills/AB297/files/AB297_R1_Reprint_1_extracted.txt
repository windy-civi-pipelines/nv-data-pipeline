Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; requiring Medicaid to provide
coverage for home visitation services for persons who are
pregnant or have recently given birth; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services to
2 administer the Medicaid program, which includes coverage for certain services
3 related to postpartum care. (NRS 422.271775) Section 2.3 of this bill requires the
4 Department to include under Medicaid coverage for optional home visitation
5 services provided to recipients of Medicaid who are pregnant or have given birth
6 within the immediately preceding 24 months. Section 2.3 also requires the
7 Department to notify recipients of Medicaid who are eligible to receive such
8 services. Section 2.7 of this bill makes a conforming change to require the Director
9 of the Department to administer section 2.3 in the same manner as other provisions
10 of law governing Medicaid.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
3 Sec. 2.3. Chapter 422 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
4 thereto a new section to read as follows:
5 1. To the extent that financial participation is available, the
6 Director shall include under Medicaid coverage for optional home

1 visitation services for recipients of Medicaid who are pregnant or
2 have given birth within the immediately preceding 24 months as
3 necessary to support a healthy pregnancy, birth, postpartum
4 period and first 24 months of the life of the child. Such services
5 may include, without limitation, educational and informational
6 support to the family, counseling relating to family planning,
7 preventative screenings, referrals to medical, social and
8 community services and coordination of care.

10 requirements for providers of services described in subsection 1,

18 indicate that such services are optional and that no person is
19 required to receive such services.

21 (a) Apply to the Secretary of Health and Human Services for

23 Plan that is necessary for the Department to receive federal
24 funding to provide the coverage described in subsection 1.
25 (b) Fully cooperate in good faith with the Federal Government
26 during the application process to satisfy the requirements of the
27 Federal Government for obtaining a waiver or amendment
28 pursuant to paragraph (a).
