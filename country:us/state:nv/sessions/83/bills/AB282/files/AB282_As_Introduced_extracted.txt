Section 1:
A.B. 282
FEBRUARY 24, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to billing for health care.
(BDR 40-785)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; requiring a medical facility or a
provider of health care who receives notice of or
otherwise discovers a potential error in a medical bill to
review the potential error and refund any overpayment;
authorizing disciplinary action for the failure to comply
with such requirements; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides every patient of a medical facility with the right to
2 examine the bill for his or her care and receive an explanation of the bill. (NRS
3 449A.106) Existing law also requires each provider of health care to timely provide
4 a patient with an itemized bill at no additional cost to the patient. (NRS 629.071)
5 Sections 2 and 3 of this bill, respectively, require a medical facility or provider of
6 health care that determines or receives notice that a medical bill may contain one or
7 more billing errors to: (1) review the potential billing errors; (2) notify the patient
8 of the review and the results of the review; and (3) refund any overpayment by the
9 patient. Sections 1 and 4 of this bill, respectively, authorize the suspension or
10 revocation of the license of a medical facility that violates section 2, and the
11 imposition of disciplinary action against and administrator of certain facilities who
12 aids or abets a violation of section 2. Section 3 authorizes the imposition of
13 disciplinary action against a provider of health care who fails to comply with the
14 requirements of section 3.

26 NRS 439A.102.
27 (f) Failure to comply with the provisions of NRS 441A.315 and
28 any regulations adopted pursuant thereto or NRS 449.2486.
29 (g) Violation of the provisions of NRS 458.112.
30 (h) Failure to comply with the provisions of NRS 449A.170 to
31 449A.192, inclusive, and any regulation adopted pursuant thereto.
32 (i) Violation of the provisions of NRS 629.260.
33 2. In addition to the provisions of subsection 1, the Division
34 may revoke a license to operate a facility for the dependent if, with

25 thereto a new section to read as follows:
26 1. Not later than 30 days after determining or receiving
27 notice from a patient or any other source that a bill may contain
28 one or more billing errors, a medical facility shall:
29 (a) Notify in writing the patient to whom the bill relates:
30 (1) Of each potential billing error;
31 (2) That the medical facility will conduct a review pursuant
32 to paragraph (b); and
33 (3) That during a review conducted pursuant to paragraph
34 (b) the medical facility will not bill the patient for any care that is
35 subject to the review.
36 (b) Review the potential billing errors. During such a review,
37 the medical facility shall not bill the patient for any care that is
38 subject to the review.
39 2. Not later than 30 days after completing a review pursuant
40 to paragraph (b) of subsection 1, a medical facility shall:
41 (a) Notify the patient in writing of the completion and results
42 of the review. The notice must include, without limitation:
43 (1) Notice of whether the review resulted in the discovery of
44 any billing errors and:

2 errors, an explanation of each billing error and the manner in
3 which the billing error was corrected; or

5 billing errors, an explanation of the reasons why the potential
6 errors identified pursuant to subsection 1 are not actual billing
7 errors; and
8 (2) Applicable coding guidelines, references to medical
9 records and other information relevant to the explanation
10 provided pursuant to subparagraph (1).
11 (b) If the review resulted in the discovery of a billing error
12 which resulted in the patient overpaying for care, refund to the
13 patient the amount of the overpayment.
