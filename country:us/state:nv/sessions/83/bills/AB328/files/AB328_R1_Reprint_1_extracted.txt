Section 1:

FIRST REPRINT A.B. 328
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 328–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS
D’SILVA AND JACKSON
MARCH 3, 2025
____________
JOINT SPONSOR: SENATOR NEAL
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Creates the Subcommittee to Study and Develop
Policy Recommendations for African Americans of
the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education
to conduct a study during the 2025-2026 interim.
(BDR S-619)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; creating the Subcommittee to Study
and Develop Policy Recommendations for African
Americans of the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
Education to conduct a study during the 2025-2026
interim; prescribing the membership, powers and duties of
the Subcommittee; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education and
2 authorizes the Committee to: (1) evaluate, review and comment upon issues related
3 to education in this State; (2) conduct investigations and hold hearings in
4 connection with such duties; and (3) make recommendations to the Legislature
5 relating to the improvement of public education. (NRS 218E.320, 218E.615)
6 Existing law requires the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education to
7 prepare and submit to the Legislature a comprehensive report of its activities in the
8 interim and its findings and any recommendations for proposed legislation. (NRS
9 218E.330) Section 1 of this bill creates the Subcommittee to Study and Develop
10 Policy Recommendations for African Americans of the Joint Interim Standing
11 Committee on Education to conduct the study set forth in section 2 of this bill
12 during the 2025-2026 interim. Section 1 prescribes the membership and officers of
-
*AB328_R1*
– 2 –
13 the Subcommittee and the compensation of the members. Section 1 also provides
14 for the filling of vacancies and requirements for meetings.
15 Section 2 requires the Subcommittee to: (1) study certain matters relating to
16 historical discrimination in this State and the related economic effects of the
17 discrimination to present day society in this State; and (2) present its findings and
18 make recommendations relating to its findings to the Joint Interim Standing
19 Committee on Education, for inclusion in its 2026-2026 interim report, for methods
20 to educate the general public about the findings and recommendations of the
21 Subcommittee, advancement of racial healing, understanding and transformation
22 and the form and scope of rehabilitation or restitution to the descendants of
23 formerly enslaved Africans, if warranted, based on the findings of the
24 Subcommittee. Section 2 authorizes the Subcommittee to collaborate and consult
25 with and invite participation by historians, scholars and other interested parties and
26 requires state agencies to provide any information requested by the Subcommittee
27 relating to its duties. Section 2 also authorizes the Subcommittee to: (1) request
28 assistance from the Division of State Library, Archives and Public Records of the
29 Department of Administration, which is required to provide assistance to the extent
30 that resources are available; and (2) direct the Legislative Counsel Bureau to assist
31 in its research investigations, review and analysis.
32 Section 2 further authorizes the Subcommittee to apply for and accept certain
33 funds for the Subcommittee in carrying out its duties.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. The Subcommittee to Study and Develop
2 Policy Recommendations for African Americans of the Joint Interim
3 Standing Committee on Education is hereby created to conduct the
4 study described in section 2 of this act during the 2025-2026
5 interim.
6 2. The Subcommittee consists of the following ten members
7 appointed by the Chair of the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
8 Education:
9 (a) Two members of the general public from a list of at least
10 three persons recommended by the President of the University of
11 Nevada, Las Vegas, or his or her designee;
12 (b) Two members of the general public from a list of at least
13 three persons recommended by the President of the University of
14 Nevada, Reno, or his or her designee;
15 (c) Two members of the Senate recommended by the Majority
16 Leader of the Senate;
17 (d) Two members of the Assembly recommended by the
18 Speaker of the Assembly;
19 (e) One member of the Senate recommended by the Minority
20 Leader of the Senate; and
21 (f) One member of the Assembly recommended by the Minority
22 Leader of the Assembly.
-
*AB328_R1*
– 3 –
1 3. Members of the Subcommittee must be recommended and
2 appointed on the basis of their education, training and experience in
3 the fields of African American studies or reparatory justice or any
4 interrelated disciplines or subject matter areas or any other relevant
5 policy or professional experience.
6 4. Any vacancy occurring in the membership of the
7 Subcommittee must be filled in the same manner as the original
8 appointment.
9 5. For each day or portion of a day during which a member of
10 the Subcommittee who is a Legislator attends a meeting of the
11 Subcommittee or is otherwise engaged in the business of the
12 Subcommittee, the member is entitled to receive:
13 (a) The compensation provided for a majority of the Legislators
14 during the first 60 days of the preceding regular session;
15 (b) The per diem allowance provided for state officers and
16 employees generally; and
17 (c) The travel expenses provided pursuant to NRS 218A.655.
18 6. While engaged in the business of the Subcommittee, the
19 members of the Subcommittee who are not Legislators are entitled
20 to the per diem allowance and travel expenses provided for state
21 officers and employees generally.
22 7. All compensation, per diem allowances and travel expenses
23 provided pursuant to subsections 5 and 6 must be paid from the
24 Legislative Fund.
25 8. The Chair of the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
26 Education shall appoint a Chair and Vice Chair of the Subcommittee
27 from among the members of the Subcommittee appointed pursuant
28 to paragraphs (c) to (f), inclusive, of subsection 2. The Chair of the
29 Subcommittee must be appointed from one House and the Vice
30 Chair of the Subcommittee from the other House. If a vacancy
31 occurs in the office of the Chair or Vice Chair of the Subcommittee,
32 the vacancy must be filled in the same manner as the original
33 appointment.
34 9. Except as otherwise ordered by the Legislative Commission,
35 the Subcommittee shall meet not earlier than November 1, 2025,
36 and not later than August 31, 2026, at the times and places specified
37 by a call of the Chair.
38 10. A majority of the members of the Subcommittee constitutes
39 a quorum, and a quorum may exercise all the powers and authority
40 conferred on the Subcommittee.
41 Sec. 2. 1. The Subcommittee created by section 1 of this act
42 shall study:
43 (a) The economic, political, social, educational and historical
44 impacts of slavery, Jim Crow laws, de jure and de facto racial
45 segregation and other forms of racial discrimination in the State of
-
*AB328_R1*
– 4 –
1 Nevada, as well as the prior organized and unorganized territories
2 that constitute a part of the current state boundaries of the State;
3 (b) Discriminatory habits or practices, including, without
4 limitation, forced slavery, chattel slavery, indentured servitude,
5 redlining, segregated school systems and segregated public goods
6 and spaces, that may have caused significant economic or societal
7 disparities of formerly enslaved Africans or their descendants; and
8 (c) The related economic effects to present day society in the
9 State of Nevada as a result of the matters studied pursuant to this
10 subsection.
11 2. On or before August 31, 2026, the Subcommittee shall
12 present its findings and make recommendations, including specific
13 policy recommendations, relating to its findings to the Joint Interim
14 Standing Committee on Education for:
15 (a) Methods to educate the general public of the State of Nevada
16 of the Subcommittee’s findings and recommendations;
17 (b) Advancement of racial healing, understanding and
18 transformation; and
19 (c) Forms of rehabilitation or restitution to the descendants of
20 formerly enslaved Africans, if warranted, based on the findings
21 of the Subcommittee. If the Subcommittee finds that any form of
22 rehabilitation or restitution is warranted, then the form and scope of
23 such measures.
24 3. The Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education shall
25 include the findings and recommendations of the Subcommittee
26 presented pursuant to subsection 2 in the report for the 2025-2026
27 interim submitted by the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
28 Education pursuant to NRS 218E.330.
29 4. The Subcommittee may conduct investigations and hold
30 hearings in connection with its functions and duties and exercise any
31 of the investigative powers set forth in NRS 218E.105 to 218E.140,
32 inclusive.
33 5. The Subcommittee may collaborate and consult with and
34 invite participation by historians, scholars and any other interested
35 parties.
36 6. Each state agency, as defined in NRS 277.186, shall
37 cooperate with the Subcommittee and timely provide any
38 information that may be requested by the Subcommittee relating to
39 its duties.
40 7. The Subcommittee may:
41 (a) Request assistance from the Division of State Library,
42 Archives and Public Records of the Department of Administration.
43 Upon such a request, the Division shall provide to the
44 Subcommittee such assistance to the extent that resources are
45 available.
-
*AB328_R1*
– 5 –
1 (b) Direct the Legislative Counsel Bureau to assist in its
2 research, investigations, review and analysis.
3 8. The Subcommittee may apply for any available grants and
4 accept any gifts, grants or donations to assist the Subcommittee in
5 carrying out its duties.
6 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
7 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
8 Sec. 5. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025, and expires
9 by limitation on December 31, 2026.
