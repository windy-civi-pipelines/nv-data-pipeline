Recommendations for African Americans of the Joint Interim
Standing Committee on Education to conduct a study during the

MARCH 3, 2025
_______________
JOINT SPONSOR: SENATOR NEAL
_______________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Creates [the Subcommittee to Study and Develop Policy
Recommendations for African Americans of the Joint Interim
Standing Committee on Education to conduct a study during the
2025-2026 interim.] certain commissions and subcommittees
for the purpose of educating the general public concerning
certain cultural affairs relating to African-Americans.
(BDR [S-619)] 18-619
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; creating the Juneteenth Educational and
Economic Commission within the Department of Tourism and
Cultural Affairs; prescribing the membership, powers and duties
of the Commission; creating the Juneteenth Educational and
Economic Fund; creating the Subcommittee to Study and Develop
Policy Recommendations for African-Americans of the Joint Interim
Standing Committee on Education to conduct a study during the 2025-
2026 interim; prescribing the membership, powers and duties of the
Subcommittee; and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Department of Tourism and Cultural Affairs whose duties
2 include, without limitation, the oversight of plans for tourism, publications and cultural
3 affairs. (NRS 231.8117, 231.8131) Section 1.6 of this bill creates the Juneteenth
4 Educational and Economic Commission within the Department and provides that the
5 membership of the Commission consists of: (1) three members, each of whom is from the
6 African-American studies department or similar cultural studies program at the Nevada
7 State College, The University of Nevada, Las Vegas and the University of Nevada, Reno,
8 respectively; (2) one representative of Juneteenth Nevada, or any successor nonprofit
9 organization in this State whose mission is focused on Juneteenth Day education, unity
10 and diversity; (3) one member who represents a professional association representing
11 African-American businesses; (4) one member who represents an African-American
12 cultural awareness advocacy group; (5) one member who represents an educational
Assembly Amendment No. 882 to Assembly Bill No. 328 First Reprint Page 4
13 advocacy group with a statewide presence; and (6) one member who represents a
14 museum or cultural history organization in this State. To the extent that funding is
15 available, section 1.6 requires the Department to provide administrative support to the
16 Commission.
17 Section 1.7 of this bill requires the Commission to: (1) create and distribute written,
18 electronic or printed materials to educate the public on the history and meaning of
19 Juneteenth Day in the State of Nevada and the United States; (2) develop strategies with
20 businesses, nonprofit organizations and community groups to support education on the
21 significance of Juneteenth Day; (3) support the efforts of businesses, community groups
22 and local governments to establish events and programs relating to Juneteenth Day
23 throughout the State; and (4) support programs in the arts to celebrate Juneteenth Day.
24 Section 1.8 of this bill creates the Juneteenth Educational and Economic Fund for
25 the support of the Commission.
26 Sections 1.2-1.5 of this bill define certain terms relating to the Commission.
27 Existing law creates the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education and authorizes
28 the Committee to: (1) evaluate, review and comment upon issues related to education in this
29 State; (2) conduct investigations and hold hearings in connection with such duties; and (3)
30 make recommendations to the Legislature relating to the improvement of public education.
31 (NRS 218E.320, 218E.615) Existing law requires the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
32 Education to prepare and submit to the Legislature a comprehensive report of its activities in
33 the interim and its findings and any recommendations for proposed legislation. (NRS
34 218E.330) Section [1] 1.9 of this bill creates the Subcommittee to Study and Develop Policy
35 Recommendations for African-Americans of the Joint Interim Standing Committee on
36 Education to conduct the study set forth in section 2 of this bill during the 2025-2026 interim.
37 Section [1] 1.9 prescribes the membership and officers of the Subcommittee and the
38 compensation of the members. Section [1] 1.9 also provides for the filling of vacancies and
39 requirements for meetings.
40 Section 2 requires the Subcommittee to: (1) study certain matters relating to historical
41 discrimination in this State and the related economic effects of the discrimination to present
42 day society in this State; and (2) present its findings and make recommendations relating to its
43 findings to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education, for inclusion in its 2026-2026
44 interim report, for methods to educate the general public about the findings and
45 recommendations of the Subcommittee, advancement of racial healing, understanding and
46 transformation and the form and scope of rehabilitation or restitution to the descendants of
47 formerly enslaved Africans, if warranted, based on the findings of the Subcommittee. Section
48 2 authorizes the Subcommittee to collaborate and consult with and invite participation by
49 historians, scholars and other interested parties and requires state agencies to provide any
50 information requested by the Subcommittee relating to its duties. Section 2 also authorizes the
51 Subcommittee to: (1) request assistance from the Division of State Library, Archives and
52 Public Records of the Department of Administration, which is required to provide assistance
53 to the extent that resources are available; and (2) direct the Legislative Counsel Bureau to
54 assist in its research investigations, review and analysis.
55 Section 2 further authorizes the Subcommittee to apply for and accept certain funds for
56 the Subcommittee in carrying out its duties.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 231 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 1.1 to 1.8, inclusive, of this act.
3 Sec. 1.1. As used in sections 1.1 to 1.8, inclusive, of this act, unless the
4 context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in sections 1.2 to 1.5,
5 inclusive, of this act have the meanings ascribed to them in those sections.
6 Sec. 1.2. “Commission” means the Juneteenth Educational and Economic
7 Commission created by section 1.6 of this act.

2 Affairs.
3 Sec. 1.4. “Director” means the Director of the Department.
4 Sec. 1.5. “Fund” means the Juneteenth Educational and Economic Fund
5 created by section 1.8 of this act.
6 Sec. 1.6. 1. The Juneteenth Educational and Economic Commission is
7 hereby created within the Department.
8 2. The Juneteenth Educational and Economic Commission is composed of
9 eight voting members appointed by the Governor based on recommendations
10 from the Director, as follows:
11 (a) One member from the African-American studies department or similar
12 cultural studies program at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas;
13 (b) One member from the African-American studies department or similar
14 cultural studies program at the University of Nevada, Reno;
15 (c) One member from the African-American studies department or similar
16 cultural studies program at the Nevada State College;
17 (d) One representative of Juneteenth Nevada or any successor nonprofit
18 organization in this State whose mission is focused on Juneteenth Day education,
19 unity and diversity;
20 (e) One member who represents a professional association representing
21 African-American businesses;
22 (f) One member who represents an African-American cultural awareness
23 advocacy group;
24 (g) One member who represents an educational advocacy group with a
25 statewide presence; and
26 (h) One member who represents a museum or cultural history organization
27 in this State.
28 3. The term of each voting member of the Commission is 4 years. Members
29 may be reappointed for 1 additional term of 4 years. Any vacancy occurring in
30 the membership of the Commission must be filled in the same manner as the
31 original appointment.
32 4. The Commission shall meet once each calendar quarter. At the first
33 regular meeting of each year, the members of the Commission shall select a
34 Chair and Vice Chair by majority vote who shall serve until the next Chair and
35 Vice Chair is elected.
36 5. A majority of members of the Commission constitutes a quorum for the
37 transaction of business, and a majority of those members present at any meeting
38 is sufficient for any official action taken by the Commission.
39 6. To the extent that funding is available, the Department shall provide
40 administrative support to the Commission as necessary for the Commission to
41 carry out its duties.
42 Sec. 1.7. The Commission shall to the extent practicable:
43 1. Create and distribute written, electronic or printed materials to educate
44 the public on the history and meaning of Juneteenth Day in the State of Nevada
45 and the United States;
46 2. Develop strategies with businesses, nonprofit organizations and
47 community groups to support education in this State on the significance of
48 Juneteenth Day;
49 3. Support the efforts of businesses, community groups and local
50 governments to establish events and programs relating to Juneteenth Day
51 throughout the State; and
52 4. Support programs in the arts to celebrate Juneteenth Day.

1 Sec. 1.8. 1. There is hereby created the Juneteenth Educational and
2 Economic Fund in the State Treasury, to be administered by the Director, for the
3 support of the Commission.
4 2. Any interest earned on money in the Fund must be credited to the Fund.
5 3. Money that remains in the Fund at the end of a fiscal year does not
6 revert to the State General Fund, and the balance in the Fund must be carried
7 forward to the next fiscal year.
8 4. The Director may accept gifts, bequests, grants, appropriations and
9 donations from any source for deposit in the Fund.
10 5. Local governments that celebrate Juneteenth Day shall, to the extent
11 practicable, contribute to the Fund and otherwise collaborate on the development
12 and coordination of formal celebrations for Juneteenth Day.
13 [Section 1.] Sec. 1.9. 1. The Subcommittee to Study and Develop Policy
14 Recommendations for African-Americans of the Joint Interim Standing Committee
15 on Education is hereby created to conduct the study described in section 2 of this
16 act during the 2025-2026 interim.
17 2. The Subcommittee consists of the following ten members appointed by the
18 Chair of the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education:
19 (a) Two members of the general public from a list of at least three persons
20 recommended by the President of the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, or his or
21 her designee;
22 (b) Two members of the general public from a list of at least three persons
23 recommended by the President of the University of Nevada, Reno, or his or her
24 designee;
25 (c) Two members of the Senate recommended by the Majority Leader of the
26 Senate;
27 (d) Two members of the Assembly recommended by the Speaker of the
28 Assembly;
29 (e) One member of the Senate recommended by the Minority Leader of the
30 Senate; and
31 (f) One member of the Assembly recommended by the Minority Leader of the
32 Assembly.
33 3. Members of the Subcommittee must be recommended and appointed on the
34 basis of their education, training and experience in the fields of African-American
35 studies or reparatory justice or any interrelated disciplines or subject matter areas or
36 any other relevant policy or professional experience.
37 4. Any vacancy occurring in the membership of the Subcommittee must be
38 filled in the same manner as the original appointment.
39 5. For each day or portion of a day during which a member of the
40 Subcommittee who is a Legislator attends a meeting of the Subcommittee or is
41 otherwise engaged in the business of the Subcommittee, the member is entitled to
42 receive:
43 (a) The compensation provided for a majority of the Legislators during the first
44 60 days of the preceding regular session;
45 (b) The per diem allowance provided for state officers and employees
46 generally; and
47 (c) The travel expenses provided pursuant to NRS 218A.655.
48 6. While engaged in the business of the Subcommittee, the members of the
49 Subcommittee who are not Legislators are entitled to the per diem allowance and
50 travel expenses provided for state officers and employees generally.
51 7. All compensation, per diem allowances and travel expenses provided
52 pursuant to subsections 5 and 6 must be paid from the Legislative Fund.

14 Sec. 2. 1. The Subcommittee created by section [1] 1.9 of this act shall
15 study:
16 (a) The economic, political, social, educational and historical impacts of
17 slavery, Jim Crow laws, de jure and de facto racial segregation and other forms of
18 racial discrimination in the State of Nevada, as well as the prior organized and
19 unorganized territories that constitute a part of the current state boundaries of the
20 State;
21 (b) Discriminatory habits or practices, including, without limitation, forced
22 slavery, chattel slavery, indentured servitude, redlining, segregated school systems
23 and segregated public goods and spaces, that may have caused significant economic
24 or societal disparities of formerly enslaved Africans or their descendants; and
25 (c) The related economic effects to present day society in the State of Nevada
26 as a result of the matters studied pursuant to this subsection.
27 2. On or before August 31, 2026, the Subcommittee shall present its findings
28 and make recommendations, including specific policy recommendations, relating to
29 its findings to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education for:
30 (a) Methods to educate the general public of the State of Nevada of the
31 Subcommittee’s findings and recommendations;
32 (b) Advancement of racial healing, understanding and transformation; and
33 (c) Forms of rehabilitation or restitution to the descendants of formerly
34 enslaved Africans, if warranted, based on the findings of the Subcommittee. If the
35 Subcommittee finds that any form of rehabilitation or restitution is warranted, then
36 the form and scope of such measures.
37 3. The Joint Interim Standing Committee on Education shall include the
38 findings and recommendations of the Subcommittee presented pursuant to
39 subsection 2 in the report for the 2025-2026 interim submitted by the Joint Interim
40 Standing Committee on Education pursuant to NRS 218E.330.
41 4. The Subcommittee may conduct investigations and hold hearings in
42 connection with its functions and duties and exercise any of the investigative
43 powers set forth in NRS 218E.105 to 218E.140, inclusive.
44 5. The Subcommittee may collaborate and consult with and invite
45 participation by historians, scholars and any other interested parties.
46 6. Each state agency, as defined in NRS 277.186, shall cooperate with the
47 Subcommittee and timely provide any information that may be requested by the
48 Subcommittee relating to its duties.
49 7. The Subcommittee may:
50 (a) Request assistance from the Division of State Library, Archives and Public
51 Records of the Department of Administration. Upon such a request, the Division
52 shall provide to the Subcommittee such assistance to the extent that resources are
53 available.
