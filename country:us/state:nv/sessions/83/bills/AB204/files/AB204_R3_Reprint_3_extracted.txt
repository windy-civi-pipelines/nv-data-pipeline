SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to medical debt.
(BDR 52-135)

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to medical debt; prohibiting certain actions by
certain entities that provide health care and collection
agencies to collect a medical debt; providing requirements
for engaging in extraordinary collection actions to collect
a medical debt; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law provides requirements governing the collection of medical debt by
2 a collection agency, including a requirement to provide certain notice to a medical
3 debtor before taking action to collect and prohibiting certain actions to collect a
4 medical debt. (NRS 649.366, 649.368)
5 Sections 14 and 19 of this bill prohibit a health care entity or collection agency
6 from engaging in certain actions, or threatening to engage in certain actions, to
7 collect a medical debt, including: (1) causing the arrest of a consumer; (2) obtaining
8 a lien or foreclosing on real property of a consumer that includes the consumer’s
9 primary residence; (3) garnishing any refund of federal income taxes due to a
10 consumer; or (4) garnishing, attaching or seizing the bank account of a consumer.
11 Section 15 of this bill prohibits a health care entity or collection agency from
12 engaging in extraordinary collection actions until at least 180 days after the first bill
13 for a medical debt is sent to a consumer and requires a notice that includes certain
14 information to be sent to the consumer at least 30 days before any extraordinary
15 collection action is taken. Section 6 of this bill defines the term “extraordinary
16 collection action” to mean: (1) selling a medical debt to a collection agency; (2) any
17 action that requires a legal or judicial process except for placing a lien on third
18 party settlements; (3) reporting, furnishing or threatening to report or furnish any
19 information regarding a medical debt to a consumer reporting agency; or (4)
20 garnishing or threatening to garnish the wages of a consumer. Section 15 prohibits
21 a health care entity or collection agency from engaging in extraordinary collection
22 actions when there is a state of emergency, declaration of disaster or public health
23 emergency or other health event in the county in which a consumer resides and tolls
-
*AB204_R3*
– 2 –
24 any statute of limitations applicable to a claim to recover on a medical debt during
25 the same period.
26 Section 16 of this bill provides additional requirements for the collection of
27 medical debts incurred for medical services, medical products or medical devices
28 provided by a health care entity, including: (1) a prohibition on engaging in
29 extraordinary collection actions unless such actions are described in the billing and
30 collection policy of the health care entity; (2) a requirement to reverse any
31 extraordinary collection actions if it is determined that the consumer is eligible for
32 financial assistance that eliminates or would have eliminated the debt; and (3)
33 requiring a consumer to be refunded within 60 days if, after the application of any
34 financial assistance, a consumer had paid an amount in excess of the amount of the
35 medical debt.
36 Section 17 of this bill provides that the protections provided by sections 13-16
37 cannot be waived.
38 Sections 2.5-12 of this bill define terms related to the collection of medical
39 debts and section 2 of this bill makes those terms applicable to sections 2-18.

9 Sec. 19. NRS 649.368 is hereby amended to read as follows:
10 649.368 A collection agency, or its compliance manager,
11 agents or employees, shall not, for any medical debt:
12 1. Take any confession of judgment or any power of attorney
13 running to the collection agency or to any third person to confess
14 judgment or to appear for the debtor in a judicial proceeding.
15 2. Commence a civil action to collect the medical debt if the
16 amount of the medical debt, excluding interest, late fees, collection
17 costs, attorney’s fees and any other fees or costs, is less than the
18 maximum jurisdictional amount set forth in subsection 1 of NRS
19 73.010. Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to prohibit the
20 commencement of a small claims action in justice court to collect
21 the medical debt.
22 3. Charge or collect a fee of more than 5 percent of the amount
23 of the medical debt, excluding interest, late fees, collection costs,
24 attorney’s fees and any other fees or costs, as a collection fee or as
25 an attorney’s fee for the collection of the medical debt.
26 4. Perform an action prohibited by sections 2 to 18, inclusive,
27 of this act.
28 Sec. 20. 1. The amendatory provisions of this act apply to:
29 (a) Any contract between a health care entity and a collection
30 agency for the purchase, transfer or collection of medical debt
31 entered into before, on or after January 1, 2026, but do not apply to
32 any action taken under such a contract before January 1, 2026.
33 (b) Medical debt incurred before, on or after January 1, 2026,
34 but do not apply to any action taken to collect a medical debt before
35 January 1, 2026.
36 2. As used in this section:
37 (a) “Collection agency” has the meaning ascribed to it in section
38 3 of this act.
39 (b) “Health care entity” has the meaning ascribed to it in section
40 8 of this act.
41 (c) “Medical debt” has the meaning ascribed to it in section 9 of
42 this act.
43 Sec. 21. This act becomes effective on January 1, 2026.
