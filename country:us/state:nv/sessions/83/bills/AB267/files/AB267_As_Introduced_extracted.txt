Referred to Committee on Legislative Operations and Elections
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the Legislature.
(BDR 17-1001)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to the Legislature; requiring the Director of the
Legislative Counsel Bureau to publish certain fiscal
information relating to the Legislature and the Legislative
Counsel Bureau; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Legislative Fund as a special revenue fund for the use
2 of the Legislature and, in certain circumstances, the Legislative Counsel Bureau.
3 (NRS 218A.150) Section 2 of this bill requires the Director of the Legislative
4 Counsel Bureau to publish for each fiscal year: (1) a detailed, line-item budget of
5 the Legislature and the Legislative Counsel Bureau; and (2) the ending balance of
6 the Legislative Fund and all accounts in the Legislative Fund.
7 Section 1 of this bill requires, on or before March 1 of each year, the Director
8 to publish, for the previous calendar year, the annual salary, including the
9 compensation for overtime of: (1) the Secretary of the Senate; (2) each employee of
10 the Senate; (3) the Chief Clerk of the Assembly; and (4) each employee of the
11 Assembly.
12 Section 3 of this bill requires, on or before March 1 of each year, the Director
13 to publish, for the previous calendar year, the annual salary, including the
14 compensation for overtime of each employee of the Legislative Counsel Bureau.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 218A of NRS is hereby amended by
2 adding thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 On or before March 1 of each year, the Director shall publish,
4 for the previous calendar year, the annual salary, including,
5 without limitation, compensation for overtime, of:
6 1. The Secretary of the Senate;
7 2. Each employee of the Senate;
8 3. The Chief Clerk of the Assembly; and
9 4. Each employee of the Assembly.
10 Sec. 2. NRS 218A.150 is hereby amended to read as follows:
11 218A.150 1. The Legislative Fund is hereby created as a
12 special revenue fund for the use of the Legislature, and where
13 specifically authorized by law, for the use of the Legislative Counsel
14 Bureau.
15 2. Support for the Legislative Fund must be provided by
16 legislative appropriation from the State General Fund. Money in the
17 Legislative Fund does not revert to the State General Fund at the
18 end of the fiscal year, and the balance in the Legislative Fund must
19 be carried forward to the next fiscal year.
20 3. Expenditures from the Legislative Fund may be made for:
21 (a) The payment of necessary expenses of the Senate;
22 (b) The payment of necessary expenses of the Assembly;
23 (c) The payment of necessary improvements to the Legislative
24 Building, other buildings used by the Legislature and all legislative
25 grounds;
26 (d) The payment of expenses for the interim operation of the
27 Legislature; and
28 (e) The payment of necessary expenses of, but not limited to:
29 (1) The Legislative Commission;
30 (2) The Legal Division;
31 (3) The Research Division;
32 (4) The Audit Division;
33 (5) The Fiscal Analysis Division; and
34 (6) The Administrative Division.
35 4. Expenditures from the Legislative Fund for purposes other
36 than those specified in subsection 3 or authorized specifically by
37 another statute may be made only upon the authority of a concurrent
38 resolution.
39 5. All money in the Legislative Fund must be paid out on
40 claims approved by the Director or the Director’s designee.
41 6. The Director shall publish, for each fiscal year:
