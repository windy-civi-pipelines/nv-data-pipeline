EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; requiring the establishment of a specific billing
category and [rate] rates of reimbursement for certain clinics that
provide mental health care to persons enrolled in Medicaid; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services to administer the
2 Medicaid program. (NRS 422.270) This bill requires the Division of Health Care Financing
3 and Policy of the Department to establish: (1) a specific category of provider for the purposes
4 of billing and reimbursement under Medicaid for certain clinics that provide education and
5 training to students of certain mental health professions; and (2) [a single,] all-inclusive per
6 diem [rate] rates of reimbursement under Medicaid for services provided by a trainee
7 during a visit to such a clinic, including certain circumstances where services are provided by
8 the clinic at a location other than the clinic.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. On or before October 1, 2025, the Division of Health Care
2 Financing and Policy of the Department of Health and Human Services shall, to the
3 extent that federal participation is available:
4 (a) Amend the Medicaid Services Manual to create a specific billing category
5 for clinics operated by an accredited university within this State or pursuant to
6 a faculty practice plan to which such a university is a party under the direction
7 of a physician licensed pursuant to chapter 630 or 633 of NRS that:
8 (1) Provide mental or behavioral health care [primarily] to persons
9 enrolled in Medicaid; and
10 (2) Provide education and training to [students seeking graduate degrees]
11 trainees completing a degree, supervised clinical hours or other requirements
12 necessary for the issuance of a registration, certificate or license, as applicable, [in
13 psychology, marriage and family therapy, clinical professional counseling, social
14 work, counseling persons with alcohol and other substance use disorders,
Senate Amendment No. 116 to Senate Bill No. 353 Page 4
1 counseling persons with an addictive disorder related to gambling, psychiatry or
2 other professions that specialize in] as a mental or behavioral health [.]
3 professional;
4 (b) Establish an all-inclusive per diem rate of reimbursement under Medicaid
5 for services provided by a [visit] trainee, other than a trainee seeking a license
6 to practice medicine as a psychiatrist or a license to practice as an advanced
7 practice registered nurse who provides psychiatric services, at a clinic
8 described in paragraph (a) ; and
9 (c) Establish an all-inclusive per diem rate of reimbursement under
10 Medicaid for services provided by a trainee seeking a license to practice
11 medicine as a psychiatrist or a license to practice as an advanced practice
12 registered nurse who provides psychiatric services, at a clinic described in
13 paragraph (a).
14 2. The per diem rates of reimbursement provided pursuant to
15 paragraphs (b) and (c) of subsection 1 must be provided for services provided:
16 [(1)] (a) At [the] a clinic [;] described in paragraph (a) of subsection 1;
17 [(2)] (b) Through a mobile clinic owned or operated by [the] a clinic [;]
18 described in paragraph (a) of subsection 1;
19 [(3)] (c) Outside [the] a clinic described in paragraph (a) of subsection
20 1 to recipients of Medicaid who do not reside in a permanent dwelling or do not
21 have a fixed mailing address; or
22 [(4)] (d) By [the] a clinic described in paragraph (a) of subsection 1
23 through telehealth, as defined in NRS 629.515.
24 [2.] 3. The Department shall:
25 (a) Apply to the Secretary of Health and Human Services for any waiver of
26 federal law, apply for any amendment of the State Plan for Medicaid or request any
27 other federal authority that is necessary to carry out the requirements of this section.
28 (b) Fully cooperate in good faith with the Federal Government during the
29 application process to satisfy the requirements of the Federal Government for
30 obtaining a waiver, amendment or other federal authority pursuant to paragraph (a).
31 [3.] 4. Any application submitted pursuant to paragraph (a) of subsection [2]
32 3 must be supported using methods for determining reimbursement rates accepted
33 by the Secretary.
34 5. As used in this section:
35 (a) “Faculty practice plan” means an agreement with an institution of
36 higher education which provides for the continued practice of and the
37 performance of research and education by a faculty member of the institution
38 of higher education.
39 (b) “Mental or behavioral health professional” means a person who is:
40 (1) Licensed, certified or registered pursuant to chapter 641, 641A,
41 641B, 641C or 641D of NRS; or
42 (2) Licensed as a physician, physician assistant or advanced practice
43 registered nurse who practices in psychiatry, addiction medicine or another
44 specialty relating to behavioral health.
45 (c) “Trainee” means a person seeking licensure, certification or
46 registration as a mental or behavioral health professional who is engaging in
47 supervised practice required to obtain such licensure, certification or
48 registration. The term includes, without limitation:
49 (1) A student enrolled in an accredited mental or behavioral health
50 program to obtain a graduate degree who is participating in a supervised
51 practicum or internship; and
52 (2) A person who has completed the academic requirements for
53 licensure, certification or registration and is obtaining hours of supervised
Senate Amendment No. 116 to Senate Bill No. 353 Page 5
1 practice required by law or regulation for licensure, certification or
2 registration.
3 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
