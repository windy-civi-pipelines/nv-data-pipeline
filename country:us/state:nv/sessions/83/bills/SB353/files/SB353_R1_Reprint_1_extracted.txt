SENATE BILL NO. 353–SENATORS DONDERO LOOP, FLORES,
NGUYEN; DOÑATE, OHRENSCHALL AND PAZINA
MARCH 13, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to Medicaid.
(BDR S-1010)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to Medicaid; requiring the establishment of a
specific billing category and rates of reimbursement for
certain clinics that provide mental health care to persons
enrolled in Medicaid; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services to
2 administer the Medicaid program. (NRS 422.270) This bill requires the Division of
3 Health Care Financing and Policy of the Department to establish: (1) a specific
4 category of provider for the purposes of billing and reimbursement under Medicaid
5 for certain clinics that provide education and training to students of certain mental
6 health professions; and (2) all-inclusive per diem rates of reimbursement under
7 Medicaid for services provided by a trainee during a visit to such a clinic, including
8 certain circumstances where services are provided by the clinic at a location other
9 than the clinic.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. On or before October 1, 2025, the Division of
2 Health Care Financing and Policy of the Department of Health and
3 Human Services shall, to the extent that federal participation is
4 available:
5 (a) Amend the Medicaid Services Manual to create a specific
6 billing category for clinics operated by an accredited university
7 within this State or pursuant to a faculty practice plan to which such
-
*SB353_R1*
– 2 –
1 a university is a party under the direction of a physician licensed
2 pursuant to chapter 630 or 633 of NRS that:
3 (1) Provide mental or behavioral health care to persons
4 enrolled in Medicaid; and
5 (2) Provide education and training to trainees completing a
6 degree, supervised clinical hours or other requirements necessary for
7 the issuance of a registration, certificate or license, as applicable, as
8 a mental or behavioral health professional;
9 (b) Establish an all-inclusive per diem rate of reimbursement
10 under Medicaid for services provided by a trainee, other than a
11 trainee seeking a license to practice medicine as a psychiatrist or a
12 license to practice as an advanced practice registered nurse who
13 provides psychiatric services, at a clinic described in paragraph (a);
14 and
15 (c) Establish an all-inclusive per diem rate of reimbursement
16 under Medicaid for services provided by a trainee seeking a license
17 to practice medicine as a psychiatrist or a license to practice as an
18 advanced practice registered nurse who provides psychiatric
19 services, at a clinic described in paragraph (a).
20 2. The per diem rates of reimbursement provided pursuant to
21 paragraphs (b) and (c) of subsection 1 must be provided for services
22 provided:
23 (a) At a clinic described in paragraph (a) of subsection 1;
24 (b) Through a mobile clinic owned or operated by a clinic
25 described in paragraph (a) of subsection 1;
26 (c) Outside a clinic described in paragraph (a) of subsection 1 to
27 recipients of Medicaid who do not reside in a permanent dwelling or
28 do not have a fixed mailing address; or
29 (d) By a clinic described in paragraph (a) of subsection 1
30 through telehealth, as defined in NRS 629.515.
31 3. The Department shall:
32 (a) Apply to the Secretary of Health and Human Services for
33 any waiver of federal law, apply for any amendment of the State
34 Plan for Medicaid or request any other federal authority that is
35 necessary to carry out the requirements of this section.
36 (b) Fully cooperate in good faith with the Federal Government
37 during the application process to satisfy the requirements of the
38 Federal Government for obtaining a waiver, amendment or other
39 federal authority pursuant to paragraph (a).
40 4. Any application submitted pursuant to paragraph (a) of
41 subsection 3 must be supported using methods for determining
42 reimbursement rates accepted by the Secretary.
43 5. As used in this section:
44 (a) “Faculty practice plan” means an agreement with an
45 institution of higher education which provides for the continued
-
*SB353_R1*
– 3 –
1 practice of and the performance of research and education by a
2 faculty member of the institution of higher education.
3 (b) “Mental or behavioral health professional” means a person
4 who is:
5 (1) Licensed, certified or registered pursuant to chapter 641,
6 641A, 641B, 641C or 641D of NRS; or
7 (2) Licensed as a physician, physician assistant or advanced
8 practice registered nurse who practices in psychiatry, addiction
9 medicine or another specialty relating to behavioral health.
10 (c) “Trainee” means a person seeking licensure, certification or
11 registration as a mental or behavioral health professional who is
12 engaging in supervised practice required to obtain such licensure,
13 certification or registration. The term includes, without limitation:
14 (1) A student enrolled in an accredited mental or behavioral
15 health program to obtain a graduate degree who is participating in a
16 supervised practicum or internship; and
17 (2) A person who has completed the academic requirements
18 for licensure, certification or registration and is obtaining hours of
19 supervised practice required by law or regulation for licensure,
20 certification or registration.
21 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
