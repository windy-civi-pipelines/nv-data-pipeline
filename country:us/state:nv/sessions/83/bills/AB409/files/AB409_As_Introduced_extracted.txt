Section 1:
A.B. 409
D’SILVA; BROWN-MAY AND TORRES-FOSSETT
MARCH 12, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Growth and Infrastructure
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to special license plates.
(BDR 43-1058)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to motor vehicles; requiring the Department of
Motor Vehicles, under certain circumstances, to issue a
special license plate to a veteran of the Space Force;
authorizing the combination of personalized prestige
license plates and certain special license plates associated
with military service; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Motor Vehicles to issue personalized
2 prestige license plates and certain special license plates associated with military or
3 public service. (NRS 482.3667, 482.3757-482.3787) Existing law requires the
4 Department to issue special license plates for the support of outreach programs and
5 services for veterans and their families to: (1) a veteran of the Army, Navy, Air
6 Force, Marine Corps or Coast Guard of the United States, a reserve component
7 thereof or the National Guard; (2) a female veteran; or (3) the spouse, parent or
8 child of a veteran or female veteran. Existing law requires the plates to be inscribed
9 with the seal of the branch of the Armed Forces, the seal of the National Guard or
10 an image representative of female veterans, as applicable. (NRS 482.3763) Section
11 1 of this bill requires the issuance, upon application, of such special license plates
12 to veterans of the Space Force and the spouse, parent or child of such veterans.
13 Existing law requires the Department to issue, upon application, a special
14 license plate to each family member of a person: (1) killed in the line of duty while
15 on active duty in the Armed Forces of the United States; or (2) who died as a result
16 of injuries sustained while on active duty in the Armed Forces of the United States.
17 (NRS 482.3785, 482.3787) Sections 2 and 3 of this bill authorize a person to
18 request that personalized prestige license plates be combined with such special
19 license plates.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 482.3763 is hereby amended to read as
2 follows:
3 482.3763 1. The Director shall order the preparation of
4 special license plates for the support of outreach programs and
5 services for veterans and their families and establish procedures for
6 the application for and issuance of the plates.
7 2. The Department shall, upon application therefor and
8 payment of the prescribed fees, issue special license plates for the
9 support of outreach programs and services for veterans and their
10 families to:
11 (a) A veteran of the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps , [or]
12 Coast Guard or Space Force of the United States, a reserve
13 component thereof or the National Guard;
14 (b) A female veteran; or
15 (c) The spouse, parent or child of a person described in
16 paragraph (a) or (b).
17  The plates must be inscribed with the word “VETERAN” and
18 with the seal of the branch of the Armed Forces of the United States,
19 the seal of the National Guard or an image representative of the
20 female veterans, as applicable, requested by the applicant. A person
21 may request that personalized prestige license plates issued pursuant
22 to NRS 482.3667 be combined with special license plates for the
23 support of outreach programs and services for veterans and their
24 families if that person pays the fees for the personalized prestige
25 license plates in addition to the fees for the special license plates for
26 the support of outreach programs and services for veterans and their
27 families pursuant to subsection 4.
28 3. If, during a registration period, the holder of special plates
29 issued pursuant to this section disposes of the vehicle to which the
30 plates are affixed, the holder shall:
31 (a) Retain the plates and affix them to another vehicle which
32 meets the requirements of this section if the holder pays the fee for
33 the transfer of the registration and any registration fee or
34 governmental services tax due pursuant to NRS 482.399; or
35 (b) Within 30 days after removing the plates from the vehicle,
36 return them to the Department.
37 4. In addition to all other applicable registration and license
38 fees and governmental services taxes, and to the special fee imposed
39 pursuant to NRS 482.3764 for the support of outreach programs and
40 services for veterans and their families, the fee for:
41 (a) The initial issuance of the special license plates is $35.
42 (b) The annual renewal sticker is $10.

4 Sec. 2. NRS 482.3785 is hereby amended to read as follows:
5 482.3785 1. Each family member of a person killed in the
6 line of duty while on active duty in the Armed Forces of the United
7 States is entitled to specially designed license plates which indicate
8 that the person is a family member of a person killed in the line of
9 duty while on active duty in the Armed Forces of the United States.
10 2. Each person who qualifies for special license plates pursuant
11 to this section may apply for not more than two sets of plates. If the
12 person applies for a second set of plates for an additional vehicle,
13 the second set of plates must have a different number than the first
14 set of plates issued to the same applicant. Special license plates
15 issued pursuant to this section may be used only on a private
16 passenger vehicle, a noncommercial truck or a motor home.
17 3. The Department shall issue specially designed license plates
18 for any person qualified pursuant to this section who submits an
19 application on a form prescribed by the Department and evidence
20 satisfactory to the Department that the person is a family member of
21 a person killed in the line of duty while on active duty in the Armed
22 Forces of the United States. The Department may designate any
23 appropriate colors for the special plates, but must ensure that the
24 design of the plates includes a gold star.
25 4. A vehicle on which license plates issued by the Department
26 pursuant to this section are displayed is exempt from the payment of
27 any parking fees, including, without limitation, those collected
28 through parking meters, charged by the State or any political
29 subdivision or other public body within this State, other than the
30 United States.
31 5. If, during a registration year, the holder of a set of special
32 license plates issued pursuant to the provisions of this section
33 disposes of the vehicle to which the plates are affixed, the holder
34 shall:
35 (a) Retain the plates and affix them to another vehicle which
36 meets the requirements of this section and report the change to the
37 Department in accordance with the procedure set forth for other
38 transfers; or
39 (b) Within 30 days after removing the plates from the vehicle,
40 return them to the Department.
41 6. Except as otherwise provided in this section, no fee in
42 addition to the applicable registration and license fees and
43 governmental services taxes may be charged for the issuance or
44 renewal of a set of special license plates pursuant to this section. If
45 the special plates issued pursuant to this section are lost, stolen or
