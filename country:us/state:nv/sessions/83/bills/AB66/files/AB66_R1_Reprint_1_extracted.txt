FIRST REPRINT A.B. 66
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 66–COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY
(ON BEHALF OF THE NEVADA SUPREME COURT)
PREFILED NOVEMBER 20, 2024
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to district courts.
(BDR 1-473)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§§ 2, 8)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to district courts; increasing the number of district
judges in certain judicial districts; and providing other
matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law: (1) divides the State into 11 judicial districts; and (2) sets forth
2 the number of district judges in each judicial district. (NRS 3.010, 3.011-3.0197)
3 The Nevada Constitution authorizes the Legislature to increase or decrease the
4 number of district judges. (Nev. Const. Art. 6, § 5)
5 Section 2 of this bill increases the number of district judges in the First Judicial
6 District, which includes Carson City and Storey County, from two district judges to
7 three district judges. (NRS 3.010, 3.011) Section 8 of this bill increases the number
8 of district judges in the Tenth Judicial District, which includes Churchill County,
9 from one district judge to two district judges. (NRS 3.010, 3.0195) Section 10 of
10 this bill sets out the timeframe for the election of the additional judges who will
11 take office on January 4, 2027.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. NRS 3.011 is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 3.011 For the First Judicial District there must be [two] three
4 district judges.
