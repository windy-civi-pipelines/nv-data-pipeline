Section 1:
A.B. 386
MARCH 10, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to assessments
administered to pupils to assess reading proficiency.
(BDR 34-828)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring the State Board of
Education to approve at least two assessments which may
be used to assess the literacy of pupils enrolled in an
elementary school; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the board of trustees of a school district or the governing
2 body of a charter school to prepare a plan to improve the literacy of pupils enrolled
3 in an elementary school. Such a plan must include procedures to assess the
4 proficiency of a pupil in reading during each grade level of elementary school, as is
5 determined to be necessary, by using assessments that have been approved by the
6 State Board of Education by regulation. (NRS 388.157) This bill requires the State
7 Board to approve by regulation at least two assessments which the board of trustees
8 of a school district or the governing body of a charter school may administer to
9 pupils to assess proficiency in reading.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 388.157 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 388.157 1. The board of trustees of each school district and
3 the governing body of each charter school shall prepare a plan to
4 improve the literacy of pupils enrolled in an elementary school.
5 Such a plan must include, without limitation:
6 (a) A program to provide intervention services and intensive
7 instruction to pupils who have been identified as deficient in the
-
*AB386*
– 2 –
1 subject area of reading to ensure that those pupils achieve adequate
2 proficiency in the requisite reading skills and reading
3 comprehension skills necessary to perform at a level determined by
4 a statewide assessment to be within a level determined by the State
5 Board for a pupil enrolled in the same grade in which the pupil is
6 enrolled. Such a program must include, without limitation, regularly
7 scheduled reading sessions in small groups and specific instruction
8 designed to target any area of reading in which the pupil
9 demonstrates a deficiency, including, without limitation,
10 phonological and phonemic awareness, decoding skills, reading
11 fluency and vocabulary and reading comprehension strategies;
12 (b) Procedures for assessing a pupil’s proficiency in the subject
13 area of reading using valid and reliable standards-based assessments
14 that have been approved by the State Board by regulation during
15 each grade level of the elementary school at which the pupil is
16 enrolled as determined necessary;
17 (c) A program to improve the proficiency in reading of pupils
18 who are English learners; and
19 (d) Procedures for facilitating collaboration between licensed
20 teachers designated as literacy specialists and classroom teachers.
21 2. The State Board shall approve by regulation at least two
22 valid and reliable standards-based assessments that the board of
23 trustees of a school district or the governing body of a charter
24 school may choose from to assess a pupil’s proficiency in the
25 subject area of reading.
26 3. The board of trustees of each school district or the governing
27 body of a charter school, as applicable, shall:
28 (a) Submit its plan to the Department for approval on or before
29 the date prescribed by the Department on a form prescribed by the
30 Department; and
31 (b) Make such revisions to the plan as the Department
32 determines are necessary.
33 [3.] 4. The Department shall adopt regulations that prescribe
34 procedures for assessing the development across early learning
35 domains of a pupil enrolled in kindergarten within the first 45 days
36 of school in a school year.
37 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
