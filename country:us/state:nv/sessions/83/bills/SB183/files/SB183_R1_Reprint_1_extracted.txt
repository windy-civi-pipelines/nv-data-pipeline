Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 183
SENATE BILL NO. 183–SENATOR SCHEIBLE
PREFILED FEBRUARY 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to caseworkers of
agencies which provide child welfare services.
(BDR 38-710)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§ 1)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to child welfare; limiting the number of children
that may be assigned to certain caseworkers of an agency
which provides child welfare services with certain
exceptions; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires an agency which provides child welfare services to
2 provide maintenance and special services to: (1) unmarried mothers and children
3 awaiting adoptive placement; and (2) children who are placed in the custody of the
4 agency, foster homes, homes of relatives other than parents or other facilities or
5 institutions. (NRS 432.020) This bill prohibits an agency which provides child
6 welfare services from assigning more than 30 children who are in the custody of the
7 agency to a caseworker who provides permanency services, except: (1) to allow
8 siblings to be assigned to the same caseworker; and (2) pursuant to a policy adopted
9 by the agency which provides child welfare services that authorizes such
10 assignment temporarily in emergency circumstances.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 432.020 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 432.020 1. An agency which provides child welfare services
3 shall:
-
*SB183_R1*
– 2 –
1 [1.] (a) Provide, to the extent that support is not otherwise
2 required by court order or pursuant to specific statute, maintenance
3 and special services to:
4 [(a)] (1) Unmarried mothers and children awaiting adoptive
5 placement.
6 [(b)] (2) Children who are placed in the custody of the agency
7 which provides child welfare services, and who are placed in foster
8 homes, homes of relatives other than parents or other facilities or
9 institutions. Except as otherwise provided by specific statute, if any
10 child is to be placed in the custody of the agency which provides
11 child welfare services, pursuant to any order of a court or request
12 made by a person or agency other than the agency which provides
13 child welfare services, this order or request may be issued or made
14 only after an opportunity for a hearing has been given to the agency
15 which provides child welfare services after 3 days’ notice, or upon
16 request of the agency which provides child welfare services.
17 [2.] (b) Except as otherwise provided by court order or specific
18 statute, return a child to his or her natural home or the home of a
19 competent relative for a probationary period any time after the
20 expiration of 60 days after the placement of the child in the custody
21 of the agency which provides child welfare services, with
22 notification to but without formal application to a court, but the
23 agency which provides child welfare services retains the right to
24 custody of the child during the probationary period, until a court of
25 competent jurisdiction determines proper custody of the child.
26 2. Except as otherwise provided in this section, an agency
27 which provides child welfare services shall not assign more than
28 30 children who are in the custody of the agency which provides
29 child welfare services to a caseworker who provides permanency
30 services.
31 3. An agency which provides child welfare services may
32 assign more than 30 children who are in the custody of the agency
33 which provides child welfare services to a caseworker who
34 provides permanency services:
35 (a) To allow siblings who are in the custody of the same
36 agency to be assigned to the same caseworker; or
37 (b) In accordance with a policy adopted by the agency that
38 authorizes the assignment of more than 30 children who are in the
39 custody of the agency to a caseworker for not longer than 30 days
40 in emergency circumstances.
41 4. The provisions of subsection 2 must not be construed to
42 apply to the assignment to a caseworker of a child who is not in
43 the custody of the agency which provides child welfare services,
44 including, without limitation, a child who is the subject of an
-
*SB183_R1*
– 3 –
1 investigation by an agency which provides child welfare services to
2 determine whether the child is or may be in need of protection.
3 5. As used in this section, “permanency services” means
4 efforts made to ensure the safety and well-being of a child who is
5 in the custody of the agency which provides child welfare services.
6 The term includes, without limitation:
7 (a) Assessing and responding to the medical, mental health,
8 developmental and educational needs of the child;
9 (b) Conducting an assessment of family functioning and the
10 safety of the child within the family; and
11 (c) Establishing and working toward achieving a permanent
12 placement for the child.
13 Sec. 2. The provisions of NRS 354.599 do not apply to any
14 additional expenses of a local government that are related to the
15 provisions of this act.
16 Sec. 3. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and
17 approval.
18 2. Sections 1 and 2 of this act become effective:
19 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any
20 regulations and performing any other preparatory administrative
21 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
22 (b) On July 1, 2026, for all other purposes.
