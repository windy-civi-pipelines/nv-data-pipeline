ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 406–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS
JACKSON AND NADEEM
MARCH 11, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Makes various changes relating to health.
(BDR 34-674)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health; prohibiting certain uses of artificial
intelligence in public schools; imposing certain
restrictions relating to the marketing and programming of
artificial intelligence systems; prohibiting certain persons
from representing themselves as qualified to provide
mental or behavioral health care; imposing certain
restrictions relating to the use of artificial intelligence by
providers of mental or behavioral health care; providing
civil penalties; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prescribes the authority and duties of a school counselor, school
2 psychologist and school social worker. (NRS 391.293, 391.294, 391.296) Section 2
3 of this bill prohibits a public school from using artificial intelligence to perform the
4 functions and duties of a school counselor, school psychologist or school social
5 worker which relate to the mental health of pupils.
6 Existing law: (1) regulates the practice and requires the licensure of certain
7 mental health professionals, including psychiatrists, psychologists, marriage and
8 family therapists, clinical professional counselors, registered nurses, social workers,
9 alcohol and drug counselors and problem gambling counselors; and (2) prohibits
10 unlicensed persons from engaging in the practices of those professions. (Chapters
11 630, 632, 633 and 641-641C of NRS) Section 7 of this bill prohibits, with certain
12 exceptions, an artificial intelligence provider from offering to users in this State an
13 artificial intelligence system that is specifically programmed to provide a user with
14 a service or an experience that would constitute the practice of professional mental
15 or behavioral health care if provided by a natural person. Section 7 also prohibits,
16 with certain exceptions, an artificial intelligence provider or a natural person who is
17 not licensed to practice professional mental or behavioral health care from making
-
*AB406_R1*
– 2 –
18 certain representations that would lead a person to believe that the provider, the
19 artificial intelligence system operated by the provider or the natural person is
20 capable of or qualified to provide mental or behavioral health care. Section 8 of this
21 bill imposes certain restrictions and prohibitions on the use of an artificial
22 intelligence system by a licensed provider of mental and behavioral health care.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. Chapter 391 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
3 thereto a new section to read as follows:
4 1. A public school, including, without limitation, a charter
5 school or university school for profoundly gifted pupils, shall not
6 use artificial intelligence to perform the functions and duties of a
7 school counselor, school psychologist or school social worker as
8 prescribed in NRS 391.293, 391.294 and 391.296, respectively,
9 which relate to the mental health of pupils.
10 2. The provisions of subsection 1 do not prohibit a school
11 counselor, school psychologist, school social worker or other
12 educational personnel from using artificial intelligence to perform
13 tasks for administrative support, which may include, without
14 limitation:
15 (a) Scheduling;
16 (b) Managing records;
17 (c) Analyzing data for operational purposes; and
18 (d) Organizing, tracking and managing files or notes
19 pertaining to a pupil.
20 3. As used in this section, “artificial intelligence” means a
21 system of machine-based technology that can simulate human
22 intelligence through one or more technological means, including,
23 without limitation, utilizing or deploying:
24 (a) Systems for machine learning;
25 (b) Large language models; or
26 (c) Systems for natural language processing.
27 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
28 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
29 Sec. 5. (Deleted by amendment.)
30 Sec. 6. (Deleted by amendment.)

7 human conversation in order to obtain professional mental or
8 behavioral health care; or
9 (c) The artificial intelligence system, or any component,
10 feature, avatar or embodiment of the artificial intelligence system
11 is a provider of mental or behavioral health care, a therapist, a
12 clinical therapist, a counselor, a psychiatrist, a doctor or any other
13 term commonly used to refer to a provider of professional mental
14 health or behavioral health care.
15 2. An artificial intelligence provider shall not make available
16 for use by a person in this State an artificial intelligence system
17 that is specifically programmed to provide a service or experience

23 “therapist,” “psychotherapist” or “counselor,” or any similar title,
24 if the person does not possess a valid credential issued by a
25 governmental entity that authorizes the person to practice
26 professional mental or behavioral health care in this State.

30 subsection 5.

35 (1) Information describing how a person may obtain
36 professional mental or behavioral health care from a licensed or
37 certified provider of professional mental or behavioral health care.
38 (2) Information about free or low-cost services or options
39 that are available to persons in this State who are experiencing a
40 mental or behavioral health crisis.
41 (3) Recommended best practices relating to the use or
42 potential use of artificial intelligence by a person who is seeking
43 care or relief from a mental or behavioral health condition, or
44 who is experiencing a mental or behavioral health event, which
45 may include, without limitation, recommendations concerning the

3 behavioral health care.
4 5. A person who violates any provision of subsection 1, 2 or 3
5 is subject to a civil penalty not to exceed $15,000 per violation.
6 6. This section shall not be construed to prohibit:
7 (a) The provision of faith-based counseling services,
8 specifically designated as such, by a member of the clergy who
9 does not hold a valid credential to practice professional mental or
10 behavioral health care.
11 (b) Any advertisement, statement or representation for or
12 relating to materials, literature and other products which are
13 meant to provide advice and guidance for self-help relating to
14 mental or behavioral health, if the material, literature or product

16 behavioral health care.
17 (c) Offering or operating an artificial intelligence system that
18 is designed to be used by a provider of professional mental or
19 behavioral health care to perform tasks for administrative support
20 in conformity with subsection 2 of section 8 of this act.

25 machine-based technology that can simulate human intelligence
26 through one or more technological means, including, without

30 (3) Systems for natural language processing.

32 (1) Means mental or behavioral health care or services
33 relating to the diagnosis, treatment or prevention of mental

35 provided by a provider of mental or behavioral health care within
36 his or her authorized scope of practice.

38 (I) Psychology, as defined in NRS 641.025.
39 (II) Clinical professional counseling, as defined in
40 NRS 641A.065.

42 NRS 641A.080.

44 NRS 641B.030.

8 1. Except as otherwise provided by subsection 2, a provider of

18 (c) Billing patients and managing records relating to billing;
19 (d) Analyzing data for operational purposes; and
20 (e) Organizing, tracking and managing files or notes relating
21 to an individual session with a patient.
22 3. If a provider of mental and behavioral health care uses an
23 artificial intelligence system for any purpose authorized in
24 subsection 2, the provider shall ensure that such use complies with
25 all applicable federal and state laws governing patient privacy and
26 the security of electronic health records, health-related
27 information and other related data, including, without limitation:
28 (a) The Health Information Technology for Economic and
29 Clinical Health Act, 42 U.S.C. §§ 300jj et seq. and 17901 et seq.;
30 (b) The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of
31 1996, Public Law 104-191, as amended; and
32 (c) The provisions of NRS 439.581 to 439.597, inclusive.
33 4. A provider of mental and behavioral health care shall
34 independently review the accuracy of any report, data or other
35 information compiled, summarized, analyzed or generated by
36 an artificial intelligence system for a purpose described in
37 subsection 2.
38 5. A provider of mental and behavioral health care who
39 violates any provision of this section is guilty of unprofessional
40 conduct and is subject to disciplinary action by the board, agency
41 or other entity in this State by which he or she is licensed or
42 certified.

44 (a) “Artificial intelligence system” means a system of

8 services relating to the diagnosis, treatment or prevention of
9 mental illnesses or emotional or behavioral disorders which are
10 provided by a provider of mental and behavioral health care
11 within his or her authorized scope of practice.
12 (c) “Provider of mental and behavioral health care” means:
13 (1) A psychiatrist licensed to practice medicine in this State

29 or her training for any of the professions listed in subparagraphs
