ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 406–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS
JACKSON AND NADEEM
MARCH 11, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Makes various changes relating to health.
(BDR 34-674)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health; prohibiting certain uses of artificial
intelligence in public schools; requiring the Department
of Education to develop a policy concerning certain uses
of artificial intelligence; imposing certain restrictions
relating to the marketing and programming of artificial
intelligence systems; prohibiting certain persons from
representing themselves as qualified to provide mental or
behavioral health care; imposing certain restrictions
relating to the use of artificial intelligence by providers of
mental or behavioral health care; providing civil
penalties; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law prescribes the authority and duties of a school counselor, school
2 psychologist and school social worker. (NRS 391.293, 391.294, 391.296) Section 2
3 of this bill prohibits a public school from using artificial intelligence to perform the
4 functions and duties of a school counselor, school psychologist or school social
5 worker which relate to the mental health of pupils. Section 2 additionally requires
6 the Department of Education to develop a policy for the use of artificial intelligence
7 by such school employees while providing therapy, counseling or other mental or
8 behavioral health services to pupils. Section 2 requires the policy to include a
9 method for the Department to examine the accuracy and efficacy of the use of
10 artificial intelligence for such purposes.
11 Existing law: (1) regulates the practice and requires the licensure of certain
12 mental health professionals, including psychiatrists, psychologists, marriage and
13 family therapists, clinical professional counselors, registered nurses, social workers,
14 alcohol and drug counselors and problem gambling counselors; and (2) prohibits
-
*AB406_R2*
– 2 –
15 unlicensed persons from engaging in the practices of those professions. (Chapters
16 630, 632, 633 and 641-641C of NRS) Section 7 of this bill prohibits, with certain
17 exceptions, an artificial intelligence provider from offering to users in this State an
18 artificial intelligence system that is specifically programmed to provide a user with
19 a service or an experience that would constitute the practice of professional mental
20 or behavioral health care if provided by a natural person. Section 7 also prohibits,
21 with certain exceptions, an artificial intelligence provider or a natural person who is
22 not licensed to practice professional mental or behavioral health care from making
23 certain representations that would lead a person to believe that the provider, the
24 artificial intelligence system operated by the provider or the natural person is
25 capable of or qualified to provide mental or behavioral health care. Section 8 of this
26 bill imposes certain restrictions and prohibitions on the use of an artificial
27 intelligence system by a licensed provider of mental and behavioral health care.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN

1 Section 1. (Deleted by amendment.)
2 Sec. 2. Chapter 391 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
3 thereto a new section to read as follows:
4 1. A public school, including, without limitation, a charter
5 school or university school for profoundly gifted pupils, shall not
6 use artificial intelligence to perform the functions and duties of a
7 school counselor, school psychologist or school social worker as
8 prescribed in NRS 391.293, 391.294 and 391.296, respectively,
9 which relate to the mental health of pupils.
10 2. The Department shall develop a policy for the use of
11 artificial intelligence by a school counselor, school psychologist,
12 school social worker or other educational personnel while
13 providing therapy, counseling or other mental or behavioral
14 health services to pupils. The policy developed pursuant to this
15 subsection must include, without limitation, a method for the
16 Department to examine the accuracy and efficacy of any artificial
17 intelligence used for such purposes. The Department may
18 collaborate with the Division of Public and Behavioral Health of
19 the Department of Health and Human Services in developing the
20 policy.
21 3. The provisions of subsection 1 do not prohibit a school
22 counselor, school psychologist, school social worker or other
23 educational personnel from using artificial intelligence in
24 accordance with the policy developed pursuant to subsection 2 or
25 to perform tasks for administrative support, which may include,
26 without limitation:
27 (a) Scheduling;
28 (b) Managing records;
29 (c) Analyzing data for operational purposes; and
-

2 pertaining to a pupil.
3 4. As used in this section, “artificial intelligence” means a
4 machine-based system that, for any explicit or implicit objective,
5 infers from the inputs the system receives how to generate outputs,
6 including, without limitation, content, decisions, predictions or
7 recommendations, that can influence physical or virtual
8 environments.

24 human conversation in order to obtain professional mental or
25 behavioral health care; or

27 feature, avatar or embodiment of the artificial intelligence system
28 is a provider of mental or behavioral health care, a therapist, a
29 clinical therapist, a counselor, a psychiatrist, a doctor or any other
30 term commonly used to refer to a provider of professional mental
31 health or behavioral health care.

33 for use by a person in this State an artificial intelligence system
34 that is specifically programmed to provide a service or experience
35 to a user that would constitute the practice of professional mental
36 or behavioral health care if provided by a natural person.
37 3. A natural person shall not represent himself or herself as
38 being qualified to provide professional mental or behavioral
39 health care, including, without limitation, by using the title of
40 “therapist,” “psychotherapist” or “counselor,” or any similar title,
41 if the person does not possess a valid credential issued by a
42 governmental entity that authorizes the person to practice
43 professional mental or behavioral health care in this State.

45 (a) May investigate potential violations of this section.

7 (1) Information describing how a person may obtain
8 professional mental or behavioral health care from a licensed or
9 certified provider of professional mental or behavioral health care.
10 (2) Information about free or low-cost services or options
11 that are available to persons in this State who are experiencing a
12 mental or behavioral health crisis.
13 (3) Recommended best practices relating to the use or
14 potential use of artificial intelligence by a person who is seeking
15 care or relief from a mental or behavioral health condition, or
16 who is experiencing a mental or behavioral health event, which
17 may include, without limitation, recommendations concerning the
18 circumstances under which such a person should seek the
19 assistance or care of a provider of professional mental or
20 behavioral health care.
21 5. A person who violates any provision of subsection 1, 2 or 3
22 is subject to a civil penalty not to exceed $15,000 per violation.
23 6. This section shall not be construed to prohibit:
24 (a) Any advertisement, statement or representation for or
25 relating to materials, literature and other products which are
26 meant to provide advice and guidance for self-help relating to
27 mental or behavioral health, if the material, literature or product
28 does not purport to offer or provide professional mental or
29 behavioral health care.
30 (b) Offering or operating an artificial intelligence system that
31 is designed to be used by a provider of professional mental or
32 behavioral health care to perform tasks for administrative support
33 in conformity with subsection 2 of section 8 of this act.

39 inputs the system receives how to generate outputs, including,
40 without limitation, content, decisions, predictions or
41 recommendations, that can influence physical or virtual

29 (a) Scheduling appointments;
30 (b) Managing records;
31 (c) Billing patients and managing records relating to billing;
32 (d) Analyzing data for operational purposes; and

34 to an individual session with a patient.
35 3. If a provider of mental and behavioral health care uses an
36 artificial intelligence system for any purpose authorized in
37 subsection 2, the provider shall ensure that such use complies with
38 all applicable federal and state laws governing patient privacy and
39 the security of electronic health records, health-related
40 information and other related data, including, without limitation:
41 (a) The Health Information Technology for Economic and
42 Clinical Health Act, 42 U.S.C. §§ 300jj et seq. and 17901 et seq.;
43 (b) The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of
44 1996, Public Law 104-191, as amended; and
45 (c) The provisions of NRS 439.581 to 439.597, inclusive.
