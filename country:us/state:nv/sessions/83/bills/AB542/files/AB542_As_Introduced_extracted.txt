A.B. 542
(ON BEHALF OF THE OFFICE OF FINANCE
IN THE OFFICE OF THE GOVERNOR)
MARCH 31, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Ways and Means
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to offenders.
(BDR 16-1132)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to offenders; revising requirements relating to the
provision of certain medical services to women in the
custody of the Department of Corrections; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the Department of Corrections to provide a woman who
2 is incarcerated access to certain necessary medical and behavioral health care
3 services. (NRS 209.3817) Section 1 of this bill requires that any such access be
4 provided in accordance with community standards of care and national guidelines
5 for the provision of such services. Section 1 also removes specific language
6 requiring this access to include an annual pelvic examination and mammography,
7 thereby requiring the Department to provide a woman who is incarcerated access to
8 pelvic examinations and mammography in accordance with community standards
9 of care and national guidelines for the provision of pelvic examinations and
10 mammography, rather than on an annual basis.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 209.3817 is hereby amended to read as
2 follows:
3 209.3817 1. The Department shall ensure that each woman
4 who is assigned to an institution or facility completes a form at the
5 time of intake to assess the needs of the woman while in the custody
-
*AB542*
– 2 –
1 of the Department. The form must include, without limitation,
2 questions relating to:
3 (a) How recently the woman has received preventative
4 gynecological care and, if applicable, obstetrical care; and
5 (b) The needs of the woman for feminine hygiene products for
6 the purpose of providing the woman with a suitable supply of
7 feminine hygiene products.
8 2. The Department shall provide a woman who is in the
9 custody of the Department access to necessary medical and
10 behavioral health care services, including:
11 (a) Counseling pertaining to mental health or a substance use
12 disorder;
13 (b) Medication prescribed for the treatment for mental health
14 issues;
15 (c) [An annual pelvic examination and mammography;] Pelvic
16 examinations;
17 (d) Mammography; and
18 [(d)] (e) Any appropriate follow-up care necessary for a woman
19 who receives an examination or mammography pursuant to
20 paragraph (c) [.] or (d).
21 3. The Department shall provide any necessary medical and
22 behavioral health care services required by subsection 2 in
23 accordance with community standards of care and national
24 guidelines for the provision of medical and behavioral health care
25 services to incarcerated women.
26 4. The Department shall provide a woman who is in the
27 custody of the Department with a sufficient supply of feminine
28 hygiene products at no cost. The Department shall provide the
29 woman with a preferred type of feminine hygiene products upon
30 request. A woman may at any time inform the Department of a need
31 to adjust the supply of feminine hygiene products necessary to
32 constitute a sufficient supply for the needs of the woman.
33 [4.] 5. The Department shall adopt regulations necessary to
34 carry out the provisions of this section.
35 [5.] 6. As used in this section:
36 (a) “Feminine hygiene product” means a sanitary napkin,
37 tampon or other similar item used during a menstrual cycle.
38 (b) “Mammography” has the meaning ascribed to it in
39 NRS 457.182.
40 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
