Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 297
SENATE BILL NO. 297–SENATORS NGUYEN,
OHRENSCHALL AND SCHEIBLE
MARCH 6, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to periods of observance.
(BDR 19-803)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to periods of observance; designating the month
of October of each year as “Menopause Awareness
Month” in this State; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, certain days, weeks and months of observance are
2 recognized in this State. (NRS 236.018-236.095) Section 3 of this bill designates
3 the month of October of each year as “Menopause Awareness Month” in the State
4 of Nevada and requires the Governor to issue annually a proclamation encouraging
5 the observance of this month.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 236 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto the provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
4 Sec. 3. 1. The month of October of each year is designated
5 as “Menopause Awareness Month” in this State.
6 2. The Governor shall issue annually a proclamation
7 encouraging the observance of Menopause Awareness Month.
8 The proclamation must, without limitation, call upon news media,
9 health care providers, educators, nonprofit groups and
10 foundations, appropriate governmental officers and other public
11 and private entities to:
-
*SB297_R1*
– 2 –
1 (a) Bring to the attention of the residents of this State factual
2 information and educational programs regarding menopause; and
3 (b) Promote access to quality health care during menopause to
4 support the optimal health and well-being of Nevadans.
5 Sec. 4. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
