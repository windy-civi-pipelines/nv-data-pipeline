Section 1:
A.B. 345
MARCH 3, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to education.
(BDR 34-97)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring the Board of Regents of
the University of Nevada to establish a data dashboard
setting forth certain statistical information relating to
student outcomes; requiring the Board of Regents to
conduct a study during the 2025-2026 interim relating
to certain financial issues facing students of the Nevada
System of Higher Education; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, the Board of Regents of the University of Nevada shall
2 require employees of the Nevada System of Higher Education to provide to the
3 board of trustees of each school district in this State information regarding the: (1)
4 number of pupils who graduated from a high school in the district in the
5 immediately preceding year and enrolled in certain remedial courses; and (2) costs
6 incurred by the System in providing such remedial instruction. (NRS 396.548)
7 Section 1 of this bill requires the Board of Regents to establish and maintain a data
8 dashboard that includes statistical data relating to the number of: (1) students who
9 graduated from a high school in this State and enroll in a university, state college or
10 community college within the System; (2) students who graduated from a high
11 school in this State and enroll in remedial courses in reading, writing or
12 mathematics at a university, state college or community college within the System;
13 (3) years it takes for each student to graduate from a university, state college or
14 community college within the System; and (4) students who graduate from a
15 university, state college or community college within the System and, within 1 year
16 of graduation, are unemployed or receiving benefits from a program of public
17 assistance. Section 1: (1) requires that the data dashboard be posted on the Internet
18 website of the System; and (2) authorizes any state agency or local government,
19 including, without limitation, a school district, to assist the Board of Regents in
20 establishing and maintaining the data dashboard.

21 Section 2 of this bill requires the Board of Regents to conduct an interim study
22 relating to the financial issues facing students of the System. The study must
23 include: (1) the development of a policy to allow any student who is within two
24 semesters of graduation and has outstanding debts or delinquent fees owed to the
25 System to complete the requirements for graduation while paying off any of the
26 outstanding debts or delinquent fees; (2) an examination of issues relating to the use
27 of open educational resources within the System; and (3) an examination of the
28 food insecurity issues experienced by students of the System and recommendations
29 for ways to reduce the food insecurity experienced by students of the System.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 396 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Board of Regents shall establish and maintain a data
4 dashboard relating to the educational and professional outcomes
5 of students that includes, without limitation, statistical data
6 relating to the number of:
7 (a) Students who graduated from a high school in this State
8 and enroll in a university, state college or community college
9 within the System;
10 (b) Students who graduated from a high school in this State
11 and enroll in remedial courses in reading, writing or mathematics
12 at a university, state college or community college within the
13 System;
14 (c) Years it takes for each student to graduate from a
15 university, state college or community college within the System;
16 and
17 (d) Students who graduate from a university, state college or
18 community college within the System and, within 1 year of
19 graduation, are unemployed or receiving benefits from a program
20 of public assistance.
21 2. The data dashboard established pursuant to subsection 1:
22 (a) Must be posted on the Internet website of the System; and
23 (b) Must not include any personal identifying information of
24 any student or other person.
25 3. Any state agency or local government, including, without
26 limitation, a school district, may assist the Board of Regents in
27 establishing and maintaining the data dashboard established
28 pursuant to subsection 1.
29 Sec. 2. 1. During the 2025-2026 interim, the Board of
30 Regents of the University of Nevada shall conduct a study relating
31 to the financial issues facing students of the Nevada System of
32 Higher Education. The study must include, without limitation:
-
*AB345*
– 3 –
1 (a) The development of a policy to allow any student who is
2 within two semesters of graduation and has outstanding debts or
3 delinquent fees owed to the System to complete the requirements for
4 graduation while paying off any of the outstanding debts or
5 delinquent fees.
6 (b) An examination of issues relating to the use of open
7 educational resources within the System, including, without
8 limitation:
9 (1) Whether it is feasible for the System to transition to the
10 use of open educational resources as a replacement for more
11 expensive textbooks and other course materials.
12 (2) Whether there are potential fiscal, legal or technological
13 obstacles or other issues that prevent the System from using open
14 educational resources as a replacement for more expensive
15 textbooks and other course materials.
16 (c) An examination of the food insecurity issues experienced by
17 students of the System and recommendations for ways to reduce the
18 food insecurity of students of the System, including, without
19 limitation, whether it is feasible for the food venders on the
20 campuses of the schools of the System to accept EBT cards from the
21 Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program. In examining these
22 issues, the Board of Regents shall create and conduct a survey of
23 students.
24 2. On or before February 1, 2027, the Board of Regents shall
25 prepare and submit a report of its findings to the Director of the
26 Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the 84th Session of the
27 Legislature.
28 3. The Board of Regents may accept gifts, grants and donations
29 to carry out the duties set forth in this section.
30 4. As used in this section, “open educational resources” means
31 teaching, learning and research resources that reside in the public
32 domain or have been released under an intellectual property license
33 that allows for free use, reuse, modification and sharing. The term
34 includes, without limitation, courses, course materials, textbooks,
35 modules, faculty-created content, videos, tests, software and any
36 other tool, material or technique used to support access to
37 knowledge.
38 Sec. 3. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
