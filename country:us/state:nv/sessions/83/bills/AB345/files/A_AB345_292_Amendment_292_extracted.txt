SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to education. (BDR 34-97)

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; requiring the Board of Regents of the University of
Nevada to establish a data dashboard setting forth certain statistical
information relating to student outcomes; requiring the Board of
Regents to establish a policy for the use of low-cost course
materials and no-cost open educational resources in courses
offered at an institution within the Nevada System of Higher
Education; requiring the Board of Regents to conduct a study during
the 2025-2026 interim relating to certain [financial] issues facing
students of the Nevada System of Higher Education; and providing
other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, the Board of Regents of the University of Nevada shall require
2 employees of the Nevada System of Higher Education to provide to the board of trustees of
3 each school district in this State information regarding the: (1) number of pupils who
4 graduated from a high school in the district in the immediately preceding year and enrolled in
5 certain remedial courses; and (2) costs incurred by the System in providing such remedial
6 instruction. (NRS 396.548) Section [1] 1.3 of this bill requires the Board of Regents to
7 establish and maintain a data dashboard that includes statistical data relating to the number of:
8 (1) students who graduated from a public high school in this State and enroll in a university,
9 state college or community college within the System; (2) students who graduated from a high
10 school in this State and enroll in remedial courses in reading, writing or mathematics at a
11 university, state college or community college within the System [;] or who are identified as
12 not college-ready in such subjects and enrolled in a corequisite support course; (3) years
13 it takes for [each student] students, on average, to graduate from a university, state college or
14 community college within the System; and (4) subject to the availability of data, the
15 number of students who graduate from a university, state college or community college
16 within the System and, within [1 year] 2 years of graduation, [are unemployed] have not
17 received wages from an employer in this State and are not enrolled in an institution
18 within the Nevada System of Higher Education or have received unemployment benefits
19 or [receiving] benefits from a program of public assistance. Section [1:] 1.3 also: (1) requires
20 that the data dashboard be posted or accessible on the Internet website of the System [;] by
21 September 1, 2026; and (2) authorizes any state agency or local government, including,
22 without limitation, a school district, to assist the Board of Regents in establishing and
23 maintaining the data dashboard.
Assembly Amendment No. 292 to Assembly Bill No. 345 Page 4
24 Section 1.5 of this bill requires the Board of Regents to establish a policy for the use
25 of low-cost course materials and no-cost open educational resources in courses offered at
26 an institution within the Nevada System of Higher Education. Section 1.5 requires the
27 policy to include, without limitation: (1) the definition of “low-cost course materials” and
28 “no-cost open educational resources”; and (2) a uniform system to identify, and mark
29 for identification within the course registration system used at each institution, each
30 course that exclusively uses low-cost course materials and no-cost open educational
31 resources.
32 Section 2 of this bill requires the Board of Regents to conduct an interim study relating to
33 [the] financial and food insecurity issues facing students of the System. The study must
34 include: (1) [the development of a policy to allow any student who is within two semesters of
35 graduation and has outstanding debts or delinquent fees owed to the System to complete the
36 requirements for graduation while paying off any of the outstanding debts or delinquent fees;
37 (2) an examination of issues relating to the use of open educational resources within the
38 System; and (3)] an examination of the financial and food insecurity issues experienced by
39 students of the System ; (2) the identification of factors that contribute to the temporary
40 withdrawal of students from enrollment in academic programs due to financial and food
41 insecurity; (3) an evaluation of potential interventions to the financial and food
42 insecurity experienced by students of the System; and (4) data-driven recommendations
43 for ways to reduce the financial and food insecurity experienced by students of the System.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 396 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto [a new
2 section to read as follows:] the provisions set forth as sections 1.3 and 1.5 of this
3 act.
4 Sec. 1.3. 1. The Board of Regents shall establish and maintain a data
5 dashboard relating to the educational and professional outcomes of students that
6 includes, without limitation, statistical data relating to the number of:
7 (a) Students who graduated from a public high school in this State and
8 enroll in a university, state college or community college within the System;
9 (b) Students who graduated from a high school in this State and enroll in
10 remedial courses in reading, writing or mathematics at a university, state college
11 or community college within the System [;] or who are identified as not being
12 college-ready in such subjects and enrolled in a corequisite support course;
13 (c) Years it takes for [each student] students, on average, to graduate from a
14 university, state college or community college within the System; and
15 (d) [Students] Subject to the availability of data, the number of students who
16 graduate from a university, state college or community college within the System
17 and, within [1 year] 2 years of graduation [, are unemployed or receiving] have:
18 (1) Not received wages from an employer in this State and are not
19 enrolled in an institution within the Nevada System of Higher Education; and
20 (2) Received unemployment benefits or benefits from a program of public
21 assistance [.] after graduating.
22 2. The data dashboard established pursuant to subsection 1:
23 (a) Must be posted on or otherwise accessible through the Internet website of
24 the System [;] on or before September 1, 2026; and
25 (b) Must not include any personal identifying information of any student or
26 other person.
27 3. Any state agency or local government, including, without limitation, a
28 school district, may assist the Board of Regents in establishing and maintaining
29 the data dashboard established pursuant to subsection 1.

1 4. As used in this section:
2 (a) “Corequisite support course” means a course taken simultaneously with
3 a first college-level or foundation course for a program of study that supports the
4 topics being covered in the first college-level or foundation course.
5 (b) “Employer” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 612.055.
6 Sec. 1.5. The Board of Regents shall establish a policy for the use of low-
7 cost course materials and no-cost open educational resources in courses offered
8 at an institution within the Nevada System of Higher Education. The policy must
9 include, without limitation:
10 1. A definition of “low-cost course materials” and “no-cost open
11 educational resources”; and
12 2. A uniform system to identify, and mark for identification within the
13 course registration system used at each institution, each course that exclusively
14 uses low-cost course materials and no-cost open educational resources.
15 Sec. 2. 1. During the 2025-2026 interim, the Board of Regents of the
16 University of Nevada shall conduct a study [relating to the financial issues facing]
17 on financial and food insecurity among students [of] within the Nevada System
18 of Higher Education. The study must include, without limitation:
19 (a) [The development of a policy to allow any student who is within two
20 semesters of graduation and has outstanding debts or delinquent fees owed to the
21 System to complete the requirements for graduation while paying off any of the
22 outstanding debts or delinquent fees.
23 (b) An examination of issues relating to the use of open educational resources
24 within the System, including, without limitation:
25 (1) Whether it is feasible for the System to transition to the use of open
26 educational resources as a replacement for more expensive textbooks and other
27 course materials.
28 (2) Whether there are potential fiscal, legal or technological obstacles or
29 other issues that prevent the System from using open educational resources as a
30 replacement for more expensive textbooks and other course materials.
31 (c)] An examination of the financial and food insecurity issues experienced by
32 students of the System . [and]
33 (b) The identification of factors that contribute to the temporary
34 withdrawal of students from enrollment in academic programs due to financial
35 and food insecurity.
36 (c) An evaluation of potential interventions to the financial and food
37 insecurity experienced by students in the System, which may include, without
38 limitation, allowing a student who has outstanding debts or delinquent fees
39 owed to the System to register for courses.
40 (d) Data-driven policy recommendations for ways to reduce the financial and
41 food insecurity of students of the System . [, including, without limitation, whether
42 it is feasible for the food venders on the campuses of the schools of the System to
43 accept EBT cards from the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program. In
44 examining these issues, the Board of Regents shall create and conduct a survey of
45 students.]
46 2. On or before [February 1, 2027,] July 1, 2026, the Board of Regents shall
47 prepare and submit a report of its findings to the Director of the Legislative Counsel
48 Bureau for transmittal to the [84th Session of the Legislature.] Joint Interim
49 Standing Committee on Education.
50 3. The Board of Regents may accept gifts, grants and donations to carry out
51 the duties set forth in this section.
52 [4. As used in this section, “open educational resources” means teaching,
53 learning and research resources that reside in the public domain or have been
Assembly Amendment No. 292 to Assembly Bill No. 345 Page 6
1 released under an intellectual property license that allows for free use, reuse,
2 modification and sharing. The term includes, without limitation, courses, course
3 materials, textbooks, modules, faculty-created content, videos, tests, software and
4 any other tool, material or technique used to support access to knowledge.]
5 Sec. 3. 1. This section becomes effective upon passage and approval.
6 2. Sections 1, 1.3 and 2 of this act [becomes] become effective on July 1,
7 2025.
8 3. Section 1.5 of this act becomes effective on July 1, 2026.
