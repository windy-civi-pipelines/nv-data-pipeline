EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; [requiring the Director of the Department of
Health and Human Services to apply for certain federal authority to
authorize tribal health benefit coordinators] authorizing Indian tribes
to determine [eligibility] if a person is presumptively eligible for
Medicaid; creating the Tribal Health Authority Council; prescribing
the procedure governing the operations of the Council; [providing for]
authorizing the appointment of the Coordinator of the Council;
prescribing the duties of the Council; creating the Account for Tribal
Health; making an appropriation to the Department of Native
American Affairs for the personnel and operating costs of the Council;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Department of Native American Affairs, which consists of the
2 Executive Director, the Nevada Indian Commission and the Stewart Indian School Cultural
3 Center and Museum. (NRS 233A.1003) Section 5 of this bill creates the Tribal Health
4 Authority Council and establishes the membership of the Council. Section 4 of this bill
5 defines the term “Council” to refer to the Council for the purposes of sections 4-9 of this bill.
6 Section 6 of this bill : (1) establishes certain requirements governing the operation of the
7 Council ; (2) authorizes a member of the Council to participate through a remote
8 technology system; and (3) provides for the election of the officers of the Council. Section 7
9 [requires] authorizes the Executive Director to appoint [the] a Coordinator of the Council.
10 Section 7 also requires the Department to provide any additional personnel, facilities,
11 equipment and supplies to the Council. Section 8 of this bill prescribes the duties of the
12 Council, which include adopting and periodically updating a plan to increase access to health
13 care and address and eliminate barriers to health care for American Indians [, Alaska Natives
14 and Native Hawaiians] in this State. Section 1.5 of this bill authorizes the Council to
15 request the drafting of not more than 2 legislative measures which relate to matters
Senate Amendment No. 220 to Senate Bill No. 312 Page 4
16 within the scope of the Council. Section 1.8 of this bill subjects those requests to certain
17 provisions that apply to requests for the drafting of legislation that are submitted by
18 nonlegislative requesters. Section 9 of this bill creates the Account for Tribal Health to fund
19 the activities of the Council, and section 10 of this bill appropriates money to the Account.
20 Section 11 of this bill requires the Director of the Department of Health and Human Services
21 to collaborate with the Council to develop a proposal to establish a program to reinvest certain
22 federal funds received by this State under the Medicaid program.
23 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services to administer
24 Medicaid. (NRS 422.270) Additionally, existing federal law requires the Secretary of Health
25 and Human Services to encourage enrollment of American Indians in Medicaid. (42 U.S.C. §
26 1320b-9) [Section] To the extent authorized by federal law, section 1 of this bill authorizes
27 an Indian tribe to determine whether a person is presumptively eligible to receive
28 benefits under Medicaid. Section 1 requires the Department to apply for any waiver of
29 federal law or apply for any amendment to the State Plan for Medicaid to authorize [certain
30 trained persons affiliated with tribes, known as tribal health benefit coordinators, to determine
31 whether a person is eligible to receive benefits under Medicaid.] an Indian tribe to make
32 such determinations. Section 1 additionally requires the Department to [collaborate with the
33 Council and tribal health clinics in this State to ensure that tribal health benefit coordinators
34 have] provide to each Indian tribe that determines presumptive eligibility the resources ,
35 training and technical assistance necessary for the Indian tribe to accurately and
36 effectively determine if a person is presumptively eligible to receive benefits under
37 Medicaid. Section 2 of this bill makes a conforming change to indicate that the provisions of
38 section 1 will be administered in the same manner as the provisions of existing law governing
39 Medicaid.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; [requiring the Director of the Department of
Health and Human Services to apply for certain federal authority to
authorize tribal health benefit coordinators] authorizing Indian tribes
to determine [eligibility] if a person is presumptively eligible for
Medicaid; creating the Tribal Health Authority Council; prescribing
the procedure governing the operations of the Council; [providing for]
authorizing the appointment of the Coordinator of the Council;
prescribing the duties of the Council; creating the Account for Tribal
Health; making an appropriation to the Department of Native
American Affairs for the personnel and operating costs of the Council;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Department of Native American Affairs, which consists of the
2 Executive Director, the Nevada Indian Commission and the Stewart Indian School Cultural
3 Center and Museum. (NRS 233A.1003) Section 5 of this bill creates the Tribal Health
4 Authority Council and establishes the membership of the Council. Section 4 of this bill
5 defines the term “Council” to refer to the Council for the purposes of sections 4-9 of this bill.
6 Section 6 of this bill : (1) establishes certain requirements governing the operation of the
7 Council ; (2) authorizes a member of the Council to participate through a remote
8 technology system; and (3) provides for the election of the officers of the Council. Section 7
9 [requires] authorizes the Executive Director to appoint [the] a Coordinator of the Council.
10 Section 7 also requires the Department to provide any additional personnel, facilities,
11 equipment and supplies to the Council. Section 8 of this bill prescribes the duties of the
12 Council, which include adopting and periodically updating a plan to increase access to health
13 care and address and eliminate barriers to health care for American Indians [, Alaska Natives
14 and Native Hawaiians] in this State. Section 1.5 of this bill authorizes the Council to
15 request the drafting of not more than 2 legislative measures which relate to matters

Senate Amendment No. 220 to Senate Bill No. 312 Page 4
16 within the scope of the Council. Section 1.8 of this bill subjects those requests to certain
17 provisions that apply to requests for the drafting of legislation that are submitted by
18 nonlegislative requesters. Section 9 of this bill creates the Account for Tribal Health to fund
19 the activities of the Council, and section 10 of this bill appropriates money to the Account.
20 Section 11 of this bill requires the Director of the Department of Health and Human Services
21 to collaborate with the Council to develop a proposal to establish a program to reinvest certain
22 federal funds received by this State under the Medicaid program.
23 Existing law requires the Department of Health and Human Services to administer
24 Medicaid. (NRS 422.270) Additionally, existing federal law requires the Secretary of Health
25 and Human Services to encourage enrollment of American Indians in Medicaid. (42 U.S.C. §
26 1320b-9) [Section] To the extent authorized by federal law, section 1 of this bill authorizes
27 an Indian tribe to determine whether a person is presumptively eligible to receive
28 benefits under Medicaid. Section 1 requires the Department to apply for any waiver of
29 federal law or apply for any amendment to the State Plan for Medicaid to authorize [certain
30 trained persons affiliated with tribes, known as tribal health benefit coordinators, to determine
31 whether a person is eligible to receive benefits under Medicaid.] an Indian tribe to make
32 such determinations. Section 1 additionally requires the Department to [collaborate with the
33 Council and tribal health clinics in this State to ensure that tribal health benefit coordinators
34 have] provide to each Indian tribe that determines presumptive eligibility the resources ,
35 training and technical assistance necessary for the Indian tribe to accurately and
36 effectively determine if a person is presumptively eligible to receive benefits under
37 Medicaid. Section 2 of this bill makes a conforming change to indicate that the provisions of
38 section 1 will be administered in the same manner as the provisions of existing law governing
39 Medicaid.
