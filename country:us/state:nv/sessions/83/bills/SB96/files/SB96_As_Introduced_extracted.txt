Section 1:
S.B. 96
PREFILED JANUARY 14, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Designates January 27 as “International Holocaust
Remembrance Day” in the State of Nevada.
(BDR 19-604)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to days of observance; designating January 27 of
each year as “International Holocaust Remembrance Day”
in the State of Nevada; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, certain days, weeks and months of observance are
2 recognized in this State. (NRS 236.018-236.095) This bill designates January 27 of
3 each year as “International Holocaust Remembrance Day” in the State of Nevada
4 and requires the Governor to issue annually a proclamation encouraging the
5 observance of this day.
1 WHEREAS, The Holocaust was the systematic, bureaucratic,
2 state-sponsored persecution and murder of approximately 6,000,000
3 Jewish persons and 5,000,000 other persons by the Nazi regime and
4 its collaborators between 1933 and 1945; and
5 WHEREAS, Other horrific genocides have occurred throughout
6 history in places such as Armenia, Cambodia, Darfur, Guatemala
7 and Rwanda; and
8 WHEREAS, In 2005, the United Nations General Assembly
9 designated January 27, which is the anniversary of the liberation of
10 Auschwitz-Birkenau, as International Holocaust Remembrance Day
11 to commemorate the victims of the Holocaust and to promote
12 education about the Holocaust throughout the world; and
13 WHEREAS, A survey released in 2024 by the Governor’s
14 Advisory Council on Education Relating to the Holocaust revealed a
-
*SB96*
– 2 –
1 significant lack of knowledge about the Holocaust and other
2 genocides among Nevadans, particularly the younger generation;
3 and
4 WHEREAS, Assembly Bill No. 231 was enacted during the 81st
5 (2021) Session of the Nevada Legislature to direct efforts to
6 improve the curricula of courses provided to pupils in Nevada about
7 the Holocaust and other genocides as well as to increase the
8 classroom resources and professional development courses available
9 to educators on genocide education; and
10 WHEREAS, The annual observance of International Holocaust
11 Remembrance Day in Nevada will preserve the memory of the
12 victims of the Holocaust and other genocides and promote public
13 knowledge of these atrocities as well as serve as a reminder for
14 continued vigilance against hatred, persecution and tyranny to
15 prevent their reoccurrence; now, therefore,
16
17 THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
18 SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
19
20 Section 1. Chapter 236 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
21 thereto a new section to read as follows:
22 1. January 27 of each year is designated as “International
23 Holocaust Remembrance Day” in the State of Nevada.
24 2. The Governor shall issue annually a proclamation
25 encouraging the observance of “International Holocaust
26 Remembrance Day.” The proclamation must, without limitation,
27 call upon the news media, state and local officers, public schools
28 and other public and private entities to bring to the attention of the
29 people in the State of Nevada information about the Holocaust
30 and other genocides throughout history and the importance of
31 remembering the victims of the Holocaust and other genocides.
