SUMMARY—Establishes requirements for certain communications relating to an
election. (BDR 24-487)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to campaign practices; requiring certain communications relating
to an election that include synthetic media to contain a disclosure; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires that certain statements and communications relating to an election
2 contain disclosures to provide the public with certain information relating to the source or
3 purpose of the statement or communication. (NRS 294A.347-294A.3495) Section 2 of this
4 bill: (1) requires that any communication made or paid for by a person, organization,
5 candidate, personal campaign committee of a candidate, committee for political action or
6 committee sponsored by a political party that is in support of or opposition to a candidate,
7 group of candidates or political party , that provides information about political or social
8 issues with the intent to influence the outcome of an election or that solicits contributions
9 for a candidate, group of candidates or political party that includes any form of synthetic
10 media in the communication must disclose that the image, video or audio has been
11 manipulated; and (2) authorizes a candidate who is depicted in any such communication that
12 fails to include such a disclosure to seek an injunction or equitable relief in a district court
13 against certain parties that made or paid for the communication.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 294A of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto the
2 provisions set forth as sections 2 and 3 of this act.
3 Sec. 2. 1. Any communication made or paid for by a person,
4 organization, candidate, personal campaign committee of a candidate, committee
5 for political action or committee sponsored by a political party that is made in
6 support of or opposition to a candidate, group of candidates or political party ,
7 that provides information about political or social issues with the intent to
Senate Amendment No. 632 to Assembly Bill No. 73 First Reprint Page 4
1 influence the outcome of an election or that solicits contributions for a candidate,
2 group of candidates or political party and includes any form of synthetic media in
3 the communication must disclose on the communication in a clear and
4 conspicuous manner:
5
6 This (image/video/audio) has been manipulated.
7
8 2. The disclosure required pursuant to subsection 1 must be provided in
9 accordance with the following requirements:
10 (a) For a communication made using visual media that is a video, the
11 disclosure must appear for the entire duration of the video; and
12 (b) For a communication made using only audio, the disclosure must be read
13 in a clearly spoken manner, in a pitch that can be easily heard by the average
14 listener and in the same language as the rest of the audio:
15 (1) At the beginning of the audio;
16 (2) At the end of the audio; and
17 (3) If the audio is longer than 2 minutes in length, interspersed within
18 the audio at intervals of not more than 2 minutes each.
19 3. Any candidate who is depicted in any communication that includes
20 synthetic media and which communication fails to include the disclosure
21 required pursuant to subsection 1 may seek an injunction or other equitable relief
22 in district court against the person, candidate, personal campaign committee of a
23 candidate, committee for political action or committee sponsored by a political
24 party who made or paid for the communication.
25 4. Nothing in this section shall be construed to impose liability for a
26 violation of subsection 1 upon:
27 (a) Any streaming service, interactive computer service provider, cloud
28 service provider or internet service provider;
29 (b) A radio broadcasting station, television broadcasting station,
30 telecommunications network or cable or satellite television operator;
31 (c) Any programmer or producer of radio or television broadcasting content;
32 or
33 (d) Any person who publishes synthetic media as part of entertainment as a
34 satire or parody.
35 5. As used in this section:
36 (a) “Artificial intelligence” means a machine-based system that, for explicit
37 or implicit objectives, infers from the input it receives how to generate outputs
38 such as predictions, content, recommendations or decisions that can influence
39 physical or virtual environments.
40 (b) “Generative adversarial network” means a framework for machine
41 learning that uses adversarial training towards the development of generative
42 artificial intelligence.
43 (c) “Generative artificial intelligence” means a technology of artificial
44 intelligence that is capable of creating content such as text, audio, image or video
45 based on patterns learned from large volumes of data rather than being explicitly
46 programmed with rules.
47 (d) “Interactive computer service” has the meaning ascribed to it in 47
48 U.S.C. § 230(f)(2).
49 (e) “Synthetic media” means an image, audio recording or video recording
50 of the appearance, speech or conduct of a person that has been intentionally
51 manipulated with the use of generative adversarial network techniques, artificial
52 intelligence or generative artificial intelligence to create a realistic but false
53 image, audio recording or video recording that produces:
Senate Amendment No. 632 to Assembly Bill No. 73 First Reprint Page 5
1 (1) A depiction that to a reasonable natural person is of a real natural
2 person in appearance, action or speech but that did not actually occur in reality
3 or was not authentically captured or recorded; and
4 (2) A fundamentally different understanding or impression of the
5 appearance, action or speech of a person than what a reasonable natural person
6 would have from viewing or listening to the unaltered original version of the
7 image, audio recording or video recording.
8 Sec. 3. (Deleted by amendment.)
9 Sec. 4. (Deleted by amendment.)
10 Sec. 5. (Deleted by amendment.)
11 Sec. 6. This act becomes effective on January 1, 2026.
