Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT A.B. 383
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 383–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS HANSEN,
DICKMAN; COLE, EDGEWORTH, GURR, HAFEN, HARDY,
HIBBETTS, NGUYEN AND O’NEILL
MARCH 10, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to programs of high-
impact tutoring. (BDR 34-767)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; authorizing the State Board of
Education to adopt regulations establishing criteria that a
vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program must
satisfy to contract with the board of trustees of a school
district or the governing body of a charter school to
provide such a program; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill authorizes the State Board of Education to adopt regulations
2 establishing the criteria that a vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program
3 is required to satisfy to contract with the board of trustees of a school district or the
4 governing body of a charter school to provide such a program. The regulations
5 must establish the criteria concerning: (1) the frequency of interactions between a
6 pupil and a tutor; (2) the standards used by the program to determine which pupils
7 will be selected to receive tutoring under the program; (3) the methods of tutoring
8 under the program; (4) the requirements for a person to be a tutor, including,
9 without limitation, training requirements; (5) the instructional materials used by the
10 program; (6) the collection and reporting of required data concerning the academic
11 progress of a pupil receiving tutoring under the program; and (7) the security and
12 privacy of data concerning pupils who are provided tutoring under the program.
-
*AB383_R1*
– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 The State Board may adopt regulations establishing the criteria
4 that a vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program must
5 satisfy to contract with the board of trustees of a school district or
6 the governing body of a charter school to provide such a program.
7 The regulations adopted pursuant to this subsection must establish
8 criteria concerning:
9 1. The frequency of interactions between a pupil and a tutor,
10 including, without limitation, a requirement that a pupil receive
11 three tutoring sessions during each week that the pupil receives
12 tutoring under the program and that each tutoring session is at
13 least 30 minutes and the minimum number of weeks for which a
14 pupil must receive tutoring under the program.
15 2. The standards used by the program to determine which
16 pupils will be selected to receive tutoring under the program.
17 3. The methods of tutoring under the program, including,
18 without limitation, a maximum pupil-tutor ratio of four pupils per
19 tutor, a requirement that the pupil receive tutoring from not more
20 than two different tutors, a requirement to use assessments
21 administered at prescribed intervals and other measures of
22 progress to guide and customize the tutoring provided to a pupil,
23 and the provision of tutoring to a pupil during the school day.
24 4. The qualifications of a person to serve as a tutor under the
25 program, including, without limitation, the training necessary for
26 a person to serve as a tutor.
27 5. The instructional materials used by the program, which
28 must be accessible, rigorous, research-based and aligned with any
29 appropriate curricula in the school in which the pupil is enrolled.
30 6. The data required to be collected by the program to
31 monitor the academic progress of pupils who are provided tutoring
32 under the program and a requirement to report the academic
33 progress of each pupil to:
34 (a) Administrators and teachers at the school in which the
35 pupil is enrolled; and
36 (b) The parent or legal guardian of the pupil.
37 7. The methods used by the program to ensure the security
38 and privacy of data concerning pupils who are provided tutoring
39 under the program, which must be consistent with relevant state
40 and federal privacy laws, including, without limitation, the Family
41 Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, 20 U.S.C. § 1232g.
-
*AB383_R1*
– 3 –
1 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
