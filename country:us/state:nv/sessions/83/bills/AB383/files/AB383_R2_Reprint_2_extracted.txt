Section 1:
SECOND REPRINT A.B. 383
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 383–ASSEMBLYMEMBERS HANSEN,
DICKMAN; COLE, EDGEWORTH, GURR, HAFEN, HARDY,
HIBBETTS, NGUYEN AND O’NEILL
MARCH 10, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to programs of high-
impact tutoring. (BDR 34-767)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; authorizing the State Board of
Education to adopt regulations establishing criteria that a
vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program must
satisfy to contract with the board of trustees of a school
district or the governing body of a charter school to
provide such a program; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill authorizes the State Board of Education to adopt regulations
2 establishing the criteria that a vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program
3 is required to satisfy to contract with the board of trustees of a school district or the
4 governing body of a charter school to provide such a program. The regulations
5 must establish the criteria, consistent with evidence-based best practices for high-
6 impact tutoring, concerning: (1) the frequency of interactions between a pupil and a
7 tutor; (2) the standards used by the program to determine which pupils will be
8 selected to receive tutoring under the program; (3) the methods of tutoring under
9 the program; (4) the requirements for a person to be a tutor, including, without
10 limitation, training requirements; (5) the instructional materials used by the
11 program; (6) the collection and reporting of required data concerning the academic
12 progress of a pupil receiving tutoring under the program; and (7) the security and
13 privacy of data concerning pupils who are provided tutoring under the program.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 The State Board may adopt regulations establishing the criteria
4 that a vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program must
5 satisfy to contract with the board of trustees of a school district or
6 the governing body of a charter school to provide such a program.
7 The regulations adopted pursuant to this subsection must establish
8 criteria, consistent with evidence-based best practices for high-
9 impact tutoring, concerning:
10 1. The frequency of interactions between a pupil and a tutor
11 under the program.
12 2. The standards used by the program to determine which
13 pupils will be selected to receive tutoring under the program.
14 3. The methods of tutoring under the program, including,
15 without limitation, a maximum pupil-tutor ratio for the program.
16 4. The qualifications of a person to serve as a tutor under the
17 program, including, without limitation, the training necessary for
18 a person to serve as a tutor.
19 5. The instructional materials used by the program.
20 6. The data required to be collected by the program to
21 monitor the academic progress of pupils who are provided tutoring
22 under the program and a requirement to report the academic
23 progress of each pupil to:
24 (a) Administrators and teachers at the school in which the
25 pupil is enrolled; and
26 (b) The parent or legal guardian of the pupil.
27 7. The methods used by the program to ensure the security
28 and privacy of data concerning pupils who are provided tutoring
29 under the program, which must be consistent with relevant state
30 and federal privacy laws, including, without limitation, the Family
31 Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, 20 U.S.C. § 1232g.
32 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
