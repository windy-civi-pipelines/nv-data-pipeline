Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to programs of high-impact tutoring.
(BDR 34-767)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; authorizing the State Board of Education to adopt
regulations establishing criteria that a vendor that provides a high-
impact tutoring program must satisfy to contract with the board of
trustees of a school district or the governing body of a charter school to
provide such a program; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill authorizes the State Board of Education to adopt regulations establishing the
2 criteria that a vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program is required to satisfy to
3 contract with the board of trustees of a school district or the governing body of a charter
4 school to provide such a program. The regulations must establish the criteria , consistent with
5 evidence-based best practices for high-impact tutoring, concerning: (1) the frequency of
6 interactions between a pupil and a tutor; (2) the standards used by the program to determine
7 which pupils will be selected to receive tutoring under the program; (3) the methods of
8 tutoring under the program; (4) the requirements for a person to be a tutor, including, without
9 limitation, training requirements; (5) the instructional materials used by the program; (6) the
10 collection and reporting of required data concerning the academic progress of a pupil
11 receiving tutoring under the program; and (7) the security and privacy of data concerning
12 pupils who are provided tutoring under the program.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 The State Board may adopt regulations establishing the criteria that a vendor
4 that provides a high-impact tutoring program must satisfy to contract with the
5 board of trustees of a school district or the governing body of a charter school to
Senate Amendment No. 663 to Assembly Bill No. 383 First Reprint Page 4
1 provide such a program. The regulations adopted pursuant to this subsection
2 must establish criteria , consistent with evidence-based best practices for high-
3 impact tutoring, concerning:
4 1. The frequency of interactions between a pupil and a tutor [, including,
5 without limitation, a requirement that a pupil receive three tutoring sessions
6 during each week that the pupil receives tutoring under the program and that
7 each tutoring session is at least 30 minutes and the minimum number of weeks
8 for which a pupil must receive tutoring] under the program.
9 2. The standards used by the program to determine which pupils will be
10 selected to receive tutoring under the program.
11 3. The methods of tutoring under the program, including, without
12 limitation, a maximum pupil-tutor ratio [of four pupils per tutor, a requirement
13 that the pupil receive tutoring from not more than two different tutors, a
14 requirement to use assessments administered at prescribed intervals and other
15 measures of progress to guide and customize the tutoring provided to a pupil, and
16 the provision of tutoring to a pupil during the school day.] for the program.
17 4. The qualifications of a person to serve as a tutor under the program,
18 including, without limitation, the training necessary for a person to serve as a
19 tutor.
20 5. The instructional materials used by the program . [, which must be
21 accessible, rigorous, research-based and aligned with any appropriate curricula
22 in the school in which the pupil is enrolled.]
23 6. The data required to be collected by the program to monitor the academic
24 progress of pupils who are provided tutoring under the program and a
25 requirement to report the academic progress of each pupil to:
26 (a) Administrators and teachers at the school in which the pupil is enrolled;
27 and
28 (b) The parent or legal guardian of the pupil.
29 7. The methods used by the program to ensure the security and privacy of
30 data concerning pupils who are provided tutoring under the program, which must
31 be consistent with relevant state and federal privacy laws, including, without
32 limitation, the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, 20 U.S.C. §
33 1232g.
34 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
