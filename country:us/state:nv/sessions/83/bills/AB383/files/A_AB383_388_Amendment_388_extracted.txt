_______________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Establishes provisions relating to programs of high-impact tutoring.
(BDR 34-767)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to education; [requiring the Department of Education to develop,
update and publish a list of high-impact tutoring programs that meet
certain criteria to be considered an evidence-based high-impact
tutoring program; requiring] authorizing the [Department] State
Board of Education to adopt regulations establishing [such] criteria [;]
that a vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program must
satisfy to contract with the board of trustees of a school district or
the governing body of a charter school to provide such a program;
and providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill [requires the Department of Education to develop, update and publish a list of
2 high-impact tutoring programs that are available to provide academic assistance to pupils in
3 this State to supplement instruction in core academic subjects and that meet the criteria
4 established by the Department to be considered an evidence-based high-impact tutoring
5 program. This bill further requires] authorizes the [Department] State Board of Education
6 to adopt regulations establishing the criteria that a vendor that provides a high-impact
7 tutoring program is required to satisfy to [be included on the list, including, without
8 limitation,] contract with the board of trustees of a school district or the governing body
9 of a charter school to provide such a program. The regulations must establish the criteria
10 concerning: (1) the frequency of interactions between a pupil and a tutor; (2) the standards
11 used by the program to determine which pupils will be selected to receive tutoring under the
12 program; (3) the methods of tutoring under the program; (4) the requirements for a person to
13 be a tutor, including, without limitation, training requirements; (5) the instructional materials
14 used by the program; (6) the collection and reporting of required data concerning the
15 academic progress of a pupil receiving tutoring under the program; and (7) the security and
16 privacy of data concerning pupils who are provided tutoring under the program.
Assembly Amendment No. 388 to Assembly Bill No. 383 Page 4
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 388 of NRS is hereby amended by adding thereto a new
2 section to read as follows:
3 [1.] The [Department shall develop and, as necessary or appropriate, update
4 a list of high-impact tutoring programs that are available to provide academic
5 assistance to pupils in this State to supplement instruction in core academic
6 subjects and that meet the criteria established by the Department pursuant to
7 subsection 2 to be considered an evidence-based high-impact tutoring program.
8 The Department shall publish the list developed and updated pursuant to this
9 subsection on an Internet website maintained by the Department.
10 2. The Department shall] State Board may adopt regulations establishing
11 the criteria that a vendor that provides a high-impact tutoring program must
12 satisfy to [be included on the list of high-impact tutoring programs developed or
13 updated pursuant to subsection 1.] contract with the board of trustees of a school
14 district or the governing body of a charter school to provide such a program. The
15 regulations adopted pursuant to this subsection must establish criteria
16 concerning:
17 [(a)] 1. The frequency of interactions between a pupil and a tutor,
18 including, without limitation, a requirement that a pupil receive three tutoring
19 sessions during each week that the pupil receives tutoring under the program and
20 that each tutoring session is at least 30 minutes and the minimum number of
21 weeks for which a pupil must receive tutoring under the program.
22 [(b)] 2. The standards used by the program to determine which pupils will
23 be selected to receive tutoring under the program.
24 [(c)] 3. The methods of tutoring under the program, including, without
25 limitation, a maximum pupil-tutor ratio of four pupils per tutor, a requirement
26 that the pupil receive tutoring from not more than two different tutors, a
27 requirement to use assessments administered at prescribed intervals and other
28 measures of progress to guide and customize the tutoring provided to a pupil, and
29 the provision of tutoring to a pupil during the school day.
30 [(d)] 4. The qualifications of a person to serve as a tutor under the program,
31 including, without limitation, the training necessary for a person to serve as a
32 tutor.
33 [(e)] 5. The instructional materials used by the program, which must be
34 accessible, rigorous, research-based and aligned with any appropriate curricula
35 in the school in which the pupil is enrolled.
36 [(f)] 6. The data required to be collected by the program to monitor the
37 academic progress of pupils who are provided tutoring under the program and a
38 requirement to report the academic progress of each pupil to:
39 [(1)] (a) Administrators and teachers at the school in which the pupil is
40 enrolled; and
41 [(2)] (b) The parent or legal guardian of the pupil.
42 [(g)] 7. The methods used by the program to ensure the security and privacy
43 of data concerning pupils who are provided tutoring under the program, which
44 must be consistent with relevant state and federal privacy laws, including, without
45 limitation, the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, 20 U.S.C. §
46 1232g.
47 Sec. 2. [1.] This [section becomes effective upon passage and approval.
48 2. Section 1 of this] act becomes effective [:
Assembly Amendment No. 388 to Assembly Bill No. 383 Page 5
1 (a) Upon passage and approval for the purpose of adopting any regulations and
2 performing any other preparatory administrative tasks that are necessary to carry
3 out the provisions of this act; and
4 (b) On] on July 1, [2026, for all other purposes.] 2025.
