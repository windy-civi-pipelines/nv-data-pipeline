EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; transferring the administration of
the Graduate Medical Education Grant Program and the
Account for the Program from the Office of Science,
Innovation and Technology in the Office of the Governor
to the Department of Health and Human Services;
expanding the purposes for which institutions that provide
programs of residency training in this State may receive
grants under the Program; making an appropriation to the
Account for the Program; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes the Office of Science, Innovation and Technology in
2 the Office of the Governor and requires the Director of the Office to implement the
3 Graduate Medical Education Grant Program. (NRS 223.600, 223.610) Existing law:
-
*SB262_R1*
– 2 –
4 (1) requires the Office to establish and administer the Program to award grants to
5 institutions in this State seeking to create, expand or retain programs for residency
6 training and postdoctoral fellowships for physicians; (2) creates the Account for the
7 Graduate Medical Education Grant Program, administered by the Director; and (3)
8 creates the Advisory Council on Graduate Medical Education to evaluate
9 applications and make recommendations for such grants. (NRS 223.631-223.639)
10 Sections 3-7 of this bill transfer the Advisory Council and the duties to administer
11 the Program and the Account from the Office to the Department of Health and
12 Human Services. Sections 1 and 2 of this bill make conforming changes to remove
13 the Program and the Advisory Council from the duties required to be performed by
14 the Director. Sections 3 and 6 expand the grants that may be made from the
15 Account under the Program to include grants to institutions which operate programs
16 for residency training that are training a number of residents that exceeds the
17 number of full-time equivalent residents for which the institution receives certain
18 payments from Medicare. Section 8 of this bill makes an appropriation to the
19 Account for the Program.
