Section 1:
EXEMPT
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 147
SENATE BILL NO. 147–SENATOR DONDERO LOOP
PREFILED JANUARY 30, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Government Affairs
SUMMARY—Creates the Southern Nevada Regional Planning for
Economic Resiliency Pilot Program. (BDR S-683)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to regional planning; establishing the Advisory
Committee on Regional Planning for Economic
Resiliency; creating the Southern Nevada Regional
Planning for Economic Resiliency Pilot Program; making
an appropriation; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill establishes the Southern Nevada Regional Planning for Economic
2 Resiliency Pilot Program. Section 7 of this bill requires the Regional
3 Transportation Commission of Southern Nevada to establish the Advisory
4 Committee on Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency to assist the
5 Commission in carrying out the provisions of sections 1-10 of this bill. Section 8
6 creates the Program to: (1) evaluate the current regional planning process and the
7 existing and future priorities and needs in the region of Clark County, Nevada, with
8 a focus on infrastructure projects, transit services and economic development and
9 redevelopment projects; and (2) identify sources of federal, state and other money
10 that will support the continued infrastructure, transit and economic needs of the
11 region. Section 9 requires the Commission to conduct the Program with a focus on
12 certain population centers in the region and to study and perform community
13 outreach on issues relating to regional planning for economic development and
14 resiliency in the region.
15 Section 10 requires: (1) the Commission to prepare a regional infrastructure
16 plan for economic growth and resiliency in the region and a written report
17 describing the activities, findings, conclusions and recommendations of the
18 Advisory Committee; and (2) the Commission to transmit a copy of the plan and
19 report to the Director of the Legislative Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Joint
20 Interim Standing Committee on Government Affairs.
21 Sections 2-6 define certain terms relating to the Program. Section 1 applies
22 these definitions to the provisions of sections 1-10.
-

23 Section 11 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Commission to establish
24 and implement the Program and for a staff member or a contractor to assist in the
25 Program and to carry out the provisions of sections 1-10.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. As used in sections 1 to 10, inclusive, of this act,
2 unless the context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined
3 in sections 2 to 6, inclusive, of this act have the meanings ascribed
4 to them in those sections.
5 Sec. 2. “Advisory Committee” means the Advisory Committee
6 on Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency established pursuant
7 to section 7 of this act.
8 Sec. 3. “Commission” means the Regional Transportation
9 Commission of Southern Nevada.
10 Sec. 4. “Program” means the Southern Nevada Regional
11 Planning for Economic Resiliency Pilot Program created by section
12 8 of this act.
13 Sec. 5. “Project” means any structure, facility, undertaking or
14 system which a local government is authorized to acquire, improve,
15 equip, maintain or operate, which may consist of all kinds of
16 personal and real property.
17 Sec. 6. “Region” means Clark County, Nevada.
18 Sec. 7. 1. The Commission shall establish the Advisory
19 Committee on Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency to assist
20 the Commission in carrying out the provisions of sections 1 to 10,
21 inclusive, of this act.
22 2. The Commission shall appoint at least nine members to the
23 Advisory Committee which must be comprised of various
24 stakeholders in the region who must have experience in regional
25 planning, economic development or redevelopment, workforce
26 development, infrastructure development or transit services.
27 3. The members of the Advisory Committee must include,
28 without limitation:
29 (a) One member from the regional development authority which
30 has entered into a contract with the Office of Economic
31 Development to serve the region; and
32 (b) At least one member each from the following local
33 governments:
34 (1) The City of Las Vegas;
35 (2) The City of Henderson;
36 (3) The City of North Las Vegas;
37 (4) Boulder City;
38 (5) The City of Mesquite; and
-
*SB147_R1*
– 3 –
1 (6) Clark County.
2 4. Each member of the Advisory Committee serves a term of 2
3 years.
4 5. The Advisory Committee shall elect a Chair and Vice Chair
5 from among its members at the first meeting of the Advisory
6 Committee.
7 6. A majority of the members of the Advisory Committee
8 constitutes a quorum for the transaction of business, and a majority
9 of those members present at any meeting is sufficient for any
10 official action taken by the Advisory Committee.
11 7. Each member of the Advisory Committee serves without
12 compensation and is not entitled to receive a per diem allowance or
13 travel expenses.
14 Sec. 8. 1. The Southern Nevada Regional Planning for
15 Economic Resiliency Pilot Program is hereby created as a pilot
16 program to support and sustain the economic resiliency of the region
17 by:
18 (a) Evaluating the current regional planning process and the
19 existing and future priorities and needs in the region, with a focus on
20 infrastructure projects, transit services and economic development
21 and redevelopment projects; and
22 (b) Identifying sources of federal, state and other money that
23 will support the continued infrastructure, transit and economic needs
24 of the growing population in the region.
25 2. The Commission shall, with advice from the Advisory
26 Committee, oversee the Program.
27 Sec. 9. 1. The Commission shall conduct the Program to
28 study and perform community outreach on issues relating to
29 regional planning for economic development and resiliency in the
30 region with a focus on the following population centers:
31 (a) The City of Las Vegas;
32 (b) The City of Henderson;
33 (c) The City of North Las Vegas;
34 (d) Boulder City;
35 (e) The City of Mesquite; and
36 (f) Any other urbanized section of the Las Vegas Valley.
37 2. The Program conducted pursuant to subsection 1 must,
38 without limitation:
39 (a) Identify existing, planned and proposed areas for economic
40 development, redevelopment and diversification, such as
41 redevelopment areas, existing underserved neighborhoods with
42 opportunities for reinvestment, opportunity zones, job creation
43 zones, Southern Nevada Strong opportunity sites, areas identified in
44 the Comprehensive Economic Development Strategy for Southern
45 Nevada developed by the Las Vegas Global Economic Alliance,
-
*SB147_R1*
– 4 –
1 innovation districts, large-scale industrial areas, any activity centers
2 identified by Southern Nevada Strong, parcels with transit-oriented
3 development or mixed-use zoning, activity centers identified by the
4 Brookings Institution and any areas with major development;
5 (b) Review existing plans for the population centers identified in
6 subsection 1 for capital improvements and other planning efforts
7 and, based on such plans, identify economic development centers
8 with planned infrastructure projects and transit services, which may
9 be at any stage in the planning process, and any unfunded needs
10 with regards to those planned infrastructure projects and transit
11 services;
12 (c) Develop and implement a community outreach plan to:
13 (1) Increase community support of regional planning;
14 (2) Obtain community input on a list of economic
15 development and redevelopment projects recommended by the
16 Advisory Committee to be regionally significant and which support
17 community goals, such as attracting target industries, diversifying
18 the economy or locating transit, jobs and housing within the same
19 location; and
20 (3) Garner community input regarding infrastructure projects
21 and transit services that are regionally significant and necessary to
22 support the list of economic development and redevelopment
23 projects and support the region overall;
24 (d) Develop planning-level cost estimates that include transit,
25 capital, operations and maintenance and infrastructure design,
26 construction and maintenance;
27 (e) Complete a high-level evaluation of the benefits of
28 infrastructure projects and transit services that support the economic
29 development and redevelopment priorities identified in the
30 Comprehensive Economic Development Strategy for Southern
31 Nevada and by Southern Nevada Strong;
32 (f) Develop additional criteria to evaluate and prioritize
33 economic development and redevelopment projects in the region;
34 (g) Develop packages of infrastructure projects and transit
35 services, which identify for each package, project and service, the
36 costs and benefits, current stage of development, resources already
37 committed and any additional money needed;
38 (h) Develop a prioritized list of infrastructure projects and transit
39 services that support the economic development and redevelopment
40 centers and projects that are regionally significant;
41 (i) Identify packages of economic development and
42 redevelopment projects that could be submitted for federal grants
43 and, if feasible, initiate an application for grants for any such
44 package; and
-
*SB147_R1*
– 5 –
1 (j) Evaluate the regional planning development process and
2 determine the steps, roles and resources needed to continue regional
3 coordination.
4 Sec. 10. On or before June 30, 2027:
5 1. The Commission shall prepare:
6 (a) A regional infrastructure plan for economic growth and
7 resiliency in the region, which must identify, without limitation:
8 (1) Infrastructure projects and transit services that support
9 access to existing, planned and proposed economic development and
10 redevelopment centers and projects that are regionally significant;
11 and
12 (2) Potential federal, state and other resources that may be
13 available to support the projects and services identified pursuant to
14 subparagraph (1); and
15 (b) A written report describing the activities, findings,
16 conclusions and recommendations of the Advisory Committee.
17 2. The Commission shall submit a copy of the plan and report
18 prepared pursuant to subsection 1 to the Director of the Legislature
19 Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing
20 Committee on Government Affairs.
21 Sec. 11. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State
22 General Fund to the Regional Transportation Commission of
23 Southern Nevada the sum of $650,000 to establish and implement
24 the Southern Nevada Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency
25 Pilot Program created by section 8 of this act and for a staff member
26 or contractor to assist the Commission and the Advisory Committee
27 on Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency created by section 7
28 of this act in carrying out the provisions of sections 1 to 10,
29 inclusive, of this act.
30 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by
31 subsection 1 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30,
32 2027, by the entity to which the appropriation is made or any entity
33 to which money from the appropriation is granted or otherwise
34 transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
35 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after
36 September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
37 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently
38 granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State General
39 Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
40 Sec. 12. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025, and
41 expires by limitation on June 30, 2027.
