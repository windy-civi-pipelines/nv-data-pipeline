SUMMARY—Creates the Southern Nevada Regional Planning for Economic
Resiliency Pilot Program. (BDR S-683)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included in

EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to regional planning; establishing the Advisory Committee on
Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency; creating the Southern
Nevada Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency Pilot Program;
making an appropriation; and providing other matters properly relating
thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill establishes the Southern Nevada Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency
2 Pilot Program. Section 7 of this bill requires the Regional Transportation Commission of
3 Southern Nevada to establish the Advisory Committee on Regional Planning for Economic
4 Resiliency to assist the Commission in carrying out the provisions of sections 1-10 of this bill.
5 Section 8 creates the Program to: (1) evaluate the current regional planning process and the
6 existing and future priorities and needs in the region of Clark County, Nevada, with a focus on
7 infrastructure projects, transit services and economic development and redevelopment
8 projects; and (2) identify sources of federal, state and other money that will support the
9 continued infrastructure, transit and economic needs of the region. Section 9 requires the
10 Commission to conduct the Program with a focus on certain population centers in the region
11 and to study and perform community outreach on issues relating to regional planning for
12 economic development and resiliency in the region.
13 Section 10 requires: (1) the Commission to prepare a regional infrastructure plan for
14 economic growth and resiliency in the region and a written report describing the activities,
15 findings, conclusions and recommendations of the Advisory Committee; and (2) the
16 Commission to transmit a copy of the plan and report to the Director of the Legislative
17 Counsel Bureau for transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Government
18 Affairs.
19 Sections 2-6 define certain terms relating to the Program. Section 1 applies these
20 definitions to the provisions of sections 1-10.
21 Section 11 of this bill makes an appropriation to the Commission to establish and
22 implement the Program and for a staff member or a contractor to assist in the Program and to
23 carry out the provisions of sections 1-10.
Senate Amendment No. 845 to Senate Bill No. 147 First Reprint Page 4
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. As used in sections 1 to 10, inclusive, of this act, unless the
2 context otherwise requires, the words and terms defined in sections 2 to 6,
3 inclusive, of this act have the meanings ascribed to them in those sections.
4 Sec. 2. “Advisory Committee” means the Advisory Committee on Regional
5 Planning for Economic Resiliency established pursuant to section 7 of this act.
6 Sec. 3. “Commission” means the Regional Transportation Commission of
7 Southern Nevada.
8 Sec. 4. “Program” means the Southern Nevada Regional Planning for
9 Economic Resiliency Pilot Program created by section 8 of this act.
10 Sec. 5. “Project” means any structure, facility, undertaking or system which a
11 local government is authorized to acquire, improve, equip, maintain or operate,
12 which may consist of all kinds of personal and real property.
13 Sec. 6. “Region” means Clark County, Nevada.
14 Sec. 7. 1. The Commission shall establish the Advisory Committee on
15 Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency to assist the Commission in carrying
16 out the provisions of sections 1 to 10, inclusive, of this act.
17 2. The Commission shall appoint at least nine members to the Advisory
18 Committee which must be comprised of various stakeholders in the region who
19 must have experience in regional planning, economic development or
20 redevelopment, workforce development, infrastructure development or transit
21 services.
22 3. The members of the Advisory Committee must include, without limitation:
23 (a) One member from the regional development authority which has entered
24 into a contract with the Office of Economic Development to serve the region; and
25 (b) At least one member each from the following local governments:
26 (1) The City of Las Vegas;
27 (2) The City of Henderson;
28 (3) The City of North Las Vegas;
29 (4) Boulder City;
30 (5) The City of Mesquite; and
31 (6) Clark County.
32 4. Each member of the Advisory Committee serves a term of 2 years.
33 5. The Advisory Committee shall elect a Chair and Vice Chair from among its
34 members at the first meeting of the Advisory Committee.
35 6. A majority of the members of the Advisory Committee constitutes a
36 quorum for the transaction of business, and a majority of those members present at
37 any meeting is sufficient for any official action taken by the Advisory Committee.
38 7. Each member of the Advisory Committee serves without compensation and
39 is not entitled to receive a per diem allowance or travel expenses.
40 Sec. 8. 1. The Southern Nevada Regional Planning for Economic
41 Resiliency Pilot Program is hereby created as a pilot program to support and sustain
42 the economic resiliency of the region by:
43 (a) Evaluating the current regional planning process and the existing and future
44 priorities and needs in the region, with a focus on infrastructure projects, transit
45 services and economic development and redevelopment projects; and
46 (b) Identifying sources of federal, state and other money that will support the
47 continued infrastructure, transit and economic needs of the growing population in
48 the region.
Senate Amendment No. 845 to Senate Bill No. 147 First Reprint Page 5
1 2. The Commission shall, with advice from the Advisory Committee, oversee
2 the Program.
3 Sec. 9. 1. The Commission shall conduct the Program to study and perform
4 community outreach on issues relating to regional planning for economic
5 development and resiliency in the region with a focus on the following population
6 centers:
7 (a) The City of Las Vegas;
8 (b) The City of Henderson;
9 (c) The City of North Las Vegas;
10 (d) Boulder City;
11 (e) The City of Mesquite; and
12 (f) Any other urbanized section of the Las Vegas Valley.
13 2. The Program conducted pursuant to subsection 1 must, without limitation:
14 (a) Identify existing, planned and proposed areas for economic development,
15 redevelopment and diversification, such as redevelopment areas, existing
16 underserved neighborhoods with opportunities for reinvestment, opportunity zones,
17 job creation zones, Southern Nevada Strong opportunity sites, areas identified in
18 the Comprehensive Economic Development Strategy for Southern Nevada
19 developed by the Las Vegas Global Economic Alliance, innovation districts, large-
20 scale industrial areas, any activity centers identified by Southern Nevada Strong,
21 parcels with transit-oriented development or mixed-use zoning, activity centers
22 identified by the Brookings Institution and any areas with major development;
23 (b) Review existing plans for the population centers identified in subsection 1
24 for capital improvements and other planning efforts and, based on such plans,
25 identify economic development centers with planned infrastructure projects and
26 transit services, which may be at any stage in the planning process, and any
27 unfunded needs with regards to those planned infrastructure projects and transit
28 services;
29 (c) Develop and implement a community outreach plan to:
30 (1) Increase community support of regional planning;
31 (2) Obtain community input on a list of economic development and
32 redevelopment projects recommended by the Advisory Committee to be regionally
33 significant and which support community goals, such as attracting target industries,
34 diversifying the economy or locating transit, jobs and housing within the same
35 location; and
36 (3) Garner community input regarding infrastructure projects and transit
37 services that are regionally significant and necessary to support the list of economic
38 development and redevelopment projects and support the region overall;
39 (d) Develop planning-level cost estimates that include transit, capital,
40 operations and maintenance and infrastructure design, construction and
41 maintenance;
42 (e) Complete a high-level evaluation of the benefits of infrastructure projects
43 and transit services that support the economic development and redevelopment
44 priorities identified in the Comprehensive Economic Development Strategy for
45 Southern Nevada and by Southern Nevada Strong;
46 (f) Develop additional criteria to evaluate and prioritize economic development
47 and redevelopment projects in the region;
48 (g) Develop packages of infrastructure projects and transit services, which
49 identify for each package, project and service, the costs and benefits, current stage
50 of development, resources already committed and any additional money needed;
51 (h) Develop a prioritized list of infrastructure projects and transit services that
52 support the economic development and redevelopment centers and projects that are
53 regionally significant;
Senate Amendment No. 845 to Senate Bill No. 147 First Reprint Page 6
1 (i) Identify packages of economic development and redevelopment projects
2 that could be submitted for federal grants and, if feasible, initiate an application for
3 grants for any such package; and
4 (j) Evaluate the regional planning development process and determine the
5 steps, roles and resources needed to continue regional coordination.
6 Sec. 10. On or before June 30, 2027:
7 1. The Commission shall prepare:
8 (a) A regional infrastructure plan for economic growth and resiliency in the
9 region, which must identify, without limitation:
10 (1) Infrastructure projects and transit services that support access to
11 existing, planned and proposed economic development and redevelopment centers
12 and projects that are regionally significant; and
13 (2) Potential federal, state and other resources that may be available to
14 support the projects and services identified pursuant to subparagraph (1); and
15 (b) A written report describing the activities, findings, conclusions and
16 recommendations of the Advisory Committee.
17 2. The Commission shall submit a copy of the plan and report prepared
18 pursuant to subsection 1 to the Director of the Legislature Counsel Bureau for
19 transmittal to the Joint Interim Standing Committee on Government Affairs.
20 Sec. 11. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to the
21 Regional Transportation Commission of Southern Nevada the sum of [$650,000]
22 $500,000 to establish and implement the Southern Nevada Regional Planning for
23 Economic Resiliency Pilot Program created by section 8 of this act and for a staff
24 member or contractor to assist the Commission and the Advisory Committee on
25 Regional Planning for Economic Resiliency created by section 7 of this act in
26 carrying out the provisions of sections 1 to 10, inclusive, of this act.
27 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1 must not
28 be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to which the
29 appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
30 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
31 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after September 17, 2027, by
32 either the entity to which the money was appropriated or the entity to which the
33 money was subsequently granted or transferred, and must be reverted to the State
34 General Fund on or before September 17, 2027.
35 Sec. 12. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025, and expires by limitation
36 on June 30, 2027.
