Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to the Department of
Indigent Defense Services. (BDR 14-492)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to indigent services; revising provisions governing
the Executive Director of the Department of Indigent
Defense Services and the State Public Defender; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law creates the Department of Indigent Defense Services and requires
2 the Governor to appoint the Executive Director of the Department, who serves at
3 the pleasure of the Governor. (NRS 180.400) Section 25.5 of this bill instead: (1)
4 requires the Executive Director to serve at the pleasure of the Board on Indigent
5 Defense Services; and (2) authorizes the Board to remove the Executive Director
6 only for good cause, unless an exception applies. Section 25.5 also specifies that
7 the Executive Director serves a term of 4 years and may be reappointed.
8 Existing law creates the Office of the State Public Defender within the
9 Department and requires the Governor to appoint the State Public Defender. (NRS
10 180.010) Section 16 of this bill instead requires the Executive Director to appoint
11 the State Public Defender. Section 16 also authorizes the Executive Director to
12 reappoint the State Public Defender.
13 Existing law requires the Executive Director and the State Public Defender to
14 be licensed to practice law in this State. (NRS 180.010, 180.400) Sections 16 and
15 25.5 instead require each such person to be a member of the State Bar of Nevada or
16 otherwise authorized to practice law in this State under the rules of the Supreme
17 Court.
18 Section 25.7 of this bill authorizes the person serving as the State Public
19 Defender on the effective date of this bill to serve the remainder of the term for
20 which he or she was appointed. Section 25.7 also provides that the person serving
-

21 as the Executive Director on the effective date of this bill is deemed to have been
22 appointed to a term of 4 years beginning on that date.

13 180.400 1. The Department of Indigent Defense Services is
14 hereby created.
15 2. The Governor shall appoint the Executive Director of the
16 Department [must be appointed by the Governor] from a list of three
17 persons recommended by the Board. The Executive Director serves
18 a term of 4 years, and until a successor is appointed and qualified.
19 The Executive Director may be reappointed.
20 3. [The] Except as otherwise provided in subsection 5, the
21 Executive Director:
22 (a) Is in the unclassified service of this State;
23 (b) Serves at the pleasure of the [Governor,] Board, except that
24 the Executive Director may only be removed [upon a finding of
25 incompetence, neglect of duty, commission of an act that constitutes

30 the State of Nevada [;] pursuant to the rules of the Supreme Court;
31 and

33 shall not engage in any other gainful employment or occupation.
34 4. The Executive Director may, within the limits of money
35 available for this purpose, employ or enter into a contract for the
36 services of such employees or consultants as is necessary to carry
37 out the provisions of this chapter.

39 the Executive Director upon a finding that the Executive Director:
40 (a) Engaged in criminal conduct, whether or not the conduct
41 occurred in office; or
42 (b) Committed an act that constitutes malfeasance or
43 nonfeasance in office. For the purposes of this paragraph, an
44 otherwise lawful action taken within the scope of the statutory

2 malfeasance or nonfeasance.
3  The Legislature declares that the purpose of this subsection is
4 to uphold the public policy that an indigent defense system must
5 be independent in order to provide constitutionally adequate
6 representation, as recognized by the American Bar Association
7 Ten Principles of a Public Defense Delivery System, as published
8 on November 9, 2023.
9 Sec. 25.7. Notwithstanding the amendatory provisions of this
10 act:
11 1. The State Public Defender who was appointed pursuant to

15 2. The Executive Director who was appointed pursuant to NRS
16 180.400 as that section existed on June 30, 2025, shall be deemed to
17 have been appointed to a term of 4 years on July 1, 2026.
