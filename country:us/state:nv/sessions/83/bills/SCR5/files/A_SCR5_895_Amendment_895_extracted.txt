EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION—Recognizing antimicrobial resistance
as a public health crisis and encouraging certain actions be taken in
response.
1 WHEREAS, Antimicrobial resistance is a growing threat to public health that
2 undermines the effectiveness of life-saving medications and leads to an estimated
3 35,000 deaths in the United States each year, according to the Centers for Disease
4 Control and Prevention of the United States Department of Health and Human
5 Services; and
6 WHEREAS, An antibiogram report prepared by the Division of Epidemiology
7 and Public Health Preparedness of Northern Nevada Public Health found a
8 statistically-significant increase in the rate of Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus
9 aureus, commonly referred to as MRSA, in Washoe County from 32 percent in
10 2022 to 36.2 percent in 2023; and
11 WHEREAS, A report published in 2023 by the National Institutes of Health
12 found MRSA to be one of the leading causes of hospital-acquired infections, often
13 leading to increased rates of morbidity and mortality, prolonged hospital stays and
14 increased costs of health care; and
15 [Whereas, The overuse and misuse of antibiotics in human medicine,
16 veterinary practice and agriculture significantly contributes to the development of
17 antimicrobial-resistant microorganisms; and]
18 WHEREAS, Rapid and accurate diagnostic testing is essential to reduce
19 inappropriate antibiotic use, ensuring that patients receive effective treatment while
20 limiting the spread of antimicrobial-resistant bacteria; and
21 WHEREAS, State Medicaid programs provide health care coverage to millions
22 of low-income persons nationwide, making each program a critical stakeholder in
23 addressing antimicrobial resistance through the development and deployment of
24 educational initiatives, data collection and tracking programs and other similar
25 policies; and
26 WHEREAS, The implementation of policies that encourage the development and
27 use of innovative diagnostic tools and stewardship programs requires collaboration
28 between public and private stakeholders; and
29 WHEREAS, Effective action to combat antimicrobial resistance requires a multi-
30 faceted approach that includes, without limitation, surveillance, prevention,
Senate Amendment No. 895 to Senate Concurrent Resolution No. 5 Page 4
1 education, research and access by persons to health care on an equitable basis; now,
2 therefore, be it
3 RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, THE ASSEMBLY
4 CONCURRING, That the Nevada Legislature hereby recognizes antimicrobial
5 resistance as a public health crisis requiring urgent and sustained action; and be it
6 further
7 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature encourages the Nevada Department of
8 Health and Human Services to integrate into and administer under Medicaid a
9 program for antimicrobial stewardship which may include, without limitation,
10 [providing incentives to providers for utilizing rapid diagnostic testing and]
11 adhering to evidence-based guidelines for the prescribing of antimicrobial drugs;
12 and be it further
13 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature supports the implementation and
14 deployment of public educational campaigns to raise awareness about antimicrobial
15 resistance, the importance of appropriate antibiotic use and the role of diagnostics
16 in improving health outcomes; and be it further
17 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature encourages the Director of the
18 Department of Health and Human Services to facilitate collaboration with and
19 among relevant public and private entities to promote awareness in combating
20 antimicrobial resistance; and be it further
21 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of the Senate prepare and transmit a copy of this
22 resolution to the Governor, the Director of the Department of Health and Human
23 Services and the Administrator of the Division of Health Care Financing and Policy
24 of the Department; and be it further
25 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon adoption.
