Section 1:
FIRST REPRINT S.C.R. 5
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 5–COMMITTEE
ON LEGISLATIVE OPERATIONS AND ELECTIONS
MAY 15, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Recognizes antimicrobial resistance as a public
health crisis and encourages certain actions be taken
in response. (BDR R-1234)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION—Recognizing
antimicrobial resistance as a public health crisis and
encouraging certain actions be taken in response.
1 WHEREAS, Antimicrobial resistance is a growing threat to public
2 health that undermines the effectiveness of life-saving medications
3 and leads to an estimated 35,000 deaths in the United States each
4 year, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention of
5 the United States Department of Health and Human Services; and
6 WHEREAS, An antibiogram report prepared by the Division of
7 Epidemiology and Public Health Preparedness of Northern Nevada
8 Public Health found a statistically-significant increase in the rate of
9 Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus, commonly referred to
10 as MRSA, in Washoe County from 32 percent in 2022 to 36.2
11 percent in 2023; and
12 WHEREAS, A report published in 2023 by the National Institutes
13 of Health found MRSA to be one of the leading causes of hospital-
14 acquired infections, often leading to increased rates of morbidity
15 and mortality, prolonged hospital stays and increased costs of health
16 care; and
17 WHEREAS, Rapid and accurate diagnostic testing is essential to
18 reduce inappropriate antibiotic use, ensuring that patients receive
19 effective treatment while limiting the spread of antimicrobial-
20 resistant bacteria; and
21 WHEREAS, State Medicaid programs provide health care
22 coverage to millions of low-income persons nationwide, making
23 each program a critical stakeholder in addressing antimicrobial
-

1 resistance through the development and deployment of educational
2 initiatives, data collection and tracking programs and other similar
3 policies; and
4 WHEREAS, The implementation of policies that encourage the
5 development and use of innovative diagnostic tools and stewardship
6 programs requires collaboration between public and private
7 stakeholders; and
8 WHEREAS, Effective action to combat antimicrobial resistance
9 requires a multi-faceted approach that includes, without limitation,
10 surveillance, prevention, education, research and access by persons
11 to health care on an equitable basis; now, therefore, be it
12 RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, THE
13 ASSEMBLY CONCURRING, That the Nevada Legislature hereby
14 recognizes antimicrobial resistance as a public health crisis
15 requiring urgent and sustained action; and be it further
16 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature encourages the Nevada
17 Department of Health and Human Services to integrate into and
18 administer under Medicaid a program for antimicrobial stewardship
19 which may include, without limitation, adhering to evidence-based
20 guidelines for the prescribing of antimicrobial drugs; and be it
21 further
22 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature supports the
23 implementation and deployment of public educational campaigns to
24 raise awareness about antimicrobial resistance, the importance of
25 appropriate antibiotic use and the role of diagnostics in improving
26 health outcomes; and be it further
27 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature encourages the
28 Director of the Department of Health and Human Services to
29 facilitate collaboration with and among relevant public and private
30 entities to promote awareness in combating antimicrobial resistance;
31 and be it further
32 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of the Senate prepare and
33 transmit a copy of this resolution to the Governor, the Director of
34 the Department of Health and Human Services and the
35 Administrator of the Division of Health Care Financing and Policy
36 of the Department; and be it further
37 RESOLVED, That this resolution becomes effective upon
38 adoption.
