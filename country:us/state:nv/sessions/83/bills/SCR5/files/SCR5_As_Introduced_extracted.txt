SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION—Recognizing
antimicrobial resistance as a public health crisis and
encouraging certain actions be taken in response.
1 WHEREAS, Antimicrobial resistance is a growing threat to public
2 health that undermines the effectiveness of life-saving medications
3 and leads to an estimated 35,000 deaths in the United States each
4 year, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention of
5 the United States Department of Health and Human Services; and
6 WHEREAS, An antibiogram report prepared by the Division of
7 Epidemiology and Public Health Preparedness of Northern Nevada
8 Public Health found a statistically-significant increase in the rate of
9 Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus, commonly referred to
10 as MRSA, in Washoe County from 32 percent in 2022 to 36.2
11 percent in 2023; and
12 WHEREAS, A report published in 2023 by the National Institutes
13 of Health found MRSA to be one of the leading causes of hospital-
14 acquired infections, often leading to increased rates of morbidity
15 and mortality, prolonged hospital stays and increased costs of health
16 care; and
17 WHEREAS, The overuse and misuse of antibiotics in human
18 medicine, veterinary practice and agriculture significantly
19 contributes to the development of antimicrobial-resistant
20 microorganisms; and
21 WHEREAS, Rapid and accurate diagnostic testing is essential to
22 reduce inappropriate antibiotic use, ensuring that patients receive
-
*SCR5*
– 2 –
1 effective treatment while limiting the spread of antimicrobial-
2 resistant bacteria; and
3 WHEREAS, State Medicaid programs provide health care
4 coverage to millions of low-income persons nationwide, making
5 each program a critical stakeholder in addressing antimicrobial
6 resistance through the development and deployment of educational
7 initiatives, data collection and tracking programs and other similar
8 policies; and
9 WHEREAS, The implementation of policies that encourage the
10 development and use of innovative diagnostic tools and stewardship
11 programs requires collaboration between public and private
12 stakeholders; and
13 WHEREAS, Effective action to combat antimicrobial resistance
14 requires a multi-faceted approach that includes, without limitation,
15 surveillance, prevention, education, research and access by persons
16 to health care on an equitable basis; now, therefore, be it
17 RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, THE
18 ASSEMBLY CONCURRING, That the Nevada Legislature hereby
19 recognizes antimicrobial resistance as a public health crisis
20 requiring urgent and sustained action; and be it further
21 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature encourages the Nevada
22 Department of Health and Human Services to integrate into and
23 administer under Medicaid a program for antimicrobial stewardship
24 which may include, without limitation, providing incentives to
25 providers for utilizing rapid diagnostic testing and adhering to
26 evidence-based guidelines for the prescribing of antimicrobial
27 drugs; and be it further
28 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature supports the
29 implementation and deployment of public educational campaigns to
30 raise awareness about antimicrobial resistance, the importance of
31 appropriate antibiotic use and the role of diagnostics in improving
32 health outcomes; and be it further
33 RESOLVED, That the Nevada Legislature encourages the
34 Director of the Department of Health and Human Services to
35 facilitate collaboration with and among relevant public and private
36 entities to promote awareness in combating antimicrobial resistance;
37 and be it further
38 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of the Senate prepare and
39 transmit a copy of this resolution to the Governor, the Director of
40 the Department of Health and Human Services and the
41 Administrator of the Division of Health Care Financing and Policy
42 of the Department; and be it further
