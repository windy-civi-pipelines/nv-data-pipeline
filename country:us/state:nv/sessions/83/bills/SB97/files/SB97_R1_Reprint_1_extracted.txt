FIRST REPRINT S.B. 97
SENATE BILL NO. 97–COMMITTEE ON COMMERCE AND LABOR
(ON BEHALF OF THE JOINT INTERIM STANDING COMMITTEE
ON HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES)
PREFILED JANUARY 14, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Commerce and Labor
SUMMARY—Makes revisions to the Silver State Health Insurance
Exchange. (BDR 57-346)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health insurance; revising the membership of
the Board of Directors of the Silver State Health
Insurance Exchange; and providing other matters properly
relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law establishes the Silver State Health Insurance Exchange to facilitate
2 the purchase of subsidized health plans by certain small employers and natural
3 persons who are citizens of the United States or otherwise lawfully present in the
4 United States. (Chapter 695I of NRS)
5 Existing law creates the Board of Directors of the Exchange which consists of:
6 (1) five voting members appointed by the Governor; (2) one voting member
7 appointed by the Majority Leader of the Senate; (3) one voting member appointed
8 by the Speaker of the Assembly; and (4) three ex officio, nonvoting members.
9 (NRS 695I.300) This bill requires that one member appointed by the Governor be a
10 member of an Indian tribe located in this State with expertise in health care
11 administration for an Indian tribe.

1 Sec. 3. NRS 695I.300 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 695I.300 1. The governing authority of the Exchange is the
3 Board, consisting of seven voting members and three ex officio
4 nonvoting members.
5 2. Subject to the provisions of subsections 3 to 6, inclusive:
6 (a) The Governor shall appoint [five] :
7 (1) Four voting members of the Board [;] who have
8 expertise in an area described in subsection 3; and
9 (2) One voting member of the Board who is a member of an
10 Indian tribe which is located in this State who has expertise in
11 health care administration for an Indian tribe.
12 (b) The Senate Majority Leader shall appoint one voting
13 member of the Board; and
14 (c) The Speaker of the Assembly shall appoint one voting
15 member of the Board.
16 3. Each voting member of the Board must have:
17 (a) Expertise in the sale or marketing of individual or small
18 employer health insurance;
19 (b) Expertise in health care administration, health care
20 financing, health information technology or health insurance;
21 (c) Expertise in the administration of health care delivery
22 systems;
23 (d) Experience as a consumer who would benefit from services
24 provided by the Exchange; or
25 (e) Experience as a consumer advocate, including, without
26 limitation, experience in consumer outreach and education for those
27 who would benefit from services provided by the Exchange.
28 4. When making an appointment pursuant to subsection 2, the
29 Governor, the Majority Leader and the Speaker of the Assembly
30 shall consider the collective expertise and experience of the voting
31 members of the Board and shall attempt to make each appointment
32 so that:
33 (a) The areas of expertise and experience described in
34 subsection 3 are collectively represented by the voting members of
35 the Board; and
36 (b) The voting members of the Board represent a range and
37 diversity of skills, knowledge, experience and geographic and
38 stakeholder perspectives.
39 5. When making an appointment pursuant to subsection 2, the
40 Governor, the Majority Leader and the Speaker of the Assembly
41 shall, as vacancies on the Board occur, ensure that not more than
42 two voting members of the Board represent any particular area of
43 expertise or experience described in paragraph (a), (b), (c), (d) or (e)
44 of subsection 3. The voting member described in subparagraph (2)
45 of paragraph (a) of subsection 2 does not represent an area of

3 6. A voting member of the Board may not be a Legislator or

24 become effective upon passage and approval.

30 federal waiver described in paragraph (b) of subsection 2 of section
31 1 of this act and performing any other preparatory administrative
32 tasks that are necessary to carry out the provisions of this act; and
33 (b) On January 1, 2028, for all other purposes.
