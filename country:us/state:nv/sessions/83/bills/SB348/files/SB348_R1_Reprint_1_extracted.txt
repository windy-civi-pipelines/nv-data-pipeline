Section 1:
REQUIRES TWO-THIRDS MAJORITY VOTE (§ 3)
FIRST REPRINT S.B. 348
SENATE BILL NO. 348–SENATORS PAZINA; AND BUCK
MARCH 13, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Health and Human Services
SUMMARY—Makes revisions relating to laboratory testing.
(BDR 40-123)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: May have Fiscal Impact.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included
in Executive Budget.
CONTAINS UNFUNDED MANDATE (§ 3)
(NOT REQUESTED BY AFFECTED LOCAL GOVERNMENT)
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to health care; establishing a fee to be charged by
the State Public Health Laboratory for certain
examinations and tests; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Existing law requires the State Public Health Laboratory to make available, at
2 such charges as may be established, to health officials, the Director of the State
3 Department of Agriculture and licensed physicians of the State, proper laboratory
4 facilities for the prompt diagnosis of communicable diseases. (NRS 439.240)
5 Existing law requires the State Board of Health to adopt regulations governing
6 examinations and tests required for the discovery in infants of preventable or
7 inheritable disorders. Existing law further requires any physician, midwife, nurse,
8 freestanding birthing center or hospital of any nature attending or assisting any
9 infant, or the person who gave birth to any infant, at childbirth to: (1) examine the
10 infant in accordance with those regulations; and (2) collect and send to the State
11 Public Health Laboratory any specimens needed for the examinations and tests that
12 must be performed by a laboratory. (NRS 442.008) Section 3 of this bill requires
13 the State Public Health Laboratory to charge a fee of $150 for the required
14 examinations and tests for the discovery in infants of preventable or inheritable
15 disorders. Section 1 of this bill makes a conforming change to clarify that the State
16 Public Health Laboratory is not authorized to establish a fee for the services for
17 which a fee is fixed by section 3.

THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. NRS 439.240 is hereby amended to read as follows:
2 439.240 1. The University of Nevada School of Medicine
3 shall maintain the State Public Health Laboratory, and may
4 designate, establish or maintain such branch laboratories as may be
5 necessary.
6 2. The purpose of the State Public Health Laboratory is:
7 (a) To make available, at such charges as may be established [,]
8 or are prescribed by NRS 442.008, to health officials, the Director
9 of the State Department of Agriculture and licensed physicians of
10 the State, proper laboratory facilities for the prompt diagnosis of
11 communicable diseases.
12 (b) To make necessary examinations and analyses of water,
13 natural ice, sewage, milk, food and clinical material.
14 (c) To conduct research into the nature, cause, diagnosis and
15 control of diseases.
16 (d) To undertake such other technical and laboratory duties as
17 are in the interest of the health of the general public.
18 3. The person in charge of the State Public Health Laboratory,
19 or the person’s designee, must be a skilled bacteriologist.
20 4. The person in charge of the State Public Health Laboratory
21 may have such technical assistants as that person, in cooperation
22 with the University of Nevada School of Medicine, considers
23 necessary.
24 5. Reports of investigations conducted at the State Public
25 Health Laboratory may be published from time to time in bulletins
26 and circulars.
27 6. If the University of Nevada School of Medicine designates a
28 branch laboratory pursuant to subsection 1 that is operated or
29 controlled by a public agency other than the University of Nevada
30 School of Medicine, the public agency and the University of Nevada
31 School of Medicine shall enter into a cooperative agreement
32 pursuant to NRS 277.080 to 277.180, inclusive, concerning the
33 branch laboratory. The cooperative agreement must include, without
34 limitation, provisions setting forth the powers and duties of each
35 party to the cooperative agreement.
36 Sec. 2. (Deleted by amendment.)
