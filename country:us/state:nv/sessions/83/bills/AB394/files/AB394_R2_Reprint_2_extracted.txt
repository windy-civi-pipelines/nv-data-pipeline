Section 1:
SECOND REPRINT A.B. 394
ASSEMBLY BILL NO. 394–ASSEMBLYMEMBER BROWN-MAY
MARCH 11, 2025
____________
JOINT SPONSORS: SENATORS BUCK, ROGICH AND TITUS
____________
Referred to Committee on Education
SUMMARY—Authorizes the Board of Regents of the University of
Nevada to require certain institutions to adopt
emergency response plans related to opioid
overdoses. (BDR 34-95)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Yes.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to higher education; authorizing the Board of
Regents of the University of Nevada to require each
institution within the Nevada System of Higher Education
to adopt an emergency response plan to address opioid-
related drug overdoses; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 This bill authorizes the Board of Regents of the University of Nevada to require
2 each institution within the Nevada System of Higher Education to adopt an
3 emergency response plan to address opioid-related drug overdoses at the institution.
4 This bill requires such an emergency response plan to include: (1) plans for
5 providing education on opioid-related drug overdoses; and (2) procedures for the
6 distribution and administration of opioid antagonists. This bill prohibits an
7 institution from adopting a plan that requires a person to complete training in order
8 to obtain or administer an opioid antagonist. This bill authorizes the Board of
9 Regents to adopt a model emergency response plan to assist institutions in
10 developing their own plans.
-
*AB394_R2*
– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 396 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The Board of Regents may require each institution within
4 the System to, in consultation with the Division of Public and
5 Behavioral Health of the Department of Health and Human
6 Services, adopt an emergency response plan to address opioid-
7 related drug overdoses at the institution. Such an emergency
8 response plan must include, without limitation:
9 (a) Plans for providing education on opioid-related drug
10 overdoses;
11 (b) Measures to respond compassionately to opioid-related
12 drug overdoses, including, without limitation:
13 (1) Emphasizing rescue breathing techniques; and
14 (2) Utilizing naloxone hydrochloride in the lowest dosage
15 effective to restore breathing;
16 (c) Procedures for the distribution and administration of
17 opioid antagonists, including, without limitation, ensuring ease of
18 access to opioid antagonists; and
19 (d) Provisions ensuring that a student will not be subject to
20 disciplinary action by the institution for obtaining an opioid
21 antagonist.
22 2. Each institution within the System may tailor the
23 emergency response plan described in subsection 1 to meet the
24 needs and capacity of the institution for implementing the plan. In
25 tailoring an emergency response plan, an institution may
26 determine:
27 (a) Whether training related to the plan will be provided in
28 person or virtually and synchronously or asynchronously;
29 (b) The locations at the institution where opioid antagonists
30 will be accessible; and
31 (c) The manner in which opioid antagonists will be distributed
32 at the institution.
33 3. An emergency response plan described in subsection 1 may
34 not require a person to complete any training in order to obtain or
35 administer an opioid antagonist.
36 4. The Board of Regents may adopt a model emergency
37 response plan that meets the requirements of this section to assist
38 institutions in developing their emergency response plans
39 pursuant to subsection 1. The Board of Regents may not require
40 an institution to utilize the model emergency response plan.
41 5. As used in this section:
-
*AB394_R2*
– 3 –
1 (a) “Opioid antagonist” has the meaning ascribed to it in
2 NRS 453C.040.
3 (b) “Opioid-related drug overdose” has the meaning ascribed
4 to it in NRS 453C.050.
5 Sec. 2. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval.
