Section 1:
S.B. 406
MARCH 20, 2025
____________
Referred to Committee on Judiciary
SUMMARY—Revises provisions relating to civil liability.
(BDR 3-1022)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: No.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT relating to civil liability; providing immunity from civil
liability to the State of Nevada, certain institutions of
higher education and certain officers or employees thereof
under certain circumstances; and providing other matters
properly relating thereto.
Legislative Counsel’s Digest:
1 Under existing law, the State of Nevada: (1) waives its immunity from liability
2 in civil actions; and (2) consents to have its liability determined in accordance with
3 the same rules of law governing civil actions against natural persons and
4 corporations. (NRS 41.031) Existing law creates certain exceptions to this waiver
5 of immunity from liability, thereby providing immunity from liability to certain
6 state actors under certain circumstances. (NRS 41.032-41.036) Section 1 of this bill
7 provides that the State, any university, college or community college which is part
8 of the Nevada System of Higher Education and any officer or employee thereof are
9 immune from civil liability for any damages sustained as a result of certain acts or
10 omissions of the State, institution of higher education, officer or employee in
11 response to or on the basis of the public-health crisis caused by the COVID-19
12 pandemic. Section 2 of this bill provides that the State retains its immunity from
13 liability in cases where immunity is granted under section 1. Section 3 of this bill
14 provides that the immunity from liability granted by section 1 applies retroactively
15 to any action commenced on or after March 12, 2020, for which a final judgment
16 has not been rendered. Section 4 of this bill provides that the immunity from
17 liability granted by section 1 expires by limitation on June 30, 2030.

– 2 –
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. Chapter 41 of NRS is hereby amended by adding
2 thereto a new section to read as follows:
3 1. The State of Nevada, any institution of higher education
4 and any officer or employee thereof are immune from civil liability
5 for damages sustained as a result of an act or omission of the
6 State, institution, officer or employee in response to or on the basis
7 of the public-health crisis caused by the COVID-19 pandemic
8 which impacted the provision of educational services by an
9 institution of higher education during the spring semester of 2020
10 if:
11 (a) The act or omission was reasonably related to public health
12 and safety and was performed in good faith; and
13 (b) The claim arises out of or in connection with tuition or fees
14 paid to an institution of higher education for the spring semester
15 of 2020.
16 2. For the purposes of subsection 1, an act or omission of the
17 State, any institution of higher education or any officer or
18 employee thereof shall be deemed to have:
19 (a) Impacted the provision of educational services by an
20 institution of higher education during the spring semester of 2020
21 if the act or omission:
22 (1) Caused the cessation of educational services for any
23 period;
24 (2) Caused the closure of a campus or facility operated by
25 or under the control of an institution of higher education in whole
26 or in part or otherwise limited access to any such campus or
27 facility in whole or in part;
28 (3) Modified the manner in which educational services
29 were provided, including, without limitation, by authorizing or
30 requiring a transition to online or otherwise remote instruction; or
31 (4) Otherwise interfered with the provision of educational
32 services.
33 (b) Been performed in good faith if the act or omission was a
34 good faith effort to comply with any applicable:
35 (1) Federal, state or local law, regulation or ordinance; or
36 (2) Written order or other document published by a federal,
37 state or local government or regulatory body.
38 3. The immunity granted by this section is in addition to any
39 other immunity recognized by law, and all such immunities are
40 cumulative, so that the application or attempted application of any
41 one does not bar the application or attempted application of any
42 other.
-
*SB406*
– 3 –
1 4. As used in this section:
2 (a) “Educational services” means any offering, program or
3 benefit provided by or in connection with an institution of higher
4 education. The term includes, without limitation, educational
5 instruction, academic support services and student services,
6 regardless of whether such services are provided directly by the
7 institution of higher education or by external entities as ancillary
8 to student engagement or development.
9 (b) “Institution of higher education” means a university,
10 college or community college which is part of the Nevada System
11 of Higher Education.
12 (c) “Student services” means any program, resource or
13 support activity provided by or in connection with an institution of
14 higher education for the purpose of enhancing the academic,
15 personal or professional development of students. The term
16 includes, without limitation, career services, health services and
17 ancillary student activities or extracurricular programs.
18 Sec. 2. NRS 41.031 is hereby amended to read as follows:
19 41.031 1. The State of Nevada hereby waives its immunity
20 from liability and action and hereby consents to have its liability
21 determined in accordance with the same rules of law as are applied
22 to civil actions against natural persons and corporations, except as
23 otherwise provided in NRS 41.032 to 41.038, inclusive, and section
24 1 of this act, 485.318, subsection 3 and any statute which expressly
25 provides for governmental immunity, if the claimant complies with
26 the limitations of NRS 41.010 or the limitations of NRS 41.032 to
27 41.036, inclusive [.] , and section 1 of this act. The State of Nevada
28 further waives the immunity from liability and action of all political
29 subdivisions of the State, and their liability must be determined in
30 the same manner, except as otherwise provided in NRS 41.032 to
31 41.038, inclusive, and section 1 of this act, subsection 3 and any
32 statute which expressly provides for governmental immunity, if the
33 claimant complies with the limitations of NRS 41.032 to 41.036,
34 inclusive [.] , and section 1 of this act.
35 2. An action may be brought under this section against the
36 State of Nevada or any political subdivision of the State. In any
37 action against the State of Nevada, the action must be brought in the
38 name of the State of Nevada on relation of the particular
39 department, commission, board or other agency of the State whose
40 actions are the basis for the suit. An action against the State of
41 Nevada must be filed in the county where the cause or some part
42 thereof arose or in Carson City. In an action against the State of
43 Nevada, the summons and a copy of the complaint must be served
44 upon:
-
*SB406*
– 4 –
1 (a) The Attorney General, or a person designated by the
2 Attorney General, at the Office of the Attorney General in Carson
3 City; and
4 (b) The person serving in the office of administrative head of the
5 named agency.
6 3. The State of Nevada does not waive its immunity from suit
7 conferred by Amendment XI of the Constitution of the United
8 States.
9 Sec. 3. The provisions of this act apply to all actions
10 commenced on or after March 12, 2020, for which a final judgment
11 has not been rendered.
12 Sec. 4. This act becomes effective upon passage and approval
13 and applies retroactively on and after March 12, 2020, and expires
14 by limitation on June 30, 2030.
