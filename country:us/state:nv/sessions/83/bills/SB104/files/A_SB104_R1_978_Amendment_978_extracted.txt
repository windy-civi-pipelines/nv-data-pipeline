S.B. No. 104—Makes an appropriation to the Other State Education Programs
Account for the creation and maintenance of school gardens.
(BDR S-539)

SENATE BILL NO. 104–SENATOR PAZINA
PREFILED JANUARY 21, 2025
_______________
JOINT SPONSORS: ASSEMBLYMEMBERS JAUREGUI,
MONROE-MORENO AND YEAGER
_______________
Referred to Committee on Finance
SUMMARY—Makes [an appropriation] appropriations to the Other State
Education Programs Account for allocation to certain
nonprofit organizations for the creation and maintenance of
school gardens [.] and to certain other nonprofit
organizations. (BDR S-539)
FISCAL NOTE: Effect on Local Government: No.
Effect on the State: Contains Appropriation not included in
Executive Budget.
~
EXPLANATION – Matter in bolded italics is new; matter between brackets [omitted material] is material to be omitted.
AN ACT [making] relating to governmental financial administration; making
an appropriation for allocation to nonprofit organizations to provide
programs for the creation and maintenance of school gardens; making
appropriations to certain other nonprofit organizations; and
providing other matters properly relating thereto.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEVADA, REPRESENTED IN
SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
1 Section 1. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
2 the Other State Education Programs Account in the State General Fund for the cost
3 of creating and maintaining programs for school gardens that meet the requirements
4 of subsection 3 the following sums:
5 For the Fiscal Year 2025-2026 ....................................................... $200,000
6 For the Fiscal Year 2026-2027 ....................................................... $200,000
7 2. The Department of Education shall allocate the money appropriated by
8 subsection 1 to nonprofit organizations to provide at a public school a program for a
9 school garden which meets the requirements set forth in subsection 3.
10 3. For a nonprofit organization to receive an allocation of money to provide a
11 program for a school garden pursuant to subsection 2, the program must:
12 (a) Create and maintain a school garden at the school.
13 (b) Have a curriculum that:
Assembly Amendment No. 978 to Senate Bill No. 104 First Reprint Page 4
1 (1) Includes a comprehensive science, technology, engineering and
2 mathematics school garden program. Such a program must include, without
3 limitation, a science, technology, engineering and mathematics curriculum for
4 outdoor or hydroponic gardens for pupils in kindergarten through grade 12 that is
5 tailored to pupils of the appropriate grade levels at the school;
6 (2) Is written specifically for Nevada and the desert environment of
7 Nevada;
8 (3) Complies with the standards of content and performance for a course of
9 study in science adopted by the State Board of Education pursuant to NRS 389.520;
10 (4) Uses experiential learning or project-based learning to teach science,
11 technology, engineering, arts and mathematics;
12 (5) Is designed with the assistance of teachers and other educational
13 personnel with experience at the appropriate grade levels at the school; and
14 (6) Involves supervised learning experiences for the pupils at the school in
15 a classroom and a school garden.
16 (c) Provide the school with assistance from members of the community,
17 including, without limitation, trained educators, local farmers and local chefs.
18 (d) Provide pupils with the:
19 (1) Ability to operate a farmer’s market to sell the produce from the school
20 garden; and
21 (2) Opportunity to have a local chef or employee of a school who works in
22 food services demonstrate how to cook a meal using the produce grown from the
23 school garden.
24 (e) Establish garden teams comprised of teachers and, if such persons are
25 available, parents and members of the community. Each garden team shall meet at
26 least once each month.
27 (f) Require any local nonprofit or community-based organization which will
28 provide services to implement the program for a school garden to have at least 2
29 years of experience implementing such a program.
30 4. Money allocated pursuant to subsection 2 may be used to:
31 (a) Provide professional development for teachers regarding the:
32 (1) Use of a school garden to teach pupils with disabilities, including,
33 without limitation, training for teaching such pupils science, technology,
34 engineering and mathematics curriculum and vocational training to create a career
35 path in horticulture;
36 (2) Development and implementation of science, technology, engineering,
37 arts and mathematics curricula that incorporate the use of a school garden;
38 (3) Development and implementation of training that may be provided to a
39 group or individually to teachers in how to establish and maintain school gardens to
40 increase the time teachers allocate to teaching science, technology, engineering and
41 mathematics; and
42 (4) Development and implementation of a food safety plan designed to
43 ensure that food grown in a school garden is properly handled and safe to sell and
44 consume;
45 (b) Pay for any travel expenses associated with the attendance of a teacher at
46 any training or conference relating to school gardens; and
47 (c) Pay for the costs of a conference regarding school gardens held in this
48 State.
49 5. As used in this section:
50 (a) “Public school” has the meaning ascribed to it in NRS 385.007.
51 (b) “School garden” includes, without limitation, a hydroponic garden.
52 Sec. 2. Upon acceptance of the money allocated pursuant to section 1 of this
53 act, a nonprofit organization agrees to:
Assembly Amendment No. 978 to Senate Bill No. 104 First Reprint Page 5
1 1. Prepare and transmit a report to the Interim Finance Committee on or
2 before October 1, 2026, that describes each expenditure made from the money
3 allocated pursuant to section 1 of this act from the date on which the money was
4 received by the nonprofit organization through June 30, 2026;
5 2. Prepare and transmit a final report to the Interim Finance Committee on or
6 before October 1, 2027, that describes each expenditure made from the money
7 allocated pursuant to section 1 of this act from the date on which the money was
8 received by the nonprofit organization through June 30, 2027; and
9 3. Upon request of the Legislative Commission, make available to the
10 Legislative Auditor any of the books, accounts, claims, reports, vouchers or other
11 records of information, confidential or otherwise, of the nonprofit organization,
12 regardless of their form or location, that the Legislative Auditor deems necessary to
13 conduct an audit of the use of the money allocated pursuant to section 1 of this act.
14 Sec. 3. Any balance of the sums appropriated by section 1 of this act
15 remaining at the end of the respective fiscal years must not be committed for
16 expenditure after June 30 of the respective fiscal years by the entity to which the
17 appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the appropriation is
18 granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any portion of the appropriated
19 money remaining must not be spent for any purpose after September 18, 2026, and
20 September 17, 2027, respectively, by either the entity to which the money was
21 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently granted or
22 transferred, and must be reverted to the State General Fund on or before September
23 18, 2026, and September 17, 2027, respectively.
24 Sec. 4. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
25 the Mob Museum the sum of $250,000 for the planning phase of its expansion.
26 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1
27 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to
28 which the appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the
29 appropriation is granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any
30 portion of the appropriated money remaining must not be spent for any
31 purpose after September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
32 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently granted or
33 transferred, and must be reverted to the State General Fund on or before
34 September 17, 2027.
35 Sec. 5. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
36 the National Council of Juvenile and Family Court Judges the sum of $100,000
37 for the purpose of supporting the Nevada Judicial Resource Center on
38 Firearms.
39 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1
40 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to
41 which the appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the
42 appropriation is granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any
43 portion of the appropriated money remaining must not be spent for any
44 purpose after September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
45 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently granted or
46 transferred, and must be reverted to the State General Fund on or before
47 September 17, 2027.
48 Sec. 6. 1. There is hereby appropriated from the State General Fund to
49 Raise the Future the sum of $250,000 to support its programs in this State.
50 2. Any remaining balance of the appropriation made by subsection 1
51 must not be committed for expenditure after June 30, 2027, by the entity to
52 which the appropriation is made or any entity to which money from the
53 appropriation is granted or otherwise transferred in any manner, and any
Assembly Amendment No. 978 to Senate Bill No. 104 First Reprint Page 6
1 portion of the appropriated money remaining must not be spent for any
2 purpose after September 17, 2027, by either the entity to which the money was
3 appropriated or the entity to which the money was subsequently granted or
4 transferred, and must be reverted to the State General Fund on or before
5 September 17, 2027.
6 [Sec. 4.] Sec. 7. This act becomes effective on July 1, 2025.
